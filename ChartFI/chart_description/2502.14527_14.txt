The figure presents a comparison of model scores for each of the unobserved turbines in wind-farm Ciabatta, utilizing two error metrics: NMSE in the top panel and JLL in the bottom panel, with dashed horizontal lines indicating mean scores per model. In the NMSE plot, values fluctuate notably from turbine to turbine, yet the meta approach (blue) tends to sit lowest overall, while the NP observed method (green) exhibits the most pronounced single spike around the mid-30s turbines where error briefly jumps above 40, standing out as a clear anomaly. Conversely, the JLL panel reveals a more consistent separation where the meta method maintains the highest mean level, hovering around the high 270s to 280, whereas CP, PP, and NP cluster together at a lower range in the mid-250s. A striking irregularity appears in the CP method's sharp dip early in the series around turbine 8, where JLL plunges far below the rest to approximately 150, implying a localized failure or mismatch for that turbine. Ultimately, for these otherwise unobserved turbines, it can be seen that on average the metamodel performs marginally better than all three of the other models for both the NMSE and JLL metrics, including the NP models despite not having observed data from these, establishing the meta approach as the most stable top performer in expectation while the other methods remain more susceptible to isolated, turbine-specific degradations.
