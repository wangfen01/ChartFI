This chart presents the normalized scores and 95% bootstrapping confidence intervals for the ranking and generative tasks on GPT-4o, segmented by demographic subgroups including Race/Ethnicity, Sex/Gender, Disability, Religion, Income, and National Origin. In the ranking task, measured by normalized mean choice value, stereotypical biases occur in the Sex/Gender category, where female students are scored significantly lower than male students, demonstrating alignment with common stereotypes. In contrast, the Disability subgroup reverses the stereotype, as physically disabled and neurodivergent students are scored higher than able-bodied and neurotypical ones. In the generative task, measured by normalized mean grade level, alignment with the reference characteristics is more substantial compared to the ranking task. Specifically, a strong ordering in the Reference characteristics at the bottom of the chart indicates alignment between the model outputs and the scoring strategy, with expert candidates receiving the highest scores followed by average and then beginner candidates.
