The heatmap illustrates the activation time in seconds for Group 1 participants across 18 attempts, where color intensity encodes the activation time and reveals a trend of decreasing activation time as learning progresses, demonstrated in lighter colors from left to right for each line/participant. Group 1 adopts the in-stages learning strategy with 5 strong-guidance attempts, 10 weak-guidance attempts and 3 no-guidance attempts, characterized by a distinct phase shift in performance stability. In the initial strong-guidance phase, activation times are highest and most variable, with several participants exhibiting large swings; most notably, P12 starts with an extremely high activation of 5.13 seconds and remains volatile, while P8, P9, and P16 also begin with notably slow activations. From attempt 6 onward, as the task transitions to weak guidance, times generally compress toward lower values, suggesting rapid adaptation, though this improvement is not strictly monotonic. Intermittent mid-phase regressions stand out as anomalies against the improving trend, such as P9’s sharp spike to 5.24 seconds at attempt 9, P17’s surge during attempts 8 and 9, and P16’s regression at attempt 12. By the final phase, 7 participants from Group 1 cannot successfully activate the Virtual Window under no guidance setting, resulting in significant missing data for P8 through P14 in the final three attempts. For the participants who do reach the no-guidance stage, activation times typically stabilize near or below 1 second, indicating continued efficiency, although individuals like P15 still show a high value of 2.4 seconds at attempt 18, implying that some participants retain residual difficulty even after guidance is removed.
