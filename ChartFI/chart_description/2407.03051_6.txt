This radar chart illustrates the relative performance of different quantized LLMs across twelve fine-grained skills, comparing a 16-bit weight baseline against four W4A16 variants: RTN, AWQ, KD, and QDPO. The 16-bit baseline maintains the most uniformly strong profile, setting a reference for stability near the 1.0 mark across nearly all axes, while the quantized models exhibit varying degrees of tradeoff. Among these compressed methods, QDPO shows a significant enhancement in most skills and achieves the abilities closest to the 16-bit weight baseline, even slightly exceeding it in areas such as harmlessness. Notably, QDPO significantly improves metacognition skills, whereas RTN, KD and AWQ significantly fall short in this dimension. KD remains generally competitive on correctness and comprehension but shows mild softness on completeness. AWQ tends to occupy the mid-pack, maintaining relatively solid scores on correctness and readability but facing pressure on insightfulness and completeness. In contrast, RTN significantly diminishes certain capabilities of the model, emerging as the most uneven approach; it displays a pronounced spike in insightfulness but suffers from clear drops in efficiency and correctness, alongside weaker performance across several reasoning-adjacent dimensions. Ultimately, the most significant shifts across methods are concentrated in insightfulness and efficiency, with QDPO providing the most reliable profile among 4-bit options due to its consistently near-baseline shape and lack of deep troughs.
