These violin plots of cold start time and components by pool size for all regions with normalized width illustrate performance distributions where dashed lines in the density plots represent quartiles. Across the five regions, larger resource pools tend to have longer median cold start times, with the ratio of median cold start times between the high and low resource pods ranging from approximately 1:1 in Region 5 to 5:1 in Region 3. While Region 3 exhibits the lowest and tightest cold start times for both sizes, Regions 1 and 2 exhibit noticeably longer upper tails, suggesting intermittent slow paths that can dominate user experience. Pod allocation time is the clearest source of extreme variability and these stages clearly show themselves in the multimodal distribution; for small resource pools, the pod allocation time tends to take less time, with a smaller proportion of cases expanding to further stages of the search with longer latency. Conversely, for larger resource pools, the search tends to expand more frequently to later stages, taking a longer time to allocate a pod in total, which explains the sporadic jumps by orders of magnitude observed in Regions 2 and 4. In terms of initialization, function code typically takes longer to deploy in larger pods, which is particularly pronounced in Regions 1, 2, 3, and 5, while Region 1 implies region-specific overhead with a broader spread. Similarly, the time taken to deploy dependencies is longer for larger pods compared to smaller ones; while most regions remain in the sub-second to low-second ranges, Region 1 stands out with a much higher large-configuration distribution consistent with slower dependency fetches. Finally, scheduling time displays a divergent pattern: for Regions 1, 3, and 4, smaller resource pods tend to have shorter scheduling times than larger pods, whereas Regions 2 and 5 see the opposite pattern, where large pods have shorter scheduling times.
