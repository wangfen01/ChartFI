This heatmap illustrates the Spearman correlation (in percentage) of test model rankings on WILDBENCH across different LLM judges using RocketEval (Supervised), where color intensity reflects the strength of the correlation and the left-side CoT column represents each model’s chain-of-thought capability score. The matrix reveals that RocketEval exhibits high consistency and reliability in evaluation, with the top tier dominated by Mistral-Nemo and Llama-3-8B, which consistently post very high correlation rates—often in the mid-to-high 90s—against most other models. Performance generally improves with model strength and size, as seen in the middle tier where models like Phi-3-mini-4k and Qwen2.5-3B show favorable results that bridge the gap between the top performers and smaller models. Notably, RocketEval brings significant improvements in cross-judge correlation, especially on the lightweight LLMs, although the lower tier, specifically Llama-3.2-1B and Qwen2.5-0.5B, still shows repeated large deficits and several strongly negative cells that signal a sharp capability cliff rather than a gradual decline. While CoT scores broadly track with overall strength, the relationship is not perfectly linear; some mid-range models with moderate CoT scores still achieve solid head-to-head consistency, suggesting that general capability and instruction tuning drive much of the advantage. Ultimately, the prevalence of high positive values across the majority of the matrix underscores how RocketEval stabilizes rankings across diverse judges, even when utilizing smaller models as evaluators.
