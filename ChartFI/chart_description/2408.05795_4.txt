These three scatterplots compare the runtime performance of various planning methods across the delivery, doors, maze, and rover domains, where each point represents a specific instance and the diagonal line indicates parity between the compared approaches. In the first panel, which compares RRT vs. LAZYRRT when using TAMPEST regardless of the refinement exploited, LAZYRRT is frequently slower and exhibits much higher variance, particularly in the Maze domain; notably, for the Delivery case, LAZYRRT timeouts in all cases while TAMPEST-RRT remains efficient. The second panel evaluates TAMPEST vs. Meta(ENHSP), both utilizing RRT and All-Refinements, revealing that delivery instances tend to sit above the diagonal, suggesting the meta approach is commonly slower than TAMPEST on that domain, while the maze domain remains highly dispersed with occasional large slowdowns. The third panel compares TAMPEST with All-Refinements against PDDLSTREAM-adaptive-RRT, showing the strongest performance shift as PDDLSTREAM-adaptive consistently times out even when TAMPEST easily finds solutions. This robustness advantage for TAMPEST stands out notably in the Maze domain, where PDDLSTREAM-adaptive-RRT is almost always slower and frequently pushed to the upper runtime cap. Across all panels, TAMPEST performs particularly well in the Maze and Delivery domains, maintaining stability and finding solutions while alternative methods often hit the maximum runtime or memory limits, as evidenced by the dense clusters of points along the top edge of the plots for the competing methods.
