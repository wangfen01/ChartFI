The two heatmaps illustrate model similarity when grouped by frequency band versus measure, revealing that frequency-band aggregation fosters strong, coherent clusters while measure-based grouping introduces fragmentation and trade-offs. In the frequency-band view, most models exhibit broad positive alignment, particularly among tree-based and linear methods. Specifically, CatBoost and XGBoost displayed a perfect positive correlation, indicating identical behavior across bands, while Lasso and Elastic Net also aligned perfectly. The primary exception to this coherence is the K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) model, which acts as a distinct outlier with negative relationships to most other methods. Notably, employing the bagging strategy to the KNN model (BaggedKNN) showcased a perfect negative correlation to the basic KNN, and more similar behavior to the other models, effectively reversing the performance pattern to align more closely with the linear regularized models. When grouped by measure, the structural consistency weakens, characterized by lower overall correlations and mixed associations. In this view, the highest correlation for the measure importance rank order was between the LassoRegression and ElasticNet model, which retained a perfect positive link, followed by the correlation between the KernalRidge and BaggedKNN model. These positive pairings, however, are separated by distinct trade-offs; for instance, the Lasso and Elastic Net pair exhibits strong negative correlations with Kernel Ridge and BKNN, suggesting that model rankings are highly sensitive to the specific performance metric applied rather than the frequency content alone.
