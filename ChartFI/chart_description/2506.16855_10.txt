The normalized Euclidean distance matrix of a collection of time series is presented for the original space in the top row and the latent space in the bottom row across four columns representing different types of noise: increasing sampling rate, decreasing sampling rate, random temporal shift, and added noise. In the original space, all four conditions preserve a strong diagonal structure with distances increasing smoothly away from the diagonal, indicating a stable, ordered progression where nearby indices remain most similar and far-apart indices most dissimilar; however, for all four types of noise, the Euclidean distance will increase quickly with the level of noise, as evidenced by the darker blue intensities in the off-diagonal regions. In contrast, the proposed framework remains effective in the presence of all four types of noise and can mine the similarity between the original time series and the ones with noise, resulting in noticeably flatter, more homogenized distance patterns in the latent space with weaker contrast between near and far pairs. While increasing or decreasing sampling rates yield broadly similar latent matrices that suggest the embedding is relatively insensitive to sampling density, the latent representation compresses the dynamic range of distances to maintain consistency. Random shift introduces the most visible irregularity in the latent representation with patchier blocks and less consistent proximity relationships, yet the latent space still manages to dampen the extremes seen in the original space. Even when noise is added, the latent space retains a faint diagonal tendency while reducing separation between distant points, demonstrating that the framework pushes the representation toward a more robust averaging that masks meaningful long-range differences caused by perturbations to ensure a more resilient similarity mapping than the original Euclidean measurements.
