This series of panels compares DIS² and ODD on estimated bounds based on features and logits versus true accuracy, alongside the observed bound violation rate versus the desired probability delta. In the Features scatter plot, predictions are systematically positioned below the diagonal reference line, indicating that both ODD and DIS² always remain valid in the case of feature space. Moving to the Logits panel, the data points tighten and shift closer to the diagonal, showing improved alignment where the methods are almost always valid in the case of logit space; notably, in both these cases, ODD improves on DIS² with a more accurate prediction of the bound. However, the third panel reveals that without the correction delta term, the cluster shifts to straddle the diagonal, meaning both methods overestimate the accuracy many times. The rightmost line chart tracks the observed violation rate, showing that DIS² maintains a consistently lower rate than ODD, indicating conservative reliability. While the observed violation rate for both DIS² and ODD remains under the allowed violation probability for most delta values, ODD marginally overshoots the allowed probability of violations for a small range of delta values.
