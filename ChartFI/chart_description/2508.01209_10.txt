The heatmaps present a sensitivity analysis on the loss controlling parameter, lambda, comparing missingness patterns across seven datasets separated into uniformly missing and structurally missing cases, where the matrix element denotes node classification accuracy in each setting. As the parameter increases from 1e-5 to 10, a distinct divergence emerges based on dataset size: smaller graph datasets, e.g., Cora, CiteSeer, WikiCS, Coauthor CS have relatively large lambda tolerances, maintaining stability or improving at higher settings, whereas larger graph datasets, e.g., PubMed, Coauthor Physics, OGBN-Arxiv share a small range of lambda before experiencing significant performance degradation. Under uniformly missing conditions, most datasets remain fairly stable across the smallest settings, but the larger datasets show clear declines as the parameter reaches 1 and 10. Specifically, OGBN-Arxiv drops sharply from 69.1 at low settings to 53.5 at 1e-1, and further to 48.4 at 10, while PubMed trends downward more gradually from 75.5 to 72.1. Conversely, reflecting the resilience of smaller graphs, Cora slightly improves around a lambda of 1, peaking at 79.5, and Co.CS rebounds at the highest setting to end at 78.2 after a mid-range dip, suggesting a benefit from stronger uniform perturbation. Under structurally missing conditions, the behavior is more mixed but reinforces the size-dependent sensitivity. Cora stays high and relatively steady, peaking at 80.1 at a lambda of 1 before easing to 78.6 at 10, while CiteSeer is mostly flat until a noticeable drop to 64.2 at 10. A notable anomaly is Co.CS, which improves markedly at higher settings under structural missingness, rising from 63.6 at 1e-5 to 71.5 at 10, implying the structural pattern interacts with the dataset to effectively regularize less informative structure. Meanwhile, the most pronounced structural sensitivity again appears in the larger OGBN-Arxiv dataset, which holds near 69.1 through 1e-2, then declines to 61.8 at 1e-1 and collapses to 53.0 at 10, indicating a threshold effect where performance deteriorates once structural missingness becomes substantial at higher lambda values.
