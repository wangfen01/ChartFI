These six heatmaps illustrate the results of positively and negatively editing the neurons identified by ValueLocate, as well as editing randomly selected neurons, on Llama-3.1-8B and Gemma-2-9B across four value dimensions as the adjustment magnitude γ increases. The data reveal a strong, monotonic relationship between the degree of neuron activation and the model’s expressed value orientations, with positive modifications enhancing value alignment and negative modifications reducing it. In Llama-3.1-8B, positive adjustments produce a steady rise across all dimensions, with the largest absolute gains in Self-Enhancement and Conservation, which climb from the 2.0–3.2 range into the high 3s and low 4s, while Openness to Change and Self-Transcendence show gentler slopes due to higher initial baselines. Conversely, negative modifications cause each dimension to decline cleanly as γ grows, while random adjustments leave scores notably stable—Self-Enhancement hovers around 2.0 and higher-baseline dimensions remain near 4.0—reinforcing that the observed effects are not artifacts of magnitude alone. Gemma-2-9B mirrors this directional behavior with even stronger separation at higher γ; positive cases show substantial upward movement for Self-Enhancement and Conservation, while Self-Transcendence remains the top-scoring dimension throughout. Under negative adjustments in Gemma-2-9B, the drop is especially steep for higher-baseline dimensions early on before continuing a downward trajectory, whereas random adjustments result in only a negligible downward drift in Self-Enhancement and Conservation. Across both models, the near-linear, monotonic response to directional editing contrasts sharply with the near-invariance of random neuron selection, suggesting that while baseline differences dominate the absolute scoring levels, the specific direction and magnitude of neuron activation primarily control the movement and intensity of the model's expressed values.
