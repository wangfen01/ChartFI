The charts illustrate the available power of a single Electric Vehicle Battery (EVB) and validate its energy efficiency (η) across varying time slots. The comparison of available power reveals that the Safe Operating Power Boundary (SOPT) from the Energy Management (EM) system is highly adaptive towards varying battery states as well as the operating conditions. This adaptiveness is distinctively shown by the system's ability to spontaneously provide a much tighter SOPT limitation under coldness; for instance, the power envelope at 10°C is significantly more restricted than at 25°C, ensuring safety under thermal constraints. In terms of efficiency validation, the EM system effectively limits η above 98% as the set restriction during the Linear Programming Control (LPC) of the fast charging scheme, maintaining high performance. Conversely, the data indicate that applying either the Simplified System Model (SSM) or Equivalent Circuit Model (ECM) occasionally exceeds the expected limitation and operates in inefficient regions, with efficiency values frequently dropping below the internal constraint line. Such inefficiency suggests that the Lithium-ion Battery (LiB) endures overcharge or over-discharge constantly, as evidenced by marked instances of infeasible dispatch and significant deviations from the optimal efficiency threshold.
