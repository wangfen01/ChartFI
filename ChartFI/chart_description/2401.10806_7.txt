The heatmap displays the binding funnel landscapes of scoring functions, where the corresponding blocks indicate the Spearman correlation coefficient between the RMSD values and the binding scores calculated by scoring functions for all ligand poses within specific ranges. Crucially, the ticks on the x-axis refer to the ranges of RMSDs (for example, 0 - 2 Å , 0 − 3 Å, etc.) rather than discrete bins, illustrating how performance evolves as the inclusion threshold for structural deviation widens. This analysis reveals a strong correlation between the docking scores predicted by DeepRLI and the RMSD values, particularly within a shorter RMSD range (e.g., RMSD < 5 Å), where the method exhibits deep blue blocks indicative of high coefficients. This correlation manifests in the formation of a funnel landscape, a pattern shared by several widely used docking scores such as AutoDock Vina, ΔvinaRF20, and DrugScore variants, where correlations are highest in the strictest 0–2 Å to 0–4 Å ranges and trend downward as the range extends toward 10 Å. In contrast to this fading trend, the X-Score family (including HM, HS, HP, and the base X-Score) stands out as unusually stable, maintaining consistently high correlations across nearly the entire cumulative RMSD range with minimal decay. Conversely, a notable anomaly appears at the bottom of the chart where ΔSAS and D-Score@SYBYL exhibit very low or even negative correlations in the narrowest 0–2 Å range, but improve markedly as the range widens to 0–6 Å and beyond, implying these metrics become more informative only when poses deviate beyond very small RMSD thresholds. Finally, GBVI/WSA-dG@MOE shows a pronounced deterioration as the RMSD range increases, dropping into comparatively weak correlations by the 0–10 Å range, which signals reduced robustness in the high-deviation regime relative to the more stable performers like DeepRLI and X-Score.
