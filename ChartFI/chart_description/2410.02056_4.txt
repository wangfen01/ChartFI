This chart compares the distributions of pitch and various spectral features—specifically Pitch Salience, Spectral Flatness, Flux, and Complexity—between generated audios in D_syn and real audios in D_small across different methods on the USD8K and NSynth datasets. Across both datasets, the Gold distributions tend to be tighter and more centrally concentrated, serving as a baseline for realism. A dominant trend observed is that Synthio-generated audios closely replicate the features of real data, showing the best alignment among all methods, while other synthetic variants often shift the location and spread in method-specific ways, introducing clear bias or extra variance. In the NSynth dataset, Pitch Salience under Gold centers around the mid-to-high range; visually, Synthio aligns most closely with this central mass, whereas vanilla augmentation and temperature-caps variants exhibit broader distributions or shifts toward higher salience values. Spectral Complexity in NSynth shows clear method separation, where vanilla augmentation and temperature-caps move the distribution to higher complexity relative to Gold, while plain Synthio sits closer to the reference, indicating that some augmentation choices systematically increase perceived complexity rather than simply adding noise. For Spectral Flatness in NSynth, Gold is sharply peaked near low-to-moderate values; Synthio largely mirrors this single-mode distribution, whereas the augmented and ERM variants introduce a pronounced secondary mass at much higher flatness, suggesting a qualitative change toward more noise-like spectra not present in the reference. Spectral Flux in NSynth is similarly anomalous for the baselines: Gold and plain Synthio remain narrowly concentrated near small negative values, while vanilla augmentation and temperature-caps spread heavily into positive flux with a long, flat plateau, implying substantially more frame-to-frame spectral change. In USD8K, Pitch Salience again differentiates methods strongly, as Gold concentrates at low salience; plain Synthio remains relatively low but broader, while the augmented, temperature-caps, and ERM variants shift markedly upward, indicating a tendency to produce more salient, tonal-like content than the real distribution. Spectral Complexity in USD8K is comparatively stable across methods with only modest rightward drift for temperature-caps and ERM, and Spectral Flatness is mostly aligned near low values for all methods, though temperature-caps shows a heavier right tail. However, Spectral Flux in USD8K reveals a large mismatch for specific baselines: while Gold and Synthio cluster tightly near zero, vanilla augmentation exhibits a broad, strongly negative distribution, indicating a systematic shift toward reduced or inverted spectral change that stands out as a method-induced artifact. Overall, Synthio-generated audios closely replicate the spectral features of the original audios, avoiding the significant distributional artifacts seen in other augmentation strategies.
