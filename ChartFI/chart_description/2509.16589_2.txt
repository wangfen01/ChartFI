These heatmaps illustrate the performance of MERaLiON and GPT modes with various temperature settings on the long and short evaluation sets, mapping accuracy scores across seven distinct question types ranging from C to DS. The data is visualized using a color gradient where dark blue represents high accuracy and deep red indicates near-zero performance, revealing distinct patterns within each model and question type. A striking disparity is evident in the DG category, where MERaLiON achieves its highest efficacy, particularly in the long evaluation set with scores hovering around 80, whereas ChatGPT exhibits a critical failure in the same category, with values plummeting to as low as 0.06. The results show that temperature tuning significantly influences model accuracy in certain categories, creating specific performance peaks rather than uniform improvements; for instance, in the MERaLiON short set, the CS category spikes to 77.23 at a temperature of 0.4, while the ChatGPT short set sees the CG category peak at 77.86 at a temperature of 0.6. Conversely, the DS category proves consistently difficult for both models, with ChatGPT showing particularly low scores in the 20s and 30s across both dataset lengths, further emphasizing the variability in model robustness across different task types and parameter settings.
