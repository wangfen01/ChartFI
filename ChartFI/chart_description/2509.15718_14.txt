This normalized heatmap evaluates the classification performance where darker diagonal cells indicate stronger class-wise accuracy. The model demonstrates robust capabilities for several modulation types, as GFSK, BPSK, and CPFSK have the highest accuracies (0.98, 0.97, and 0.97 respectively). Similarly, PAM4 and AM-DSB achieve high correct classification rates of approximately 0.96 and 0.95, suggesting the system separates these signal types reliably with minimal leakage into other categories. Despite this general strength, the matrix reveals that QAM16 and QAM64 show lower but still reasonable accuracies (0.59 and 0.62), driven by substantial mutual confusion where the model struggles to distinguish between these two higher-order QAM constellations. There is some confusion between certain pairs of classes beyond the QAM types, such as WBFM and AM-DSB; while WBFM retains 0.80 accuracy, it exhibits a pronounced misclassification into AM-DSB of 0.17, implying overlapping spectral or envelope characteristics that the classifier is not fully disentangling. Additionally, QPSK generally performs well at 0.84 but shows a meaningful drift toward 8PSK at roughly 0.094, consistent with confusion between phase-shift keying orders. Aside from these specific pockets, most other off-diagonal values are near zero, indicating errors are not broadly distributed but instead driven by a few interpretable, structurally similar class pairs.
