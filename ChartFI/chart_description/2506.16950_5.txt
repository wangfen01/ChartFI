This chart illustrates the relationship between distortion intensity and each model’s average accuracy across the ImageNet-C and LAION-C benchmarks, revealing how performance degrades as perturbations strengthen from levels 1 through 5. Visual foundation models evaluated on ImageNet-C maintain high accuracy, with a minimal drop across increasing intensity levels that follows a steady, near-linear erosion. In contrast, on the LAION-C dataset, the models exhibit a sharper decline in accuracy, highlighting the benchmark’s effectiveness in measuring model robustness. While both datasets cluster in the high-accuracy range at level 1, a significant gap opens by level 2 as LAION-C performance drops precipitously compared to the more gradual decline of ImageNet-C. From levels 3 to 5, LAION-C shows a pronounced nonlinear collapse, with many models falling into very low accuracy ranges, indicating a substantially higher sensitivity to severe corruptions. The average performance column reinforces this divergence, as ImageNet-C maintains a mid-to-high average with moderate variability, while the LAION-C average is notably lower and more dispersed, suggesting inconsistent robustness across different architectures. A key pattern is the widening separation between the dashed trend lines as intensity increases, implying that robustness conclusions drawn from ImageNet-C may not transfer to LAION-C under stronger shifts. The most salient anomaly is the heavy tail of near-floor LAION-C results at higher intensities, where a subset of models fails catastrophically rather than degrading smoothly, signaling significant brittleness under distribution shift rather than simple noise tolerance limits.
