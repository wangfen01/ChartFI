The heatmaps visualize the refusal score changes when ablating true and false refusal vectors across three models, plotted against transformer layers on the x-axis and the relative token positions of post-instruction tokens on the y-axis. Under the True Refusal condition, each model exhibits a pronounced mid-layer band of high-magnitude negative values, indicating that the strongest effects concentrate in the middle transformer layers with comparatively weaker activity in the earliest layers and a gradual tapering toward the final layers. Compared with this true refusal vector distribution, the false refusal vectors—observed in the lambda = 0 and lambda = 0.6 rows—generally have a lower refusal score change when ablated. As lambda increases, the overall refusal score changes less, leading to a dominant pattern of attenuation where the negative region becomes less continuous at lambda = 0 and largely near-neutral with only faint residual pockets at lambda = 0.6. Despite this reduction in magnitude, there is a position overlap between the two kinds of vectors, particularly evident in the persistence of signal location in the first two rows. A distinct anomaly appears in Llama-3-8B-Ins; while its most effective candidate true refusal vectors are concentrated at layer 13, the most effective candidate false refusal vectors shift to be mostly at layer 24 as lambda increases from 0 to 0.6, contrasting with the Llama-2 models where the signal, though dampened, remains more spatially consistent.
