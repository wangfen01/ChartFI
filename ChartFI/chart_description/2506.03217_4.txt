The panels illustrate a comprehensive comparison of Centiloid values and Tau SUVr obtained with SPM-based pipelines and the value provided by B-PIP, assessing cross-method consistency across a wide dynamic range and various diagnostic groups. In the amyloid analysis, Centiloid values obtained from the two pipelines were highly correlated (R2 = 0.90; ICC = 0.95), with B-PIP Centiloids rising almost one-for-one with SPM Centiloids as indicated by a regression slope of approximately 0.88. While the relationship is generally tight, with higher values dominated by MCI and dementia A+ cases and CN and A- cases clustering near the low end, the plots reveal specific distributional nuances. Although the high ICC suggests excellent reliability, variability widens as the mean increases, and notably, a subset of CN A+ individuals showed systematically higher CL values with the SPM-based pipeline compared to B-PIP, appearing as distinct positive outliers in the difference plot. Turning to the tau measures, the chart compares Tau PET quantification using SUVr values from the SPM-based CenTauR pipeline versus B-PIP. This comparison also demonstrated a strong correlation (R2 = 0.84) and strong reliability (ICC = 0.91), maintaining a similar linear trajectory with a slope of 0.84. Separation by disease stage becomes clearer as SUVR increases, and while mean differences remain near zero suggesting little systematic bias overall, the dispersion at the upper end of pathology warrants caution for extremely high values.
