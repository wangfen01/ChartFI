The three heatmaps compare nonlinear correlation structures among features in the balanced representation across the same set of variables under CFR, after adding SBRL, and after adding SBRL with HAP. Under the initial CFR configuration, the balanced representation exhibits a strong correlation between features, characterized by a broadly high and uniform intensity that suggests variables move together with limited separation. Upon the direct integration of representation balancing and stable training techniques, the CFR + SBRL configuration reduces the average HSIC_RFF, causing the matrix to become more structured with clearer bands and blocks where some variable pairs weaken while others remain strong. This trend is amplified in the final configuration, where CFR + SBRL-HAP can further decrease the average HSIC_RFF. As shown, the feature correlation is reduced by SBRL, and further decreased by incorporating HAP, creating a pronounced grid-like contrast where strong correlations concentrate into defined patterns while several cross-block regions drop markedly. The most notable shift is the transition from diffuse, consistently high dependence to sharper, more modular dependence, indicating that the combined approach effectively disentangles relationships and highlights distinct feature groupings.
