These heat map visualizations illustrate the effects of two hyperparameters, the KL Parameter (lambda_KL) and the Dis Parameter (lambda_Dis), set to values between 0 and 1, on the performance of the model across five datasets. A consistent pattern emerges where the performance of model is generally better when the values of lambda_KL and lambda_Dis are mediate; conversely, when lambda_KL and lambda_Dis are too large, the accuracy of the model is low, suggesting an over-regularization regime that hurts performance. The sensitivity to these parameters, however, varies significantly by dataset. For instance, the Computers dataset exhibits the strongest interaction effect, with accuracy remaining high for low-to-mid parameter values but dropping dramatically to form a clear failure band in the high-KL, high-Dis region. Cora shows a similar, though less severe, pattern with broadly high and stable accuracy that degrades noticeably only in the extreme upper-right corner. In contrast, Pubmed is largely flat and high-performing, but it contains sharp, isolated failures near the high-KL edge at higher Dis values, implying a threshold effect where pushing parameters too far can abruptly destabilize results. Citeseer is more variable overall, with accuracy fluctuating across the surface and a few localized dips even at low KL and low-to-mid Dis values, indicating weaker robustness. Finally, the Photo dataset appears comparatively insensitive to these parameters, maintaining the highest and most consistent accuracy with only mild, scattered variation, which suggests a wide margin for tuning without major performance risk.
