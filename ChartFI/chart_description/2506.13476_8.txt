The four confusion matrices illustrate the distribution of true positives, false positives, and false negatives across defect categories for YOLOv8, YOLOv9, and two post-processing strategies, Weighted Box Fusion (WBF) and Soft-NMS, on the ESRPCB_C dataset. Across all configurations, the results indicate that ESRPCB significantly reduces false negatives, particularly for missing holes and spurious copper defects, where fine-grained details are essential for accurate classification. For core defect classes, performance is near-saturated and largely stable, with missing_hole and open_circuit achieving nearly perfect scores; YOLOv9, WBF, and Soft-NMS show 1.00 on these diagonals, while YOLOv8 is only marginally lower for missing_hole at 0.99. Other categories like mouse_bite, spur, and short maintain high accuracy in the 0.97 to 0.99 range, with the most consistently challenging defect, spurious_copper, showing notable improvement from 0.90 in YOLOv8 to 0.94 in YOLOv9 and Soft-NMS. Despite these strong diagonals, the primary source of error remains the background being predicted as a defect class, a false positive tendency that is especially pronounced for the spur and mouse_bite categories. While YOLOv9 exhibits a higher skew of background being labeled as spur (0.37) compared to YOLOv8 (0.25), the application of WBF and Soft-NMS helps mitigate this spike to 0.34 and 0.32, respectively. Ultimately, while incremental improvements in spurious_copper recognition and diagonal tightening are evident across the models, the systematic background-to-defect false positive pattern persists as the most significant area for further optimization.
