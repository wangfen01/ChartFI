The composite visualization evaluates the performance of attribute recognition models—specifically APN, TransZero, PSVMA, and PSVMMA+—against Ground Truth (GT) benchmarks through a detailed comparison of confidence scores and attribute alignments. In the left panel, a hybrid chart delineates performance metrics where the blue, green, orange, and purple bars denote the results of APN, TransZero, PSVMA, and PSVMMA+, respectively. The bar with a different mark corresponds to the image with the same mark, while the superimposed line graph represents the average results of three randomly selected images. A critical analysis of these three intra-class instances reveals that the PSVMMA+ method successfully recognizes that the rats do not possess the “yellow” property, a distinction missed by the APN method which exhibits a high confidence spike for this trait. Instead, the PSVMMA+ shows strong probabilities of being “nocturnal”, “fast”, and “new world”, tracking the red GT line closely. Overall, the average attribute predictions of the three images are more consistent with the GT compared to the APN and TransZero methods, which show significant deviations across various attributes. This trend of superior alignment is further illustrated by the radar chart in the right panel, where, in comparison to the APN and TransZero, the points of the PSVMA+ are closer to the center of the chart, signifying a closer alignment with GT by minimizing false positives for irrelevant attributes such as hooves or flippers.
