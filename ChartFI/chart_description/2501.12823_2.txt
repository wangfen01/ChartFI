The chart tracks the normalized aggregated measuring frequency, calculated from all seeds and years, for five measured features (LAI, SM, NuptakeTotal, TAGP, and NAVAIL) represented by different marker shapes. Shaded bands emphasize the measuring action frequency throughout the growing year under three distinct cost scenarios, represented by different colors: Realistic (blue), No-cost (red), and Flat-cost (green), with a vertical line showing the average flowering day in early June. The No-cost scenario is characterized by a persistently high measurement frequency that dominates the entire season, with no clear pattern of measuring. Activity oscillates between approximately 0.3 and 0.7 from autumn through late summer, including significant intensity during winter months when other scenarios are inactive. In stark contrast, the introduction of financial constraints fundamentally alters this approach. In the scenarios with costs, the agent typically starts measuring close to the crop’s flowering stage, concentrating its efforts in a targeted window. The Realistic scenario exemplifies this, with frequency remaining near zero through winter before ramping up sharply from late March to a peak of nearly 0.4 in May, then tapering off post-flowering. This surge is most prominent for LAI and SM, indicating a strategic focus on canopy development and moisture dynamics during rapid pre-flowering growth.
