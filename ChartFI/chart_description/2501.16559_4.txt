The heatmaps depict the adapter transferability cost (ATC) between pairs of six source and target models—SD-v1.5, SD Eff-v1.0, RV-v3.0, SDXL, SSD1B, and RVXL-v3.0—for both left and right subspace similarities. The ATC is normalized to be between 0 and 1, with higher values, indicated by warmer colors, signifying a higher cost of transferring adapters. A clear two-family structure emerges in both subspaces: for both the left and right subspace similarities, ATC among Family 1 (SD-v1.5, SD Eff-v1.0, and RV-v3.0) is less than 0.5, indicating low-cost, efficient adapter transfer within this group. Conversely, the cost of transferring adapters between this family and the second group (SDXL, SSD-1B, and RVXL-v3.0) is consistently high, with ATC values often exceeding 0.75. Within this second family, a striking anomaly is the near-zero ATC between SDXL and RVXL-v3.0 (approximately 0.0015), which denotes almost effortless transferability. This is particularly notable as both models exhibit a very high transfer cost when paired with models from Family 1. While the overall two-family structure persists across both subspaces, the transfer costs are generally lower in the right subspace, as seen in the reduced cross-family ATC values. For instance, the cost between SDXL and SSD-1B is moderate in both cases but increases slightly from 0.43 in the left subspace to 0.47 in the right, suggesting a marginally greater difficulty in transfer within that specific subspace.
