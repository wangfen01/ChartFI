This plot combines feature importance with feature effects to illustrate which variables most influence the model’s predictions regarding students' help-seeking behaviors. Each dot represents an observation’s contribution, and as feature values increased, the color of the dot tended to become redder, while as feature values decreased, the color of the dot leaned towards bluer. GenAI fluency (CF) and age emerged as the top variables in the analysis, serving as the dominant drivers. Specifically, the results indicated that higher levels of ChatGPT fluency (CF) and ChatGPT distortions (CDS) are associated with an increased tendency among university students to seek help from ChatGPT, evidenced by the concentration of red points on the positive side of the x-axis. Conversely, the influence of age suggests a divergent pattern where university students of higher age tended to show a greater inclination toward using traditional information search tools (i.e., Google). Among other significant features, CT stands out as a directional contrast where higher values more frequently appear on the negative SHAP side, indicating that increasing CT tends to reduce the predicted outcome. Features such as EB and FEF show moderate influence with broader spreads and some overlap of colors, implying heterogeneous effects across the population, while long tails and outliers in CF and FEF signal specific segments where the model is particularly sensitive. Finally, lower-ranked variables like gender, ARM, and grade remain tightly clustered around zero, indicating they fine-tune predictions rather than drive the primary trends.
