The four heatmaps illustrate the variation in Macro-F1 scores across the ACM, DBLP, Aminer, and Freebase datasets as the hyperparameters lambda_Intra and lambda_Inter are adjusted within the range of 0.2 to 1.0. A structural distinction is evident where the axes on the left, belonging to the ACM and DBLP datasets, display high and relatively stable performance bands, whereas the axes on the right, belonging to Aminer and Freebase, exhibit clearer gradients and greater sensitivity to the parameter settings. A key trend observed in the data, particularly for ACM, DBLP, and Freebase, is that performance improves as lambda_Inter increases; this phenomenon aligns with the principle of the proposed sampling method, where intercontrast has more hard negative samples, thereby necessitating stronger weighting to effectively distinguish classes. Specifically, ACM stays tightly clustered in the high 88 to low 90 range, peaking at 90.54 when lambda_Intra = 0.6 and lambda_Inter = 1.0. DBLP is consistently the strongest and most robust, hovering between 91 and 93 with a peak of 92.54 at lambda_Intra = 0.4 and lambda_Inter = 1.0. Freebase further corroborates the benefit of high inter-weighting, achieving its optimal score of 62.47 when both parameters are set to 1.0. However, Aminer presents a notable exception to the positive correlation with lambda_Inter, instead showing that Macro-F1 increases markedly as lambda_Intra rises while favoring lower lambda_Inter, culminating in a clear maximum of 73.44 at lambda_Intra = 1.0 and lambda_Inter = 0.2. Thus, while DBLP and ACM tolerate broad choices, Aminer and Freebase require more deliberate balancing, generally favoring high intra-weights while responding differently to the intensity of inter-contrast regularization.
