This chart comprises four subplots, where each subplot shows the evaluation setting for an n-player game (n=2, 3, 4, 5), plotting agent scores against various training configurations. As a reference point, the self-play (SP) performance of R3D2 is shown as the leftmost bar on each plot, consistently achieving high scores near the top of the scale. Additionally, the cross-play performance of n-player agents combined with R3D2-M is displayed in blue. The dominant pattern across all evaluations is that agents achieve their highest scores when the training player count matches the evaluation player count, with the top-performing R3D2 and R2D2-text variants reaching scores in the high teens to mid-20s. Conversely, performance in zero-shot transfer scenarios—where training and evaluation player counts are mismatched—degrades sharply as the gap between the two settings grows. Despite this general trend, R3D2 agents demonstrate strong zero-shot generalization to novel settings, consistently outperforming other models in these cross-play (XP) transfers. In contrast, R2D2-text seems to be unable to match R3D2’s transfer performance, especially when transferring from a setting with a large number of players to one with a smaller number. The weak performance of baseline models, with the R2D2 agent scoring low in most transfers and the Random Agent remaining near zero, reinforces that the observed peaks are driven by methods that capture player-count-specific dynamics rather than general skill. Overall, the results indicate strong agent specialization to the specific player count of their training environment.
