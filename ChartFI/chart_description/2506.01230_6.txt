The performance of logistic regression, measured by AUC, across the Adult, Employee, Credit Card, and SQF datasets reveals a consistent decline as the percentage of missing values (MVs) increases from 10% to 30% and 50%, with dashed red lines indicating a higher reference level that no method reaches once corruption is introduced. Across most scenarios, less than 10% MVs in a single column is sufficient to cause a drop of more than 0.05 in AUC for most methods, except for Diffprep on the Credit Card dataset, which maintains higher relative stability. As missingness reaches 30%, AUC drops exceed 0.15 across most scenarios except for Diffprep and KNN-imputer on the Credit Card dataset, and methods on the SQF dataset, which remains the most resilient with AUC values staying in a narrow band between approximately 0.58 and 0.63. In the Adult dataset, performance is relatively strong at 10% missing but collapses at 50% for most approaches; however, KNN-imputer performs exceptionally well on the Adult dataset, remaining an outlier with an AUC in the upper 0.6s while others fall toward the 0.35–0.55 range. A similar pattern occurs in the Employee dataset, where most methods converge around the low 0.4s at 50% missingness, yet KNN stays meaningfully higher in the mid-to-high 0.5s despite showing the largest uncertainty. Diffprep achieves the best AUC on the Credit Card dataset, leading the field at all levels of missingness and settling around 0.6 at the 50% mark while other methods drop closer to 0.48–0.55. Ultimately, while monotonic degradation and increasing variance are standard as data quality worsens, the SQF dataset exhibits the strongest overall robustness regardless of method, whereas Diffprep and KNN-imputer demonstrate unique late-stage advantages in maintaining predictive power on the Credit Card and Adult datasets, respectively.
