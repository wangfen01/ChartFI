The left chart tracks information entropy (red) and corresponding accumulated attention scores (blue) on one sequence, revealing that attention-critical tokens (with high accumulated attention scores) do not necessarily possess high entropy. While both metrics exhibit volatility with sharp, isolated peaks, observations reveal that a substantial number of these attention-critical tokens do not exhibit correspondingly high information entropy, suggesting the absence of a linear relationship between the two metrics. The right chart compares F1 scores across three datasets and four conditions, revealing a consistent ranking by dataset difficulty or headroom: MultiFieldQA scores highest, QASPER sits in the middle, and NarrativeQA is lowest across all methods. Removing compression yields the best performance in every dataset, with the largest advantage on MultiFieldQA, indicating that compression likely discards information that matters more for complex, multi-field retrieval and reasoning. Random and w.o. attn_critical underperform the no-compression baseline and are especially weak on MultiFieldQA, while DAC method consistently provides the best performance among the modified approaches, substantially closing the gap to the uncompressed baseline and demonstrating its superior ability to retain crucial, task-relevant information.
