This set of heatmaps presents bias scores for each demographic subgroup across different models and datasets, utilizing a color scale where darker hues represent higher bias and lower scores are considered better. The visualization is stratified into two primary perspectives: the top panels display metrics for each model averaged across tasks and datasets, while the bottom panels illustrate results averaged across models for each specific task, distinguishing between the generative task and various ranking datasets. In the analysis of primary models, the Income subgroup shows the highest average bias, a trend that is visually paralleled by the References category, which consistently exhibits deep saturation indicating elevated bias levels. When we consider the maximum difference bias, the data reveals that Disability and Religion also show very high bias, marking a significant shift in severity compared to the mean absolute bias metrics. Shifting focus to the task-based breakdown, the heatmaps highlight how bias fluctuates across different contexts; specifically, compared with the ranking task, we observe bias scores for generation at a level between the WIRED and machine-generated tasks. This positioning indicates that while the generative task introduces notable bias, it sits within a spectrum defined by the specific nature of the underlying datasets, such as the high bias observed in Gen. WIRED versus the relatively lower bias in standard WIRED or MATH-50 tasks.
