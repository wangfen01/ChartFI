The figure displays the per-attribute Pearson correlation between models on McRae×THINGS and Binder datasets, revealing that overall, all reasonable model correlations are quite high. For the McRae×THINGS norms and Binder attributes, we see distinct modality clusters, where vision encoders (with the exception of Max ViT IN-1K) are correlated with each other, and likewise the static word embedding models and the LLMs Gemma and DeBERTa v3 form a cohesive group. In the McRae×THINGS panel, the strongest cluster is formed by self-supervised and multimodal vision models—such as DINOv2, LLaVA-1.5, and SigLIP—which tend to correlate in the mid-to-high .90s, while text-heavy baselines like GloVe and FastText sit slightly lower against that vision cluster. We also see some cross-modal correlations, with CLIP (text) correlating relatively higher with vision models in general (not only the CLIP image encoder), effectively bridging the gap between the text and vision clusters. Moving to the Binder matrix, the correlations rise across the board and the matrix becomes more homogeneous; notably, Swin-V2 correlates more highly with language models on Binder, suggesting a stronger alignment with semantic structures in that benchmark. Despite the general uplift in Binder, the exception of Max ViT (IN-1K) persists, highlighted by a significant dip in its relationship with DeBERTa v3 (dropping to the high .60s), which contrasts with the otherwise strong alignment in the matrix. Throughout both panels, Random SigLIP stands out as the clearest anomaly, showing markedly lower correlations with nearly everything else, although it aligns slightly better within the tighter structure of the Binder benchmark.
