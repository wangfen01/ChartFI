These two radar charts illustrate the average SVA scores of evaluated models across various societal value dimensions for Morocco and Egypt, highlighting how different large language models align with cultural, political, and socio-economic frameworks. In both countries, model profiles cluster most tightly around mid-range values for social values, norms, and stereotypes, perceptions of security, and political culture and political regimes, while the widest spreads appear in religious values and political interest and political participation, indicating higher model sensitivity or disagreement on these topics. NileChat demonstrates substantial improvements over the baseline across most societal-value dimensions for both Moroccan and Egyptian contexts, often achieving the best results for Morocco across several dimensions and remaining competitive in others. Specifically, for Morocco, NileChat surpasses the baseline in all dimensions except Religious Values and the Index of Postmaterialism. For Egypt, NileChat notably excels in Perceptions of Migration, Political Culture and Political Regimes, Happiness and Wellbeing, and Perceptions about Science and Technology, outperforming the baseline across all dimensions except Political Interest and Political Participation and the Index of Postmaterialism. While NileChat shows strong overall performance, models such as Jais-13B and ALLaM-7B show slightly stronger performance in certain other dimensions, particularly in Egypt where Jais-13B reaches close to the outer ring in religious values, creating a visible anomaly. Ultimately, the transition from the Moroccan to the Egyptian profile reveals an increase in volatility and occasional extremes around religion and civic-political engagement, even as core dimensions like security and economic values remain comparatively steady across the model suite.
