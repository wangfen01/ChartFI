This stacked bar chart illustrates the proportion of bugs attributed to each component across five different engines—LCP, vLLM, DS, TRT, and MLC—to explore potential implementation differences and the prevalence of vulnerable components across these systems. The y-axis represents the percentage of bugs attributed to each component, with proportions exceeding 10% annotated to highlight the most significant areas of vulnerability. LCP exhibits a relatively balanced distribution of bugs, led by Model Conversion Tools at 18% and Operators at 16%, with Resource Manager and Backend Adapter both at 13%, suggesting that vulnerabilities are broadly distributed across its toolchain. In contrast, vLLM shifts its bug concentration toward the Model Loader at 21%, while maintaining significant proportions in Operators at 17% and Resource Manager at 10%, indicating a higher susceptibility to issues during the model ingestion phase. DS stands out with a high concentration of bugs in Operators at 27% and a notable presence in the Engine Initializer at 18%, alongside 16% in the Model Loader and 12% in the Communicator, pointing toward a more runtime- and orchestration-oriented vulnerability profile. TRT is the clearest outlier, with Model Conversion Tools dominating the bug distribution at 54% and Operators dropping to 11%, signaling a conversion-centric architecture where optimization and translation layers are the primary sources of defects. MLC shows a different pattern of concentration where Model Conversion Tools remain high at 28%, but Setup Tools rise sharply to 22% and Backend Adapter reaches 14%, highlighting a stack where bugs are frequently found in environment integration and backend portability layers. Overall, the most significant shift across systems is the swing in bugs attributed to Model Conversion Tools from a moderate ~18% in LCP and vLLM to an extreme 54% in TRT, while DS and MLC differentiate themselves by allocating more vulnerabilities to runtime building blocks and integration layers, respectively.
