The violin plots depict the distribution of scores achieved across attempts for each evaluated scheme, differentiating between complex and standard tasks to illustrate performance consistency and variability. In general, more complex tasks, characterized by multiple sub-requests, tend to increase the likelihood of errors in LLMs, resulting in lower success rates compared to standard tasks, as observed across most schemes. This trend is visually pronounced in the comparison between the two panels; while schemes such as GPT4o SR, CGPT4o R, and GPT4o NR show viable score distributions in standard tasks, their performance collapses to near-zero values in the complex setting. Similarly, the GPT4o Sole configuration remains consistently ineffective, showing a flat distribution at the bottom of the scale regardless of task difficulty. However, with the proposed full framework, GPT4o-Full, the performance gap between complex and standard tasks narrows significantly. This configuration maintains a high density of scores near the maximum value of 100 in both scenarios, demonstrating superior robustness and stability that effectively mitigates the performance degradation typically caused by increased task complexity.
