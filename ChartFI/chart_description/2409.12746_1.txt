The chart illustrates the distribution of results per subject, measuring inter-rater agreement via Cohen’s Kappa for all models in both Spanish and English. Subjects with the highest scores tend to be Biology, Biochemistry, Fundamentals of Computer Science, and Psychology, where agreement is consistently strongest; Biochemistry and Fundamentals of Computing cluster near the top with medians around the high 0.8s to low 0.9s in both languages, while Biology also remains high. Moving down the performance scale, a slight decrease in scores is observed in Economics and Business Administration and Management (BAM), as well as in Literature and Spanish Language. Within this middle tier, Economics shows a noticeable language split, with English tending to score higher and Spanish exhibiting a wider spread and lower lower-quartile values, suggesting more variability and occasional failures among models in Spanish for that domain. The worst results are observed in the three Mathematics subjects, with slightly higher results in Mathematics Applied to Social Sciences compared to its counterparts. Mathematics and especially Advanced Mathematics display the lowest medians and the widest dispersion, with several model points dipping toward zero or even slightly negative, signaling inconsistent labeling and occasional systematic disagreement. Overall, the dominant pattern indicates that domain difficulty drives agreement more than language, although English is marginally more stable in several subjects, whereas Spanish shows larger variance and more low-end outliers in Economics categories.
