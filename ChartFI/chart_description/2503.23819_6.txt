The two scatter plots compare classwise label guarantee values for the ground-truth label when it appears within the top two predicted confidences, presenting data for the ISIC2019 dataset on the left and the ASAN dataset on the right. For the ISIC dataset, all the classwise scatter plots have concentrated clusters towards the higher confidence regions, and some scattered points nearer to zero. While classes 0 through 4 exhibit dense vertical bands indicating a high frequency of correct top-two inclusion, the sparser distribution in classes 5 through 7 suggests fewer instances meeting the criteria, yet the vertical dispersion across most classes reveals that even when the ground truth is included in the prediction set, the model’s assurance can occasionally drop to low values. Similarly, for the ASAN dataset, the visualization tracks ground-truth prediction guarantees among the top two predictions of the set, where all the classwise scatter plots have dense clusters towards the higher guarantee regions, and only some scarce points around zero. Ultimately, the presence of occasional mid-to-low guarantee points in otherwise high-performing classes across both datasets hints at hard-to-classify cases or overlap in visual features, signaling that top-two inclusion alone does not ensure uniformly strong confidence.
