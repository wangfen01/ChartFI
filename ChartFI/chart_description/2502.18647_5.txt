Daily climatic variables measured on the office campus during the 2024 summer time reveal tightly synchronized day-to-day variability across land covers from mid-June to mid-September, characterized by distinct stratification based on surface type. Air temperature measurements showed a consistent pattern of higher temperatures in urbanized or exposed areas ('Parking' and 'Lawn') compared to areas having more urban greening ('Tree' and 'Forest'). While the overall thermal trend follows a repeating warm-cool cycle peaking in late June and early August near the upper 20s °C before a sharp cool dip in early September, the daily air temperature was highest at the 'Parking' location. In contrast, the 'Forest' site exhibited the lowest daily air temperature, while the 'Lawn' and 'Tree' locations had moderate temperatures with only small separations on most days. Wind speed is the most episodic variable, dominated by short spikes rather than a continuous trend; the exposed Parking and Lawn sites repeatedly experience the highest gust events, including peaks above 2 m/s and a significant spike in early to mid-September, whereas the Forest remains consistently lowest and comparatively stable, suggesting stronger sheltering under the canopy. Relative humidity moves inversely with temperature, featuring frequent surges into the 80 to 95 percent range and troughs closer to the mid-50s to 60s; consistent with the thermal hierarchy, the Forest tends to maintain the highest humidity and Parking the lowest. Solar radiation data further highlights the contrast in vegetated environments, as the Tree location consistently records much higher radiation than the Forest, with early peaks around 70 to 80 W/m² softening to the 30 to 50 W/m² range later in the season, while the Forest stays lower and steadier around the mid-teens to mid-20s. Collectively, these patterns suggest that canopy cover dampens wind and radiation while sustaining higher humidity and cooler temperatures, whereas open, impervious surfaces amplify heat and wind exposure.
