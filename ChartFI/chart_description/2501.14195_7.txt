The two radar charts present a comparison of watermark extraction accuracy across different methods under various distortions, evaluated on the ModelScope and Stable-Video-Diffusion video models. Across both platforms, our method demonstrates significantly greater robustness compared to all baseline approaches across almost all types of distortions, achieving an average accuracy of 0.983 on ModelScope and 0.955 on Stable-Video-Diffusion, respectively. In stark contrast, the baseline methods exhibit significant vulnerabilities and uneven performance profiles. While these approaches show competence against spatial and photometric changes such as brightness, resize, and random drop, their accuracy weakens most noticeably on blur-related and temporal disruptions. Our method, however, maintains exceptionally high accuracy even against these challenging corruptions, demonstrating resilience where other techniques falter. This highlights that the apparent robustness of baseline methods can mask narrow but important vulnerabilities to real-world issues like motion blur or frame-ordering problems, weaknesses our approach successfully overcomes.
