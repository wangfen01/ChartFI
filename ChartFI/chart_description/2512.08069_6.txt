This polar plot illustrates the phase error for a full tag rotation considering different scenarios, specifically comparing the magnitude of error across angles for Tag A Centered, Tag A Tilted, and Tag D Centered. In Scenario (1), where the centers are aligned, this Tag A Centered condition stays relatively low and smooth, maintaining a profile close to zero through several opposing directions with only modest fluctuations. In contrast, the Tag A Tilted series exhibits a pronounced directional bias, manifesting as a slightly tilted, figure-of-eight shaped error with a strong surge in the 300–330 degree region and a secondary elevated band around 150–180 degrees. This distinct anisotropic pattern occurs due to the polarization of the reader antenna not being perfectly circular. Finally, the Tag D Centered condition is generally higher and more variable than the centered Tag A profile, featuring broad elevation from about 210–270 degrees and a sharp spike near 270 degrees that stands out as the most notable anomaly in the visualization. Overall, while centering Tag A yields the most stable, low-amplitude profile, tilting the tag introduces specific geometric error patterns driven by antenna polarization, and Tag D produces the largest irregular fluctuations.
