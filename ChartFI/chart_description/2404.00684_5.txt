The figure presents an example of alignment matrix comparisons between a short passage and a query, contrasting MVDR in the document-to-query direction with GR in the query-to-document direction. In the MVDR panel, we observe a pronounced phenomenon of exact matches, characterized by a distinct diagonal structure where the song name and people’s names are completely matched with high scores. This suggests that the MVDR mechanism relies heavily on direct token-to-token correspondence, distributing weights along the diagonal where identical or closely related tokens align. In contrast, the GR panel demonstrates that this phenomenon is less obvious; the strict diagonal alignment dissolves into a more selective pattern. Instead of matching corresponding terms, each document token pays more attention to the people’s names and song names, creating vertical bands of high intensity on these specific entities. This shift indicates that while MVDR prioritizes structural, lexical alignment, GR amplifies the significance of key semantic terms, causing the retrieval relevance to hinge on a few high-value tokens rather than cumulative evidence across the sentence. This results in a notable increase in contrast and dynamic range in the GR model, which suppresses background noise to lock onto the most salient entities.
