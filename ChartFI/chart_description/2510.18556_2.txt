The bar charts display ethnicity prescription bias across different model architectures (GPT-2, Llama3, Mistral) trained on three datasets (HC4, SlimPajama, FineWeb) for opioid and non-opioid medications. Each bar represents the Net Bias Prescription Score (NBPS), calculated as the difference between the number of medications with statistically significant higher prescription probabilities and those with statistically significant lower probabilities relative to ethnicity-neutral prompts; positive values indicate overprescription bias, while negative values show underprescription bias. Models trained on HC4 exhibited distinct patterns, generally standing out as the setting with the most pronounced positive skew for both medication types. Specifically, Llama-HC4 and Mistral-HC4 consistently overprescribed opioid and non-opioid pain medication for "American Indian or Alaska Native" and "Middle Eastern or North African" groups, often without any corresponding underprescription. In the opioid category, this positive bias is driven most strongly by Llama3, where nearly every ethnicity segment is positive and the total stack is the largest in the figure. Moving to SlimPajama, the direction flips: all three architectures become net negative for opioids, with Mistral dipping the most. FineWeb produces the strongest and most consistent underprescription bias, especially for non-opioids where it pushes all three architectures decisively negative. A notable anomaly is the magnitude of FineWeb’s negative stacks in non-opioids, where multiple ethnic segments contribute sizable negative values simultaneously, suggesting a broad-based shift rather than one driven by a single group. However, for opioids, FineWeb amplifies divergence by architecture: GPT-2 becomes the most negative with deep underprescription across most ethnicities, while Llama3 rebounds to a clear positive overprescription bias and Mistral remains moderately negative, indicating that the corpus–architecture interaction is substantial and not monotonic. Across both medication classes, the most significant shift is the corpus change from HC4 to FineWeb, which generally moves bias from overprescription to underprescription, while Llama3 is the least stable across corpora for opioids, swinging from strongly positive to negative and back to positive.
