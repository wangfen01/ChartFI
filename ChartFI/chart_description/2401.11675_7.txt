The quantitative assessment of various image fusion approaches across 20 image pairs from the RoadScene dataset reveals distinct performance patterns across eight quality metrics, with the average for all metrics displayed in each panel's legend. The red curve representing our method consistently maintains a leading position, achieving the best average values on four evaluation metrics, specifically Average Gradient (AG), Spatial Frequency (SF), Qabf, and Feature Mutual Information (FMI). In the AG and SF panels, our method generally sustains the highest levels with repeated peaks, such as those around the mid-sequence image pairs, indicating superior activity, contrast, and information content retention compared to competing methods that track similar rises and dips but at lower amplitudes. The Qabf metric shows the most pronounced separation, where our method stays well above the pack across nearly all pairs, implying a more consistent edge and structure transfer advantage with less degradation of salient boundaries. While our method ranks second in the Entropy (EN), Visual Information Fidelity (VIFF), and Structural Similarity Index (SSIM) quality metrics, it remains highly competitive; for instance, in EN, it maintains high levels of information preservation, and in SSIM, it trends slightly higher toward the end of the sequence, indicating strong structural consistency. Standard Deviation (SD) and VIFF exhibit more moderate variability and occasional convergence among methods, yet our method rises strongly at the final image pair in the VIFF panel, highlighting a significant shift where perceptual information fidelity becomes a decisive differentiator. Across all panels, the dominant pattern is a content-driven oscillation shared by all methods, but our method consistently rides above or near the top of these fluctuations, demonstrating that it achieves satisfactory objective performance and highlighting the crucial role of the proposed framework and the segmented pixel loss function in maintaining robustness across diverse scenes.
