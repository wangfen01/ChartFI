This visualization compares participant responses to nine 6-point Likert statements for IntentTagger and a chat-based interface, featuring diverging Likert bars on the left ranked by effect size and a companion panel on the right displaying mean differences (MD) with 95% confidence intervals calculated via the studentized bootstrap method. Across all nine dimensions, participants rated IntentTagger higher than the chat-based system, with the most significant improvements observed in the iterative process, where users felt that after seeing generated slides, they could provide more information to refine the generation toward their intended outcome (MD = 2.83, p=0.003). IntentTagger also provided a substantially greater sense of control over guiding the slide generation process toward an intended outcome (MD = 2.67, p=0.002) and offered more valuable tips and suggestions that helped improve the presentation (MD = 2.58, p=0.004). Users reported that the system helped them conduct their tasks more successfully (MD = 2.33, p=0.003) and efficiently (MD = 2.33, p=0.002) than the chat-based alternative, leading to a strong consensus that this technique is better than the technique they usually use (MD = 2.0, p=0.005). While IntentTagger made it easier to communicate intentions to the generative system (MD = 1.83, p=0.003) and helped users become more aware of their own intentions and expectations to express them to the AI (MD = 1.58, p=0.012), the effect sizes for these communication-focused metrics were comparatively smaller. The final dimension, regarding whether the generated output aligns with expectations, showed the narrowest margin of improvement (MD = 0.92); although the trend remained positive, the difference was not statistically significant (p=0.065) and the confidence interval nearly reached zero, suggesting that while the procedural experience and sense of control are vastly superior with IntentTagger, the alignment of the final output remains a more modest gain.
