The figure tracks overall effects alongside contemporaneous and lagged components across a broad set of commodities from late 2021 through late 2023, revealing that while the contemporaneous component generally stays relatively small and stable around zero, the lagged component explains the bulk of the movement and drives the major swings in the overall series. A clear regime break is marked by the vertical dashed line around early 2022, after which distinct structural roles emerge across the markets. The net contributors of shocks mainly include climate change, corn, wheat, oats, soybean meal, and soybean oil, a dynamic visually substantiated by the grains and oilseeds sector where wheat and oats move into sustained positive territory through much of 2022. Similarly, soybean meal shows a strong, long-lasting positive lagged contribution that lifts the overall series through 2022 and into 2023; however, raw soybeans diverge from this pattern with an early spike dominated by contemporaneous effects that fades into a sustained negative lagged phase, implying the initial move was more immediate compared to the persistent positive carryover in meal and oil. Conversely, cocoa, lean hog, juice, and barley mainly act as risk receivers. Among them, barley is the biggest receiver of net spillovers, characterized by a series that remains predominantly negative for an extended period, indicating a prolonged drag rather than a short-lived shock. Lean hogs also illustrate this receiver status with sharp negative lagged impacts immediately after the early-2022 break that later mean-revert. The remaining markets alternate between being transmitters and receivers, displaying mixed behaviors such as the deep mid-2022 negative trough in rice followed by a recovery, a late-2022 upswing in sugar driven by lagged effects, and a downward trend in coffee into 2023.
