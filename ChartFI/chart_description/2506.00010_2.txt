The chart presents a comparison of cumulative early-stage private investment for the periods 2006–2013 and 2014–2021 across various climate-tech sectors, detailing both the proportional mix of investment types—service, optimization, process, component, and end product—and the total funding in billions of dollars. Overall, the data reveals a $10.8 billion increase, primarily in other cleantech, transportation, agriculture, and advanced materials. Within this surge, private sector investment in agricultural climate-tech increased by nearly a factor of five from 2006–2013 to 2014–2021, aligning with the massive absolute jumps seen in transportation and cross-cutting categories. Conversely, the later period saw a $1.0 billion decrease, primarily in solar and energy efficiency, with smaller decreases in energy storage, wind, and fuel cells & hydrogen. Alongside these absolute funding shifts, the proportional allocation within sectors underwent significant realignment. Correcting the initial assumption of a universal shift toward hardware, mature sectors like solar and wind actually show a noticeable rebalancing away from earlier mixes, with a decreased share of end-product and component investment—evidenced by dashed lines—and a corresponding shift toward service and optimization. Similarly, energy storage and smart grid saw components and end products take a smaller share in 2014–2021, suggesting a pivot toward software and service-oriented models as underlying enabling technologies matured. In contrast, areas like nuclear and geothermal remain comparatively smaller in total dollars and exhibit more fragmented mixes with less dramatic directional movement, indicating slower early-stage momentum. Ultimately, sectors with the strongest dollar growth tend to maintain robust allocations in tangible processes and end products, whereas sectors experiencing funding declines have largely transitioned their early-stage venture focus toward service and optimization.
