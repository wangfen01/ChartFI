The dataset covers 6 main categories—Life & Emotion, Art & Programs, Travel & Events, Sports & Outdoors, Knowledge, and Tech & Fashion—which are further subdivided into 43 specific subcategories representing a broad genre, format, and cultural expression. The distribution of content across these categories is fairly balanced, with the dataset encompassing a full spectrum of content types of video and music content. Annotation lengths are balanced across the 6 main categories and are heavily concentrated in the upper ranges, with the 300–350 and 350–400 word bands dominating counts, while the 200–250 range is consistently minimal; the average number of words in HarmonySet annotations is 352.65 words for each video-music pair. Art & Programs and Travel & Events exhibit especially high volumes in the longest annotation range, whereas Sports & Outdoors shows a stronger presence in the 300–350 band, suggesting slightly more moderate labeling needs. Video durations are concentrated between 10 seconds and 60 seconds, with a rich number of videos in each time segment and an overall range between 2.96 and 63.38 seconds at an average duration of 31.5 seconds. The video length distributions follow a clear bimodal pattern with peaks around 15–20 seconds and 55–60 seconds, indicating two common content formats: short clips and near-minute segments. Knowledge is the most distinctive category, exhibiting the strongest density around the longer-duration peak, which is consistent with explanatory or instructional content, while Travel & Events and Art & Programs lean more toward the shorter peak. Notably, longer annotations do not uniformly map to longer videos, as categories like Art & Programs pair very long annotations with relatively shorter videos, implying a higher semantic density per second and more complex labeling requirements despite a concise runtime.
