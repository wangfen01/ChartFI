The two confusion matrices compare entity classification performance for GPT CoT versus LLAMA-3-8B-FT across eight labels, illustrating the performance of GPT-CoT and Llama-FT on recognizing different entities in the NER task. While GPT CoT maintains strength in classes such as Number and Date, the chart reveals significant structural weaknesses, most notably a pronounced failure mode where Time is frequently predicted as Date; this results in a large off-diagonal mass of 69 misclassifications against only 34 correct predictions, indicating systematic boundary confusion between temporal expressions and calendar dates. Additionally, there is a marked drop in the recognition accuracy of Person entities by GPT, with 95 correctly recognized, as the model exhibits notable leakage into the generic Other class. A similar trend is observed for Organization entities, with 70 classified correctly by GPT CoT compared to significant misclassification into Other and Location categories. In contrast, LLAMA-3-8B-FT tightens performance across nearly all labels, producing a much cleaner diagonal with fewer cross-class confusions. The fine-tuned model effectively corrects the Time versus Date confusion, recovering Time as a distinct class with 138 correct predictions, and drastically improves specific entity identification, with Person entities rising to 139 correctly recognized by Llama-FT and Organization entities reaching 134 correctly recognized by Llama-FT. This suggests that fine-tuning improves the model’s ability to recognize Person and Organization entities, reducing reliance on the Other category and establishing more consistent decision boundaries.
