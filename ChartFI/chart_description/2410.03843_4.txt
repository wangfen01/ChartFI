The figure presents a comparative analysis of waveforms for a noisy sEMG signal and enhanced sEMG outputs generated by TrustEMG-Net, an IIR filter, an EMD-based method, a CEEMDAN-based method, and a VMD-based method. In the raw input, the noisy sEMG segment was at an SNR of -0.47 dB, exhibiting heavy contamination that obscures the underlying structure. Traditional processing via IIR filters had almost no effect on the removal of WGN, resulting in a negligible SNR improvement of -0.26 dB with persistent high-frequency jitter. Moving to decomposition techniques, the EMD- and CEEMDAN-based methods lift the SNR to 2.25 dB and 2.72 dB respectively; however, while these approaches can remove most contaminants, they tend to discard sEMG signals, resulting in visible amplitude attenuation and loss of peak morphology. The VMD-based method performs better in sEMG reconstruction, reaching an SNR of 4.21 dB, yet the contaminant is still visually apparent in the sEMG segments, particularly in the inactive state where no sEMG exists. Ultimately, TrustEMG-Net effectively removed contaminants and reconstructed the sEMG, yielding the highest SNR among all the methods at 5.73 dB. This standout performance is characterized by a consistent overlap with the clean trace, preserving signal integrity in both transient and quiet intervals without the residual noise or signal loss observed in the other methods.
