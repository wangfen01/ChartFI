While all models generally show a decline in AP as the evaluation criteria become stricter, their performance trajectories and stability differ significantly. However, the proposed method's performance rapidly improves, reaching a peak near an IoU of 0.65 where it surpasses all other models. As the threshold continues to tighten past 0.70, the performance hierarchy shifts decisively; our proposed method (represented by the yellow dashed line) maintains higher average precision (AP) at higher Mask IoU thresholds, consistently outperforming the baselines. In stark contrast, models like YOLOv5 experience a steep performance drop, becoming the weakest performer at high IoUs. This divergence in performance is explained by the ΔAP curves, which indicate that our method exhibits smaller performance fluctuations at different Mask IoU thresholds. Conversely, models such as YOLOv5, YOLOv6, YOLOv7, and YOLOv8 experience larger performance fluctuations, particularly at higher Mask IoU thresholds, signifying less stability. An interesting exception is CenterNet, which shows a pronounced rebound in its ΔAP around an IoU of 0.80, suggesting a relative stabilization in its performance decay. Ultimately, the chart demonstrates that model ranking is highly dependent on the stringency of the IoU threshold, with the proposed method's superior stability and precision making it the most robust choice under the most demanding evaluation conditions.
