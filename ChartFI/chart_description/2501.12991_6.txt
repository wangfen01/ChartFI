The three panels track the sum rate (Rsum), 5-percentile rate (Rperc), and Rscore for three proposed offline MARL schemes—C-MARL-CQL (SAC), I-MARL-CQL (SAC), and CTDE-MARL-CQL (SAC)—over 300 training episodes, comparing them against two benchmark schemes, C-MARL (SAC) and C-MARL (DQN). Regarding the sum rate, the three MARL schemes almost achieve the same sum rate by the end of training, consistently outperforming the C-MARL (SAC) benchmark. While CTDE-MARL-CQL (SAC) and I-MARL-CQL (SAC) maintain high performance throughout, hovering in the high 16s to low 18s, C-MARL-CQL (SAC) begins significantly lower before climbing steadily and showing a distinct late-stage acceleration after episode 220 to converge with the other two, though none of the methods reach the higher performance ceiling set by the C-MARL (DQN) benchmark. The primary distinction among the methods emerges in the other metrics, where C-MARL-CQL (SAC) has the highest 5-percentile rate. This is driven by a significant improvement trajectory where its Rperc increases from around 0.16 to stabilize near the C-MARL (SAC) benchmark level of 0.27, following a sharp step-up around episodes 175-200. This breakthrough is mirrored in its Rscore, which improves consistently from just under 1.0 to approximately 1.5, ultimately surpassing the C-MARL (SAC) benchmark. In contrast, CTDE-MARL-CQL (SAC) demonstrates stable, high performance, as it outperforms the C-MARL (SAC) Rscore benchmark throughout the training and finishes with only a tiny gap with C-MARL-CQL (SAC), though its Rperc oscillates around 0.23–0.25 without a sustained upward trend. Meanwhile, I-MARL-CQL (SAC) shows early promise but its performance degrades over time, with its Rperc trending downward from the mid-0.20s toward 0.20.
