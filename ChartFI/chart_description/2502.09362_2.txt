This histogram presents the articles in our comprehensive-futuring sample (blue) and in the fleeting-futuring sample (green) by year on the x-axis and by publication venue on the y-axis, where bar length corresponds to the number of articles in each category and empty space indicates that no relevant articles were published in the relevant year and venue. The data reveal a growing rate of future-oriented papers from 2008 to 2023, with overall activity heavily concentrated in CHI, which serves as the dominant driver of the total trend through a clear ramp-up from the early 2010s into a sustained high-output period. Within CHI, comprehensive contributions become increasingly prominent after the mid-2010s, yet the fleeting futuring category’s dominance is evident across the broader dataset as these entries remain consistently present and often sizable, suggesting a stable pipeline of shorter-form work alongside growing depth in longer-form publications. Outside of CHI, most channels exhibit intermittent, low-volume bursts rather than steady trajectories, with CSCW and DIS showing occasional spikes in the late 2010s and early 2020s that reflect episodic attention shifts rather than structural growth. While venues like UIST and IUI maintain a relatively steady but modest presence characterized largely by fleeting-futuring work, IMWUT appears only in a narrow window around the late 2010s, and TOCHI shows a late-period uptick in both categories, hinting at a redirected emphasis toward journal-style outputs near the end of the series.
