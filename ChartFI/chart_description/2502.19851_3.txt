The provided scatter plots illustrate how the Risk Gap and the Monotone Risk Gap relate to three calibration metrics: Delta_dCE, Delta_Cutoff, and Delta_ECE, with point color intensity representing increasing alpha. A distinct contrast in predictive power is evident across the metrics; Delta_dCE has a noisy—and at times negligible—relationship with either gap, characterized by weak Spearman correlations of approximately 0.18 to 0.19. Unlike the other metrics, when Delta_dCE is small, there is no guarantee that the risk gaps will also be small, as indicated by the wide vertical spread of data points even at low metric values. In contrast, Delta_Cutoff and Delta_ECE track both outcomes closely, producing clear monotonic trends; Delta_Cutoff exhibits a very strong rank association for Risk Gap (Spearman 0.96) and a strong relationship for Monotone Risk Gap (0.85), while Delta_ECE shows similarly high correlations of 0.96 and 0.82, respectively. Consistent with these strong correlations, Delta_Cutoff and Delta_ECE being small corresponds to smaller risk gaps for both standard and monotone measures. Across the visualizations, higher alpha values tend to coincide with larger Delta_Cutoff and Delta_ECE values and correspondingly larger gaps, suggesting that threshold shifts and miscalibration changes are primary drivers of disparity.
