The figure compares efficacy versus fidelity across four watermarking methods (Unigram, KGW, AAR, EXP) and three model settings (Llama-3-70B, Llama-2-7B, Qwen2-72B), where the y-axis indicates Efficacy, measured by ROC-AUC, while the x-axis indicates Fidelity, measured by P-SP. We draw the Pareto front of B^4 by LOWESS, which traces a clear upward curve across the panels, indicating a relationship where higher fidelity generally coincides with higher ROC-AUC values. Crucially, the Pareto front for B^4 consistently lies below the data points of other methods such as RP, Base, and DIPPER, which tend to cluster at relatively high efficacy even when fidelity is only moderate. This positioning signifies that B^4 outperforms baselines across all settings; specifically, under the same fidelity constraints, B^4 is more effective in scrubbing the watermark, reflected by lower ROC-AUCs relative to the comparison groups. AAR appears to present a tighter trade-off where B^4 rises smoothly to meet the baselines, whereas KGW shows more dispersion in the non-blue points.
