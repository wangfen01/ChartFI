This chart visualizes participants’ success and time spent through individual session timelines, where stacked segments represent successive issues tackled and are colored by outcome to reveal performance patterns. The data indicates a total success rate of 55%, with participants being satisfied with the final patch for 16 of these tasks. While Complete Success is visible, often appearing as shorter segments or within multi-issue sequences, Patch Success is frequent and occupies significant portions of the timelines, particularly for Feature Requests and UI Changes. Conversely, a substantial number of sessions end in incomplete outcomes, demonstrating that resolution often remained partial even after significant effort. Time spent varies widely across the cohort, ranging from short sessions of approximately 20–30 minutes to extended efforts exceeding an hour, though longer durations do not consistently correlate with better outcomes. Semantic Bugs and Feature Requests emerge as the most common issue types; however, Semantic Bugs are more frequently associated with incomplete results or blockages, such as one participant exhibiting a long “No Progress” stretch. Furthermore, 4 of these tasks were abandoned for external reasons, including specific instances where a UI Change was abandoned early in favor of a later task, or where a Semantic Bug fix was discarded entirely.
