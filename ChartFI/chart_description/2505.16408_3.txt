These three heatmaps illustrate the culture-specific classification performance of Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct based on the ranks of adaptation results across ten distinct cultures, where lower numerical values and darker colors indicate a superior rank. In the WVS baseline, performance is uneven and strongly culture-pair dependent; while Spanish and Turkish adapters consistently produce high ranks (1–3) across multiple test cultures, there are clear weak spots where ranks drop into the 8–10 range for specific pairings. The transition to the Wikipedia-augmented dataset (WVS+Wiki) shifts the matrix toward a more diagonalized pattern, where darker diagonal elements indicate stronger cultural distinctiveness and reduced cross-cultural interference. This shift suggests that while Wikipedia augmentation can enhance culture-specific alignment, it also introduces a broad degradation in relative ranking for off-diagonal combinations, evidenced by the emergence of large blocks of high ranks (7–10) that reduce general cross-cultural robustness. With the introduction of NormAd (WVS+NormAd), the heatmaps become even more diagonal and show reduced cross-cultural interference, though the pattern becomes more polarized. Under NormAd, several adapters like Portuguese and Greek show localized gains and sharper cultural specialization, yet this comes at the cost of pronounced failures where off-diagonal ranks spike to 9–10. Ultimately, while the WVS baseline relies on a few dominant adapters, the integration of Wikipedia and NormAd progressively trades broad consistency for selective improvements, amplifying cultural distinctiveness while increasing the performance gap between native and non-native cultural test environments.
