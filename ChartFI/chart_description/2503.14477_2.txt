This plot presents the Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) for samples, categorized into four classes by mapping semantic entropy against verbal uncertainty (VU). The visualization reveals distinct regimes for complying versus abstaining behaviors. In the complying region, correct answers cluster tightly at low verbal uncertainty and low semantic entropy, indicating confident, consistent outputs. Conversely, hallucinated answers display a critical anomaly: while they generally shift toward higher entropy, a large segment of these complying answers exhibits high Semantic Entropy—a proxy for Semantic Uncertainty (SU)—paired with low VU. This specific alignment of high SU and low VU indicates miscalibration and highlights the primary area requiring intervention. In the abstained region, responses concentrate near the high end of verbal uncertainty as expected, but separate vertically based on entropy. Partly abstained responses retain high entropy, suggesting inconsistent content, while consistently abstained responses drop to low semantic entropy. Although this combination of low SU and high VU in the consistently abstained cluster mirrors the inverse of the hallucination miscalibration, it is not a problematic mismatch; rather, it is an artifact of using semantic entropy as a proxy for SU, representing cases where the model "knows that it does not know" and deals with them appropriately through stable refusal patterns.
