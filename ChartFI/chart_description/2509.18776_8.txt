This grouped bar chart evaluates the performance metrics of nine distinct large language models across a spectrum of tasks categorized into three primary levels, denoted as 5-1, 5-2, and 5-3. These figures also include horizontal lines representing the average performance for each level and sub-level, which quantitatively highlight a diminishing return in model efficacy as task complexity increases. Specifically, the aggregate average score drops from 61.30 in the initial document processing tasks to 46.20 in the architectural and structural design evaluation phase, and finally to 36.62 in the generative tasks. A similar downward trend is also evident across the three sub-levels of the Knowledge Application level, reinforcing the correlation between task sophistication and reduced model accuracy. Through this comparative analysis, both major inter-level gaps and fine-grained intra-level differences were successfully identified. Notably, the first level exhibits the highest internal variance, where tasks such as Document Classification and Proofreading yield scores consistently above 80, whereas Compliance Checking and Brand Compliance Verification drop significantly, often falling below 40, contrasting with the latter levels where performance is more uniformly suppressed.
