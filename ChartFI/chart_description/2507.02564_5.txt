This heatmap displays the percentage of interviews in which a specific requirement was elicited across four distinct prompts and scenario combinations, revealing significant variations based on prompt length and context. Overall, requirements R1, R2, and R5 were elicited best over both scenarios; R1 remains perfectly stable at 100% across all conditions, while R5 improves from 66.7% in the Long-Salon setting to 100% in all others. A critical observation is that the short prompt not only performed better in eliciting a large set of requirements in general but also managed to elicit all requirements at least once over all interviews. In contrast, the long prompt fails to do so for two requirements of the Salon scenario, specifically R4 and R8, which both register 0.0% in the Long-Salon column. R4 is particularly volatile, recovering from zero to 66.7% in Short-Salon and peaking at 87.5% in Long-Ski, suggesting sensitivity to both scenario and prompt length. Generally, the Ski scenarios yield stronger results than the Salon scenarios, with requirements R3 and R7 rising from low-to-mid values in Salon to high performance (87.5% to 100%) in Ski. However, this trend is not universal; R6 declines from a ceiling of 100% in Long-Salon to 62.5% in Long-Ski, indicating it is not reliably reinforced by the Ski framing. R8 remains the clearest underperformer, staying low across all conditions regardless of the prompt. Finally, requirements R9 through R12 appear exclusively in the Ski scenarios, where the short prompt tends to enhance performance—most notably for R12, which reaches 100%—though R9 remains flat at 25%, implying that while shortening the prompt helps newer requirements, the effect is not uniform.
