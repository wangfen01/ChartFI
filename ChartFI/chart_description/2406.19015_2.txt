The chart presents the estimations of the logarithmic probability densities of the distributions of current, cell voltages, and State of Charge (SOC), as well as the probability density of temperatures across 29 segments. The estimated current, voltage, and SOC densities are plotted on a logarithmic scale to highlight behavioral diversity and boundary effects. Current distributions are generally centered near 0 A, suggesting substantial time spent at rest or low-load transitions, but frequently skew negative with broad discharge-heavy operation extending to roughly −200 to −300 A, while a smaller number display meaningful positive tails up to about 150–200 A. The distribution of voltage shows that some batteries were overcharged (> 3.6 V) or undercharged (< 2.0 V), or both at some point; while the dominant operating window is tightly clustered around 3.0–3.6 V, clear regime differences appear where segments spread down toward near 0–1 V or show sparse high-voltage outliers beyond 4.5 V. Regarding energy storage levels, the observed Depth of Discharges (DODs) are predominantly limited to the upper half of the SOC range, and much time was spent fully charged for most systems, evidenced by densities heavily piled up near the 90–100% boundary. Finally, the thermal data indicate a wide temperature distribution where most readings are in a range between 10 and 45 °C, a lot wider than the distributions of most other systems; notably, many temperature measurements are below room temperature, suggesting that some systems were operated outdoors or in unheated environments, while occasional warmer tails appear episodic rather than systemic.
