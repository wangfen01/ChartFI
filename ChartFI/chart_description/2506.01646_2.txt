The chart illustrates the relationship between model size and zero-shot accuracy across 50 LLMs evaluated on the ESGenius benchmark, plotting model sizes on a log 10 scale (in billions of parameters) ranging from 0.5B to 671B against accuracy percentages. Dot colors denote five model families—DeepSeek, Gemma, GPT, Meta-Llama, and Qwen—revealing a moderate positive correlation between model size and performance where larger models generally perform better, though the relationship exhibits diminishing returns. While sub-1B to ~2B models span a wide accuracy band from the high 20s to around 60%, suggesting smaller models are highly sensitive to instruction tuning, the majority of models cluster between 55% and 70% accuracy once they reach the efficient 7B to 14B range. A critical driver of performance at the top end is architectural specialization; models augmented with explicit reasoning capabilities demonstrate enhanced performance on ESG tasks compared to non-reasoning models of comparable scale. For instance, the reasoning variant of o3 attained an accuracy of 72.54%, the best model score observed, exceeding the scores of similarly sized models without such enhancements. Conversely, sharp underperformers appear at moderate sizes, such as a ~27B model near the low 20s and a ~32B model around 39%, which break the general scaling trend and point to mismatched objectives or training issues rather than capacity limits. Overall, the pattern suggests strong returns up to the mid-teens of billions of parameters, followed by a plateau where model quality is driven more by advanced tuning and reasoning capabilities than by sheer scale alone.
