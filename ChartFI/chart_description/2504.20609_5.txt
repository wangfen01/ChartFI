This radar plot shows model performance on WenyanBench, comparing nine language models across six Classical Chinese tasks with values normalized to a 0-1 scale. It is evident that WenyanGPT has achieved the highest score in six Classical Chinese tasks, demonstrating that it can achieve better performance than the previous open source LLMs in these specialized domains; its dominance is particularly pronounced in Named Entity Recognition (NER), Punctuation, and Word explanation. In contrast, the performance of the other models is uneven across the different capabilities, with most clustering in the mid-to-high range on Punctuation and Part-of-speech tagging. While Translation scores are relatively consistent across most models, NER shows a wide performance gap, with models like Baichuan2-7B-Chat exhibiting a pronounced weakness that creates a visible "dent" in its performance shape. Word explanation appears to be the most challenging task for the group, as several models score near zero. A second tier of models, including gpt-4o and GLM-4-9B-Chat, maintains a more balanced, albeit lower, performance profile but does not approach WenyanGPT's peak levels in any category.
