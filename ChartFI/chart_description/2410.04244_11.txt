The figure presents the solar irradiance curve, the frequency of update signal occurrences, and the distribution of three fitted model parameters—Rs, Rsh, and KD—from Nov. 08 to Nov. 14. The irradiance data reveals distinct meteorological conditions across the observed week: on Nov. 9th, the irradiance curve gently slopes at a certain point of high value, indicating a clear sky and sunny day, while the 14th experienced a brief cloudy period in the morning, followed by sunny conditions that resulted in a high, sustained plateau. In contrast, on November 8th, 10th, 12th, and 13th, numerous ripples and spikes observed in the irradiance curve denote cloudy days with frequent partial shading moments. Furthermore, November 11th was characterized by significantly low solar irradiance and can be defined as an overcast day with the sky fully covered by clouds. These environmental fluctuations correlate with the update signal, which is intermittent during stable periods like Nov. 09 but becomes almost continuously active during the volatile or overcast days, particularly from Nov. 11 to Nov. 13. Across the daily parameter distributions, Rs is lowest and tightest on the sunny Nov. 09, then becomes more variable with pronounced high-end outliers on Nov. 10–Nov. 13, indicating occasional excursions to higher series resistance during unstable weather; Nov. 14 shifts upward in central tendency while remaining relatively tight. Rsh is broadly centered around the low-to-mid 200 Ω range, though Nov. 08 stands out with the widest spread and several high outliers, and Nov. 10 shows a distinct low-side tail implying brief drops in shunt resistance. KD remains tightly clustered near ~1.1 throughout, with only subtle widening around the overcast conditions of Nov. 11–Nov. 13, indicating this parameter is comparatively stable and less sensitive to the changing conditions than Rs or Rsh.
