This multi-panel chart illustrates the time series of PRBs allocated to our test UE and to other UEs alongside the rate gap, network delay, Google Congestion Control (GCC) state, and target rate over a 3.5-second sender timeline. Initially, Physical Resource Block (PRB) usage is dominated by the experimental UE with relatively stable delay near the baseline, but the presence of significant cross traffic, indicated by the yellow bars, commences at around time 0.8 s (marked by 1). During this period, the number of PRBs assigned to our test UE decreases substantially as demand shifts toward other UEs, signaling contention and reduced available capacity for the sender. The second subplot displays the rate gap, where the packet rate frequently exceeds the PHY transport block rate, resulting in a positive rate gap that indicates the PHY layer cannot sustain the packet sending rate. This mismatch coincides with increasing latency 2, where delay drifts upward with heightened volatility from low tens of milliseconds to over 240 ms. As sustained queuing persists, GCC detects Overuse at approximately 1.6 s (marked by 3), triggering a sharp step down in the target rate from 6 Mbps to below 3 Mbps to mitigate congestion. A notable shift occurs near the 2.5-second mark when delay decreases rapidly (marked by 4) back toward baseline levels, aligning with a reduction in cross-traffic pressure and a transition of the GCC state from overuse back to normal and eventually underuse. Despite this recovery, the target rate remains conservative through the end of the trace, suggesting a cautious recovery period or hysteresis following the prolonged congestion event.
