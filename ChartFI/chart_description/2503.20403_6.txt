This chart illustrates the adaptation of alpha and beta parameters with respect to prediction error for tests 11 and 12, comparing the performance of UKF and EKF models. The UKF results are better because the parameters are more stable, demonstrating that the UKF model parameters adapt and stabilize as the prediction time progresses. Specifically, in the UKF panels, Alpha and Beta rise rapidly from near zero to roughly 0.9 within the first 10–15 minutes and then asymptotically approach 1.0, with the two curves closely tracking each other. Although the associated error signal is mostly low-to-moderate with intermittent spikes, UKF Test 12 is notable for a pronounced late spike around the 60-minute mark, suggesting a transient disturbance despite the parameters remaining saturated near 1. In sharp contrast, the EKF model parameters are non-stable, exhibiting continuous oscillations of the statespace parameters alpha and beta alongside slower adaptation and mid-run drift. In EKF Test 11, Alpha settles into a long plateau around 0.6 while Beta drops near zero before a slow recovery, resulting in highly volatile error rates. EKF Test 12 reveals even greater instability, where Alpha declines into negative territory near 20–30 minutes before gradually recovering to only the mid-0.4s, while Beta trends upward steadily. This parameter imbalance in the EKF model coincides with error levels that rise through the second half and peak strongly around 60–65 minutes, confirming that while the UKF achieves robust convergence, the EKF suffers from significant parameter bias and delayed correction.
