This radar chart provides an intuitive comparison between our model and other state-of-the-art 3D Large Multimodal Models (LMMs) across a suite of 3D understanding benchmarks, where the overall shape of each method's profile reveals its performance consistency. The proposed model, represented by the outermost red profile, achieves the best results among the competitors on most of the benchmarks, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance across these datasets. It establishes clear leadership in referring expression tasks with scores of 43.3 on ScanRefer and 42.7 on Multi3DRefer; remarkably, it achieves 92.6% CiDEr on the competitive ScanQA dataset. On the Scan2Cap family of metrics, ours maintains consistently high scores, indicating balanced and robust captioning capabilities rather than a spike on a single metric. In contrast, a second tier of models, including Chat-Scene and LEO, tracks close to the leader on several axes but lacks the same consistency across the full spectrum of tasks. Other baselines exhibit more pronounced trade-offs, resulting in uneven, star-like shapes, with Grounded 3D-LLM, for instance, showing sharp performance dips that suggest specialization that does not generalize well. The most significant pattern revealed is that the top-performing model does not merely peak on one benchmark but maintains superior performance across referring expression, question answering, and captioning tasks simultaneously, signaling a strong capacity for robust generalization in 3D scene understanding.
