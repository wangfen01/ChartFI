The figure presents the quantitative attack results of different attack methods in face verification settings on commercial APIs (Face++ and Aliyun), benchmarking recent methods including 4 unrestricted makeup-based methods and 5 unrestricted facial semantical invariant methods. Overall, results are consistently higher on Face++ than on Aliyun, indicating the attacks transfer more effectively or the target is more vulnerable on Face++ across nearly all methods. On Face++, the top-performing methods cluster in the low-to-high 70s, yet Adv-CPG has higher and more stable confidence scores than state-of-the-art noise-based and makeup-based facial privacy protection methods. DiffAM and GIFT remain competitive in the low-to-mid 70s; in contrast, DPG is a clear underperformer on Face++, centered in the mid-50s. On Aliyun, there is a pronounced downward shift for most defenses, with central values commonly landing in the 40s to high 50s. Despite this drop, the results demonstrate that the average confidence scores of Adv-CPG on each API are about 77.5 and 63.0, respectively, which is superior to current SOTA methods. While P3-Mask remains relatively robust in the low-to-mid 60s on Aliyun, DPG drops further to the low 40s, becoming the weakest method by a wide margin. Ultimately, the attack effectiveness is comparatively stable for Adv-CPG, whereas variability appears larger for several mid-tier methods on Aliyun, implying less consistent outcomes for competing defenses.
