The heatmap illustrates the impact of visual context on translation quality by comparing average chrF++ scores for text+image (T+I) and text-only (T) settings across various languages and models, where the image in the T+I setting is randomly sampled. Each cell displays paired scores (T+I / T) and is color-coded by the difference, with the left panel showing X to English translation and the right panel showing English to X. Unlike scenarios where the image corresponds to the translated sentence, unrelated images consistently lower chrF++ scores compared to the text-only setting, as evidenced by the predominance of red cells across nearly all model and language pairs. In the X to English direction, Gemini 2.0 Flash and the Gemma models maintain relatively high baseline scores but still exhibit a clear performance degradation when presented with irrelevant visual noise. Qwen2.5-VL 7B appears particularly susceptible to this interference, showing sharp declines in low-resource or non-Latin-script languages such as Oromo (om_eth) and Igbo (ig_nga), where the T+I score collapses significantly compared to the text-only baseline. While the larger Qwen2.5-VL 32B demonstrates greater stability than its 7B counterpart, it still follows the downward trend, suggesting that increased model scale mitigates but does not eliminate the negative impact of unrelated images. In the English to X direction, the degradation is even more pronounced and polarized. The average row for both panels confirms that unrelated images consistently lower chrF++ scores compared to the text-only setting across the board, with the most severe penalties clustering in lower-resource linguistic settings and smaller model configurations, highlighting a cross-model vulnerability to irrelevant visual distractors.
