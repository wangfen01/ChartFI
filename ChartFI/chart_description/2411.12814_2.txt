The figure positions IMed-361M as a large, multi-modal medical imaging resource containing 6.4 million images, 87.6 million ground-truth (GT) masks, and 273.4 million interactive masks. The modality wheel highlights broad coverage spanning CT, multiple MR sequences, ultrasound, X-ray, and endoscopy, signaling that interactive supervision is a major component rather than a small add-on. Anatomically, the GT annotations are categorized into six groups: Head and Neck, Thorax, Skeleton, Abdomen, Pelvis, and Lesions. The log-scaled counts within these regions reveal strong long-tail behavior, where a handful of categories dominate while many others trail by orders of magnitude, suggesting potential class imbalance. Regarding image quality, over 83% of the images have resolutions between 256 x 256 and 1024 x 1024, with a sharp drop in volume beyond 1024 pixels, indicating that most training signals come from moderate-resolution imagery. Mask availability shifts systematically with object size, as most masks occupy less than 2% of the image area; however, interactive masks provide over one million instances across different coverage intervals. These interactive masks remain consistently more abundant than GT labels across all size bins, implying that larger or more extensive segmentations are more often captured through interactive workflows. Finally, the dataset-size comparison demonstrates that IMed-361M far exceeds other resources, providing 14.4 times the number of masks compared to the MedTrinity-25M dataset, thereby reinforcing its primary differentiator of scale combined with a breadth of modalities.
