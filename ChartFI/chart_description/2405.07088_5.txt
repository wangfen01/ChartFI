This SHAP summary plot illustrates the feature importance and impact on the model's Situation Awareness (SA) predictions, where the y-axis shows the importance ranking of the features, ranging from the most to the least significant, and the x-axis is determined by the SHAP value, where every point on the plot represents a feature’s SHAP value for a given instance. The color scale, ranging from blue (low) to red (high), indicates the magnitude and direction of a feature’s impact on the predicted SA score, while overlapping points are jittered in the y-axis direction to describe the distribution of the SHAP values per feature. The most important feature was age, which exhibits the widest spread of SHAP values; the distribution reveals that higher age values generally push the prediction downward while lower age values push it upward, indicating large swings in predicted outcome across the age range. AvKnowledge is the next strongest factor. Among physiological signals, mean_gsr, mean_HR, and mean_HRV show moderate but consistent influence, where higher values of these signals tend to be associated with higher model output, while lower values pull predictions down, suggesting the model is sensitive to arousal or workload-related variation. Eye-movement features such as number_of_fixations_game and number_of_fixations_center have a smaller average impact but still show meaningful directional structure, where higher fixation counts more often align with positive SHAP contributions. Conversely, dispersion and duration metrics across game, center, left, right, and odometer regions are tightly clustered around zero, implying they add incremental refinement rather than driving predictions. Gender sits near the middle with a relatively narrow SHAP range, indicating limited influence compared with age, knowledge, and physiology. A notable pattern is the sharp drop-off in importance after the top few variables, with most remaining features exhibiting minimal impact, suggesting the model’s predictive power is concentrated in a small set of high-signal inputs dominated by participant characteristics and physiological signals.
