These heatmaps visualize the learned Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MCD) between sub-windows for primary synthetic data sets, where the horizontal axis represents the locations of the current sub-window at each time step and the vertical axis encompasses preceding sub-windows in the same window. Each cell indicates the learned MCD between sub-window pairs, with significant distributional shifts highlighted by drift indicators consisting of red dashed vertical lines and arrows. Across the four panels, intensity is generally low and diffuse for most of the x-range but repeatedly collapses into sharp, high-intensity regions immediately after these markers. In GM_Sud, the field is relatively steady and muted until a single pronounced shift around x ≈ 20,700, where sudden drift manifests as a distinct lower triangular pattern starting at the drift point, suggesting one dominant sudden-change episode. GM_Rec demonstrates recurring drift as two clear lower triangles, one centered near x ≈ 15,000 and a second near x ≈ 25,200, implying repeated but temporally separated reactivation. GM_Grad exhibits the most frequent and structured patterning, with multiple markers spanning roughly 12,000 through 25,000; here, gradual drift progressively forms lower triangular patterns from top to bottom, pointing to a process punctuated by several consistent transitions rather than one-off shocks. GM_Inc also shows clustered markers and three major periods around 12,000, 18,600, and 25,200, where incremental drift results in fainter lower triangles due to more subtle distributional changes, with the densest clustering near 25,000. A notable anomaly across panels is the long, pale stretches between these active periods, especially beyond x ≈ 27,000, where activity drops off sharply, indicating the system settles into a low-variation state after the final major transition.
