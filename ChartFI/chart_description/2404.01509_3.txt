This correlation heatmap illustrates the relationships between various ImageNet-related measures, SIN, and PGD, revealing that performance on many ImageNet benchmarks is moderate to strongly correlated while highlighting the existence of clear outliers. Overall, the IN family is strongly interrelated, with ImageNet accuracy strongly correlating with the average (r = 0.79) of the other benchmarks (excluding SIN and PGD); the tightest alignment within this group is between IN and IN-V2, which are nearly perfectly correlated at 0.99. Notably, performance on IN-C correlates well with most other benchmarks and shows an even stronger correlation with the remaining benchmarks (r = 0.83) than ImageNet itself. While IN-R and IN-S form a secondary strong pairing that connects well to the IN-C cluster, the matrix reveals distinct boundaries with specialized metrics. Adversarial robustness (here: PGD) is not correlated to most other benchmarks, effectively behaving independently except for a unique, small negative correlation with IN-Real. Similarly, SIN is largely uncorrelated with the main IN construct, though it shares a moderate positive relationship with the IN-R and IN-S subcomponents. Just like ImageNet, IN-C does not correlate with PGD and SIN, confirming that while standard accuracy metrics are highly predictive of one another, they do not generalize to these distinct types of robustness.
