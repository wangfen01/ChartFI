The two panels compare the performance of eight detection methods across FDI types 1–6 and a MIX setting, utilizing AUC and MAP@20 metrics to evaluate separability and ranking quality. Overall, the ensemble-style methods, Arith and Geo, emerge as the most consistently strong performers, maintaining a high performance band on AUC that peaks sharply around FDI type 4 and demonstrating robust ranking capabilities with near-perfect MAP@20 scores in the same scenario. In contrast, the results demonstrate that the correlation-based methods exhibit excellent performance in detecting FDI1; however, they perform poorly in detecting FDI5 and FDI6, showing a pronounced downward trend in these latter types. Regarding the unsupervised clustering methods, especially CFSFDP and LOF, the data reveals they have quite high values of AUC in detecting FDI4, FDI5, and FDI6, effectively countering the decline seen in correlation metrics. Among the black lines representing these baselines, CFSFDP is found to have the best performance in detecting FDI5, FDI6, and type MIX among all the clustering methods, and the MAP@20 of CFSFDP is much higher than that of LOF for these types. Conversely, FCM exhibits poor performance across most types, notably collapsing to near-zero metrics at FDI type 4, with its only relative recovery occurring at FDI6.
