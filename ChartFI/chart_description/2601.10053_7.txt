The heatmap visualizes the activation intensity of eight block indices, represented as rows, across 256 prototype indices on the horizontal axis, where each column indicates a prototype within the corresponding concept memory. Values range from approximately 0 to 0.9, with darker tones signifying lower intensity and brighter yellow indicating higher intensity. The overall distribution is highly heterogeneous, characterized by frequent, rapid column-to-column fluctuations rather than smooth gradients, which suggests that the measured signal varies sharply by prototype. A key observation is the widespread use of active prototypes across blocks; rather than the signal collapsing to only a few dominant slots, a wide range of prototypes exhibit nonnegligible activation responses. This distribution is well spread across all eight blocks, as mid-range purples and oranges dominate the visualization without any single block appearing uniformly higher or lower. While intermittent bright spikes and isolated low-response outliers appear sporadically, they are scattered throughout the prototype axis, reinforcing the conclusion that the system maintains a broad, block-specific sensitivity across the concept memory rather than relying on a limited set of universally strong prototypes.
