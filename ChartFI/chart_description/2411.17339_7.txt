This set of heatmaps illustrates the frequency of the macro space and candidate operations in the real Pareto architecture set compared to the architecture set searched by KEGNAS-NSGAII across multiple datasets, ranging from Cora to ArXiv. Significant differences can be observed in the macro space distribution of the searched architectures in different datasets, particularly when contrasting the algorithm's output with the ground truth. In particular, small-scale datasets such as Cora, CiteSeer, and PubMed prefer shallow topologies, including F, G, H, and I, within the searched results, indicating a distinct structural preference for these lighter tasks. In addition, there are significant differences between the architecture set obtained by KEGNAS-NSGAII and the real Pareto architecture set on small-scale datasets; for instance, while the real Pareto set for Cora heavily favors topology A, the searched architectures shift focus toward topologies C and G. Conversely, the other datasets exhibit a more balanced distribution across the macro space. Regarding the candidate operations displayed in the lower panels, both the real Pareto and searched sets show a consistent high frequency of Fully Connected (FC) and Residual Connection (RC) operations across most datasets, suggesting that operation selection remains relatively stable compared to the variability observed in macro space topology choices.
