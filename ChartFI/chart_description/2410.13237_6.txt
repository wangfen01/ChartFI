This composite visualization illustrates the relationship between language confusion and text reconstruction performance, measured in BLEU, within the context of Multilingual Textual Embedding Inversion attacks. The analysis is divided into evaluation languages in the upper panel and training languages in the lower panel, utilizing a dual-axis format where bar heights represent confusion entropy at the line level and overlaying trend lines indicate reconstruction performance across three evaluation steps. The upper chart shows directly that lower-resourced languages, especially those using non-Latin scripts such as Gujarati, Punjabi, and Urdu, present lower confusion entropy. Crucially, these languages are simultaneously more vulnerable to inversion attacks, evidenced by their significantly higher reconstruction performance scores compared to high-resource counterparts like Finnish or Hungarian, which exhibit high confusion and lower BLEU scores. Moving to the training dynamics in the lower section, the inversion models are categorized into three distinct settings: monolingual, in-family and in-script, and cross-family and cross-script. The data demonstrates that monolingual training consistently renders lower language confusion for each training language. Furthermore, when comparing paired training languages, in-script and in-family training configurations render higher language confusion compared to cross-script and cross-family training, highlighting how linguistic proximity in training data impacts the model's entropy levels.
