This chart evaluates the impact of tokenizing Rainbow-lite with unscaled (top) and scaled (bottom) architectures, reporting Median, IQM, Mean, and Optimality Gap scores across Impala and CNN families. By exploring both PerConv and PerFeat tokenization schemes using sum or mean aggregation over convolutional features, the data demonstrates that not flattening the convolutional outputs can lead to significant improvements, although the results vary depending on the architecture used and whether features are summed or averaged over. Impala proves to be the consistently stronger and more stable family, particularly within the scaled representation (bottom panel), where the baseline is weak; here, tokenization yields significant performance gains, driving the median and IQM into a much higher band. Conversely, in the unscaled architecture (top panel), while PerFeat variants generally sit furthest to the right indicating reliable gains, PerConv variants for Impala actually dip below the baseline, refining the observation that tokenization provides a uniform lift. CNN shows a markedly different profile with lower, more dispersed scores and mixed movement, indicating high sensitivity to the tokenization choice and less consistent robustness than Impala. This instability is most pronounced in the Optimality Gap column, where CNN exhibits larger gaps and wider spreads, implying that even when its mean improves, it does not reliably translate into near-optimal behavior. Ultimately, while this simple change can yield statistically significant improvements for Rainbow-lite, the benefits are most predictable in Impala using PerFeat tokenization, whereas CNN struggles to achieve consistent scores.
