This figure evaluates the efficacy of proprietary and open-source vision-language models in identifying and handling unanswerable questions across various categories and subplot densities. In the heatmaps, proprietary models such as GPT-4o and GPT-4V demonstrate robust dominance with consistently high identification rates across most question types, whereas open-source models exhibit significant performance dispersion and specific vulnerabilities. For example, IDEFICS 2 Chatty incorrectly responds to nearly 90% unanswerable questions about the title, X- and y-axis labels, yet correctly identifies more than 90% of unanswerable questions about intersections of lines and the presence of the legend. Conversely, IXC 2 correctly responds to 80% questions about names of title, X- and y-axis labels that are unanswerable, yet fails to identify unanswerable cases for the difference in tick values when ticks are categorical or the difference is not constant. When analyzing performance changes relative to visual complexity, a distinct divergence appears between task types. For descriptive questions, performance generally degrades as the number of subplots increases, but the magnitude of this drop differs significantly by model class; with 6+ subplots, the deterioration is 30% - 50% for open-source models and only 10% - 30% for proprietary models. In contrast to the descriptive tasks, there appears to be no clear correlation between reasoning capabilities and the number of subplots, indicating that increased visual fragmentation does not systematically impair reasoning performance in the same manner it hampers descriptive accuracy.
