The three histograms compare the 1-shot accuracy distributions of Chain-of-Thought (COT) and RFF on the GSM-Symbolic dataset across three progressively challenging settings, with the average accuracy score of the 50 subsets displayed in the upper right of each chart. In the GSM-SYM setting, both methods exhibit mid-to-high 70s performance with substantial overlap, where RFF maintains a slight lead with an average of 75.61 compared to 74.37 for COT. As the task difficulty increases in GSM-P1, the center of mass for both distributions shifts toward the low-to-mid 60s, yet RFF shows more stable and better distributed results at high accuracy, averaging 65.93 against 62.48 for COT while developing a more pronounced right tail. This trend intensifies in the GSM-P2 setting, where overall accuracy falls into the 30s and 40s; however, RFF sustains a clearer performance advantage with an average of 40.82 versus 36.84 for COT. Across all partitions, the data reveals a stepwise degradation in performance from SYM to P1 to P2, but RFF consistently demonstrates greater robustness and upside potential, characterized by a broader spread and a longer right tail that reaches toward higher accuracy levels even as the underlying tasks become more difficult.
