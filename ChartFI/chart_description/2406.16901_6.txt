This correlation heatmap illustrates the Pearson Correlation Coefficient (PCC) between reconstructed ECG leads and their original counterparts across various masking conditions, where each matrix entry at index (i, j) represents the PCC between the j-th reconstructed lead and the j-th original lead given that the input mask applied is C_{i}. The data reveals a predominantly high level of consistency, with correlations typically clustering between 0.85 and 0.99, though a distinct two-tier structure emerges based on the specific lead being reconstructed and the mask applied. A particularly strong correlation is observed between leads V3, V4, V5, and V6, which maintain exceptionally tight relationships frequently reaching 0.95 to 0.99. In contrast, lead III exhibits a lower correlation with the other leads, manifesting as a visible vertical band of weaker association where values drop into the 0.41 to 0.51 range, especially under masks C_{V1} through C_{V6}. While lead II alone demonstrates the ability to reconstruct all ECG leads with an accuracy of 0.87, the mask C_{V1} is the most challenging to reconstruct, as evidenced by the broader distribution of lower coefficients across that row compared to others. These patterns suggest that while most leads, particularly the precordial V2–V6 group and limb leads I and II, share a highly coherent signal structure, lead III remains a systematic anomaly with a weaker association that may stem from unique physiological characteristics or derivation differences.
