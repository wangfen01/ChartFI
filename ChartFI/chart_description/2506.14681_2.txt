The visualization presents a comprehensive analysis of how various instruction-tuning datasets influence downstream benchmark performance across multiple model families, beginning with a main heatmap that displays the average performance change for diverse benchmarks from each baseline model after supervised fine-tuning (SFT), with each column min-max scaled to a range of -1 to 1. General-purpose datasets like Alpaca and UltraChat generally deliver consistent performance boosts across nearly all categories, including instruction-following and broad evaluation suites, whereas LIMA, despite being a carefully curated dataset for SFT, did not yield substantial performance gains in this controlled setting compared to Alpaca and UltraChat. Specialized datasets show more targeted effects, as MathInstruct and OpenMathInstruct particularly boost MATH and GSM8K, while Magicoder benefits coding benchmarks yet still improves a wider task range than the math corpora. In contrast, the FLAN-style ClassicNLP datasets are detrimental to most tasks, exhibiting a notable pattern of negative transfer, except for Natural Questions which aligns with its specific domain. Notably, English-only SFT already transfers to Japanese (MMLU-jp) and Chinese (MMLU-zh) evaluation, indicating cross-lingual capabilities. Small-multiples heatmaps visualize these performance changes for each model individually, revealing that while overall tendencies are similar, there are also considerable differences across models; some models benefit from almost all training data, whereas others demonstrate minimal gains. A pairwise correlation matrix of performance changes across all SFT models, with a corresponding hierarchical-clustering dendrogram superimposed, reveals that the performance structures of the models are quite similar in general. Models belonging to the same family exhibit high correlations, suggesting that even with additional training, the impact of SFT remains similar within each family, and the language in which a model was initially trained does not appear to substantially affect its overall similarity to others. Finally, a cumulative explained variance ratio plot obtained by applying PCA to all concatenated results shows that about five principal components explain over 90% of the total variance, indicating a considerable degree of similarity in how different datasets influence SFT outcomes and suggesting that the landscape of instruction-tuning impact is governed by a small number of underlying factors.
