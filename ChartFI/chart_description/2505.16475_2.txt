The task-dataset hierarchy distribution of ReflectEvo-460k and the error type distribution of corrected thoughts identified by reflection reveal a corpus heavily weighted toward complex cognitive challenges and the specific failure modes they elicit. The task composition is dominated by Logical Reasoning at 47.8% and Mathematics at 30.9%, indicating that nearly four out of five items target reasoning-heavy skills, while the remaining share is spread across smaller categories such as Reading Comprehension at 6.0%, Contextual QA at 5.1%, and Social Reasoning at 3.3%. This concentration suggests the dataset is optimized for evaluating structured reasoning and quantitative problem solving rather than broad everyday knowledge. Complementing this, the error taxonomy, identified through human calibration, consists of five coarse-grained and nine fine-grained error types that highlight where these reasoning processes typically fail. The most common errors are Logic and Reasoning Errors at 55.3% and Instruction Violation at 35.8%, with Flawed Rationale Error alone accounting for 43.1% and Internal Inconsistency adding 12.2%. This distribution indicates that the primary weakness in the sampled outputs is not simple arithmetic slips but fundamental breakdowns in coherent justification and self-consistency. Instruction Violations are further driven by Context Misinterpretation at 22.1% and Incomplete or Irrelevant Responses at 13.1%, pointing to a substantial gap in correctly grounding on the prompt. Mathematical Errors are comparatively small at 7.3% and are mostly straightforward Calculation Errors at 6.1% rather than Algorithm Errors at 1.2%, while Factual Errors are rare at 1.1% and instances of No Errors appear only 0.5% of the time, implying a stringent evaluation where almost every sampled output exhibits at least one identifiable failure mode.
