The chart displays the category distribution of tokens over different pre-activation value ranges for a neuron related to math content (GSM8K, left), and a neuron related to code content (MBPP, right). For the math-related neuron, the lowest pre-activation region is dominated by operator/syntax tokens, but their share collapses as pre-activation increases, with the mid-range shifting toward names/nouns and numerical-related tokens. This pattern culminates in a notable late shift at the highest pre-activation bin, where Programming-related keywords surge sharply to become the primary contributor. In contrast, the code-related neuron's activation pattern is more monotonic: miscellaneous and name/noun tokens account for the lower pre-activation mass, while the contribution from code-structured tokens rises steadily. Specifically, these neurons are highly activated by Programming-related keywords, Operator/Syntax tokens, and Tokens with Parentheses, which become increasingly prominent at the top end, indicating that stronger activations align with more explicit syntactic structure. While they both show positive activations for Numerical-related tokens, the comparison highlights different activation dynamics; the code-related neuron shows a smoother transition, implying a consistent coupling between activation strength and programming syntax, whereas the math-related neuron exhibits a sharper separation between general language/numeric content and a high-activation pocket of programming-like tokens. This case study demonstrates that neurons are multi-functional, responding to varied token types across different activation levels.
