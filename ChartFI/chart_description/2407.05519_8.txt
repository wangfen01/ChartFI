This heatmap illustrates the distribution of research activity by tracking the number of papers per year in each developer tool and workflow category from 2000 to 2021. While early years are sparse and dominated by a few foundational areas such as reporting and learning, the 2013–2021 period shows a clear expansion across many categories, indicating a maturing ecosystem with more specialized tooling. The category of ‘Code quality’ stands out as the foremost area of focus, comprising 140 out of the total 462 papers. Examining the annual distribution, the highest number of papers dedicated to discussing automation tools is in ‘Code quality’ (18) in 2017, followed by the papers in ‘Code quality’ and ‘AI-Assisted’ (15 each) in 2021. Testing also rises steadily to become the second most prolific category with 104 total papers, remaining consistently high through the second half of the timeline. AI Assisted emerges later but ramps quickly, culminating in the aforementioned spike at the end of the series. Support and Code review similarly strengthen over time, with visible surges in the mid-to-late 2010s, suggesting increased emphasis on collaboration and process rigor. Several categories show mid-period emergence followed by sustained presence, as Dependency management, Security, Monitoring, and Code search become more common after the early 2010s, aligning with growing complexity and operational needs. By contrast, areas like Desktop tools, Time tracking, Mobile CI, and Deployment remain comparatively low and intermittent. Overall, the pattern reflects a shift from basic tooling toward quality, automation, and intelligence-driven assistance.
