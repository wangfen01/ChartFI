This performance comparison evaluates existing PID control against learned SAC policies—specifically SAC-CD, SAC-RD, and SAC-ND—using wastewater treatment data from September 15th, 2022, focusing on the concentration of phosphate in the effluent (Cup) and the control actions involving the dosage of metal salts (QJSF and QPAX) over 720 steps. The effluent concentration Cup exhibits a consistent cyclic pattern across all methods, yet the SAC agents, particularly the one trained under random delay conditions (SAC-RD), demonstrated superior control over the phosphate levels by maintaining the concentration closer to the target value with fewer fluctuations than the PID controller. While the PID trace repeatedly overshoots with the highest peaks and more pronounced oscillations, the SAC variants track the cycle more tightly with damped amplitudes, with SAC-RD sitting lowest at peaks to better suppress concentration excursions. These dynamics highlight a critical trade-off between costs and target deviations; although the PID controller sometimes achieves lower total costs—such as 41% lower than SAC-ND and 26% lower than SAC-CD—it shows a significantly higher target deviation, specifically 77% more deviation than SAC-RD and 59% more than SAC-CD. The manipulated flow variables reveal the operational cost of this precision, as QJSF and QPAX are highly intermittent and spiky, with SAC-CD and SAC-ND producing the largest bursts while SAC-RD remains comparatively restrained and closer to zero for long stretches. Over time, QPAX activity generally diminishes for most methods, but SAC-ND exhibits a notable late-stage reactivation with sustained high QPAX near the end, indicating a regime shift or instability in that policy’s actuation pattern. The reward panel mirrors these trade-offs, where PID suffers frequent deep negative drops exceeding -100 due to larger tracking errors and aggressive actuation penalties, whereas the SAC methods cluster near zero with smaller dips, implying more stable overall performance despite the higher control effort required by some variants.
