This radial bar chart presents alignment results for pre-defined preferences related to harmless, helpful, and humorous on the P-Soups dataset, comparing six distinct methods across these categories. Each bar represents the average rewards corresponding to specific user preferences, encompassing either single or dual dimensions. The results demonstrate that PAD (Ours) can effectively align with various preferences, outperforming the baselines in terms of achieving a superior frontier. While MetaAligner records the highest value in the Helpful category at 1.48, PAD achieves a strong score of 1.31, representing a meaningful lift above the Base baseline near 1.06 and other alignment methods clustered roughly between 0.9 and 1.0. The most significant separation appears in the Humor dimension, where PAD reaches 1.50 while the Base method drops to -0.93; this stark contrast highlights a notable anomaly where non-PAD methods show weak or negative movement, suggesting PAD is uniquely effective at generating humor. Furthermore, PAD maintains robust performance in safety-related dimensions, leading the Harmless category with a score of 1.03 and dominating the combined Harmless Humor and Humor Helpful categories with scores of 1.37 and 0.92, respectively. By consistently matching or exceeding the best alternatives without trading safety for style, the findings reveal that PAD has achieved substantial improvements for all three objectives.
