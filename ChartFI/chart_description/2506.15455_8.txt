This scatter plot illustrates the relationship between the average accuracy across all mutated test sets and the original test set on the x-axis and the standard deviation in accuracy on the five mutated test sets w.r.t the raw test set on the y-axis for various language models. Model structures are differentiated by color, with the same model represented by the same color, while marker shapes indicate the prompting strategy: dots indicate models prompted only with original examples, crosses represent models prompted only with mutated examples, and triangles denote models prompted with both original and corresponding mutated examples. The bottom-right corner reflects the ideal balance of high performance on both raw and mutated sets, a region occupied by models like GPT-o1, which achieve high accuracy with comparatively low variance, signaling robust behavior under different example constructions. Overall, most models achieve significantly higher accuracy on the test variations when given both original and mutated examples, compared to using only original GSM8K examples or only mutated examples as in-context examples. This trend is clearly highlighted by the arrowed movement for the Llama3 family, where shifting from raw-only to paired examples moves the model from a higher standard deviation point near 65 percent accuracy down toward a lower standard deviation around 70 percent accuracy, implying that pairing can stabilize outcomes and improve accuracy simultaneously. While accuracy ranges from the mid-60s to nearly 90 percent, a notable anomaly exists for GPT-4 and Llama3-70B, which exhibit the highest standard deviations—peaking near 25 and 21 respectively—despite having moderate-to-high average accuracy, suggesting a high sensitivity to the choice or structure of in-context examples. In contrast, the Phi family and Llama3.1 show varying degrees of dispersion, but generally, the transition to paired in-context examples (triangles) consistently shifts models toward the bottom-right, reducing variability and enhancing performance across diverse test conditions.
