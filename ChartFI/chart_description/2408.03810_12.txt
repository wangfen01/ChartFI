These stabilization diagrams illustrate the evolution of identified modal content with model order across frequency for the Hawk T1A Aircraft, comparing MISO N4SID and MISO iLF configurations with overlaid magnitude traces in dB and point color indicating damping. In the MISO N4SID panel, the field of stable poles appears relatively sparse, and while candidates become more concentrated above roughly 120 Hz, the lack of consistent vertical alignment indicates significant ambiguity; consequently, only one stable mode is identified in the MISO case, despite the magnitude curve showing local dips that typically suggest resonant structure. In marked contrast, the MISO iLF panel displays a significantly different stabilization pattern characterized by strong vertical columns of repeated poles that persist across model orders, particularly where the magnitude trace shows pronounced variability. This iLF approach proves far more sensitive, as it can identify many new modes (8.7% more than the SISO), in particular in the 68–84 Hz interval, as well as capturing most of the SISO modes which appear as distinct, highly persistent, and lightly damped candidates. Although the dense and repetitive stabilization field in the iLF panel might initially imply algorithmic artifacts, the natural frequencies identified by different cases are coherent and show little deviation, showing confidence in the proposed iLF identification approach. This consistency confirms that the iLF configuration effectively resolves complex modal density and physical modes in the mid-to-high frequency range that the N4SID method fails to stabilize.
