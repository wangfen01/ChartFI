This visualization illustrates the relationship between data heterogeneity and model performance for CIFAR-10 and Google Speech across 20 devices, featuring bubble charts that show the class-wise data distribution per client on the left and learning curve comparisons on the right. The bubble matrices reveal the non-IID distributions of CIFAR-10 and Google Speech, where most clients have data from only a few classes, indicating highly non-IID conditions; specifically, CIFAR-10 exhibits moderate imbalance while Google Speech shows a much more uneven, sparse pattern where many device–class combinations are small and a few are disproportionately large. Each learning curve chart shows the accuracy of five local models as well as the global model’s accuracy, with the purple curve corresponding to the global model trained with the logit ensemble method and the blue curve corresponding to the global model trained with conventional model aggregation. For CIFAR-10, the blue model-averaging curve is highly volatile with frequent spikes and dips, yet the accuracy of a small model trained via FL provides remarkably more accurate logits than the logit ensemble, which remains low and relatively flat alongside the individual local models. In contrast, for Google Speech, the blue model-averaging curve rises rapidly and continues a steady climb toward the mid-30% range, demonstrating that conventional aggregation provides a significant generalization boost when local data are highly skewed. While the purple logit ensemble improves more gradually into the low-20% range and local models trend upward but stay below the aggregated approach, the overall divergence between tasks highlights that Google Speech achieves sustained, monotonic gains compared to the instability and weak improvement seen in CIFAR-10.
