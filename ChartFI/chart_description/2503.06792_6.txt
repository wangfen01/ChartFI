This visualization compares the Llama-3.1-8B and Mistral-7B models across a range of occupations using three aligned metrics: the percentage of female biographies in the underlying data, a bias coefficient sorted in descending order, and an internal coefficient defined as the Spearman correlation between the distribution of biographies and the predicted probability for each occupation. In both models, the bias coefficient closely tracks gender composition, with strongly positive values for female-dominated roles such as dietitian, nurse, and interior designer, and strongly negative values for male-dominated roles like pastor, physician, and rapper, indicating a consistent tendency to associate occupations with the majority gender represented in the biographies. While the bias coefficient and internal coefficient are moderately correlated, the internal coefficient exhibits a weaker and less uniform relationship to gender mix that diverges significantly between models. Llama-3.1-8B displays larger-magnitude internal swings, including pronounced negative correlations for several male-coded roles and a notable positive spike for teachers (0.45†), suggesting stronger internalized directional effects that are not purely proportional to representation. In contrast, Mistral-7B’s internal coefficients are generally more compressed, though it still shows significant positive internal values for roles like interior designer (0.42†) and teacher (0.32†) alongside moderate negatives for a cluster of male-coded roles including rapper and comedian. A key anomaly is that occupations with similar female-bio shares often do not receive similarly sized internal coefficients; for instance, in Llama-3.1-8B, internal effects can flip sign or vary sharply despite comparable representation, pointing to occupation-specific semantics or training artifacts beyond simple demographic frequency. Statistical significance is denoted where p < 0.001 (†) and p < 0.05 (*), confirming that while both models display strong representation-aligned external bias, the internal bias is more model-dependent, with Llama-3.1-8B appearing more polarized and Mistral-7B more damped.
