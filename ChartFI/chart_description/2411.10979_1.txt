This radar chart illustrates the top MLLMs’ performance, evaluating 15 tasks across 5 aspects of video composition understanding: Cinematography Analysis, Character Understanding, Narrative Understanding, Scene Perception, and Making Analysis. The data reveal that top models perform well on basic perception tasks (e.g. action perception), evidenced by strong clustering on axes such as A-P and CMP-P, and they remain comparatively robust on S-M, suggesting that narrative comprehension is a shared strength. Despite these capabilities in fundamental areas, the models fall short in comprehending complex video compositions, particularly in cinematography, where performance metrics like CamM-P and SS-P remain compressed near the chart's center, indicating a universal difficulty in analyzing camera movement and shot types. While Cinematography represents the area of lowest overall competence, the Making Analysis sector highlights the greatest performance volatility; variables such as Cut-C and SE-P display pronounced spread, identifying editing- and shot/sequence-level reasoning as key differentiators. Scene perception remains mixed, with generally solid performance on B-P contrasting with higher variance on S-C, implying that recognizing broader scene context is easier than consistently parsing finer scene components. Among specific architectures, LLaVA-OneVision-72B appears the most balanced, maintaining a competitive outer-ring position across most axes, whereas GPT-4o exhibits a notable dip on Cha-C, marking a specific inconsistency in character consistency analysis relative to its otherwise strong profile.
