While the ground-truth performance shows a clear monotonic decline, most methods fail to capture this trend; in fact, other state-of-the-art training-free NAS algorithms, including TE-NAS, grad norm, Synflow, Snip, ZiCo, and EProxy perform poorly in differentiating network accuracy attributed to different activation functions. This highlights that while most proxies exhibit architecture-specific biases that create significant rank inversions, RBFlex-NAS reliably preserves the true ordering for model selection.
