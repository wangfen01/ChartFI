The heatmap displays the Mahalanobis distance of different datasets, embeddings, and fine-tuning methods, organized such that from top to bottom the three groups are image, question, and joint shift scores respectively. In this visualization, the darker blue represents a higher distance score which indicates larger distribution shifts. Overall, shift scores increase from left to right, with a corresponding darkening in color, as the columns transition from in-domain datasets on the left to OOD-oriented datasets and averages on the right. Within the image-only block, most methods sit in the high 20s to high 30s on in-domain columns, while TextVQA and VizWiz jump into the mid 30s to low 40s. The question-only block demonstrates a stronger shift where OOD columns surge into the mid-to-high 40s; however, an outlier is CV-VQA, which has the lowest shift scores, appearing as a notable dip against neighboring columns and hinting at a dataset-specific artifact or alignment. The combined (v,q) block is the most extreme on OOD, where TextVQA and VizWiz repeatedly reach about 49–51, producing the highest OOD averages overall and implying that jointly modeling vision and language amplifies detectable shift rather than smoothing it out.
