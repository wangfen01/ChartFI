This heatmap illustrates model performance on the MultiPL-E benchmark across High-Resource Programming Languages (HRPLs) and Low-Resource Programming Languages (LRPLs), where darker colors indicate higher performance scores. A vertical dashed line separates HRPLs on the left from LRPLs on the right, visually demarcating the distinct resource categories. Overall, the data reveals consistently higher scores for HRPLs like Python and Java compared to LRPLs such as R and Julia, while low-resource results are lower, patchier, and more model-dependent. Specifically, there are significantly higher scores for Python compared to LRPLs for most LLMs. Across the high-resource section, the top tier is dominated by models like Stable-code-3b and StarCoderBase-7B, which cluster in the mid-to-high 20s and low 30s across Python, Java, JavaScript, and C++. Python is generally the strongest column for many models, featuring a notable peak from Phi-1 at 51.2 that stands out as the single largest value in the chart; however, Phi-1 drops sharply on other high-resource languages, indicating a pronounced specialization rather than broad strength. Rust and PHP tend to be weaker than Python, Java, and JavaScript for most models, highlighting reduced robustness outside the most common training distributions. In the low-resource block, scores fall markedly and become uneven, though Lua shows occasional spikes for the best models, such as CodeGemma-2B reaching 25.2. Conversely, many model-language pairs are near zero, especially for Racket and Julia in smaller or older models. Stable-code-3b presents an interesting anomaly: it is among the strongest on high-resource languages yet hits zeros on Swift and Racket, underscoring that even strong generalists can have blind spots. The clearest pattern is that larger, more recent code-focused models maintain some capability on low-resource languages, but the gains are inconsistent and concentrated in a few languages, implying that low-resource performance is driven more by specific exposure or transfer affinity than by overall model strength alone.
