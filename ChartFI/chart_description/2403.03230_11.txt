This heatmap displays the item difficulty correlation among LLMs and human experts, utilizing Spearman correlation coefficients to quantify the relationships. The data reveals a fundamental dichotomy in alignment: LLMs have an average correlation of 0.75 (±0.08) with one another, whereas human experts have an average of 0.15 (±0.03) with LLMs. This disconnect is visually anchored by the dark bottom row, where human-model agreement scores are uniformly low—ranging from roughly 0.09 to 0.19—highlighting a pronounced gap between human judgments and model outputs. In the upper sections of the matrix, model-to-model agreement is consistently strong, particularly among base and non-chat variants where many pairings cluster in the high 0.8s. For example, the Galactica and Falcon families exhibit high internal coherence, with a notable anomaly being the exceptionally high correlation of 0.97 between Falcon-40B and Falcon-40B (instruct), which implies near-identical preference patterns between these two configurations. However, chat-tuned Llama-2 variants diverge from this consensus, often registering lower correlations in the 0.60 to 0.70 range against the broader set, whereas base Llama-2 models show significantly higher alignment, such as the 0.91 correlation between Llama-2-7B and Llama-2-13B. Mistral-7B and its instruct version maintain strong alignment with other base models, generally in the high 0.7s to mid 0.8s, reinforcing the observation that while general instruction tuning can preserve similarity, specific chat optimizations—particularly in Llama-2—introduce distinct behavioral divergences.
