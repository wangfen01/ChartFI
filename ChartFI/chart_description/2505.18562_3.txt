These four radar charts provide a fine-grained performance comparison across 22 semantic classes in the test set, categorized into Global Knowledge (red), Perceptual Experience (green), and Cultural Ideologies (blue). In the baseline condition, both Llama and Qwen start from relatively low, uneven baselines, with Qwen demonstrating value orientations more aligned with Chinese culture while Llama exhibits stronger alignment with English cultural values, particularly in the Cognition and Value classes. After applying CultureSteer, both models show a clear uplift in cultural awareness, though with domain-dependent effectiveness; while global knowledge categories like World and Animals remain difficult to align culturally, perceptual experience categories such as Action and Space show notable gains, especially within English-speaking cultures. Llama shows a dramatic and uniform expansion of its radar shape, with many categories rising into the upper mid to high range, indicating broad-based improvement across multiple social and abstract domains like religion, law, and cognition/language. However, Llama still exhibits gaps between English and Chinese contexts after steering, whereas Qwen achieves a more balanced cross-cultural performance. Although Qwen_CultureSteer improves meaningfully versus its baseline, its profile is less uniformly elevated than Llama’s, yet it succeeds in reducing baseline sparsity to create a more balanced performance across categories. Ultimately, the transition from low, patchy baselines to broader coverage highlights that while CultureSteer significantly enhances cultural alignment, the magnitude of gains differs by model, with Llama showing higher overall peaks and Qwen reaching a more consistent equilibrium between diverse cultural contexts.
