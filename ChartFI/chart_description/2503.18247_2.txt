The chart illustrates the AfriSenti Macro F1 results for ten African languages, comparing the performance of the base model AfroXLMR and its domain-specialized counterpart, AfroXLMR-Social, against a suite of open-source and proprietary Large Language Models (LLMs). While proprietary models like gpt-4o and gemini-1.5 maintain high and consistent scores generally sitting in the mid-60s to high 70s, AfroXLMR-Social frequently surpasses them, reaching peaks near 80 in Igbo and Hausa. In contrast, the base AfroXLMR model exhibits significant variability, most notably collapsing to a very low score in Swahili while other models remain in the mid-50s to low 60s, suggesting a language-specific weakness that the social-domain adaptation of AfroXLMR-Social successfully addresses. The performance gap between specialized encoder models and general-purpose LLMs is most pronounced in harder or lower-resourced languages like Portuguese and Twi, where many LLMs drop into the 30s–40s range while AfroXLMR-Social maintains a competitive edge above 50. Ultimately, the data indicates that we still need encoder-only models for low-resourced African languages and suggests that future LLMs are expected to include more training data for underrepresented languages to close the gap with specialized models that currently benefit from targeted social-domain adaptation.
