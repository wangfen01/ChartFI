The heatmap presents the country-level accuracy of each model on the cultural visual grounding task across 15 countries, comparing one specialist model (Grounding DINO) and four generalist models (KOSMOS-2, MiniGPTv2, Qwen-VL-7B, LLaVA-1.5-13B). Although the overall performance across models is rather poor, among all models, the specialist model Grounding DINO shows a relatively higher average performance (47.99%) compared to the generalist models. It is followed closely by Qwen-VL-7B at 46.95, while KOSMOS-2 (39.74), LLaVA-1.5-13B (36.39), and MiniGPTv2 (35.46) trail significantly, indicating that generalist capability does not translate uniformly into stronger results. Performance varies sharply by region, with Canada serving as a standout anomaly where KOSMOS-2 surges to 84.61 and Qwen-VL-7B peaks at 73.08, far exceeding their typical levels. In contrast, Vietnam illustrates a systematic failure mode for generalist models, with KOSMOS-2, LLaVA-1.5-13B and MiniGPTv2 plummeting to 5.00 and Qwen-VL-7B to 2.50, whereas Grounding DINO remains stable at 50.00. Similar disparities appear elsewhere; for example, in Poland, Grounding DINO reaches 70.00 while MiniGPTv2 drops to 27.50, and in China, the specialist scores 56.41 compared to 25.64 for MiniGPTv2. While Qwen-VL-7B shows strength in Latin America, such as 62.79 in Mexico, the dominant pattern highlights higher stability and fewer catastrophic drops for the specialist model, contrasting with the high volatility and frequent underperformance of the generalist approaches.
