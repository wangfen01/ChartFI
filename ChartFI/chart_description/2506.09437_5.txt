These charts illustrate the total variation distance (TV) between true and estimated densities obtained with different models across six data generating processes (DG1 to DG6), revealing that performance is highly dataset dependent and variability often matters as much as the median. DG1 represents the clearest low-TV case where most GFPT variants cluster very close to zero with tight spreads, while OPT and APT introduce occasional large outliers that push TV up sharply despite low central values. In the higher-TV regimes of DG2, DG3, and DG6, a recurring pattern emerges where increasing the parameter for GFPT2 tends to decrease TV markedly; specifically, the GFPT2 variants with lower alpha_0 yield the highest medians. A notable contrast is observed in DG4, where using the GFPT2 prior with alpha_0 = 2 achieves almost a perfect density estimate, evidenced by distributions collapsing back near zero. This stands in strong opposition to the pronounced right tails and multiple extreme points displayed by OPT and APT in the same dataset. Similarly, DG5 shows GFPT2 variants performing with moderate stability while OPT and APT are characterized by wide variability and occasional spikes. Ultimately, the OPT and APT models yield comparable performance to the GFPT2 model in all settings except when data are generated from DG4 and DG5, in which case the GFPT2 models achieve superior density estimates.
