This visualization illustrates LoRA popularity through a combined bar chart and heatmap, where rows represent individual test datasets and columns correspond to specific LoRA adapters. The bar chart at the top shows the sum of weights, revealing that while certain adapters emerge as “popular” choices with a stronger influence, adapter support is spread across the library, showing that the model benefits from multiple adapters rather than relying on a few only. The heatmap below uses color intensity to indicate the frequency and weight of selection, with values below 0.1 omitted and an empty diagonal due to the leave-one-out strategy. A clear polarized selection is observable between driving datasets in the top left and generic ones in the bottom right, though even adapters from unrelated domains are often selected, suggesting the existence of shared useful features. Within the driving category, specific dependencies are evident as MUSES-snow-day heavily relies on ACDC-snow and vice-versa, contributing to dense blocks of high-weight concentrations among ACDC and MUSES weather/time variants. While each category still predominantly influences related datasets, the most prominent LoRAs are characterized by their versatility across many moderately strong pairings. Conversely, a few adapters exhibit a sharp drop in popularity with sparse, faint heatmap presence, indicating they are either rarely selected or utilized in narrow, single-purpose contexts rather than as part of broader mixing workflows.
