This grid of histograms presents a comparative analysis of test statistics for embedding decompositions across three distinct models: SBERT, Knowledge Graph Embeddings (KGE), and word2vec. The visualization contrasts the performance of real embedding pairs against a baseline of noise, where the red dashed vertical lines represent the mean scores of the predicted uˆ learned from real embedding pairs, and the blue bars illustrate the frequency distribution of scores derived from 100 random permutations. A consistent pattern emerges across all rows, where real pairings achieve higher cosine similarity and retrieval accuracy with significantly lower L2 loss than the permuted baselines, validating the predictive power of the real embeddings. For the Knowledge Graph Embeddings, the separation is particularly pronounced, showing an L2 loss of 0.04, a cosine similarity of 0.97, and a Hits@1 accuracy of 0.94, all of which are distinct outliers relative to the random distribution. The word2vec model exhibits similar behavior, where the linear system loss is 39.85, a value notably lower than the minimum loss of the random system at 43.91, and the cosine similarity is 0.44, which is greater than all instances of the random baseline. SBERT mirrors these trends, with the real pair statistics for L2 loss, cosine similarity, and retrieval accuracy falling well outside the range of the permuted data, confirming that the learned decompositions capture meaningful structural relationships absent in the randomized control.
