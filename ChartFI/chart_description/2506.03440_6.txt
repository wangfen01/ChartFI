The visualization of segmentation and labeling on the MPHOI72 dataset compares ground-truth action timelines for two subjects against predictions from GeoVis-GNN and CATS across a sequence of approach, lift, pour, place, approach, cheers, lift, drink, place, and retreat. GeoVis-GNN presents more reasonable and robust segmentation results in all sub-activities, generally preserving the overall structure and capturing the broad ordering of activities with fewer and smaller deviations than its counterpart. For Subject 1, while GeoVis-GNN tracks the main phases well, it shows minor boundary drift around the first lift-to-pour transition and near the final place-to-retreat region. In contrast, CATS provides some unexpected abnormal results in certain sub-activities, such as pour and place, where durations and boundaries deviate more noticeably from the ground truth. Furthermore, CATS directly recognizes the static action sit rather than the ongoing action retreat following place at the end of the activity for Subject 1, introducing a spurious detection where a continuous retreat should occur. For Subject 2, GeoVis-GNN slightly delays or shortens some early segments, particularly around the first approach, lift, and the pour-to-place handoff, yet it remains more stable than CATS, which shows pronounced inconsistencies including a brief, incorrect segment inserted at the transition between the drink and place intervals. Across both subjects, errors tend to concentrate at transitions between closely related manipulation actions such as lift, pour, and place, implying that while both models handle coarse sequencing, GeoVis-GNN is significantly more reliable at fine-grained boundary placement and avoiding the categorical instabilities observed in the CATS model.
