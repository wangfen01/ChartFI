This radar chart evaluates the performance of five vision-language models—Dolphin, Qwen2-VL-7B, GPT-4o, Claude3.5-Sonnet, and GOT—across a comprehensive suite of benchmarks including page-level parsing of plain and complex documents, element-level parsing of text paragraphs, tables, and formulas, and running efficiency measured in frames per second (FPS). The chart is structured such that the outer area represents better performance, and the red trace belonging to Dolphin forms the most expansive and consistently large polygon, indicating that Dolphin exhibits the best performance in most evaluations. Specifically, Dolphin achieves state-of-the-art performance across diverse page-level and element-level parsing tasks, maintaining a near-perfect reach on the Formula-SPE (CDM), Formula-SCE (CDM), Table-PubTab1M (TEDS), and Table-PubTabNet (TEDS) axes while simultaneously dominating the ED-related categories such as DolphinBlock, FoxBlock, and FoxPage. Furthermore, Dolphin exhibits considerable advantages in running efficiency, significantly outperforming the other models on the FPS spoke where competitors like GPT-4o and Claude3.5-Sonnet collapse toward the center. While GPT-4o generally tracks as the next-best tier, they show more pronounced tradeoffs, with their shapes dipping noticeably on several ED axes and the FPS dimension, implying a sensitivity to document-style extraction and lower operational speed compared to Dolphin. GOT and Claude3.5-Sonnet appear more volatile, with GOT showing mid-to-strong performance on some ED dimensions but clear drop-offs on multiple CDM and TEDS spokes, making its overall profile less reliable for structured reasoning. Ultimately, the most significant shift across the chart is the widening separation between Dolphin and the rest of the field, as Dolphin remains uniformly high across all benchmarks while competitors fluctuate, demonstrating that its primary differentiator is the ability to maintain peak performance and high efficiency across all task types simultaneously.
