The chart presents benchmark results on dataset-1, dataset-2, and dataset-3, comparing multiple drift detection approaches across TAUC, sTAUC, and AUC metrics. Generally, there is a discrepancy in detectors of the highest AUC and the highest TAUC, a trend visible where classical detectors cluster near the low end on TAUC but show mixed behavior on sTAUC and AUC. On all datasets, autoencoder-based systems reach among the best detectors for both TAUC and AUC, significantly outperforming traditional baselines. Within these AE-based methods, those using a multivariate test in their latent space, specifically the AE-MMD variants, reach better scores than those using an aggregation of multiple univariate tests, such as AE-mean-KSWIN. While AE-MMD demonstrates the largest upward shift and sensitivity to training configurations, the RandomGuessDetector highlights the divergence between metrics; it reached the highest AUC score on dataset-1, where it ranges on all three datasets among the last ranks in the TAUC score, indicating that high AUC values can occur even with conceptually uninformative models. Furthermore, performance varies significantly by scenario, with dataset-1 typically scoring the highest. In contrast, although some cluster-based systems achieve good AUC scores on dataset-3, none of the benchmarked algorithms is capable of computing a score that can be used to recover the true drift segments, resulting in small TAUC scores for all algorithms on this specific dataset.
