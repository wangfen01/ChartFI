The figure presents a comparative analysis of model outputs across three distinct projections: a t-SNE projection of Relevance on the left, a t-SNE visualization of Semantic Richness in the middle, and a comparison of Redundancy on the right. In the Left panel, triples and question embeddings are plotted in 2D with yellow dots marking question positions; while the original points form several distinct clusters spread widely, EoG triples are closer and more concentrated around these points compared to the dispersed, noisy clusters of other methods like KG-CoT and RoG, which largely overlap the main mass or place points into peripheral secondary modes. The Middle panel utilizes t-SNE and KG-BERT semantic scoring, where circle sizes represent triple semantic scores and larger circles denote higher scores. In this visualization, EoG covers a significantly larger total area and concentrates in the upper regions, whereas RoG and KG-CoT display smaller semantic footprints with notable outliers in low-density regions, indicating occasional instability. The Right panel visualizes triple redundancy as a density distribution of pairwise Euclidean distances, where the EoG distribution is distinctly shifted to higher values with a broader right tail. This indicates that EoG triples exhibit greater spacing, whereas the other methods and the original data are more concentrated at lower-to-mid ranges, implying higher variance and a greater tendency for EoG to generate diverse, less redundant outputs relative to the baseline.
