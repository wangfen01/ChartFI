The heatmaps illustrate Exact Match (EM) performance on the Babilong task across five question groups (qa1–qa5), where the y-axis represents these groups and the x-axis represents the length of the input ranging from 0k to 128k. In this visualization, green indicates higher performance, while red signifies lower performance. The reported average accuracy (Avg acc) for vanilla models and SEGMENT+ (GPT-4) denotes the mean score of all colored cells; conversely, for SEGMENT+ (ChatGPT) and SEGMENT+ (Mistral-7B), two average scores are calculated: the initial score represents the average over valid contexts for comparison with vanilla models, while the subsequent score indicates the average over all cells. Overall performance separates clearly by model family, with ChatGPT leading the base models with the highest average accuracy (57.40) ahead of GPT-4 (53.63), while Mistral-7B trails substantially (31.84). Across context lengths, the dominant pattern in the base models is degradation as the window grows, most visible for GPT-4 where qa1 falls steeply from perfect performance at 0k to the 24 by 128k, and qa2 collapses to single digits at longer contexts. However, SEGMENT+ enhances overall accuracy and maintains stable performance as context length increases, effectively flattening or reversing the long-context drop. This improvement is most dramatic for Segment+ GPT-4, which jumps to a much higher and more stable average of 77.37, with qa1, qa4, and qa5 remaining near-saturated across nearly all context sizes. Segment+ ChatGPT shows moderate stabilization with averages of 62.60/58.91, while Segment+ Mistral-7B rises modestly to 38.72/34.51. Despite these gains, Segment+ Mistral-7B exhibits a notable anomaly where qa2 and qa3 deteriorate to zero at 64k and 128k, suggesting a failure mode triggered by very long contexts despite segmentation. Ultimately, the data demonstrates that while longer context alone often leads to performance erosion, the segmentation approach delivers dramatic robustness for GPT-4, moderate gains for ChatGPT, and limited, sometimes unstable improvements for Mistral-7B.
