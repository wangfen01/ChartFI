This chart compares the performance of various LLMs across different circuits within 20 iterations, where the height of the bars represents the average number of iterations, with lower values indicating better optimization performance. Error bars show the range of iterations observed across ten attempts, success rates smaller than 100% are labeled on the bar, and hatched bars indicate that the model failed to optimize specific circuits in all ten attempts. OpenAI o3 exhibited the best overall performance, achieving a 100% success rate across all circuits with the fewest iterations and the lowest variability, despite requiring relatively more iterations on the ring oscillator. While R-load is uniformly easy with minimal spread, and INV remains low for most models aside from a bump for Gemini 2.0 Flash, complexity rises sharply on NAND and OSC. On NAND, Claude 3 Sonnet and GPT-4o mini exhibited similar trends, with lower success rates and higher variability. The oscillator (OSC) proved particularly volatile; GPT-4.1 performed comparably on most circuits; however, it achieved only a 40% success rate on the ring oscillator, and Claude 3 Haiku, which succeeded on digital circuits, consistently failed on the oscillator. XOR shows a bifurcation where Gemini 2.0 Flash performed worse on the XOR gate; however, it achieved higher success rates on the other circuits, despite exhibiting larger variations. The most significant shift appears on 5T-OTA, where Claude 3.5 Sonnet also achieved a 100% success rate; however, it required noticeably more iterations on the 5T-OTA circuit compared to the leading models. On this hardest task, Claude 3 Haiku consistently failed again, and iterations surged for nearly all non-OpenAI models, while OpenAI O3 and GPT-4.1 remained markedly lower, highlighting a strong robustness advantage as task complexity scales.
