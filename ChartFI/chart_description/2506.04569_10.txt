This case study of KPIRoot presents a three-panel time series analysis where the top Alarm KPI is monitored against a fixed threshold of 70, revealing a distinct host-cluster overload window centered around the mid-period. Before the overload, the Alarm KPI fluctuates between the mid-50s and low-60s, remaining safely below the threshold; however, as the overload window begins, the Alarm KPI ramps sharply to peak near 90, indicating a clear incident period. The middle panel identifies VM1 KPI as the root cause, which rises ahead of and into the overload window, reaching its highest levels just as the alarm breaches the threshold. Once the "Begin Throttling" phase is initiated, the overall traffic is limited, and VM1 KPI begins a sustained decline, falling steadily toward low values by the end of the timeline. Consequently, the alarm KPI returns to a normal range quickly, dropping below the threshold and settling into the 70s with normal volatility as the overload window concludes. In contrast, VM2 KPI remains moderate early on but becomes increasingly volatile following the intervention, showing intermittent spikes after VM1 is throttled. This divergence suggests that while VM1 pressure was the primary driver of the alarm escalation and throttling successfully mitigated its load, the redistribution of resources or secondary contention effects led to increased variability in neighboring workloads like VM2, highlighting a successful mitigation strategy that nonetheless impacted broader workload stability.
