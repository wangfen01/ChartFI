This pair of heatmaps illustrates the difference ratio in Attack Success Rate (ASR) between a reference model, BERT-base models across eight datasets, comparing the Q-faker method with CT-GAT. Cell values represent the performance shift, where a positive value, with colors closer to green, indicates that an attack method has a higher ASR on the comparison model than the reference model, while a negative value (red) indicates lower attack performance; values closer to zero signify more consistent performance across various models. For the Q-faker method, most cells exhibit positive values or are close to zero, which shows that the method generally maintains consistent target-agnostic attack capability across various target models, indicating high transferability. For example, the cell value for (HSOL, BERT-small) is -0.011, which means the method shows very similar performance on both models. While effects are mixed, there are several clear gains, most pronounced on the Eron dataset for RoBERTa (2.7) and XLNet (1.7), alongside moderate improvements on FAS, CGFake, and Amazon-Ib. Conversely, the Assassin dataset consistently shows negative performance, suggesting a systematic mismatch. In stark contrast, the CT-GAT method exhibits negative values in all cases, demonstrating a broad, near-universal degradation across almost all datasets and backbones. Many cells are deep red with values clustered at or near the lower bound of -1.0, particularly for the Eron and Assassin datasets, implying a widespread instability or failure to generalize. The only relatively less negative region is CGFake, where declines are smaller but still predominantly below zero, signaling a significant and pervasive performance collapse for the CT-GAT method.
