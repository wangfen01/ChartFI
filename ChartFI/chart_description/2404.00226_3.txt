This radar chart compares the performance of six training strategies—Random Initialization, ImageNet Pretraining, GLoRIA, MRM, MGCA, and the proposed method—across ViT and ResNet backbones, evaluated on classification accuracy for BUSI and AUITD, segmentation Dice for BUSI and DDTI, and detection AP for BUSI and DDTI. The overall visualization reveals that the proposed method demonstrates balanced and nearly the best performance across various tasks, forming the largest and most stable radar shape in both architectures. In contrast, most of the previous state-of-the-art methods struggle to achieve balanced performance across various downstream tasks; for instance, MRM achieves excellent segmentation performance but performs poorly in classification, and random initialization consistently produces the smallest footprint with the weakest results, particularly in detection and segmentation. On the ViT backbone, the proposed approach achieves competitive performance across all tasks, reaching the outer rings in AUITD classification and DDTI segmentation while maintaining high scores in BUSI classification and segmentation. On the ResNet backbone, the proposed method again leads with clear advantages in both segmentation metrics and solid classification. Ultimately, the data illustrate a significant shift from the inconsistent, task-dependent performance of generic initialization or single-source pretraining toward a more consistently high, cross-task profile under the proposed method, which maintains stability even in the more challenging detection and segmentation objectives.
