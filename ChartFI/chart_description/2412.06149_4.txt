The six histograms illustrate the distribution of the entropy prediction results of clean samples and backdoored samples after applying STRIP to the proposed attack across various datasets, including CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, ImageNette, GTSRB, and VGG-Flower variants. A critical analysis of the data reveals that the prediction results of the backdoored samples have a similar entropy distribution to benign samples for all datasets, a convergence that makes it difficult to differentiate the backdoored samples from the benign samples. In CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, the distributions are heavily overlapping and tightly concentrated, indicating that entropy remains stable and the backdoor effect is subtle; CIFAR-100 is particularly notable for being sharply peaked near the upper end of its narrow range, offering no separation signal. While ImageNette shows a small, noticeable left shift for backdoored samples and the VGG-Flower variants display moderate shape changes—with VGG-Flower-h exhibiting a broader spread—these variations do not disrupt the overall pattern of similarity. Even in GTSRB, which presents the most distinct visual difference with backdoored samples shifted toward higher entropy relative to the clean baseline, the distributions still share a significant range of values, preventing reliable classification based on uncertainty alone. Consequently, across all six panels, the data demonstrates that the attack successfully mimics the entropy profile of clean data, rendering entropy a weak discriminator for detecting the presence of the backdoor.
