The four panels illustrate the forecasting accuracy of OMP and LASSO models compared to a live IDFull benchmark, measuring Mean Absolute Error (MAE) across delivery hours and varying lags to delivery. In scenarios (a) through (d), the difference of the MAE between the forecast and the live IDFull is shown, revealing that OMP’s performance is highly hour-dependent; while alternative parameterizations in (b), (c), and (d) frequently sit below zero to indicate improvement over the benchmark, they exhibit sharp intraday fluctuations and pronounced negative troughs during the mid-afternoon to early evening window. The summary of scenarios (e) and (f) displays the MAE averaged across all days and hours, where forecast errors for all methods increase as the lag to delivery h_lag grows. In this aggregate view, scenario (e) achieves the overall smallest forecast errors, as the OMP-based forecast beats the live IDFull for all h_lag=2 and leads to considerable smaller MAE compared to scenario (f) for all h_lag. Detailed hourly breakdowns further distinguish these approaches: under OMP feature selection in scenario (e), MAE differences versus Live are frequently negative for longer lags, particularly from late morning through evening, suggesting OMP becomes increasingly robust relative to the benchmark as the forecast horizon extends. Conversely, under LASSO feature selection in scenario (f), the differences are often positive and can spike dramatically at specific hours, especially for short lags where MAE differences exceed 20 units, indicating severe instability and occasional underperformance that drives LASSO’s higher average error across all lag intervals.
