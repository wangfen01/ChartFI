This lower-triangular similarity heatmap visualizes the dataset similarity between the datasets included within SEB, revealing distinctly different clusters of datasets where darker cells and higher numerical values indicate stronger alignment between representations. The most pronounced structure is the tight Danish cluster at the top, where datasets such as DKHate, Da Political Comments, and LCC show consistently very high mutual similarity, frequently clustering around 0.9 and above. Norwegian resources form a second coherent block with solid internal agreement, notably characterized by the high similarity between SNL Retrieval and NorQuad, though the group exhibits slightly more variability than the Danish set, particularly regarding the Norwegian parliament dataset, which tends to be a weaker match. Swedish datasets sit lower still in terms of cross-dataset similarity, with several pairings landing in the low-to-mid 0.7s; this indicates greater domain or annotation differences, specifically highlighting distinct datasets such as SweFAQ, which appear as local minima against otherwise high baselines. Finally, a notable cross-cutting pattern is evident where broad-coverage resources like Massive Intent and Massive Scenario are extremely close to each other and align strongly with the multilingual ScaLA resource, implying a shared semantic space that generalizes across the distinct linguistic clusters.
