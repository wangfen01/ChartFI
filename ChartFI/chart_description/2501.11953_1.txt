These charts break down the performance of four models on literal and figurative proverb translation across eight different language directions, evaluated by BLEU and COMET scores. A clear performance hierarchy emerges among language pairs across both metrics: all models perform reasonably well in DE-EN pairs, which consistently form the outermost spike, while the BN-EN and EN-BN pairs are the most challenging tasks, consistently yielding the lowest scores. A consistent model hierarchy is also evident, as Llama3.1-70b is generally the strongest performer with clear advantages on high-scoring pairs, while nllb-3B is most often the lowest-performing model, exhibiting the sharpest drops on harder pairs; GPT4o-mini and gemma2-9b tend to cluster in the middle, with their relative performance varying by direction. Overall, all models perform better on literal translation in all translation directions. This effect is most pronounced in the BLEU scores, which show wider separation and more volatility, indicating that surface-form n-gram matching is highly sensitive to the stylistic variation of figurative language. In contrast, COMET scores are more compressed in a tighter band with smaller model-to-model gaps, suggesting that while figurative phrasing reduces exact overlap, overall translation adequacy and fluency remain comparatively stable. A minor exception to the general trend of literal superiority is observed in the COMET score for the nllb-3B model in the ID-EN direction, where figurative performance is slightly higher than literal.
