This charts compare the F1-Score performance of five models against the event Reduction Ratio as the parameter θ_dup is varied from 2 to 10 across the HDFS and BGL datasets. In the HDFS dataset, model performance demonstrates significant sensitivity to θ_dup. While in HDFS, as θ_dup increases from 2 to 7, the number of event reductions rises from approximately 18% to a peak of over 80%; however, for most models, this coincides with a trend where the model’s performance diminishes. Specifically, DecisionTree and RobustLog begin with near-perfect F1-Scores, but their performance degrades at higher θ_dup values, while IsolationForest and SVM also exhibit a general decline. The LR model is exceptionally volatile, plummeting from a high initial score before spiking to a peak that aligns with the maximum reduction ratio at θ_dup=7, after which both its performance and the reduction ratio collapse. In stark contrast, for the BGL dataset, as θ_dup increases, the model’s performance remains consistent, with LR, DecisionTree, SVM, and RobustLog tightly clustered around a stable F1-Score in the high-50s. IsolationForest is an outlier, performing consistently lower. Concurrently, the reduction ratio in BGL fluctuates only slightly between 67% and 71%, and while it does end lower than its starting point, it does not drastically decrease, indicating parameter robustness. Overall, the pronounced performance variations in HDFS suggest that tuning θ_dup is critical, whereas the stability in BGL implies less sensitivity. Across both datasets, a setting of θ_dup = 2 is a relatively optimal parameter, as it yields the highest or near-highest F1-Scores for most models in HDFS and corresponds to the highest reduction ratio in BGL where performance is otherwise stable.
