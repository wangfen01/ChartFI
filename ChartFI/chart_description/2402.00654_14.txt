The figure presents a summary plot of absolute SHAP values and swarm plots of each mode choice using the SHAP game theory approach to explain feature influences on the overall RF model. The initial panel displays the ratio of SHAP values across all five modes by measuring the mean absolute SHAP values for each feature over randomly selected samples, where the features are listed by their total SHAP impacts from top to bottom. The remaining plots describe the SHAP values corresponding to each of the five modes’ output probabilities, providing a granular view of feature directionality. In these detailed distributions, the x-axis shows the SHAP values; a dot with a negative effect falls at the left side of the x-axis, and one with a positive effect falls at the right side of the x-axis, while the feature value of each dot is denoted from high to low using a red-to-blue scale. Analyzing the specific drivers reveals distinct hierarchies: the great circle distance (“gc_dist”) has a strong influence on privately owned trucks’ mode choice, holding the highest SHAP value for this category, while several features with high SHAP values for privately owned trucks include routed distances for different modes (e.g., “M4”). In contrast, single weight (“sw”) and single value (“sv”) are the most influential features for for-hire trucks. Specifically, a single weight (“sw”) had the highest SHAP value for for-hire trucks and parcel service, though parcel service displays wider spreads for sw and v2w, implying more heterogeneous behavior and stronger interaction-like variability than trucking. Finally, the data suggests that exported products are more likely to be transported via air or other modes; this is evident in the air and other modes panels where export exhibits a distinctly positive influence at high values with a long right tail, indicating that for these segments, a small number of factors such as export status explain most of the model's predictive power.
