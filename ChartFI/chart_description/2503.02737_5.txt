This heatmap illustrates the average Macro F1 performance of large language models (LLMs) across 20 individual languages and 20 cross-lingual combinations, with results calculated across all prompting strategies and baseline models indicated in bold. The left-hand side highlights the average performance across prompting techniques for each LLM in monolingual settings, grouped by language families such as Italic, Germanic, and Slavic within the Indo-European category, alongside an "Others" group where languages marked with an asterisk use a non-Latin script. Mistral Large 123B demonstrates strong performance across both individual languages and cross-lingual combinations, maintaining scores largely in the mid-70s to mid-80s for monolingual pairs and remaining comparatively robust in cross-lingual transfer. While capabilities to process languages vary among LLMs, C4AI Command R+, which was trained on 23 languages, exhibits high performance across many languages, joining DeBERTa v3 Large in a second tier that shows more visible degradation as tasks involve lower-resource languages. Across monolingual pairs, scores are generally strongest for Italic and Germanic languages but noticeably weaker for Slavic and several non-Indo-European languages; for instance, GTR-T5 Large collapses sharply on several Indo-European pairs, notably the Bulgarian-related cell, and Multilingual-E5 Large shows a pronounced dip on some Slavic pairs. Overall, performance declined in cross-lingual settings, with an average decrease of approximately 4.5%, especially for 10B- LLMs. This systematic decline is most concentrated in pairs involving Southeast Asian and some Semitic languages, where several cells fall into the low-50s or below for mid-tier models, while Mistral Large more often holds in the high-60s to mid-70s. The resulting patterns indicate that while larger, more recent instruction-tuned LLMs lead in ranking, the performance gap widens as linguistic distance and resource scarcity increase, making cross-lingual robustness a primary differentiator between models.
