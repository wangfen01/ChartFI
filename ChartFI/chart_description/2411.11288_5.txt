This visualization presents the updated temporal micro-prototype in each phase for a randomly selected skeleton sequence, comparing the evolutionary trajectory of feature activations against temporal intra-class compactness metrics. The heatmaps are organized into two distinct streams: the first row denotes the updated process without the proposed temporal memory mechanism, while the second row is equipped with this guidance. A critical divergence in pattern stability is evident between the two conditions; in the first row, the updated temporal micro-prototype without the memory mechanism guidance becomes chaotic as frames increase, progressing from sparse, isolated activations in the First Phase to a dense, broadly distributed activity field in the Second and Third Phases. This chaotic progression is characterized by frequent vertical streaks and high-variance patterns that suggest unchecked variability and noise. In contrast, the second row, guided by the memory mechanism, maintains a more structured and localized activation pattern throughout the phases. Complementing these qualitative observations, the radar chart quantifies the temporal intra-class compactness metrics of corresponding phases, demonstrating that all of them achieve better performance with the learning process ongoing. The metrics reveal a clear hierarchy of improvement, where the Third Phase consistently encompasses the largest area, indicating superior cluster quality. FMI and ARI peak in this final phase, indicating increasing agreement with the underlying class structure, while compactness metrics show a notable shift: CH is substantially higher in the Third Phase than in the earlier stages, pointing to better-separated clusters, and Silhouette increases most strongly, signaling improved cohesion relative to separation.
