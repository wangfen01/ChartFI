This set of scatter plots illustrates the losses and accuracies of various methods with different preferences across three image datasets: Multi-MNIST, Multi-Fashion, and Multi-F+M. The horizontal and vertical axes represent results for objective 1 and objective 2, respectively, creating a multi-objective evaluation space where different colored dashed arrows indicate various preference vectors and different markers denote the solutions obtained by different methods, with marker colors matching the preferences. In the accuracy panels, the data shows consistently high performance with most methods clustering near the upper-right of each plot, aiming to maximize both objectives simultaneously; for instance, Multi-MNIST accuracies are tightly grouped from the high 0.80s to about 0.90, suggesting task saturation, while Multi-Fashion and Multi-F+M show a wider spread and more distinct trade-offs between the two objectives. Conversely, the loss panels reveal a clearer separation in optimization quality, where the ideal position is the bottom-left origin. While some approaches maintain low losses along the intended vectors, others exhibit significant deviations; notably, the results show that FOOPS is better at obtaining large hypervolumes, effectively pushing the Pareto frontier outward. Overall, the visualization highlights a dichotomy where simpler datasets yield diminishing returns in accuracy, while more complex distributions like Multi-F+M expose increasing instability and method differentiation, particularly regarding the balance between broad solution coverage and precise adherence to specified preference vectors.
