The five heatmaps display the Sliced Wasserstein Distances (SWD) between reduced train and test set embeddings across Areas of Interest (AOIs) in the leftmost panel, alongside the expected value of the same metric conditioned on each of four terrain properties—ESAWC, Mean Elevation, GHSBUILTS, and MODISVEG—in the remaining four panels. The leftmost matrix reveals a significant covariate shift in the distribution of the embeddings produced by the masked autoencoder across AOIs; while diagonal cells remain consistently near zero, representing the expected baseline when training and testing on the same AOI, off-diagonal values rise substantially. This indicates that cross-region generalization is the dominant driver of higher scores, with Europe emerging as the most distributionally distinct region where pairings frequently reach the upper end of the scale, around 5 to 6. In contrast, the Global row and column generally occupy the mid-range, suggesting it functions as a more average domain that avoids the extreme spikes seen in more idiosyncratic regions. The overall averages across the panels are clustered tightly between 2.77 and 2.92, with the unconditioned SWD holding the highest average at 2.92 and Mean Elevation the lowest at 2.77. As seen in the four right-hand matrices, although there is some reduction in the most extreme cases—such as ESAWC and MODISVEG, slightly smoothing specific off-diagonal peaks—the expected value of sliced Wasserstein distance conditioned on similar terrain properties is usually not substantially lower than the unconditioned case. For instance, GHSBUILTS maintains an overall level similar to ESAWC and MODISVEG but retains pronounced Europe-related peaks, confirming that built-environment information does not fully reconcile the mismatch between Europe and other regions, leaving the fundamental pattern of high cross-AOI transfer costs largely intact.
