The chart illustrates the frequency distribution of occlusion recovery rates (ORR) across various ego and cooperator configurations, with superimposed probability density distributions of inter-agent distances stratified by ORR intervals. Across the six panels, ridgeline densities show where distances concentrate while side bars indicate how frequently each ORR band occurs. For the Connected and Automated Vehicle (CAV), the cooperation benefits provided by the truck are substantial in most scenarios, with the occlusion recovery rate exceeding 0.8 in over 60% of cases. In these high occlusion recovery scenarios, most truck-CAV distances are within 50 meters, but as this distance increases, the cooperation benefits deteriorate rapidly, a pattern reflected in the density plots where higher ORR intervals are associated with broader, flatter distance distributions. In contrast, the cooperation benefits provided by the Roadside Unit (RSU) follow an entirely different trend; the occlusion recovery rate predominantly falls between 0.4 and 0.6, and the RSU is particularly effective in mitigating occlusions at near to mid ranges, whereas the truck significantly reduces the occlusions it causes to the CAV. For the RSU, the cooperation benefits provided by the truck exhibit a trend similar to that of the truck benefiting the CAV, with an even higher proportion of cases where the occlusion recovery rate exceeds 0.8. However, the cooperative contribution of the CAV to the RSU is relatively weak, with the occlusion recovery rate mostly below 0.4. When the truck acts as the ego vehicle, the RSU provides slightly greater cooperation benefits than the CAV, with occlusion recovery rates primarily between 0.4 and 0.6, while those from the CAV are concentrated between 0.2 and 0.4. Additionally, a distinct spatial constraint is visible in these interactions: as the distance to the truck decreases, the cooperation benefits from both the RSU and the CAV decline, manifesting as pronounced short-distance spikes at the lowest ORR bands that diminish quickly as reliability improves.
