The figure compares five heatmaps showing how each method assigns nine clusters to pancreatic cell types, where darker squares indicate a stronger correspondence. While scDeepCluster, scNAME, scGNN, and scCDCG display more diffuse patterns with several mid-intensity off-diagonal cells suggesting partial mixing of cell identities, these methods also exhibit distinct failures in classification. Specifically, scDeepCluster could not identify the cell type for cluster 1, and scNAME could not identify cell types for clusters 2 and 7, nor could it assign a unique cell type to cluster 0. Furthermore, both scGNN and scCDCG could not assign an endothelial cell to a specific cluster, indicating a lack of consistent one-to-one mapping in these models. In contrast, scSiameseClu shows the clearest structure, where high-intensity cells align more tightly along a diagonal-like pattern with fewer competing off-diagonal signals. This distinct concentration of signal into peaks implies reduced ambiguity and fewer mixed clusters, demonstrating that the method accurately annotated clusters with cell types, achieving over 90 % similarity in marker genes for most clusters.
