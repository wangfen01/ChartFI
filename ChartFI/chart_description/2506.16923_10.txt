This chart illustrates the per-query runtime on a logarithmic scale for approximately 300 queries, sorted by the time to compute the output tuples, and decomposes the total duration into baseline query execution, lineage computation, and LExaShap attribution overhead at various percentiles. The baseline query execution forms a smooth, gradually rising profile from sub-second runs to tens of seconds, while the additional overhead components introduce significant volatility and heavy-tailed behavior. Lineage computation remains a modest addition for most queries but becomes more material in the slower tail, occasionally approaching the same order of magnitude as the execution time. The LExaShap contributions represent the primary source of runtime anomalies, where explanation time exceeds query execution in 57% of queries and exceeds it by over 10x in 25% of cases. Specifically, for LExaShap, the overhead is typically higher as only 47% of queries complete attribution for all output tuples faster than query execution, and only 51% do so when considering query execution plus lineage. This pattern of extreme outliers is further evidenced by the fact that in 19% of queries, at least one output tuple incurs a 2 OOM slowdown, shifting the workload from predictable runtimes to a regime driven by occasional but severe computational costs.
