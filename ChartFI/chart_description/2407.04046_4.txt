The provided heatmaps compare the Pearson correlation between instance-level measurements over all configurations for Llama 2-Chat and GPT 3.5, revealing distinct structural relationships among evaluation criteria. A primary observation is that conventional metrics show high correlations among themselves, forming a tight cluster centered on ROU, BER, and Sci, with BLE moderately aligned to this group; notably, the strongest relationships are between BER and Sci, which exhibit near-identical strength at roughly 0.91–0.92 across both models. Conversely, these conventional metrics negatively correlate with the generated output length, as the word count (WC) consistently moves in the opposite direction to the core quality cluster. Distinct from this group, the NLI metrics show little to no correlation with the conventional measures, suggesting they evaluate fundamentally different aspects of performance. Specifically, correlations to the NLI-based metrics, identified as TRU (TRUE) and Sum (SummaC), are low, and TRUE and SummaC are less correlated with each other compared to conventional metrics. While this separation is consistent, a minor variation exists between models: Sum behaves differently in GPT 3.5, becoming noticeably more aligned with ROU (correlation of 0.36) compared to Llama 2-Chat, yet it still reflects the broader trend where NLI-based indicators remain largely independent of the dominant BER–Sci–ROU structure.
