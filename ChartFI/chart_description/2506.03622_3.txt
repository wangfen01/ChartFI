The beamforming gain and power allocation across various schemes are illustrated through azimuth responses for three users, two targets, and two eavesdroppers, alongside a comparative power distribution analysis. In the beamforming gains of the common stream Wc, Scheme 2 forms distinct main lobes covering both the users’ and targets’ directions, rising to near the top of the plotted range, while Scheme 1 lacks a main lobe entirely and stays close to the floor around the mid −40 dB region. For the user beams w1, w2, and w3, the two proposed schemes have slightly higher and lower gains, respectively, in the LUs’ direction and the eavesdroppers’ direction. Specifically, in w1 and w3, all methods exhibit a comb-like structure with repeated deep notches where Scheme 1 consistently provides the highest curve at the main lobes, followed by Ben1 and then SDMA. In the W2 beam, SDMA forms a strong positive peak near broadside while the other methods remain negative, indicating that SDMA concentrates energy into that beam direction more aggressively. The beamforming gain of wv highlights a significant divergence where both SDMA and Scheme 1 have the main lobes in the targets’ direction, whereas in Scheme 2, sv is regarded as artificial noise (AN), so the gain in the LUs’ direction is relatively lower. Consequently, the BF gain for the AN (Wc in Scheme 1 and Wv in Scheme 2) is lower, with Scheme 2 being dramatically suppressed across nearly all angles. The power allocation with different schemes reveals significant variation in total consumption and distribution; SDMA consumes the most significant power, while Scheme 2 and Ben1 consume the least power. This redistribution is characterized by Scheme 1 spreading power heavily into Wv, while Scheme 2 and Ben1 shift the majority of power into Wc with almost negligible Wv, and SDMA stands out by allocating its largest shares to W2 and Wv.
