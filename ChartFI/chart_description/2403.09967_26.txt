The simulated and measured beam patterns generated from NR-Surface using 1-bit GPIO outputs are illustrated as heatmaps that map the desired main-lobe angle on the vertical axis against the measured angle on the horizontal axis, with color indicating signal strength. These power simulation and measurement results are arranged such that each row represents a single beam pattern desired to be steered towards +10 degrees to +70 degrees, with the overall matrix spanning desired main-lobe angles from 10 degrees to 70 degrees. In the simulation, power spans from approximately 10 dB down to -20 dB and shows a clear concentration of higher power where measured angles track the intended steering direction, suggesting a coherent main-lobe alignment. The power measurement matrix has a similar appearance to the simulation, having a strong diagonal demonstrating each beam pattern is successfully steered to the desired angle, although the dynamic range is shifted to lower absolute levels with RSSI varying approximately from -95 dB to -120 dB. Across both panels, the dominant diagonal tendency confirms successful beam steering and validates the effectiveness of the NR-Surface design.
