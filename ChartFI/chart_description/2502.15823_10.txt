This set of nine donut charts presents a breakdown of error types for three models—Llama-70b, o1-mini, and o3-mini—across ISL, L-OSL, and R-OSL configurations, revealing distinct patterns in how models fail. Across the board, errors are generally dominated by the Wrong Rules category rather than the Missing Rule. Detailed analysis of the outer rings shows that among wrong rules, the majority are completely wrong, followed by a notable fraction of too general, although there is also a non-negligible number of too-specific errors. Conversely, among missing rules, the most common issue is completely missed, where the model fails to identify the relevant pattern at all; in contrast, too specific errors in this category are relatively rare. Finally, a correct condition but a wrong transformation occurs relatively infrequently across all charts. Examining specific model behaviors, Llama-70b consistently shows Wrong Rules outweighing Missing Rule, with the shift from ISL to L-OSL and R-OSL significantly increasing the volume of errors; notably, L-OSL exhibits a massive expansion of the Wrong Rules segment driven by completely wrong outcomes. For o1-mini, while ISL already displays a heavy completely wrong component, the R-OSL configuration swings sharply toward severe failure, showing a substantial increase in both Missing Rule and Wrong Rules to become the most error-heavy configuration for this model. Similarly, for o3-mini, while ISL and L-OSL are comparatively smaller and more balanced, R-OSL shows a clear step-change upward in total errors, again driven mainly by completely wrong and completely missed types. The most consistent anomaly is the R-OSL condition, which repeatedly introduces a significant jump in missing rules and amplifies catastrophic misapplication, indicating that this setting increases both omission risk and outright failure rather than producing nuanced, near-miss mistakes.
