This chart displays the Intent Coverage Rate on AmbiK for UNAMBIGUOUS (a), PREFERENCES (b), COMMON SENSE KNOWLEDGE (c), and SAFETY (d) tasks, comparing the performance of GPT-3.5, GPT-4, Llama-2-7B, and Llama-3-8B across four response formats: KnowNo, LAP, LofreeCP, and Binary. A clear overall pattern is that the ICR performance on PREFERENCES, COMMON SENSE KNOWLEDGE and SAFETY tasks is particularly weak compared to UNAMBIGUOUS tasks, where all models achieve their highest scores. Across all task types, the choice of response format is a critical determinant of performance, as the one-step Binary method produced more accurate prediction sets than KnowNo, LAP, and LofreeCP in most cases, consistently yielding the highest ICR for every model. This effect is most pronounced in the Unambiguous task, where GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 achieve ICRs in the upper 0.6–0.7 range. Following this, the performance of the logit-free LofreeCP method on Llama-2-7B establishes it as the second-best approach overall, proving especially favorable for the Llama models and enabling Llama-2-7B to show pronounced gains across all tasks. In contrast, KnowNo produces moderate ICR for the Llama models but is weaker for GPT-4, and LAP is the clear underperformer, with ICRs frequently near zero for the GPT models. Interesting model-specific behaviors also emerge, as GPT-4 tends to lag GPT-3.5 on KnowNo and LAP yet becomes highly competitive under the Binary format, suggesting its ICR is more sensitive to response framing. Furthermore, the Preferences task reveals the widest performance separation between models when using the Binary format, with Llama-3-8B leading while Llama-2-7B's performance drops markedly, indicating a task-specific divergence between the two Llama generations.
