This chart illustrates the directional gain profiles for five beamforming algorithms—ALMCI, CCPA, MCQT-SCA, ZF, and MMSE—over a ±90° angular spread, configured for a system with L = 20 antennas serving K = 2 users in the presence of N = 4 targets. The analysis is divided into two scenarios, AP-1 and AP-2, which feature two dominant main lobes positioned around ±50° and ±40° respectively, corresponding to the user locations, alongside suppressed regions intended for interference rejection. In the AP-1 scenario, while the five curves largely track each other with modest separation in sidelobe levels, distinct morphological differences appear in the main lobes; specifically, the ALMCI algorithm shows sharp, distinct peaks patterning a strong directional focus, whereas the CCPA and MCQT-SCA algorithms demonstrate broader peaks. ALMCI and MCQT-SCA generally sit higher at the mainlobe peaks compared to CCPA, which exhibits a more conservative pattern with less peak emphasis. The AP-2 scenario reveals a much clearer divergence in algorithmic behavior, particularly in the central region where ALMCI introduces very deep, narrow nulls that drop dramatically toward the bottom of the plotted range, implying aggressive interference cancellation at specific angles. Conversely, MMSE and ZF maintain a more uniform suppression band without such extreme dips, while MCQT-SCA exhibits pronounced oscillations and elevated sidelobe structures, indicating a potential tradeoff favoring mainlobe fidelity over sidelobe control. Overall, the most significant shift is the emergence of deeper, sharper nulling for ALMCI in AP-2, signaling that this scenario presents a more challenging environment where algorithmic differences in interference handling and directional focus become critically visible.
