This set of heatmaps illustrates the acceptance rates for baseline and knowledge-distilled prompts, segmented by model performance and specific actions across various animal class combinations. The upper section breaks down acceptance rates by animal class, revealing a clear hierarchy in generation quality where prompts involving birds generally yielded the most accurate depictions, followed by mammals, while models consistently struggled at generating images containing reptiles. In the baseline analysis, FLUX.1-dev excelled in Bird-Bird prompts, reaching a 72% acceptance rate, whereas Stable Diffusion 3.5 Large performed best under Mammal-Mammal scenarios at 52%. However, both of these otherwise strong models struggled with reptile-related prompts. Notably, Playground v2.5—despite its low overall acceptance—performed comparatively well on Reptile-Reptile prompts with a 46% rate, surpassing even top-performing models in this specific category. Comparing the baseline results to the knowledge-distilled acceptance rates reveals a substantial performance improvement across the board, with the latter showing significantly higher saturation and success rates, such as FLUX.1-dev reaching 95% in Bird-Bird scenarios. The lower section details acceptance rates across various animal classes and actions, illustrating several recurring issues in the baseline data, which appears sparse and indicates difficulty in rendering specific interactions. Conversely, the knowledge-distilled action heatmaps display increased density and higher success counts for complex dynamic interactions like biting, eating, and fighting, particularly within Mammal-Reptile and Mammal-Mammal combinations, demonstrating that knowledge distillation effectively mitigates the generation failures observed in the baseline models.
