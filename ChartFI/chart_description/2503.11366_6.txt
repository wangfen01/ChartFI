This set of bar charts illustrates the F1 score advantage of the Comet method compared to the baselines FIR, RR, and CL for an MLP model across three datasets from CleanML, revealing distinct sensitivity to budget and error types. In the Airbnb dataset with scaling errors, Comet consistently maintains a positive edge, where the difference between Comet and the baselines increases as the invested budget increases; the data shows a steady, tightly clustered upward drift, indicating that Comet yields marginal but progressive gains over the baselines without the instability seen elsewhere. In contrast, the Credit dataset with scaling errors demonstrates a two-phase pattern: initially, Comet achieves an F1 score advantage of up to 4 percentage points compared to FIR, RR, and CL, particularly visible in the early budget stages. However, this performance lead undergoes a pronounced regime change around the low 20s budget mark, where the advantage collapses and turns negative, suggesting a threshold where the baselines begin to outperform Comet. The Titanic dataset with missing values presents the most volatile behavior, where small positive advantages at low budgets quickly cross into sustained negative territory from roughly the high teens onward; this section is characterized by frequent oscillations and deeper negative dips, implying that under conditions of missingness, increasing the budget tends to favor the baselines rather than sustaining the advantage for Comet.
