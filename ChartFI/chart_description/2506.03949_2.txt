The visualizations present a comprehensive analysis of temporal and spatial self-attention across three MI datasets—BNCI2014001, BNCI2014002, and OpenBMI—encompassing temporal attention heatmaps of time windows, spatial attention heatmaps of EEG channels, and the quantification of electrode channel importance. The temporal self-attention maps exhibit prominent vertical stripe structures, where certain time windows consistently receive higher attention weights regardless of the query time step, reflecting the model's tendency to focus on a fixed subset of informative temporal positions rather than local or long-range pairwise dependencies. Within these temporal patterns, BNCI2014001 and OpenBMI display stable vertical structures where certain time windows consistently carry higher values, while BNCI2014002 shows a more finely grained distribution across a longer sequence of time steps. Parallel to this, the spatial self-attention maps reveal both diagonal activations corresponding to self-focus on individual channels and distinct off-diagonal activations reflecting inter-channel interactions. These spatial maps reinforce that the strongest values concentrate around central and centro-parietal regions, with BNCI2014002 generally operating at a higher overall intensity level than BNCI2014001, while OpenBMI sits between them with sharper contrasts for a subset of channels. Furthermore, channel importance based on incoming off-diagonal attention weights is quantified in the ranked bar charts, where channels around C and CP dominate the top end across all datasets, indicating that central sensorimotor areas contribute most strongly to the measured metric. The distribution of these weights reveals dataset-specific characteristics: OpenBMI shows the widest spread with the lowest-ranked channels dropping furthest from the top, implying greater heterogeneity across electrodes and a stronger concentration of signal in a smaller set of top central channels, while BNCI2014001 and BNCI2014002 exhibit similarly tighter ranges suggesting more uniform channel contributions across electrodes.
