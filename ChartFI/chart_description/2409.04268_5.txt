The time series of the normalized population abundance across varying Pe values reveals a clear transition from stable, high-abundance states to lower-abundance regimes characterized by persistent temporal variability, though the normalized population abundance is always greater than 1. At Pe = 0, the population quickly rises and settles into a steady plateau in the mid-1.4 range with minimal variability, whereas Pe = 3 maintains a similar mean level but introduces sawtooth-like fluctuations. As Pe increases to 10 and 20, the mean abundance drops toward the low-1.3 range and the signal becomes noisier; however, at sufficiently large times, the population abundance converges to oscillations with a well-defined period. The highest case, Pe = 25, exhibits the lowest long-run mean near the mid-1.2s and shows pronounced early-time volatility that gradually damps into a regular, small-amplitude oscillation, suggesting a transient adjustment before reaching a new dynamical regime. These temporal dynamics are further elucidated by the log-log power spectrum of the time series, which shows how dominant timescales shift with Pe. Lower Pe cases concentrate power at lower frequencies with distinct, sharp peaks—most notably the strong low-frequency peak for Pe = 3—consistent with slower, more coherent oscillations. In contrast, higher Pe values shift power toward higher frequencies and broaden the spectrum, reflecting faster fluctuations and a dispersion of variability that suppresses a single dominant rhythm, as seen in the flatter, more distributed power profiles for Pe = 20 and 25.
