The distribution of local Shapley values for the sixteen best models is arranged for each machine learning approach by row and for each mass model by column, ranking physical features by their overall importance to the model output. In these plots, the horizontal position indicates whether a feature pushes predictions up or down, while the predictor value color demonstrates if the value for the given input predictor was high or low, with the vertical spread in points representing how many values are located in the same region. A recurring pattern across the panels is that features exhibiting a color gradient from left to right are best described as being either monotonically increasing or decreasing, such as the mass number A, where high values of A indicate negative Shapley values and low values of A indicate positive Shapley values. The magnitude of these effects varies notably by panel; several plots show relatively modest influence ranges clustered within a few tenths around zero, while others expand to much larger spans reaching the order of 1 to 3 SHAP units. For the models shown in the last column, the N_E and Z_E values play a highly impactful role, consistently appearing in the top three features, whereas the higher-order deformation parameters beta_4 and beta_6 were either among the four least impactful of the models or were not used at all. While most panels show distributions tightly centered around zero with marginal, near-symmetric effects for lower-ranked variables, the top-ranked features often display long, sparse tails and isolated points signaling occasional extreme impacts or rare regimes where the model response changes sharply. Some panels also display mixed-color clusters on both sides of zero for the same feature, suggesting non-linear behavior or complex interaction effects where the same feature value can contribute differently depending on other variables. Overall, the models rely on a stable core of leading physical features, but the strength and clarity of their directional impact shift across different scenarios.
