This chart compares demographic subgroup rank according to real-world prevalence, the Pile co-occurrence counts, and Llama3 logits for 15 diseases where real-world prevalence is available, utilizing a scale where 1 represents the most prevalent and 5 the least prevalent. The visualization highlights discrepancies and alignments between dataset co-occurrence representations and actual demographic prevalence of diseases, revealing that patterns are significantly more stable by gender than by race. In the Male and Female panels, the data show tight, repeated placements with minimal spread, indicating that the three sources agree on relative ordering when stratified by sex. Conversely, the race panels illustrate discrepancies between Llama3’s logits compared to The Pile and real-world prevalences, characterized by large cross-source divergence and condition-specific shifts. A dominant signal is that Llama3 generally tracks The Pile’s ranking behavior more closely than it matches real-world data, leading to systematic misalignments in specific subgroups. This gap is most pronounced in the Asian and Indigenous panels, which display inverse anomalies: in the Asian subgroup, the Pile and Llama3 cluster toward high prominence while real-world data indicates low prevalence, whereas in the Indigenous subgroup, real-world data indicates high prevalence while the models assign lower prominence. These findings suggest that while model-derived distributions may mirror each other, they frequently overemphasize or underrepresent certain conditions relative to observed data in heterogeneous racial groups.
