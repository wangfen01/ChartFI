The heatmap displays the correctness scores of various models when prompted with dialectal instructions across multiple Arabic dialects, revealing a clear stratification by model tier where the strongest models consistently produce higher scores across nearly every geography. Larger models, such as GPT-4o, Claude-3.5-Sonnet, and CMDR+, consistently exhibit higher scores; for example, GPT-4o achieves 8.1 for Egypt and 8.4 for Tunisia, while Claude-3.5-Sonnet peaks at 7.9 in Egypt and Tunisia. In contrast, smaller models (e.g., Llama-3.1-8B and Jais-13b) generally perform below 4.0, demonstrating significantly less robustness in handling diverse dialects. Performance also varies by country, with some dialects presenting more difficulty than others. Specifically, Egypt and Tunisia tend to yield higher scores, with Egypt emerging as the most uniformly high-scoring market where many models cluster in the 6.5–8.1 range, and Tunisia acting as a strong "lift" market that accentuates the gap between top and mid-tier models. Conversely, Morocco, Yemen and Palestine often yield lower scores for multiple models; Palestine is the clearest difficulty hotspot where scores compress sharply toward the low 2s to low 4s, and even top models experience a visible dip. A notable pattern is that several models show a U-shaped geography profile—high in Egypt, dipping in Palestine and Morocco, then recovering in Tunisia—implying country-specific linguistic or domain challenges rather than random noise.
