This chart presents a comparison of computational complexity versus mean absolute error (MAE) for different configurations of per-CIR and multi-CIR approaches, specifically plotting Per-CIR time-based order (blue), Per-CIR fixed order (orange), and Multi-CIR (green) on a complexity scale. The data spans a complexity range from approximately 10^4 to over 10^7, with MAE values generally clustering between 0.42 and 0.65 m, though the full range extends from roughly 0.40 to 0.95 m. A defining feature of the analysis is the red dashed line along which the Pareto-optimal solutions are numbered, delineating the most efficient trade-offs between computational cost and accuracy. Multi-CIR patching dominates the low-complexity region of the Pareto front, particularly evident in the lower-numbered solutions where the method achieves competitive accuracy with minimal computational resources. However, as the analysis moves toward higher computational loads, the performance dynamics shift; for increasing complexity and performance, the per-CIR patching becomes the optimal configuration with the lowest MAE for per-CIR patching with fixed ordering. This trend is reinforced by the density of orange markers at the lower bound of the error range in the mid-to-high complexity zones, whereas the Per-CIR time-based order points sit slightly higher on average, commonly around 0.45–0.70 m. While Multi-CIR is efficient at the low end, it shows the widest overall spread, including high-error outliers reaching roughly 0.85–0.95 m, implying sensitivity to configuration. Ultimately, the chart illustrates a region of diminishing returns around 10^6 to 10^7 complexity, where increasing complexity beyond the optimal fixed-order configurations yields negligible improvements in error reduction.
