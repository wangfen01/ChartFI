The charts present Gaussian Kernel Density Estimations of the Node Homophily Ratio (NHR), denoted as h, for various meta-paths across three datasets (ACM, DBLP, Yelp), alongside binary joint density estimations comparing meta-path pairs on the Yelp dataset. In the univariate density plots, it can be observed that there is a significant distribution of nodes in both high and low homophily ratio regions across any given meta-path, with different meta-paths often concentrating in distinct NHR regimes. On the ACM dataset, the PAP meta-path is heavily skewed toward high NHR values near 1.0, whereas PSP is multimodal with pronounced peaks in the mid-to-high range, suggesting PAP consistently identifies high-homophily nodes while PSP is sensitive to varied structural regimes. For DBLP, the APA meta-path exhibits a U-shaped distribution with density at the extremes, while APTPA concentrates in the lower-mid range (around 0.3–0.4) and ACPPA is tightly focused at a high NHR (around 0.8–0.9), highlighting that these meta-paths operate in starkly different homophily regimes. On Yelp, BSB is bimodal with strong peaks in the higher NHR region (0.6–0.9), BUB is more broadly centered in the mid-range, and BLB is multimodal with substantial mass in the low-to-mid range. The pairwise joint density plots for Yelp reinforce these divergences. Specifically, nodes with a high NHR based on BSB exhibit a varied NHR based on BUB; while some nodes display a high NHR on both, a prominent off-diagonal cluster shows that others have a low one, indicating systematic disagreement. Similarly, the BSB versus BLB comparison shows frequent deviations where high BSB values correspond to moderate BLB values. The BUB and BLB pairing is comparatively more aligned, with density closer to the diagonal, but still exhibits significant spread, implying partial agreement with consistent meta-path-specific offsets rather than a one-to-one relationship.
