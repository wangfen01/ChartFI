This chart compares the day-of-year timing across various plant species by plotting the study ground truth with mean/std in blue against the AI estimate with mean/std in red. The species reveal a steady upward progression from approximately the high 170s–200 DoY on the left to around 300–340 DoY on the right. It is important to note that the calculation excludes three of the species not having enough samples left to estimate their species’ mean DoYs. With these exclusions, the overall replication error drops to 14 days, indicating a generally high level of correspondence between the series. This metric is visually supported by the observation that for a majority of species, the estimated mean DoYs now lie within the standard deviations reported in the original study. Despite this alignment, the AI estimates frequently sit slightly below the ground truth, implying a mild tendency to predict earlier dates. Furthermore, uncertainty varies meaningfully across the dataset; error bars are relatively tight for many mid-range species but widen noticeably among later-season species on the right, where both ground-truth variability and AI uncertainty increase. Consequently, mismatches become more pronounced in the late DoY range, with several cases where AI estimates deviate by multiple weeks, suggesting the model struggles more with late-season phenology even though it successfully preserves the overall ranking of early-to-late species timing.
