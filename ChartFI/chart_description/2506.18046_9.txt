This heatmap illustrates the classification results (Aff-F1) of univariate datasets categorized by five distinct characteristics: Trend, Seasonality, Stationary, Transition, and Shifting, providing a comprehensive comparison across non-learning, traditional machine learning, deep learning, and pre-trained LLM-based models. Performance is strongly stratified by method family, where most methods perform better on time series with trend, stationarity, and shifting characteristics, while time series with seasonality and transition characteristics make it difficult to detect anomalies. LLM-based and time-series pre-trained models typically exhibit good performance on most data with varying characteristics, consistently occupying the top tier of the heatmap and performing notably well on data with trend and shifting characteristics. Within the deep learning category, prediction-based methods such as iTrans, TsNet, CALF, and UniTS frequently perform well on data with trend and seasonality characteristics, whereas contrast-based methods, including DC and ConAD, exhibit relatively weak performance across all characteristics. Density-based methods like LODA and PCA perform a bit worse across most characteristics, and classical statistical baselines such as DAGMM sit near the bottom across regimes, showing particularly low values under shifting. A notable anomaly among traditional approaches is KMeans, which spikes markedly on stationary and transition data with scores exceeding 0.8, outperforming many deep learning models in those specific regimes, though it fails to sustain this advantage on shifting data.
