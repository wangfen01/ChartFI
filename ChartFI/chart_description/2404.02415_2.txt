This heatmap visualizes the cosine similarity between target tasks across a wide set of vision-language benchmarks, where deeper reds indicate stronger positive alignment and blues indicate negative alignment. Overall, most tasks move together, forming a broad positively correlated block, particularly clustering around the VQA family and closely related datasets like VQAv2, OK-VQA, A-OKVQA, GQA, and TextVQA. COCO Caption, Flickr30k, and TextCaps also show broadly positive relationships with these VQA tasks, though the connections appear slightly weaker and more variable, implying that captioning gains are related yet not perfectly interchangeable with VQA gains. A notable shift appears with tasks that behave more idiosyncratically, most prominently OLIVE, which features a unique transfer profile and has very low cosine similarity with other tests. With a mean similarity of -0.06, OLIVE is the third least similar to other target tasks, appearing as a distinct band of neutral to cool tones that contrasts sharply with the dominant red block. These scattered blue bands and low-similarity zones signal potential specialization effects, suggesting that optimizing for synthetic reasoning, explanation ranking, or unique profiles like OLIVE’s can diverge significantly from the improvements that drive gains on the dominant VQA and captioning cluster.
