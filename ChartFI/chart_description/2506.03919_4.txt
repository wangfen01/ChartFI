This scatter plot visualizes the relationship between the untrained pre-training expressivity (tau_pre) and the post-training expressivity (tau_post) of pruned GIN and GCN models across multiple datasets, where each marker represents the mean over the samples obtained for the models and color indicates the prune percentage (ρ in [10, 90]%). A dashed line from (0,0) to (1,1) indicates hypothetical pre- and post-training equivalence, yet all data points fall below this dashed tau_post = tau_pre line, indicating that tau_post is generally lower than tau_pre and that sparse GNNs rarely transition from low to high expressivity during training. While the reduction in expressivity becomes more pronounced as the pruning percentage increases—with warmer colors tending to sit further beneath the parity line—low pruning percentages apparently exaggerate the effect, and pre-training expressivity was low even at conservative pruning rates on some datasets. At low tau_pre values near zero, tau_post remains near zero regardless of pruning, suggesting a floor effect where recovery from a relatively low expressivity sparse initialization is rare during training if only non-zero weights are updated, even if theoretically possible. In the mid-range of tau_pre from 0.3 to 0.7, tau_post often drops substantially, indicating where pruning has the greatest relative impact and where sensitivity differs most by dataset, while at high tau_pre values between 0.85 and 1.0, many models retain relatively strong tau_post between 0.6 and 0.9. However, several notable cases show sharp collapses from near-perfect tau_pre to much lower tau_post, highlighting anomalies where pruning removes critical structure that cannot be recovered through training.
