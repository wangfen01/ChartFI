This heatmap presents a matrix of scores where the results of each individual combination of teammates are shown, with Agent 1 on the x-axis and Agent 2 on the y-axis. The submatrices highlighted in red concern intra-algorithmic cross-play (intra-XP), with their diagonal comparisons of the same seed being equivalent to self-play (SP), while all other comparisons concern inter-algorithmic cross-play (inter-XP). A clear performance hierarchy emerges, as R3D2 achieves significantly better inter-XP compared to the baselines while maintaining a competitive SP and intra-XP. This superiority is demonstrated in the lower-right block, where R3D2-S and R3D2-M pairings form a stable high-performance plateau with scores consistently between 21.5 and 22.7. In inter-XP scenarios, R3D2's collaborative strength is also evident, as OBL collaborates better with R3D2, achieving scores from 8.9 to 11.5, than with any other baseline. In stark contrast, baseline agents exhibit a critical weakness in heterogeneous pairings, most notably in the pronounced performance trough where IQL or OP are matched with OBL, causing scores to plummet to a 2.4-6.9 range. This incompatibility is further underscored by the observation that R3D2-S paired with OP achieves a higher score (5.3-8.7) than OBL with OP (2.5-6.9). While baselines like IQL and OBL can achieve high scores in self-play (up to 23.8 and 24.2, respectively), their poor performance in inter-algorithmic pairings reveals a lack of versatility compared to the robust and adaptable R3D2 variants.
