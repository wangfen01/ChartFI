The heatmaps illustrate the correlation between individual community smells and SATD types across all projects, revealing a distinct evolution in relationship dynamics as project size increases. In small projects, there exist strong and statistically significant positive correlations between specific community smells and particular types of SATD; notably, Black Cloud Effect (BCE) and Truck Factor (TF) exhibit perfect correlations with Design debt and high correlations with Requirement debt. The Organization Silo Effect (OSE) shows strong correlations with multiple SATD categories, including Code, Documentation, Testing, and Defect, while Radio Silence (RS) demonstrates a moderate link to Design and Requirement debt, and smells like Sharing Villainy (SV) and Solution Defiance (SD) show moderate or weak associations. Compared to small projects, the associations in medium projects are generally weaker but reveal meaningful patterns where Truck Factor (TF) exhibits the strongest and most consistent correlations across all SATD types, particularly with Defect, Documentation, and Requirement debt. In this medium-sized band, Organizational Silo Effect (OSE) and Radio Silence (RS) show moderate correlations with several SATD categories, especially in Design, Defect, and Requirement debt, whereas smells such as Unhealthy Interactions (UI) and Organizational Skirmish (OS) exhibit weak or even negative correlations across most SATD types. For large repositories, the results demonstrate strong and more widespread associations compared to small and medium-sized projects, characterized by Toxic Communications (TC) and Prima-donna Effect (PDE) exhibiting particularly strong correlations across multiple SATD categories. Specifically, TC shows the highest correlations with Code debt and notable associations with Documentation, Design, and Requirement debts, while PDE is strongly related to Code, Design, and Defect debt. Additionally, Radio Silence (RS) emerges as a prominent factor in large projects, moderately correlated with five SATD types, most notably Requirement and Test debt, while smells like Organizational Silo Effect (OSE) and Sharing Villainy (SV) show weaker or more isolated correlations, indicating that as systems scale, specific community smells and technical debt categories co-occur in a more stable and predictable manner.
