The figure compares training dynamics and distributional behavior for correctly labeled samples versus two types of mislabeled data, Mis-H and Mis-L, initially examining a setting with 50 percent Mis-H before introducing an additional 15 percent Mis-L. In the initial comparison, loss for correctly labeled data declines steadily, whereas Mis-H resists fitting early, starting at a higher value and remaining elevated before eventually converging to the same floor; this separation is reinforced by the distribution of normalized loss, where correct samples concentrate at low values while Mis-H clusters significantly higher. When both mislabel types are present, the loss trajectories reveal a distinct hierarchy where Mis-H maintains a higher average loss in the early stage of training, while the average loss for Mis-L is lower compared to Mis-H but remains above that of correct samples. Although the loss distribution of Mis-L may have an overlap with Correct and Mis-H, the joint analysis of loss and uncertainty facilitates a clear distinction between the groups. Specifically, samples exhibiting small loss and low uncertainty are classified as Correct, whereas the mislabeled groups occupy distinct regions: Mis-L is identifiable by moderate loss and high uncertainty, and Mis-H can be separated accordingly by its high loss and low uncertainty. This demonstrates that while uncertainty alone diminishes late in training, the correlation between loss and uncertainty provides a robust mechanism for distinguishing clean data from different categories of label noise.
