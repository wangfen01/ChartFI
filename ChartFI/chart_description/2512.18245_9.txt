The visualization presents the band importance of four hyperspectral images from the HOD-1 dataset within the SGG Module, where importance maps have been normalized to highlight variations in spectral significance across a total of 96 bands. Across the panels for Image IDs 0002, 0040, 0104, and 0229, the feature importance scores exhibit a broadly consistent morphology: moderate importance in the early bands, a pronounced mid-band spike, a long low-importance trough through the middle-to-late bands, and a renewed rise in the highest band indices. Demonstrating that the SGG module effectively isolates key features and selects the most critical spectral bands, the most dominant and stable shift is a sharp, needle-like peak at band index 41, which reaches the maximum normalized score of 1.0 in every image. This primary hotspot is accompanied by a smaller neighboring peak, indicating a highly influential spectral region that persists across cases. Outside this central feature, the early bands (roughly 0–10) vary more by image, with Image 0002 showing a notably stronger early peak than the others, while Images 0040 and 0104 are comparatively smoother and lower in that region. The late bands (about 85–95) consistently climb again, though magnitudes differ; Image 0104 exhibits the strongest sustained late-band plateau, whereas Image 0229 rises more modestly and remains uneven. Meanwhile, the extended low-importance stretch from roughly band 55 to 80 indicates bands that contribute little across all images, suggesting these specific spectral ranges may be candidates for dimensionality reduction without substantial loss of signal.
