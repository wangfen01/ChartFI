This series of box plots evaluates the distribution of Agent Skills across five distinct latent dimensions—Abductive Recall, History and Events, Scientific Facts, Cultural Records, and Complex Semantics—comparing the performance of Humans against various automated systems such as API Agents, Large-Scale LLMs, and RAG models. A red dashed line indicates the mean effective difficulty of each dimension, providing a baseline for assessing competency relative to the task complexity. The visual analysis reveals that humans' and QA systems’ skills are strikingly different across these latent axes, highlighting varying agent skills across the latent dimensions. Humans exhibit a distinct and substantial advantage in Abductive Recall, achieving median scores significantly higher than all computational models. However, this trend reverses in knowledge-intensive domains; in categories such as History and Events and Cultural Records, large-scale LLMs like GPT-4-TURBO and LLAMA-3-70B demonstrate superior ability in retrieving specific information about events and locations, outdoing humans on questions loaded with entity-specific details.
