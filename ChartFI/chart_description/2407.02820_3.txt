The charts illustrate the Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curves for the temporal semantic change task, specifically SemEval-2020 Task 1 (English), comparing a pre-trained CWE model (XLM-RoBERTa) and a fine-tuned SCWE model (XL-LEXEME). Performance is evaluated using full dimensions (Raw) versus feature-selection variants where PCA and ICA utilize the top 5%, 10%, 20%, 50%, and 100% of axes. In the pre-trained CWE setting, the use of the top 5% to 20% axes transformed by PCA is more effective in temporal semantic change detection than when all of the Raw dimensions are used; specifically, the Raw baseline sits at an AUC of 0.699, while PCA improves discrimination, peaking at the top-5% with 0.732 and remaining above the baseline for the top-10% and top-20%. Conversely, ICA returns the lowest performance in this setting, starting at a negligible AUC of 0.497 for the top-5% and only reaching 0.634 when all components are retained. Shifting to the fine-tuned SCWE context, the overall separability increases substantially, and PCA-transformed axes achieve the same AUC scores as Raw, with the top-20%, top-50%, and top-100% configurations matching the Raw model's peak AUC of 0.801. While fine-tuning improves ICA performance relative to the pre-trained state—clustering between 0.667 and 0.682—it consistently returns the lowest performance compared to Raw and PCA methods, indicating that ICA-based representations remain less aligned with the classification signal regardless of the model tuning.
