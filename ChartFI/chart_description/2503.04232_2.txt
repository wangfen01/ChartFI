The sunburst chart illustrates the distribution of the level-1 and level-2 error types in TGEA, revealing a hierarchical landscape where specific structural and semantic failures predominate. The inappropriate combination, omission, and redundancy errors account for 64.85% in the dataset, indicating that traditional grammatical issues remain central. Redundancy is the dominant category at 31.62%, and while the initial summary suggested a focus on object-related issues, the outer ring reveals that modifier and function word subtypes are the primary contributors, suggesting over-specification in descriptive phrases and grammatical particles. Inappropriate Combination follows at 25.23%, driven largely by predicate-object and subject-predicate pairings, which points to mismatched predicate–argument structures. In addition to these errors, the chart highlights substantial logic and context-level issues, specifically 18.96% commonsense errors and 10.48% discourse errors. The commonsense category is fragmented across diverse subtypes like taxonomy, behavior, and causation, implying that many failures stem from world-knowledge consistency rather than purely grammatical form, whereas Discourse Error is heavily concentrated in coreference, identifying a singular, focused weakness in entity tracking. Finally, the classification system proves comprehensive, as we observe that only 5.7% of cases cannot be categorized into the defined error types, appearing as "Other Error."
