This set of violin plots illustrates the distribution of confidence differences for benign and poisoned samples across four detection methods—SCALE-UP, MSPC, IBD-PSC, and CT—under distinct attack scenarios including BadNets (A2O), BadNets (A2A), WaNet (A2A), and BadNets (UT). A distinct behavioral divergence is evident in the first column; for BadNets (A2O), benign and poisoned samples exhibit significantly different confidence difference patterns. In contrast to this clear separation, the remaining scenarios demonstrate a high degree of distributional overlap. Under A2A and UT attacks, both benign and poisoned samples display similar confidence difference patterns across all four detection metrics. In these cases, the confidence differences of poisoned samples also approach 1, similar to those of benign samples, suggesting that the distinguishing characteristics visible in the A2O attack are largely absent in the A2A and UT attack scenarios.
