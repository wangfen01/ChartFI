Across the three panels, the charts track CUSUM-style detection statistics over time with a fixed red threshold and a dashed vertical line marking the true change point. In the left panel, the robust CUSUM statistic is tested on three scenarios comprising LFL, Random, and I.P.I.D. observations. The statistic stays low through most of the pre-change window, then rises sharply after the true change time nu = 54, with all three series crossing the threshold shortly after the change; this indicates strong sensitivity, as the Random and I.P.I.D. traces climb slightly faster than the LFL series. The middle panel illustrates the robust test using 10 different random observation sets, where the statistic begins trending upward around the true change time. Despite noticeable variability in how quickly the paths ramp up, the detection delays are consistently less than 10, reinforcing that the method is reliably triggered under the changed regime. The right panel contrasts these results with a non-robust CUSUM test. Here, pre-change volatility is significantly higher, and even though the threshold is raised to 6.9 = log(1000) to prevent false alarms, premature stopping remains in 2 out of 10 cases where trajectories produce sizable spikes well before the true change. This suggests that the non-robust behavior is more sensitive to outliers and less consistent in separating noise from the structural shift compared with the robust versions.
