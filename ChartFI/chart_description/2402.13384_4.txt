This sunburst visualization summarizes the taxonomic composition of the operational taxonomic units (OTUs) found and their relative abundance, utilizing the posterior mean of the marginal occurrence probabilities of each species as the measure of abundance. The chart demonstrates how observations are split first between the two dominant phyla, Basidiomycota and Ascomycota, before cascading into classes, orders, and families. Basidiomycota occupies the larger share overall, driven primarily by the class Agaricomycetes, where Polyporales and Agaricales are the dominating orders; within these, a few families stand out as the biggest contributors, including Polyporaceae at about 6% and Fomitopsidaceae around 5%, followed by Meruliaceae near 4%, while many other Agaricales families sit in the low single digits alongside a sizable "21 more" segment that signals a long tail of smaller families. Conversely, the Ascomycota sector is characterized by a more diffuse structure where Helotiales and Lecanorales are the dominating orders, differing from the more concentrated Basidiomycota profile. While classes such as Leotiomycetes and Dothideomycetes are prominent, the phylum shows pronounced fragmentation through multiple "10 more" and "13 more" groupings, with specific family-level concentrations like Hyaloscyphaceae and Parmeliaceae each at roughly 4% and Mycosphaerellaceae around 3%. Ultimately, the dataset is shaped by a handful of highly represented basidiomycete lineages contrasted against a taxonomically diverse ascomycete component spread across numerous minor families.
