This sunburst chart provides an overview of collected datasets, illustrating the composition of a derived bilingual collection that aggregates 54 datasets across 16 major domains. The visualization splits the data by language, with Chinese (Zh) represented in blue on the left and English (En) in orange on the right, while the concentric rings further break down each language into specific domains and then into representative datasets. On the Chinese side, coverage is heavily dominated by the Biomedical domain, characterized by a dense concentration of corpora such as the various CCKS iterations and CBLUE, alongside a robust News sector featuring well-known benchmarks like MSRA and PeopleDaily2014. Additionally, the Chinese segment displays a distinct cluster for Address data and Mixed resources, while Social Media appears present but occupies a smaller proportion compared to the dominant Biomedical and News categories. In contrast, the English section demonstrates a significantly more diversified domain mix; while it maintains substantial weight in Biomedical and Social Media, it expands strongly into niche and application-oriented areas, including clear representation in Scientific, Traffic, Wikipedia, and General domains. This indicates a broader topical spread in English rather than reliance on a few dominant categories. A key shift between the languages is that English pairs its core strengths with significant breadth in specialized fields, whereas Chinese resources remain more anchored in traditional newswire and medical benchmarks. The outer ring suggests English also has more fragmentation into many smaller datasets, which implies a wider variety but potentially greater heterogeneity in annotation schemes. Ultimately, while the prominence of biomedical data in both languages signals that medical NER is a shared priority, the relative thinness of Chinese resources in domains that are visibly established in English—such as Wikipedia-like general corpora and scientific datasets—highlights a structural divergence that may translate into cross-domain performance gaps for Chinese models.
