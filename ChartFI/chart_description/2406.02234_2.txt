The charts display the Euclidean (top) and loss-based (bottom) PH dimension plotted against the generalization gap for a range of learning rates and batch sizes across three model–dataset settings. For the FCN-7 on CHD and FCN-7 on MNIST experiments, the results align with those reported by Dupuis et al. [2023], as both the Euclidean and loss-based measures have a positive correlation with the generalization gap. In the MNIST panels, this relationship appears as a tight, monotonic band where dimensions increase smoothly alongside the accuracy gap, while the CHD results show a similar upward association, particularly in the loss-based metric, despite some saturation in the Euclidean dimension at small loss gaps. However, the AlexNet on CIFAR-10 experiment presents a significant deviation; while the Euclidean dimension continues to trend upward, we obtain negative correlations for the loss-based measure. Observing the data reveals this is due to several points with a high learning rate achieving very high PH dimension at the lower end of the accuracy gap, creating a non-monotonic distribution that contrasts sharply with the stable, positive trends observed in the other settings.
