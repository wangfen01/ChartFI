The three radar charts present a broader visual comparison of model performance across four datasets—abbreviated as Bo. for Boulder, Du. for Dundee, Pa. for Palo Alto, and Pe. for Perth—and two forecast horizons. In these plots, smaller values closer to the center circles indicate better forecast accuracy for MSE and MAE, whereas larger values further from the center indicate better forecast accuracy for R2. The HyperCast model configuration, depicted as a blue circle, achieves the best overall performance across the four datasets and both forecast horizons (Bo.3, Bo.7, Du.3, Du.7, Pa.3, Pa.7, Pe.3, Pe.7). Specifically, the R2 for HyperCast remains high and relatively uniform at the outer edge of the chart, whereas most baselines show noticeably lower and more variable R2, with some graph-based variants dipping toward weak or even negative territory. The error charts reinforce this separation, as HyperCast's MSE and MAE values cluster tightly in the lowest band across all scenarios while many baselines expand outward substantially, especially in the Boulder setting where their errors inflate the most. This contrast highlights that while baseline performance degrades sharply as conditions shift, the proposed approach maintains both accuracy and consistency. Consequently, the results demonstrate that HyperCast is not tailored to a single city but generalizes effectively across different charging infrastructure layouts, affirming the substantial improvements offered by the model as it consistently outperforms a wide array of forecasting techniques across diverse datasets and forecast horizons.
