The chart compares F1 performance across multiple evaluation datasets when training on two-dataset mixtures, categorized by Mocheg-based mixtures on the left and Fakeddit-based mixtures on the right, with a single-dataset baseline included for reference. The data illustrate that fact-checking models are particularly brittle to domain shift, as most mixtures cluster around the midrange near 45 to 55 F1 on many targets, with pronounced spikes and dips indicating strong dataset compatibility effects rather than uniform gains from mixing. In the Mocheg panel, adding Fakeddit produces the most dramatic lift on the FAK evaluation set, jumping from a baseline near 40 to over 90 F1. The largest volatility appears on HAX and MMH, where some mixtures drop into the mid-20s while others rise sharply, including a notable peak on HAX for the Mocheg+Hax mixture. PUB is another standout where the Mocheg+Pub mixture surges to the mid-70s, suggesting strong in-domain transfer when PUB is included, yet we do not find that inter-domain knowledge transfer from toxicity/hate speech detection aids in fact-checking, as seen by the lack of consistent improvement across diverse targets when adding Tox or Hax. In the Fakeddit panel, the FAK evaluation set remains consistently high across all mixtures and close to the baseline, implying performance there is largely saturated. The clearest positive shift again occurs on PUB, where the Fak+Pub mixture reaches the mid-70s and exceeds other mixtures by a wide margin, mirroring the in-domain advantage seen on the left. By contrast, HAX exhibits a pronounced anomaly where Fak+Fev collapses to the high-20s while other mixtures remain much higher, signaling a harmful interaction specific to that pairing. Across the remaining datasets such as POS, PRE, and PST, differences between mixtures are modest and mostly hover around the high-40s to low-50s, suggesting limited cross-domain benefit and that mixing mainly helps when the added dataset closely matches the evaluation domain or provides a particularly complementary signal.
