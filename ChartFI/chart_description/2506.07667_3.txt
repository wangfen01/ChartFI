This horizontal bar chart illustrates AutoMod’s recall for subsets of hate examples directed towards various communities, specifically with all filters under the Discrimination & Slurs category turned on. The opaque portion of each bar indicates the fraction of messages that are pre-filtered, while the extended lighter sections represent the total recall performance across three distinct datasets: SBIC, DynaHate, and ToxiGen. We observe that hate speech directed towards Men, Black Folks, and Mentally Disabled Folks is more effectively blocked by AutoMod than for the other target groups, revealing significant variance in detection capabilities. For instance, within the SBIC dataset, the system is most effective at identifying ableism; "Ableism: Mental" reaches a recall of about 0.37, becoming the dominant category for detection, while "Ableism: Physical" is blocked at a more modest rate of roughly 0.09. Similarly, the DynaHate dataset results are heavily skewed toward the effective blocking of "Misandry" and "anti-Black Racism," which reach recall levels of approximately 0.37 and 0.35 respectively, with "anti-Semitism" also elevated around 0.18 and "Islamophobia" around 0.12. In contrast, ToxiGen remains comparatively low across all shown categories, with blocking effectiveness topping out near 0.10 for "anti-Black Racism" and "anti-Semitism," and dropping to between 0.02 and 0.05 for ableism and Islamophobia. The most notable anomaly is the magnitude of detection for SBIC’s "Ableism: Mental" and DynaHate’s "Misandry," both reaching the highest observed levels; this suggests that dataset-specific coverage or collection biases may lead to models that overemphasize these categories while under-flagging others.
