These heatmaps illustrate the transferability of training data choices to different evaluation dimensions, where each cell shows the performance gain (delta) from training on the row configuration and evaluating on the column configuration. Overall, the dominant pattern is that alignment between what the model is trained on and what it is evaluated on produces the largest improvements, while mismatches are usually small, mixed, or occasionally negative. In the trajectory-format view, the biggest lifts cluster on the diagonal blocks; notably, all entries within the CSP and Z3 blocks show positive gains. Text-only training tends to deliver modest, broadly distributed gains, but it rarely matches the magnitude of specialized formats and shows limited transfer to highly structured tasks. In the reasoning-type view, the “like-to-like” effect becomes even clearer: Deductive training most strongly benefits Deductive evaluation, Inductive training benefits Inductive evaluation, and Abductive training benefits Abductive evaluation. Within this framework, all entries within the Abductive block show improvements, indicating consistent generalization for this reasoning type. Additionally, specific cross-type transfers are notable; for instance, all entries in the Deductive-CSP column show improvements. While cross-type transfer is generally uneven with some combinations dipping below zero, the high magnitude of CSP-aligned improvements and the consistent gains in aligned blocks underscore the critical role of training-evaluation alignment.
