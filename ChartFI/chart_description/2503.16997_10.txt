The performance comparison of U-Net and MedSAM under standalone training and SynFoC across four datasets—Prostate, Fundus, M&Ms, and BUSI—demonstrates that SynFoC effectively mitigates U-Net’s overfitting and further advances MedSAM’s performance in downstream tasks, showcasing superior capabilities in handling significant domain gaps in training data. In the Prostate benchmarks, SynFoC-MedSAM consistently emerges as the top performer, reaching the high 80s across several sites and providing clear jumps over standalone MedSAM, while SynFoC-U-Net significantly stabilizes performance, most notably on the BIDMC site where standalone U-Net collapses to 41.91% compared to SynFoC-U-Net’s 87.63%. For the Fundus dataset, results are tightly clustered in the mid-to-high 80s, yet SynFoC provides incremental improvements across all domains, with the most pronounced recovery on Domain 2. In the M&Ms dataset, performance varies by vendor, with Vendor 4 showing the highest overall segmentability and Vendor 1 the weakest; here, SynFoC-MedSAM trends highest across most categories, implying that the enhanced model generalizes better to vendor-dependent image characteristics. The BUSI dataset reveals a consistent pattern where malignant cases score higher than benign for all methods, and SynFoC-MedSAM delivers the largest gains in both classes, pushing malignant scores to a peak of 75.75% and providing a substantial boost to the more challenging benign class. Across all four datasets, the integration of SynFoC consistently lifts Average Dice scores and reduces cross-domain variability, proving most impactful when baseline performance is stressed by site-specific failure modes or difficult class distributions.
