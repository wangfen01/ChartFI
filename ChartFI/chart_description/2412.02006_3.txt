The embedding-level cross-attention alignment presented in the heatmap highlights the difference between the averaged attention scores for Healthy Control (HC) and Parkinson’s Disease (PD) groups in the DDK task of the GITA test set. The visualization reveals a clear differentiation between HC and PD groups in this embedding-level attention analysis, demonstrating that the model focused on different informed speech features depending on the group. Instead of a uniform distribution or a monotonic trend along the axes, the activity is concentrated in specific columns acting as hotspots, separated by large dark regions of minimal group separation. A defining structural characteristic is that for each one of these informed features, in most cases, nearly all SSL embedding dimensions play a significant role, resulting in sharp, continuous vertical stripes that span the entire y-axis rather than scattered clusters. While the early feature range (indices 0–3) and specific columns like feature 23 are dominated by HC signals (teal), other areas, particularly feature 5 and the dense band at feature 30, exhibit repeated PD-dominant activations (orange). These patterns indicate that while the embedding dimensions contribute uniformly within an active feature, the primary discriminative signal is driven by the specific speech features associated with each condition, making these distinct vertical bands the most informative candidates for interpreting group differences.
