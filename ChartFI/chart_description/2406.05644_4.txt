The heatmaps visualize the decoding results of all intermediate hidden states for 500 normal inputs of various topics, tracking the top five token candidates across layers 0 to 31. In this representation, higher Top-K Intermediate Consistency indicates that, for different inputs, the model tends to have more similar intermediate hidden states at that layer, which is analogous to a brighter color in the heatmap. In the early layers (0 to about 15), the top-ranked tokens are characterized by multilingual or fragmented strings such as penas, guez, sterd, and Einzeln, suggesting the encoding of low-level orthographic features rather than stable semantics. A distinct transition occurs in the middle layers (roughly 16 to 24), where the model forms similar positive emotional guesses, evidenced by the high-intensity clustering of tokens like glad, welcome, pleasure, and Hello. However, when processing the hidden states from the middle layers, the models generate tokens within coarse-grained emotions, which differ significantly from the final output. As the progression reaches the final layers (26 to 31), these emotional tokens recede and the distribution abruptly shifts toward function-word and certainty-related tokens, with "certain" and "of" dominating the top ranks, indicating a convergence toward generic syntactic or output-calibration features.
