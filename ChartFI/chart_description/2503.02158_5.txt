The bar charts illustrate the comparative influence of elevon deflection and rotor tilt on pitch and roll moments across a range of angles from −63° to +63° under four distinct motor throttle settings. In the pitch moment analysis, rotor tilt is significantly more effective at generating pitch moments than elevon deflection for high throttle settings of 50% and 70%, where the separation between motor levels widens at high positive angles to indicate a strong interaction where higher power amplifies pitch authority. Conversely, for relatively lower throttle settings of 0% and 30%, elevons are capable of producing considerable pitch moment compared to tilt rotors, though the overall pitch response remains slightly negative at negative angles before crossing to positive around +18.9° and rising sharply toward +63°. Regarding roll moments, elevon deflection is more effective at generating roll moments than rotor tilt for different throttle levels and tilt/deflection angles, with rotor tilt contributing comparatively little and staying close to zero across most of the range. The roll data shows a clear sign reversal, transitioning from strongly positive at negative angles to increasingly negative at positive angles, with a notable asymmetry where the negative roll magnitude at +37.8° to +63° exceeds the positive roll at corresponding negative angles. Higher motor levels consistently intensify roll magnitude, suggesting that power primarily scales control effectiveness rather than altering the direction of the response. Ultimately, the data highlights the specialized strengths of each actuator, with rotor tilt inducing a stronger influence on pitch control and elevon deflection offering superior roll moment control.
