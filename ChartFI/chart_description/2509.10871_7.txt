This UMAP visualization reduces high-dimensional chemical space into a two-dimensional representation, mapping three chemical datasets into a shared coordinate system where color indicates dataset membership and each point was re-dimensioned to represent their respective BitBIRCH cluster sizes. The most prominent structural distinction across variables is the variation in density and dispersion, which notably highlights the poor structural diversity within the BBBP case. BBBP compounds form a tight, high-density core centered near the middle of the map, suggesting that while these compounds dominate the common mainstream region with many small points, they fail to extend into broader chemical territories. In contrast, TRPA1 shows more spread and well-formed clusters, spreading broadly across both UMAP axes and occupying many peripheral regions. These large TRPA1 bubbles at the edges of the embedding stand out as potential chemotype hubs that are underrepresented in BBBP and only sporadically matched by BACE. BACE sits between these two extremes, exhibiting moderate spread with several separated islands, including noticeable concentrations in the upper portion of the map, implying subfamilies that are more dataset-specific than BBBP’s central mass but less extreme than TRPA1’s far-flung outliers. Overall, the visualization suggests that while there is a shared central overlap, TRPA1 contributes the strongest long-tail of distinct chemical neighborhoods, whereas BACE adds intermediate, semi-separated regions, and BBBP remains largely confined to the dense, shared core.
