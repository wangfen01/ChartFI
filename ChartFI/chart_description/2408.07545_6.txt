The plots show six combinations of density predictions under two simultaneously applied interventions for the Student data set, overlaying the chiSPN model estimate (orange curve) against ground-truth histograms for variables labeled Q, M, C, and T. Structurally, the top left two graphs capture the distributions after intervention on the cultural activities and test scores variables (C, T), while the top right two graphs capture the distributions after intervention on motivation and cultural activities (M, C). Across the panels, the chiSPN is able to faithfully capture the interventional distributions, which generally remain centered close to zero with broadly similar spreads. In terms of central tendency, the modes are generally matched well or offset slightly, indicating that the interventions do not dramatically shift the location of these variables. However, while distribution shapes generally match, they show increased errors as distributions flatten out; this is most evident where variables C and T display wider dispersion or heavier tails, causing the ground-truth histograms to appear flatter or more skewed relative to the model’s smoother bell shape. Furthermore, there are slight biases in the captured distributions, manifesting as a recurring anomaly in the tails where the model curve frequently assigns more probability to extreme values near ±1 than the ground truth shows, producing visible tail bumps that are not supported by the histograms.
