The heatmaps illustrate the localization performance of four SENTINEL variants—SENTINEL-NONE, SENTINEL-FGSM, SENTINEL-PGD, and SENTINEL-MIM—where the X-axis represents eight testing devices and the Y-axis corresponds to five different building floorplans. Each cell within the heatmaps indicates the average prediction error in meters across all reference points for a specific combination of test device and building floorplan, with darker shading signifying higher error levels. Across all variants, there is a notable increase in prediction errors when moving from Building 1 to Building 5, indicating that floorplan characteristics exert a more dominant influence on performance than individual device variations. SENTINEL-FGSM consistently exhibits the lowest prediction errors, maintaining a highly uniform distribution mostly between 2 and 6 meters and effectively reducing device-to-device spread. This is followed by SENTINEL-PGD, which shifts errors upward into the mid-to-high single digits, and SENTINEL-NONE, where errors are generally moderate but show significant clustering around 8 to 10 meters in Building 3. SENTINEL-MIM exhibits the highest prediction errors and the most dramatic degradation, particularly in Buildings 4 and 5 where errors jump sharply into the low-to-mid teens across nearly all devices. This creates a pronounced bimodal pattern that highlights a severe interaction between the MIM condition and specific environments, establishing SENTINEL-MIM as the least accurate variant while SENTINEL-FGSM remains the most stable and lowest-error condition across the board.
