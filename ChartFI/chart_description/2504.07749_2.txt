This heatmap shows the performance delta-scores for a set of Nordic-focused and general large language models across multiple-choice QA and sentence completion tasks, where higher values, indicated in blue, mean higher performance. The LMs generally perform better on the knowledge-based benchmarks NorOpenBookQA (NOBQA), NorCommonsenseQA (NCSQA), and NorTruthfulQA Multiple Choice (NTRQA MC), as opposed to NRK-QuizQA and the sentence completion task NorIdiom. Within the stronger areas, performance on NCSQA is broadly high for nearly every model, suggesting this task is comparatively solved and does not strongly differentiate the models, though AI-Sweden/Llama-3-8B-IT stands out with a peak score of 20. While NOBQA also trends positive, it shows more spread, with Viking-7B achieving a high score of 16 while NorMistral-11B is a notable outlier with a negative value. The NTRQA MC benchmark creates the most pronounced model ranking shifts, with several models scoring positively but others, such as Meta/Llama-3-8B-IT, swinging to a strongly negative score. In contrast, performance on NRK-Quiz-QA is generally weaker, with most models scoring in negative territory. The most significant pattern, however, is the systematic performance degradation on the NorIdiom (EM) task, where many models that appear strong on the accuracy-based columns collapse to large negative values, such as -22 for NorMistral-7B-scratch and AI-Sweden/Llama-3-8B. This sharp drop implies that idiomatic understanding is a distinct capability not captured by the other benchmarks. Interestingly, instruction-tuned models (those with an '-IT' suffix) demonstrate a notable robustness on the NorIdiom task, where several score a neutral 0 in stark contrast to the deeply negative scores of many other models, suggesting instruction-tuning may mitigate this specific weakness.
