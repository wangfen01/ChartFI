This radar chart evaluates the performance of nine vision-language models across eleven distinct dimensions of bias and discrimination, where higher values represent the accuracy of each category at a medium level. The two Qwen2-VL variants, specifically Qwen2-VL-7b and Qwen2-VL-72b-AWQ, consistently form the outermost profiles with the highest detection accuracy across nearly every category, while the InstructBLIP family and smaller LLaVA variants cluster closer to the center, indicating generally lower sensitivity. The evaluated LMMs demonstrate high accuracy in categories such as Sexism and Neurological Discrimination, yet accuracy is lower in categories like Classism and Subcultural Discrimination, suggesting that the detection difficulty across different categories varies and certain categories require further attention. This discrepancy may stem from the relative scarcity of toxic data in these latter categories, leading to reduced sensitivity in the models for detecting such content. While mid-tier models like LLaVA-1.5-13B and LLaVA-NeXT-LLaMA3-8B show moderate performance with noticeable peaks in areas like homophobia, a specific anomaly appears in BLIP2-FlanT5-XXL, which exhibits a sharper spike in nationalism compared to its otherwise moderate profile. Overall, the data suggests that while models are most adept at identifying gender- and race-related prejudices, the lower-performing clusters remain relatively flat, implying a consistent need for more robust training data to improve detection across less prevalent discrimination types.
