The visualization compares three reconstruction-related metrics across five datasets—CelebA, CIFAR-10, dSprites, FashionMNIST, and MNIST—for three model families: β-VAE, INFO, and WAE. The data demonstrates how training affects landscape norms differentially, depending on model and dataset choices, and specifically highlights how training affects variability in latent-space models. While the distributions for the summed reconstruction norm cluster tightly within each model–dataset combination, there are clear shifts in level and variability by model type; β-VAE and WAE tend to sit in a similar band, whereas INFO shows a modest upward shift and noticeably wider spread. This disparity becomes most pronounced in the summed gradient-related norm, where INFO exhibits the largest dispersion and several extreme values, making it possible to detect outliers that deviate significantly from the core distribution. Conversely, β-VAE and WAE remain comparatively compact in this metric. The PD metric mirrors this behavior, as INFO generally yields higher and more variable PD with occasional spikes while the other models remain stable. Consequently, the data suggests that the main anomaly across the comparison is the substantial jump in variability and outlier frequency under the INFO framework rather than a uniform shift across all models.
