The t-SNE analysis of the CLIP embedding vectors for generated and ground truth images across Diffusion Models, VQ-GAN, and MAE reveals distinct patterns in how unlearned representations align with their respective target distributions. In the Diffusion Models panel, the forget set (red squares) and retain set (blue squares) exhibit strong separation, with the forget set concentrated in the lower-left and the retain set in the upper-right; notably, after unlearning, the CLIP embedding vector on the retain set is close to or overlapping with the ground truth images (orange x's), while most of the generated forget images diverge far from the ground truth (green x's), indicating successful selective removal. In contrast, the VQ-GAN panel shows a significant distributional shift where the unlearned forget points collapse into a tighter, more centralized cluster in the lower middle, and most of the generated images on the forget set diverge far from the ground truth, while the unlearned retain points appear less consistently co-located with their broadly spread ground truth. The MAE panel displays the least structural clarity, as both forget and retain ground-truth points are widely dispersed and intermingled, and the unlearned points mirror this mixing with substantial overlap between regions; although the generated retain samples are close to or overlapping with the ground truth in some areas, the lack of clear clustering boundaries and the fact that most of generated forget images diverge far from the ground truth suggests a noisier unlearning outcome with reduced class separability compared to the cleaner structure observed in the diffusion model.
