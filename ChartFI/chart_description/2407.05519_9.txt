The comparison between software tool categories in marketplace products and research papers reveals a distinct divergence between practitioner focus and academic interest, characterized by eight categories exhibiting more than a 10 percentage point difference between the two domains. Marketplace products concentrate heavily on operational and delivery-oriented capabilities, with Utilities, Reporting, and Publishing showing very high product shares relative to papers; specifically, Utilities and Reporting stand out as the most product-dominant categories, while Deployment and API management also lean strongly toward commercial application. Conversely, research papers disproportionately focus on developer workflow and quality assurance, with the categories of Code quality (19.09 pp), AI assisted (17.17 pp), Testing (14.21 pp), and Support (13.7 pp), along with 17 other categories, exhibiting a higher percentage of research papers compared to marketplace products. While most categories show a clear researcher-practitioner split, a subset including Mobile, Time tracking, API Management, Desktop tools, Mobile CI, and Dependency management shows the least percentage point difference, ranging from 0.2 pp to 0.98 pp, indicating a rare alignment between market availability and academic study. Notably, three categories present on the GitHub Marketplace—Game CI, Backup Utilities, and Content Attachments API—showed a complete absence of corresponding research papers, highlighting specific areas of practitioner activity that remain entirely unaddressed by the current systematic mapping of academic literature. This distribution suggests that while commercial offerings emphasize shipping and integrating systems, research remains deeply invested in process, verification, and emerging AI-assisted practices.
