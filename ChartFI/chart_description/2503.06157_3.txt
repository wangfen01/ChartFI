This heatmap illustrates the correlation of cognitive abilities across 16 task metrics categorized into Recall, Perception, Reasoning, and Navigation, where a higher value indicates more similar performance on the two tasks and suggests that the embodied cognitive abilities needed for these tasks are similar. The data reveal that most relationships are positive, particularly within the Recall cluster, indicating that stronger performance in one capability tends to coincide with proficiency in others. The Recall variables (T1–T5) exhibit the tightest alignment, with consistently high correlations across trajectory captioning, sequence recall, object recall, scene recall, and start/end position, suggesting these measures capture a shared memory and scene-understanding factor. Causal reasoning (T11) shows some of the highest associations with the Recall block, implying that better recall is closely linked to stronger causal inference. In contrast, Perception variables are more varied; while landmark position (T8) and cognitive map (T10) track well with the Recall core, proximity (T6) and duration (T7) exhibit weaker, near-zero relationships with many other tasks, indicating they may reflect distinct underlying processes. Notable anomalies appear in counterfactual reasoning (T12) and association (T13), which show clear negative correlations with several Recall and Navigation measures. Navigation outcomes, including progress evaluation (T14), high-level planning (T15), and action generation (T16), are generally positively related to the core cluster, with action generation and progress evaluation showing stronger alignment than high-level planning, suggesting that execution-oriented navigation benefits more directly from shared recall and reasoning strengths than abstract planning does.
