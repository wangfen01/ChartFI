The chart depicts the GPU memory footprints during 100 epochs of privately training a CNN on CIFAR10 with FS-RDP and three of the most common DP accountants: RDP, f-DP, and PRV. As shown, the memory usage of FS-RDP remains constant at 14,826 MB, demonstrating a predictable and steady consumption profile independent of training dynamics. In sharp contrast, similar memory usage is observed for other Opacus accountants that use the Poisson subsampling mechanism (i.e., f-DP and PRV), which display highly volatile behavior characterized by frequent step-like jumps and drops rather than gradual drift. Specifically, the memory usage varies from 7,984 MB to 23,280 MB for Poisson subsampled RDP in Opacus, reaching peak demands that are almost twice as large as FS-RDP. The most notable instability occurs in the mid-training window around epochs 45–65, where all three Opacus methods exhibit clustered high-usage episodes, including multiple peaks near the top of the chart, before settling more often into a lower band after about epoch 70. While PRV shows an additional late spike around the low 80s and f-DP appears particularly bursty with pronounced peaks earlier in the run, the overall pattern highlights that FS-RDP provides stable memory behavior, whereas the Opacus methods introduce substantial variability and occasional near-capacity surges that could increase the risk of out-of-memory events.
