This visualization presents the t-SNE embeddings of channel and prototype embeddings within the ETTh1 and ETTh2 datasets, where each point represents a channel within a sample and varying colors indicate different channels. In the ETTh1 dataset, the embedding separates into two clearly distinct lobes that reveal a stratified structure: we discern a pronounced clustering of channels 0, 2, and 4 in the upper-left region, suggesting that they may be capturing related or redundant information within the dataset, while concurrently, channels 1, 3, 5, and 6 coalesce into another cluster in the lower-right. Prototypes, marked by red stars, are positioned within these dense regions to represent the dominant features of each group. The inset heatmap for ETTh1 reinforces this separation with sharp contrasts and strong within-channel similarity that match the clear split in the embedding. In contrast, the ETTh2 dataset maintains this two-lobe structure, but Channel mixing is more prevalent in ETTh2, implying reduced separability among channels compared to the distinct groupings in ETTh1, a trend supported by the ETTh2 heatmap, which displays slightly more uniform off-diagonal values consistent with greater cross-channel alignment. A mild anomaly visible in both panels is the presence of a few points bridging the gap between lobes, likely representing transitional behaviors or mixed-channel patterns rather than a third stable cluster.
