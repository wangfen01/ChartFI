The visualization of the confusion matrices across epochs 50, 250, and 450 illustrates that after applying GV-SM, the model can utilize the open-set data set with higher pseudo-labeling accuracy on the seen classes. This progression marks a clear shift from early-stage uncertainty toward stronger class separation, particularly for airplane and automobile, which remain consistently strong with airplane hovering in the low-to-mid 0.60s and automobile stable around 0.89–0.90. The most notable improvements occur for bird, which rises from 0.45 to roughly 0.65, and cat, which jumps from a low 0.14 to roughly 0.30–0.31, although cat samples are frequently misclassified as unseen. Deer also strengthens steadily from 0.74 to 0.88, becoming one of the top-performing classes; however, this strengthening coincides with a specific anomaly where the mismatching from unseen classes to the seen class deer slightly increases from 0.19 to 0.23. Conversely, the dog category represents the main regression, improving slightly at epoch 250 before dropping to 0.46 at epoch 450, a decline driven by a pronounced increase in dog samples being mapped to the unseen class, which rises to 0.49. While the unseen class remains highly self-consistent around 0.76, it acts as a persistent sink for ambiguous animals, suggesting that while GV-SM improves separability for most categories, it induces a tradeoff involving increased leakage into the unseen bucket for specific classes like dog and slight boundary confusion regarding deer.
