The joint distribution of initial PSNR and correction PSNR illustrates the performance of this method across one aligned view, Shift-0, and three misaligned views, Shift-4, Shift-6, and Shift-8, under a selected sequence. Across all panels, the relationship remains broadly consistent, with most observations clustering at low-to-mid initial PSNR values while correction PSNR concentrates in the low-to-mid 30 dB range, indicating that the correction stage pulls results toward a relatively stable quality band regardless of the starting point. Relative to the Shift-0 baseline, the shifted conditions show only subtle redistribution rather than a clear change in central tendency, as the densest region stays anchored around similar initial and correction PSNR levels. The marginal density plots on the right further demonstrate that the corrected PSNR spread and peak location are largely preserved across shifts, with no obvious drop in performance. Any differences appear only as small contour shape changes and minor variability in the frequency of particular initial-to-corrected combinations, rather than a systematic upward or downward shift. The absence of prominent anomalies, such as new secondary modes or heavy tails emerging under higher shifts, highlights the robustness of the dual-view setting to misalignment, as the correction process does not introduce notable degradation even as shift magnitudes increase.
