The visualization illustrates the performance of selected algorithms in comparison to the charging and discharging prices, presenting a dual-panel analysis of economic signals and resulting power flows. The top chart displays time-varying electricity rates where discharge prices consistently exceed charge prices, creating an arbitrage spread that drifts downward in the morning before rising sharply to a peak around 16:10. This late-afternoon spike constitutes the dominant pricing shift and the main signal for energy export. The bottom chart contrasts total power trajectories against a transfer limit, incorporating inflexible loads and solar generation that reduces net demand midday. A distinct disparity in control strategies is evident; utilizing uncontrolled charging (AFAP) results in higher transformer overloads, characterized by aggressive morning peaks that significantly breach the upper transfer limit rather than simply approaching it. Conversely, the MPC and optimal solutions efficiently utilize the power transformer limits to their maximum capacity, tracing the constraint boundary to optimize throughput without violation.
