The two panels display Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) plots of UMAP embeddings for Wikipedia and NormAd datasets, illustrating the density distributions of the data in the reduced-dimensional space. In the Wikipedia dataset, most languages collapse into a tight, highly overlapping core centered roughly between -12 and 0 on UMAP Dimension 1 and 7 to 12 on UMAP Dimension 2, indicating a relatively similar feature structure across languages. While minor deviations exist, such as secondary lobes extending toward the mid-teens on the first dimension and a downward tail in the second dimension for the Greek language, these remain connected to the central mass. On the NormAd dataset, the geometry shifts as clusters stretch diagonally and spread across a much wider range on both axes, with a pronounced vertical extension downward on UMAP Dimension 2 for the Korean and Bengali cultures reaching as low as -70. Despite these broader and more elongated contours, there is no distinct separation between cultures within a dataset, as the density distributions for various groups continue to show significant overlap. This lack of clear clustering suggests that semantic differences in the data are not the primary factor influencing downstream differences after training, as the representations for both Wikipedia and NormAd maintain a shared variance that prevents the emergence of isolated, culture-specific clusters.
