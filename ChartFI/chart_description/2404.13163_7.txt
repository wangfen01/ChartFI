The heatmap visualizes the coverage trends for various fields of study (2-digit CIP titles) across five time periods from 2000–2003 to 2016–2017, revealing a broad, systemwide decline where many high-coverage fields fade from the mid-50% range to the low-40s or high-30s. Despite this general erosion, fields such as Engineering and Social Sciences show a consistent coverage over 40% throughout the observed timeframe, with Engineering easing only slightly from the mid-50s to the mid-40s and Mathematics and Statistics remaining similarly robust. Other disciplines exhibit more volatility; Architecture acts as an outlier by rising into the low-50s during 2012–2015 before falling back to roughly 40%, while Computer and Information Sciences stabilizes with a slight uptick in the final period after earlier declines. On the other hand, several fields like Military Technologies and Natural Resources and Conservation have consistently zero coverage, a pattern shared by categories such as Construction Trades and Precision Production, which creates a clear split between consistently measured academic fields and unrecorded vocational areas. Among the lower-coverage areas that do report data, Mechanic and Repair Technologies demonstrates a distinct U-shaped pattern, dropping to single digits in the middle periods before rebounding sharply to over 26% by 2016–2017, a significant shift that contrasts with the near-zero flatline seen in Personal and Culinary Services.
