The chart displays the yearly, i.e. 52 weeks, moving average load (black), estimated and forecasted level for models GAM (dark green) and GAM Covid Oracle (light green), and in-sample residuals (grey) of the first stage GAM from May 24th, 2016 to May 18th, 2021 for France and Germany. In the period leading up to 2020, the data exhibits relative stability, with the moving average hovering modestly above zero and residuals fluctuating around the trend with occasional spikes but no sustained breaks. A distinct structural shift occurred in early 2020, where the load level suddenly and considerably decreased due to COVID-19 lockdowns. The GAM Covid Oracle model forecasts a level in the pre-COVID range, providing a baseline that highlights the magnitude of the deviation; specifically, there is a 5.6 GW and 3.3 GW decrease in load for France and Germany, respectively, during lockdowns. While the annual moving average reflects a smoothed, sustained depression in demand, the load levels in terms of first-stage GAM residuals during these lockdowns not only decreased sharply but also rebounded quickly, almost reaching pre-lockdown levels in France, after restrictions had been lifted. This comparison reveals that while both nations faced material declines, France experienced a significantly deeper drop compared to Germany, followed by a volatile but rapid recovery in the residual components once lockdown measures eased.
