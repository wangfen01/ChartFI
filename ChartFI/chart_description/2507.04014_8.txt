This heatmap compares model performance on Trap and Interpretation questions across three metrics: Non-Zero Score Count (scores of 2, 1, or -1, indicating an attempt at a culturally relevant response), Weighted Sum (aggregated score), and Positive Score Count (scores of 2 or 1). Columns represent each version, with color intensity standardized within each model to highlight relative strengths across conditions defined by language and prompt specificity. Overall, Interpretation consistently produces higher activity and stronger totals than Trap, with English+Specified tending to be the most productive condition, while Korean+Neutral is frequently weaker or more variable depending on the model. In non‑zero and positive counts, top models cluster at the high end across nearly all conditions, with Gemini 1.5 Pro and the Claude models generally showing the largest volumes, especially on Interpretation where counts often reach the highest cells. Two key trends emerge in Trap questions: a distinct lift in performance when moving from Neutral to Specified prompts—particularly in English where many models jump from modest counts in English+Neutral to much higher counts in English+Specified—and a pattern where Korean also benefits from specification but typically remains below English at the same specificity level. The weighted sum panel amplifies these differences, as leading models show very large positive totals on Interpretation under Specified prompts, whereas several smaller or older models show muted totals even when their non‑zero counts are moderate. A notable anomaly is the presence of negative weighted sums for some models in Trap and occasionally in Interpretation under Korean+Neutral, despite having non‑zero and even positive counts; this suggests a mix of scoring outcomes where negatives outweigh positives in aggregate. Ultimately, while some models exhibit relatively balanced non‑zero/positive counts, they diverge sharply in weighted sum, indicating that volume alone is not driving performance and that the strongest consistent shift is the step change from Neutral to Specified prompts on Interpretation, where both engagement and strength rise markedly for the top tier.
