These heatmaps compare eight instruction-tuned models across five steering variants—LCE, CoSMIC-LCE, Substring-ACE, CoSMIC-ACE, and ACE—evaluating Harmful Attack Success Rate (ASR) using Llama Guard 3 on 512 prompts from D_harmful^(test) and Induced Refusal Rate on 512 harmless ALPACA prompts via substring matching. Overall, most models exhibit high harmful attack success across several variants, while refusal behavior is often near-saturated under LCE and CoSMIC-LCE before becoming more mixed under Substring-ACE and CoSMIC-ACE, indicating that high refusal rates do not consistently translate into lower harmful success. On the Harmful ASR heatmap, the Llama and Qwen families generally remain elevated across LCE and CoSMIC variants with values frequently clustered in the 0.8s, though a notable shift occurs for Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct, where ASR drops to 0.57 under Substring-ACE and CoSMIC-ACE. Gemma models show more heterogeneity: gemma-7b-it displays a pronounced spike under Substring-ACE (0.93), while gemma-2-27b-it appears as an outlier with an ASR of 0.0 under CoSMIC-LCE and 0.22 under Substring-ACE; however, these results are qualified by the fact that LCE did not converge on Gemma-2-27B-IT and ACE was not tested on newer models due to the requirements of subjective manual evaluation. Regarding the Induced Refusal Rate, LCE and CoSMIC-LCE are frequently near-maximal (0.98–1.0) for Meta-Llama-3-8B and the Gemma 7B/9B models, whereas Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct declines from 0.73 under LCE to 0.30 under CoSMIC-ACE. A sharp discontinuity is visible for gemma-2-27b-it under CoSMIC-LCE, where the refusal rate is near zero (0.02) despite being 1.0 under Substring-ACE. Ultimately, the results are complicated to compare since each steering technique and direction selection method combination results in different steering results, making it difficult to fairly compare the tradeoff between model utility and compliance systematically, as high refusal-inducing setups often coexist with high Harmful ASR.
