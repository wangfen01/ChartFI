The visualization presents a comparative analysis of real and generated time-series data, showcasing the latent space representation on the left and the original series space on the right. The dataset comprises five real samples and four generated samples: the real set of data is represented by blue points, while a distinct real outlier is highlighted in red; correspondingly, generated examples around non-outliers are shown in green, and generated examples around the outlier appear in black. In the latent representation, the real samples form a tight cluster around Dimension 1 near −0.1, and some generated samples are correctly placed in the distribution of these four real samples, indicating strong alignment with the learned manifold. Conversely, the real outlier is displaced to the right, and two other generated samples are closer to the fifth real outlier, with the outlier-generated samples showing a clear separation from the main cluster. The circles in blue represent the neighborhood area of each latent point, calculated with the number of neighbors set to 2. While the neighborhoods around the clustered points are small, the area around the outlier is significantly larger, which explains the reported metrics: the precision does not detect the outlier and produces a perfect measure of 1, whereas the density metric overcomes the existence of an outlier and does not produce a perfect measure, yielding a value of 1.25 (where 1.25 != 1). This suggests the generator produces samples that look plausible but occupy overrepresented regions of the latent space. In the series space on the right, the real samples share a consistent oscillatory pattern, which the good generated samples (green) track closely, preserving peak timing and waveform shape. In contrast, the real outlier deviates with noticeable phase and amplitude differences, and the outlier-generated samples (black) resemble the outlier more than the typical real series, indicating the model reproduces and potentially amplifies anomalous dynamics.
