The six heatmaps illustrate the accuracy scores of different methods trained on row-indexed communities and tested on column-indexed communities, where the MGTAB dataset is partitioned into 10 sub-communities using the Louvain algorithm to serve as distinct data folds for assessing generalization capability. Each heatmap displays the performance of a specific model—RGCN, RGT, GraphMAE, SEBot, CBD, and BotHP (RGT)—by training or fine-tuning on the i-th fold and testing on the j-th fold, with the reported average accuracy across all off-diagonal elements serving as a primary metric for generalization. While overall averages are tightly clustered in the high 80s, BotHP (RGT) leads with the highest mean of 89.39, followed by CBD at 88.64, SEBot at 88.50, and GraphMAE at 88.27, while RGT and RGCN trail at 87.68 and 87.28, respectively. Across all models, the strongest results concentrate on the diagonal and near-diagonal cells, where training and testing on the same or closely related communities often yield performance in the mid-to-high 90s. A significant pattern emerges around test set index 4, which acts as a "hard" community where many methods experience pronounced performance drops into the 50s–70s across several training sets, creating a visible cool-colored vertical band that suggests a substantial domain shift or distribution mismatch. Conversely, test sets toward the right side of the heatmaps, specifically indices 7–9, are consistently easier to generalize to across all training sets, maintaining broadly high scores in the 90s with fewer collapses. Methodological differences are most apparent in robustness; BotHP (RGT), CBD, and SEBot maintain higher off-diagonal performance and exhibit fewer severe degradations in the difficult test regions, whereas RGCN and RGT show deeper troughs and greater variability, and GraphMAE occupies a middle ground by maintaining strong diagonal performance while remaining sensitive to the same dataset shifts.
