The two symmetric heatmaps illustrate the pairwise Jaccard similarity among seven membership inference attack (MIA) methods across two metrics, Coverage and Stability, calculated with six instances at a false positive rate (FPR) of 0.1 on the CIFAR-10 dataset. Each cell represents the average Jaccard similarity of four experiment runs, with values ranging from 0 to 1. Overall, both coverage and stability show low similarity between most attack pairs, with Jaccard similarity generally below 0.4 for coverage and 0.1 for stability, highlighting a significant MIA method disparity. In the Coverage matrix, the strongest alignment occurs between loss-cali and losstraj at 0.63, standing out as the dominant high-value cell, followed by a second tier of similarity where reference tracks closely with losstraj (0.50), loss-cali (0.47), and LIRA (0.45). Conversely, the LOSS method is consistently the most behaviorally distinct, reaching a maximum similarity of only 0.24 with Class-NN and dropping as low as 0.03–0.04 with the loss-based variants. The Stability matrix exhibits a stark compression toward near-zero values, with most pairs falling between 0.00 and 0.01. The only modest elevations in stability cluster around LIRA’s relationships with reference (0.18), loss-cali (0.16), and losstraj (0.16), as well as the losstraj-reference pair (0.15). This notably lower Jaccard similarity in stability compared to coverage underscores the significant disparities in consistently detected vulnerabilities across different MIAs. The resulting decoupling between the two metrics suggests that methods achieving similar coverage outcomes do not necessarily maintain consistent behavior across runs, as evidenced by the loss-cali and losstraj pair, which is highly aligned in coverage yet fails to show elevated stability.
