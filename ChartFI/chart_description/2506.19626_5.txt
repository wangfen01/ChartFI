This visualization evaluates multiple symbolic regression methods across three performance metrics—recovery rate, model complexity, and test R²—under increasing target noise levels ranging from 0 to 0.1. The unbiased search UDFS + Aug is on average more accurate, as it can recover more known ground truth expressions, and also more robust, recovering more ground truth expressions from noisy data than the state of the art. While UDFS without variable augmentation compares fairly well to the state of the art, UDFS + Aug performs even better by reducing the effective size of the search space. Recovery rate exhibits the strongest sensitivity to noise; although UDFS and UDFS + Aug lead with the highest recovery, performance degrades and the spread across runs widens as noise increases. The third-best regressor in terms of recovery, AIFeynman, gives significantly larger and worse-fitting models when it cannot recover the ground truth, showing a marked drop in R² at higher noise levels. In contrast, UDFS + Aug produces small models with an average R² test score that is close to the optimal score of 1.0, maintaining among the lowest complexity solutions across all noise levels. While a large group of regression models produces extremely well-fitting models with an average R² close to 1.0, these models are mostly approximations of the ground truth functions, as their recovery rate is significantly lower and the model complexity is quite high. This is exemplified by MRGP, which maintains a strong R² despite a dramatic complexity explosion at higher noise levels, reaching models orders of magnitude larger in a classic sign of overfitting. Ultimately, even in terms of standard model fitting performance, UDFS and UDFS + Aug place reasonably well among the other symbolic regression methods, distinguishing themselves by capturing stable underlying structures rather than merely fitting noise with increasingly elaborate expressions.
