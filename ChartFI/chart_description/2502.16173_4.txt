This hierarchical clustering dendrogram visualizes the relationships among the top 100 language models, utilizing sqeuclidean as the metric and median as the linkage method to define the structural connections. The vertical axis represents the Kullback-Leibler divergence in bits per byte (bpb), where the values correspond to the q-coordinates divided by sqrt{2N} to ensure accuracy. This axis uses the symmetric logarithmic scale (with a linear threshold of 250), meaning that lower merge heights indicate more similar output distributions while higher merge heights indicate stronger differences. The resulting tree structure reveals that most models form several tight subclusters that merge at relatively low KL levels, suggesting that many variants within the same family behave similarly. A notable pattern is that models labeled within the same family color tend to cluster together and merge early, implying consistent family-specific behavior, whereas the largest jumps in merge height occur when these family clusters connect, signaling the most significant distributional shifts between major model lineages. The presence of a few branches that remain isolated until higher KL levels suggests outlier models whose behavior diverges more strongly from the rest, and the "others" group appears more dispersed, consistent with heterogeneous architectures or training regimes that do not align tightly with a single dominant cluster.
