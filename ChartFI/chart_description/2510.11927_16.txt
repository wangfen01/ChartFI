The colored matrix compares the selected sketches‘ behavior patterns and the rationale for choosing them across 12 participants and eight topics, revealing the consistency between user intent and visual selection. In this design, the bottom triangle represents the behavior pattern demonstrated through the rationale, and the top triangle represents the assigned behavior pattern of the selected sketch during the primary study; single-colored cells indicate a match between the behavior pattern of the selected sketch and the rationale, while two-colored cells indicate a mismatch. Overall, Replicator and Trend Keeper dominate the landscape, with topics like Unemployment skewing strongly toward Replicator, whereas the Tourists dataset is distinct for a heavier presence of the De-noiser pattern. While most sketch selections (76/96) matched participants’ rationale, 20 instances showed mismatches, particularly in datasets like Chicago, Temperature, Flights, and Unemployment. The Temperature dataset had the most mismatches (8/12), marking a significant divergence where participants often provided rationale aligned with Replicator behavior but chose Trend Keeper sketches, indicating a conflict between their stated desire to preserve the signal and their visual preference for a smoothed trend. Additionally, two Gray triangles indicate instances where the participant's rationale did not align with predefined behavior patterns, further emphasizing that while consensus was high in areas like Unemployment, ambiguity in sketch definitions or topic cues led to notable inconsistencies in others.
