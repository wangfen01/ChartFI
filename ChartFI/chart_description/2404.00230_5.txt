The radar chart presents a comprehensive evaluation of multiple watermarking methods across two primary themes: Image Quality, captured by SSIM, NIQE, PIQE, and FID, and Robustness, measured by BitACC and TPR under constructive, reconstructive, and destructive attacks. This visualization serves to illustrate the trade-off between image quality and watermark robustness in existing methods, where competing approaches tend to specialize rather than perform holistically. For example, baselines such as TreeRing exhibit uneven profiles with clear weak spots; while they retain decent image quality, they drop sharply on constructive, reconstructive, and destructive attack metrics, creating pronounced inward dents on the robustness axes. In contrast, the proposed LW significantly reduces the contradiction between the two, achieving the best quality–robustness balance. The three LW variants form the outer envelope on nearly every axis, maintaining high SSIM and favorable NIQE/PIQE scores while simultaneously demonstrating the highest and most balanced robustness scores across all attack types. Among these settings, the 64-step variant appears marginally strongest and most uniform, with the 56 and 48 variants close behind, indicating that the method provides consistent performance gains without the dramatic trade-offs observed in other techniques.
