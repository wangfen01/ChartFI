The figure presents spatial heatmaps visualizing the entropy of linear and angular velocity directions of the RRT and greedy datasets for the IiwaBimanual (IB) task and the AllegroHand (AH) task. Across these panels, warmer colors signify higher directional diversity, while cooler colors indicate more consistent, concentrated directions, and white indicates that there is no data. In the IB task, which plots the object velocity direction entropy, both Contact RRT and Greedy Search show broadly elevated linear-direction entropy across the central workspace; however, the RRT-based planner has higher entropy in most of the positions than the greedy planner. This trend is particularly pronounced in the angular-direction entropy for IB, where Contact RRT maintains a relatively wide band of mid-to-high entropy, whereas Greedy Search exhibits a clearer shift toward lower entropy over large areas. Similarly, in the AH condition, Contact RRT produces a compact region of high linear-direction entropy centered in the middle of the plotted area, whereas Contact PRM shows markedly lower linear-direction entropy overall, implying more constrained or repeatable linear direction choices. For angular directions in AH, Contact RRT again shows higher and more widespread entropy than PRM, whose angular entropy appears more blocky and bounded. Overall, having a more spread-out distribution, the RRT-based planner exhibits consistently higher entropy than the alternative planners, suggesting greater exploration and a broader set of feasible direction outcomes compared to the more regularized or sparse solutions observed in the Greedy and PRM results.
