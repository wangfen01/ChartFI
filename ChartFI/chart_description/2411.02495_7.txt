The figure displays classifier weight distributions for each network, comparing normalized weights across two settings: the gen-level weights in the left panel and the joint distribution weights in the right panel. For all networks, we see a dominant peak around a weight of 1, indicating that most mass is concentrated where the generative model closely matches the target. However, the distributions reveal distinct tail behaviors that correspond to specific corrections in the phase space; the tail towards lower weights indicates events that should not be there and which the classifier weight tries to remove, while the right tail marks events and phase space regions underpopulated by the generative unfolding network. In the generated distribution weights, C-SB shows the sharpest peak, indicating strong concentration, while SB is broader with noticeable mass across sub-unit weights. A clear separation in method performance becomes evident in the joint distribution weights. While for the conditional CFM and the conditional DM-networks (C-DiDi and C-SB) the difference to the left panel is marginal, preserving their peaked structure and stability, the unconditional DM-networks (SB and DiDi) show little structure and large overflow bins. These unconditional methods exhibit a flattened density with significant spikes at the distribution edges, contrasting with the consistent, lighter-tailed behavior of the conditional approaches which appear to drop off faster as weights increase.
