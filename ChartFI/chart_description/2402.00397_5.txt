The provided bar charts present a comprehensive evaluation of five spatiotemporal forecasting models—STFGNN, DSTAGNN, FOGS, DCRNN, and GWN—comparing their performance under the Reptile meta-learning approach versus the Multi-Scale Traffic Pattern Bank (MTPB) framework. Performance is measured via RMSE, MAE, and MAPE across four datasets (PEMS-BAY, METR-LA, Chengdu, and Shenzhen) at forecasting horizons of 10, 60, 120, and 180 minutes. A fundamental trend across all settings is that error metrics rise steadily with the forecast horizon, with the steepest degradation typically occurring between 10 and 60 minutes; dataset complexity also plays a significant role, as Shenzhen remains the lowest-error setting. Crucially, the visual evidence illustrates the efficacy of the multi-scale traffic pattern bank in enhancing the performance of STmodels, as the MTPB variants (represented by darker bars) consistently achieve lower errors than their Reptile counterparts. When integrated into the MTPB framework, all STmodels exhibit remarkably enhanced performance, and this performance enhancement is more pronounced for the mid-term and long-term future steps, effectively mitigating the sharp error increases usually seen at 120 and 180 minutes. Among the specific architectures, simple STmodels such as Graph Wavenet (GWN) have shown more significant performance improvement, positioning them as the most robust performers at longer horizons. Notably, simpler STmodels exhibit greater flexibility, and the metaknowledge readily adapts to these models with ease, whereas the separation between models is less distinct at shorter intervals. The consistent alignment between RMSE, MAE, and MAPE rankings implies that these improvements reflect broadly better point forecasting, highlighting that while architectural differences matter, the integration of the MTPB framework is the dominant factor in stabilizing long-term predictions.
