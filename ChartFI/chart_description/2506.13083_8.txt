This density map visualizes the standard deviation of the final output class probability distribution of GCN and EFGNN models at 2, 16, and 32 layers across five different datasets. The results on all datasets demonstrate that the standard deviation of 2-layer GCNs is comparable to that of EFGNN, with both models showing distributions in the higher range, indicating relatively confident predictions. However, with the number of layers increasing, a stark contrast emerges as the standard deviation of the 16-layer and 32-layer GCNs is positioned at the far left in the graph, which suggests that deep GCN models cannot make prediction with sufficient confidence. This is most evident on the Cora and Citeseer datasets, where GCN(16) and GCN(32) exhibit an extreme density spike near a standard deviation of zero. In sharp contrast, EFGNN models of all depths consistently produce sharp, narrow peaks at high standard deviation values, indicating stable and confident predictions across datasets; for instance, on Cora and Citeseer, EFGNN peaks are tightly clustered around 0.38–0.42, and on Pubmed, they are concentrated even higher, near 0.55–0.60. While the degradation in deep GCNs is extreme on Cora and Citeseer, on datasets like Computers and Photo it manifests as a broader, often bimodal distribution with a significant portion of its mass shifted towards lower values, such as the secondary hump around 0.2 for GCN(32), further highlighting a failure to maintain prediction confidence that is not observed in the consistently high-performing EFGNN models.
