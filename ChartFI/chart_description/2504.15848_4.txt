These charts provide a comprehensive assessment of sentiment intensity for SR and IR in both ground truth data and Chimera-generated content across three distinct Twitter datasets. In the ground-truth data, the sentiment intensity of IR demonstrates a noticeable bias toward positive values, consistently skewed with a pronounced spike near the upper bound of 1.0, while SR content concentrates much more tightly around a neutral score, forming a tall peak near 0 with relatively thin tails. Although this general separation is consistent, the sentiment intensity distributions of Twitter-2015 and Twitter-2017 reveal distinct patterns, with the polarization between positive IR and neutral SR being most extreme in Twitter-2017, where the IR spike is the dominant mass. The Political Twitter dataset stands out with a more multimodal structure, where SR shows a clear negative mode around -0.8 in addition to its neutral peak, and IR, while still positive-heavy, displays more mid-range density. In the Chimera-generated content, the model largely preserves IR's tendency toward very positive scores, but the SR distributions shift noticeably away from the ground-truth's neutral concentration. For Twitter-2015 and Twitter-2017, Chimera-generated SR becomes more dispersed and slightly positive-leaning, with a reduced zero-centered peak. For the Political Twitter dataset, Chimera's SR retains its negative and neutral modes but appears more structured, while its IR becomes more centered in the mildly positive region rather than being as heavily concentrated at the extreme positive end, indicating the most significant shift introduced by the generator is the weakening of SR’s tight neutral peak in favor of broader, often more positive sentiment.
