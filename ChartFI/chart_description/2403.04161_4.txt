The heatmap displays the Spearman coefficients of all metrics on one task with one given search space, illustrating how a set of methods performs across multiple benchmarks including TNB101, NB201, and NB301. The results clearly demonstrate the exceptional predictive capability of SWAP-Scores across diverse types of search spaces and tasks, as both SWAP-Scores notably outperform 15 other metrics in the majority of the evaluations. One interesting observation is the significant enhancement in SWAP-Score’s performance when regularization is applied, noted as reg_swap, which dominates almost everywhere. This improvement is particularly evident in cell-based search spaces, including NAS-Bench-101, NAS-Bench-201, NASBench-301, and TransNAS-Bench-101-Micro; for instance, on NB101-CF10, reg_swap rises to 0.75 compared to swap's 0.47, and on NB201-CF100, reg_swap peaks at 0.90 while swap remains at 0.81. Conversely, regularization does not appear to impact the correlation results in stack-based space, TransNAS-Bench-101-Macro, where both variants yield identical high scores, such as 0.86 on the Object task and 0.83 on the Scene task. A second tier including nwot and flops is also consistently strong, particularly on the TNB101 macro tasks where they reach the highest values in that block, though they generally remain below the swap variants on other benchmarks. In contrast, methods such as plain, grasp, fisher, grad_norm, and snip show frequent negative correlations on the TNB101 macro benchmarks, with grasp exhibiting one of the sharpest drops to -0.64 around the TNB101 MACRO-OBJECT column, and fisher being notably negative early before turning positive and improving substantially on the NB201 tasks. Ultimately, while performance becomes broadly more positive and less volatile as the chart moves into the NB201 columns, the swap-based methods distinguish themselves by avoiding the instability seen in other pruning or gradient-based scores, maintaining high, steadily improving performance across nearly the entire benchmark set.
