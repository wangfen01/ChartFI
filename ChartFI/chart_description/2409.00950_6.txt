This chart illustrates the auto-correlation sequence of the Fourier transforms of different windows, comparing normalized peak values across samples for traditional windows, a near-optimal (AO) window, and the BS-W-MUSIC method at three SNR levels. All methods concentrate their maximum response around the central region near sample 512, converging to a dominant peak close to 1, yet they diverge sharply in their off-peak behavior. Among the traditional options, the Hamming and triangle windows suppress sidelobes aggressively, with the Hamming window generally achieving the lowest off-peak levels in the 10^-4 to 10^-3 range, whereas the rectangular window exhibits pronounced oscillatory sidelobes that frequently rise into the 10^-2 to 10^-1 band. Distinct from these conventional profiles, the width of the mainlobe of the AO window is invariably lower than that of all the traditional windows, offering superior resolution. However, while both the mainlobe and the sidelobes associated with the AO window are remarkably sharp, the sidelobes are more prominent compared with those of the BS-W-MUSIC and conventional windows, exhibiting high-frequency oscillations that indicate strong leakage. In contrast, the BS-W-MUSIC curves match the sharpness of the narrow central spike but transition abruptly to flat, constant floors rather than oscillating; these floors shift upward as noise increases, rising from approximately 10^-4 at SNR=10 to 10^-2 at SNR=-10, highlighting that while BS-W-MUSIC maintains excellent localization, its dynamic range is strictly SNR-limited unlike the fixed geometric leakage of the window functions.
