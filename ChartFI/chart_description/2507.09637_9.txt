This transition heatmap illustrates the transition probabilities between themes during code review, where darker cells indicate higher probabilities of moving from the row state to the column state. The transition rates are normalized to 1 over the outgoing edges, while the sum over the incoming edges can be higher or lower; for example, for less frequent themes such as end review, which occurs at most once for every review, the sum of the incoming transition rates is much lower than 1. Visually, three denser clusters appear: one in the top left quadrant between the orientational themes, one in the bottom right quadrant with the analytical themes, and one less dense in the top right quadrant showing the transition from orientational to analytical themes. In the early orientational phase, behavior is strongly structured, as starting a review most often leads to selecting a review task at 0.59, which then splits primarily into understanding rationale at 0.21 and understanding context at 0.12. There is notable self-reinforcement within this quadrant, especially understanding rationale transitioning to itself at 0.51 and understanding context to itself at 0.31, indicating sustained time spent consolidating mental models rather than rapidly switching topics. A clear midstream shift appears when reviewers enter the analytical cluster in the bottom right, where understanding implementation most often loops back to itself at 0.42 and frequently moves into assessing implementation at 0.31, while assessing implementation also reinforces itself at 0.44. Selecting next action acts as a hub, most commonly feeding back into itself at 0.32 and moving into assessing implementation at 0.23, consistent with iterative cycles of inspection and evaluation. Assessing change shows a more distributed pattern, with meaningful transitions to understanding implementation at 0.30. Event states stand out as anomalies; pause review transitions to start review at 0.67 and to resume review at 0.33, while end review transitions back to start review at 0.94. Resume review is unusually concentrated, splitting evenly into selecting a review task, selecting next action, and assessing implementation at 0.33 each. Overall, the dominant pattern is a two-stage workflow that begins with sustained rationale and context building and then shifts into iterative implementation understanding and assessment loops.
