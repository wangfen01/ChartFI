This visualization illustrates the contribution of each lead for feature reweighting across all 12-lead features for different populations, specifically UKB-HYP and UKB-MI, by comparing pairwise relationships among ECG leads for low-risk versus high-risk groups. In both cohorts, the low- and high-risk matrices share a similar overall structure, suggesting the underlying inter-lead correlation pattern is largely preserved regardless of risk status. Within this shared framework, the three augmented leads (aVR, aVL, aVF) contributed the least, appearing as blue regions, while the network typically pays more attention to chest lead I and precordial leads (V2-V6), which are characterized by red regions. Despite these broad similarities, significant variations distinguish the risk categories; upon comparing the attention patterns derived from high-risk groups to those from low-risk groups, as illustrated in the column titled ‘Difference’, there are observed elevated activation values within the high-risk groups. These changes appear as concentrated hotspots where adjacent lead pairs change together, consistent with physiologically related leads moving in tandem, rather than as widespread anomalies or systematic scaling issues. While UKB-HYP displays deviations clustered around specific lead combinations, UKB-MI shows a comparable magnitude of change but with a slightly more patchy, heterogeneous difference pattern, implying that myocardial infarction risk is associated with more lead-specific alterations in inter-lead coupling.
