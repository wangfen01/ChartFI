These four heatmaps visualize the zero-shot cross-lingual performance of BLOOM checkpoints of size 560m and 1b1 on POS tagging and XNLI tasks, mapping target languages against global training steps in thousands. While there is an initial upward trend in scores from the earliest checkpoints, the models generally display weak zero-shot transfer abilities since their performance on target languages is mostly random, particularly among lower-resource or distant languages like Urdu and Hindi, which remain volatile compared to Romance languages. The most significant pattern observed is that an unexpected drop of cross-lingual alignment may appear during the training process, manifesting as a sharp collapse at step 600k for the 560m model and a distinct dip at 400k for the 1b1 model. Crucially, this instability is not restricted to cross-lingual transfer; the models’ performance also worsens in-language, showing a performance degradation in the source language English, where the score falls precipitously during these intervals—for example, dropping from 81.9 to 43.3 in the 1b1 XNLI chart. This systemic collapse suggests that multilingual LMs can pass through highly suboptimal regions in the loss landscape. However, the aftermath of this instability varies by model size: while the 560m model ends the observed sequence in this degraded state, the 1b1 model recovers strongly by steps 500k and 600k, indicating that the larger model can navigate out of these suboptimal regions while the smaller model fails to do so within the same timeframe.
