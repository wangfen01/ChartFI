These radar plots illustrate the performance of the proposed PNVC codec against nine other conventional and neural video codecs, evaluating coding efficiency via BD-rate measured by PSNR and MS-SSIM on UVG and MCL-JVC datasets relative to an HM 18.0 (LD) baseline, alongside decoding speeds (FPS) and coding latency. In the Conventional setting, the learned baselines show a clear trade-off where VTM variants sit closer to the BD-rate “best” region but at the cost of much lower decoding speed, while HM variants decode faster but with weaker rate–distortion performance. In contrast, the PNVC panel stands out as the most consistently rounded profile, demonstrating excellent performance in all aspects. Specifically, on both UVG and MCL, the proposed model evidently outperforms VTM (LD) and HiNeRV in BD-rate, measured in both PSNR and MS-SSIM. Furthermore, the proposed method is associated with much lower encoding latency compared to existing INR-based videos and a faster decoding speed over autoencoder-based neural video coding models. By maintaining low latency and strong BD-rates while keeping decoding FPS near the top of the scale, it is evident that the proposed PNVC yields the most balanced and best overall rate-distortion-complexity trade-off, representing a meaningful shift of the Pareto frontier rather than a simple rebalancing.
