The visualization presents the original data, the TSINR reconstructed data, and the corresponding anomaly score across five distinct datasets (SMD, PSM, MSL, SMAP, UCR), with shaded regions marking the labeled anomaly windows. A consistent pattern emerges where the smooth normal points are well-fitted by the reconstruction model, maintaining stability prior to the anomaly window; conversely, the discontinuous abnormal points are not well-fitted, creating a divergence between the original and reconstructed series. Consequently, in the anomaly area, anomaly scores significantly increase, aligning with the onset of irregular behavior or regime changes. Specifically, SMD displays a sudden spike where the original data jumps sharply while the reconstruction remains lower, resulting in a large error gap and the highest anomaly-score peak. PSM exhibits a pronounced step drop at the anomaly onset where the reconstruction lags, yielding a sustained elevated anomaly score that gradually tapers. MSL rises to a peak before transitioning into a downward shift, causing the anomaly score to spike strongly at the boundary before declining. SMAP is characterized by intermittent, high-amplitude oscillations that the reconstruction smooths, leading to multiple anomaly-score bursts rather than a single dominant peak. Finally, the UCR dataset features a gradual upward trend that the reconstruction tracks more closely than the abrupt shifts in other datasets, resulting in small and highly fluctuating values of anomaly scores that culminate in a late spike near the end of the horizon where the divergence becomes most pronounced.
