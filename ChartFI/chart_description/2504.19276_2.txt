These radar charts compare the performance of five approaches—Anyprefer, Self Rewarding, Meta Rewarding, VLFeedback, and the original Target Model—across four key capability areas, with larger filled areas indicating stronger performance. Overall, Anyprefer demonstrates significant improvements across various applications, consistently forming the outer envelope in most charts to suggest the most robust and well-rounded gains, while the Target Model generally has the smallest footprint and serves as a clear baseline. In Natural Language Generation, Anyprefer achieves up to a 10.92% increase in accuracy on the GSM8K and ARC datasets compared to the best baseline, expanding markedly on these axes while other methods cluster closer to the baseline. In the domain of Natural VL Understanding, performance differences widen, and Anyprefer again leads, outperforming the self-rewarding approach. Here, VLFeedback shows a noticeable spike on GQA but does not sustain this advantage, creating a jagged profile that signals specialization, whereas Self Rewarding and Meta Rewarding deliver steadier, moderate gains. This pattern of dominance is most pronounced in Medical Image Analysis, where Anyprefer delivers the best performance with a substantially higher capability across nearly all tasks, while the other methods bunch together at a lower band, contracting sharply on the IU sub-tasks. Finally, in Visuo-Motor Control, Anyprefer again leads on both grasp and success metrics, with observed success rate increases of up to 14.5% across various tasks; the largest gaps appear on success rates rather than grasp quality, suggesting it improves end-to-end execution reliability more than intermediate control, a key area where the Target Model underperforms.
