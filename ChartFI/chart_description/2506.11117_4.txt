The cognitive-skill composition of multiple QA and retrieval benchmarks is evaluated using a six-level taxonomy ranging from C1 (Remembering) through C6 (Creating), with each dataset represented as a 100% stacked bar. In the BeIR-QA group, MSMARCO and NQ are heavily concentrated in C1 and C2, while HotpotQA’s 97.4% distribution across C1–C3 reveals its inherent limitations in supporting analytical reasoning despite its multi-hop design, as it remains overwhelmingly dominated by Remembering at 92.9%. FiQA-2018 stands out in this group by exhibiting a more balanced cognitive distribution, spreading mass across Remembering (21.3%), Understanding (16.7%), and Analyzing (16.8%), with a notably large Evaluating component at 29.2%. The BIRCO group exhibits highly polarized cognitive distributions; for instance, WhatsThatBook consists almost entirely of basic tasks in C1–C2 with Remembering at 93.0%, whereas ArguAna is skewed towards higher-level reasoning with 81.0% of its tasks in C5 (Evaluating). Other BIRCO datasets cluster around mid-level tasks with minimal variation, such as Clinical-Trial and RELIC, which are primarily centered on C4 (Analyzing) at 86.0% and 75.0% respectively, while DORIS-MAE focuses overwhelmingly on high-level creative tasks, with C6 (Creating) reaching 93.3%. Within the Scientific and Reasoning-intensive group, LitSearch tends to skew towards foundational recall (C1) at 61.8%, while DataFinder prioritizes creative tasks with 91.1% in C6. In contrast, BRIGHT presents a more balanced distribution of questions, combining Applying (36.8%) with substantial Analyzing (21.8%) and Evaluating (15.6%). The ScIRGen-Geo framework achieves the most comprehensive cognitive coverage through three key design innovations: progressive scaffolding with 40.0% foundational questions (C1-C2) enabling knowledge anchoring, a targeted 34.8% emphasis on C4-level analytical reasoning—which is significantly higher than BEIR’s maximum of 3.4%—and 6.0% dedicated to C6 creative synthesis. This balanced approach allows ScIRGen-Geo to reach a diversity score of 0.91, surpassing the more specialized or recall-dominant profiles of BEIR (0.62 ± 0.11) and BIRCO (0.38 ± 0.29).
