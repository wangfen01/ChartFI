The visualization presents an estimated PDF of Ranks alongside node number versus molecule count histograms of filtered Enzyme and Protein datasets to analyze embedding performance and structural heterogeneity. The left panel, where histograms are normalized to densities for better viewability, demonstrates that all graphs in the molecular benchmarks were faithfully embedded in <= 10 latent dimensions. The rank distributions separate into two distinct groups: the small-molecule datasets, QM-9 and Zinc, cluster at lower ranks, while the biological datasets, Enzymes and Proteins, shift toward higher ranks. Zinc exhibits the tightest clustering with sharp peaks around ranks 4 and 5, indicating consistently low ranks with minimal spread, whereas QM-9, though also centered in the low-rank region, is broader and slightly left-shifted. In contrast, Enzymes display a rightward shift with a concentration around ranks 6 to 7, and Proteins appear as the most dispersed distribution, extending into the highest ranks with a heavier upper tail. This shift in rank corresponds to the structural complexity observed in the right-hand panels; these node number versus molecule count histograms show that while both biological datasets are strongly right-skewed, Proteins exhibits a more extreme tail reaching significantly higher node counts than Enzymes. This aligns with the broader, higher-rank density observed for Proteins, suggesting that greater structural heterogeneity and the presence of high-value outliers necessitate higher-dimensional embeddings.
