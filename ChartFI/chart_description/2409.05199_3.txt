This precision-coverage scatterplot for the Yelp dataset compares automatically extracted n-grams, represented by grey points, and prompt-based rules, represented by red points, including all rules with precision higher than 0.5 and coverage higher than 0. The chart is divided into a grid where the displayed numbers show the count of n-gram/prompt-based rules on the corresponding grid, revealing that prompt-based rules can achieve relatively higher precision and coverage than n-gram rules. While n-gram rules cluster heavily at lower precision levels with generally modest coverage, prompt-based rules occupy a much wider precision range and dominate the high-precision region, particularly beyond the 0.8 threshold where n-gram candidates become increasingly scarce. As precision increases from 0.5 toward 1.0, coverage tends to thin out for both methods, yet prompt-based rules maintain a significant presence of candidates with meaningful coverage even at very high precision levels, such as the 24 rules found in the 0.9 to 1.0 precision and 10k to 15k coverage bracket where no n-gram rules exist. A notable pattern is the presence of several prompt-based outliers with very high coverage at mid-precision, reaching into the 15k to 25k range, indicating that certain prompts can generalize broadly without a total collapse in precision. Ultimately, while achieving near-perfect precision usually necessitates reduced coverage, the prompt-based approach shifts the performance frontier outward by offering more high-precision, non-trivial coverage solutions compared to the dense mass of low-coverage, lower-precision n-gram rules.
