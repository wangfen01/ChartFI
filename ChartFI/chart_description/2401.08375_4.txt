These four heatmaps display the correlation matrices of the 28 most influential stocks, specifically those with an index weight larger than 0.6%, comparing pairwise return correlations across four distinct PCA-based treatments. In the initial state where there is no removed PC, the matrix reveals a broadly positive correlation structure with distinct sector-style clustering, most notably a dense mega-cap tech block including MSFT, GOOG/GOOGL, META, AAPL, AMZN, and NVDA, while defensive staples like KO, PEP, PG, and JNJ exhibit moderate internal cohesion and weaker ties to high-growth names. Applying Ordinary PCA markedly flattens this matrix by pulling most off-diagonal correlations toward near-zero, effectively removing the dominant common market factors but leaving behind a landscape that resembles idiosyncratic noise with very few residual associations. The proposed T-Rex PCA reverses this over-whitening by restoring a more structured correlation landscape where strong within-cluster relationships re-emerge, indicating that the method preserves meaningful shared variation while reducing diffuse market-wide coupling. This effect is further refined in the proposed T-Rex Thresh. PCA variant, which sharpens the separation between groups to produce crisper, high-correlation blocks and clearer low-correlation gaps, implying a more selective retention of robust co-movement links and the suppression of weaker, potentially spurious correlations. Throughout the progression from raw data to the T-Rex approaches, there are few signs of systematic negative correlation, suggesting that the primary shift is a redistribution of correlation mass from broad market co-movement to concentrated, interpretable clusters that highlight the underlying structural relationships between these influential equities.
