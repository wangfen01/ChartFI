The figure compares the density of scores for in-distribution (ID) data versus out-of-distribution (OOD) data across two backbones, ResNet18 and WideResNet, plotting likelihood values for CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 respectively. For each dataset configuration, the top row presents the far-OOD context, where separation is strongest; OOD densities concentrate at much lower log p(x) values while in-distribution densities peak clearly to the right, producing a pronounced gap. This separation is particularly notable for WideResNet, where the in-distribution peak is sharper and more right-shifted compared to ResNet18. In contrast, the bottom row presents the mixed-OOD context, and conversely, the near-OOD context. As we move from the top to the bottom row, it is apparent that the overlap between likelihood plots increases significantly, with the distributions moving closer together and broadening as the semantic closeness of the datasets compresses the score gap. Despite this trend where OOD difficulty drives overlap, it is crucial to highlight that even under near-OOD conditions, the highest likelihood of falsely accepted OOD samples never exceeds that of the highest accepted ID sample, indicating that the models consistently assign the absolute highest likelihoods to in-domain samples.
