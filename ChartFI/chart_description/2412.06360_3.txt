The figure illustrates the evolution of household carbon intensity in India across two distinct periods, 2000–2011 and 2011–2022, utilizing waterfall charts to depict aggregate national, urban, and rural trends alongside heatmaps that decompose these changes across various states. At the national level, carbon intensity surged by 236.1% during the first period but decelerated significantly to a 16.8% increase in the second; this bifurcation is most pronounced in urban households, where intensity rose by 157.1% initially before plateauing with a mere 1.6% growth post-2011, whereas rural households experienced a steeper initial rise of 323.2% and a sustained, albeit slower, increase of 23.5% thereafter. Income growth (NSDP per capita) acts as the dominant upward driver across all categories and periods, consistently pushing emissions higher. However, the post-2011 stagnation in urban emissions is largely attributable to countervailing forces, specifically improvements in energy intensity and cleaner emission factors, which exert strong negative pressure on carbon intensity. While the results of the decomposition for most states were consistent with those at the national level in 2000–2011 and 2011–2022, the heatmaps reveal notable heterogeneity and specific anomalies during the earlier period. Although aggregate energy intensity generally drove emissions upward between 2000 and 2011, the energy intensity of urban households in states such as Sikkim, Delhi, Gujarat, Uttarakhand, and Tamil Nadu showed a negative contribution to the growth of carbon intensity during this timeframe, indicating early localized efficiency gains. Rural households in Sikkim exhibited similar characteristics, diverging from the broader rural trend where efficiency improvements were less effective in offsetting income-driven growth. Ultimately, the data highlights a transition where efficiency gains and decarbonization have begun to offset economic growth effects, a phenomenon that is currently more effective in urbanized regions than in rural India.
