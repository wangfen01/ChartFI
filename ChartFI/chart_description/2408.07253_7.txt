The figure displays two triangular heatmaps representing the Inter-Class Pairwise Angles (ICPAs) calculated using AllNC on the CIFAR10-LT dataset, comparing class means on the left with classifier vectors on the right. In the class means panel, the upper-left region is consistently high, with angles clustering roughly between 100 and 110 degrees, before values steadily decline toward the lower-right where the coolest pocket appears with a minimum around 76 degrees. In contrast, the classifier vectors panel exhibits a noticeably flatter structure where extremes are less pronounced; although localized dips remain, such as a distinct low around 81 degrees at the bottom-right corner, the mid-matrix shows moderate variation with many cells situated in the mid-to-high 90s. This distribution reveals that the ICPAs computed using this method are close to the optimal value of 96.4 degrees. The visual compression of the range in the classifier vectors, relative to the class means, indicates that AllNC effectively mitigates complete minority collapses, leading to the convergence of class means and classifier vectors towards a simplex ETF structure that satisfies NC2 and NC3.
