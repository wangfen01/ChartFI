The bar charts illustrate the average normalized performance on DMC, ManiSkill2, and Miniworld tasks in the OOD setting, comparing the returns of two agents, DreamerV3 (green) and TD-MPC2 (purple), across various visual encoders and pretraining schemes, where thin black lines denote the standard error. In the DMC combined tasks, DreamerV3 generally achieves higher returns, yet the data reveals a distinct hierarchy in representation learning: even autoencoders trained in-distribution on task-specific data perform worse compared to training an encoder from scratch. This is especially evident for the DMC environments where the PVRs (Pretrained Visual Representations) perform worse than training from scratch, correcting the misconception that reconstruction-based or pretrained features inherently outperform end-to-end learning; indeed, the "From Scratch" configuration yields the highest returns for both agents in this domain. In the ManiSkill2 combined tasks, absolute returns are much smaller for all settings. The Miniworld PickupObjects-v0 panel highlights the largest separation between methods, where DreamerV3 reaches high returns from scratch and with VC-1, whereas VIP and autoencoding lag substantially. Overall, the dominant signal is that encoder choice drives large swings in OOD performance, and while DreamerV3 is more robust, the most reliable performance often comes from learning from scratch or selecting specific, high-performing representations like VC-1, rather than relying on broad pretraining or autoencoding strategies.
