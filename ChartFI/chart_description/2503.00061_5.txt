These two heatmaps present a cross-evaluation of various adaptive attacks and defenses for the Vicuna-7B and Llama3-8B agents, where each grid represents the attack success rate (ASR) of adversarial strings trained under specific adaptive attacks against certain defenses. In these grids, "None" indicates no defense, "Original" refers to the indirect prompt injection (IPI) attacks without adversarial strings, and "None|GCG" denotes the Greedy Coordinate Gradient attack for the no-defense condition. Overall, Vicuna-7B appears broadly vulnerable across most attack–defense pairings, showing consistently high success rates even when defenses are applied, whereas Llama3-8B exhibits a much sharper separation between weak and strong defenses, with many cells dropping to near-zero under certain protections. For Vicuna-7B, the baseline Original row is already moderate to high across several defenses, and most attack variants push success into the 70–90 range against many defenses, especially under None, In-context Prompt (IP), and Deep Prompt Inspection (DPI) where values frequently cluster in the high 80s to mid 90s. For the top three strongest defenses for the Vicuna-7 B-based agent—the fine-tuned detector (FD), sandwich prevention (SP), and adversarial finetuning (AF)—as well as defenses specifically targeting adversarial strings, such as paraphrasing (P) and perplexity filtering (PF), we observe that the adaptive attack for each defense significantly outperforms other attacks. For instance, while the FD defense column stays relatively low across nearly all attacks, the FD|M-GCG adaptive attack achieves an 84 percent success rate against it, and similarly, the P|T-GCG attack reaches 79 percent against the P defense. For both agents, the adversarial strings trained for specific defenses achieve the highest attack effectiveness against the defense they were trained on in most cases, highlighting the necessity of conducting adaptive attacks to evaluate the robustness of these defenses. For Llama3-8B, the Original row is uniformly low across defenses, indicating strong inherent resistance absent explicit attack optimization, but once attacks are introduced, success spikes dramatically under None and often under AF. However, several defenses sharply reduce effectiveness for Llama3-8B; the P defense column is near-zero for most attacks, and FD and LD frequently keep success in the single digits, except when targeted by their respective adaptive attacks like FD|M-GCG and LD|M-GCG, which reach success rates of 74 and 81 percent. A key anomaly is that some attack–defense combinations remain highly effective despite generally strong defenses, such as LD|M-GCG staying very high under LD and AF, and multiple attacks retaining high success under IP and AF, indicating these defenses can be bypassed by specific attack constructions. The dominant pattern across both agents is that AF and no-defense conditions tend to be high-risk, but Llama3-8B benefits far more from certain defenses, especially P and FD, than Vicuna-7B, where attack success remains high and more uniform even after defenses are applied.
