These three radar charts analyse the alignment of human and automatic judgments across different revision types by comparing evaluation metric performance using pairwise accuracy, Cramér’s V, and Cohen’s kappa. A clear hierarchy of performance emerges across the five task categories. For most categories, LLM-Choice is the most reliable evaluation approach, as it and the similar Average LLM likert signal consistently achieve the highest and most stable scores. Following this top tier, ParaPLUIE emerges as a strong performer; for cases where paragraph content is minimally altered (Rewriting Light, Rewriting Medium and Concision), ParaPLUIE appears as a good alternative. This is particularly evident in the Rewriting-light category, where ParaPLUIE shows a pronounced spike in pairwise accuracy, indicating its notion of quality may align particularly well with such edits. In contrast, traditional lexical overlap metrics like ROUGE-L and GLEU, along with BERTScore, form a lower tier. Their performance drops noticeably on tasks like Concision, which challenges overlap-leaning approaches by shortening text without obvious lexical cues, and compresses toward the middle on Content Deletion, implying these edits are harder for many metrics to score in a way that matches comparative judgments. A notable anomaly is BLANC, which is consistently the lowest-performing metric and even dips to near-zero or negative Cohen's kappa values in some categories, signaling its poor reliability versus the preference labels.
