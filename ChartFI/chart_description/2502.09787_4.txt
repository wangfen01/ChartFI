These timelines illustrate the participants’ spreadsheet programming activities for creating a spreadsheet from scratch versus from a template, with a red vertical line denoting the 15-minute mark. While creating spreadsheets from scratch, 4 of the 7 participants completed the task in the 15 minutes allotted, 2 participants completed the task given extra time, and only P5 did not complete the task. Although each participant followed their own unique process to implement a spreadsheet, distinct behavioral patterns emerged between the two conditions. In the scratch condition, implementing dominates and is often consolidated into long runs, accompanied by heavier early Prototyping and fragmented switching that suggests exploratory iteration. Transitioning to the template condition, the most visible change is the increase in assessment time, which replaces early prototyping with longer, more contiguous blocks of assessment and knowledge updating; this indicates substantial effort is spent understanding and adapting the template. Despite this shift, several participants (N=4) entered multiple rounds of implementation and prototyping, indicating that spreadsheet development is iterative even while working with templates. Implementing remains the backbone in both views, but in the template condition, it is more frequently interrupted by assessment blocks, implying evaluation and alignment work rather than pure construction. Consequently, several template sessions extend past the 15-minute reference line, suggesting that templates may introduce additional decision or integration overhead. Verifying appears comparatively small in both views and tends to cluster late, indicating that final checks are brief and not the primary driver of total time differences.
