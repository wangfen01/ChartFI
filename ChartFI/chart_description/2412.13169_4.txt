The chart displays the JS Distance of six subpopulation groups in Experiment 2 across survey waves, illustrating how JS distances reveal representational variation influenced by group information and complexity. Consistent with the observation that the most variation is observed for education and leaning party variables, these categories exhibit distinct structural disparities compared to the tighter clustering found in other groups. Education degree shows the clearest separation: respondents with no school diploma sit markedly higher than all other education categories and trend upward in later waves, indicating a persistent and widening gap by educational attainment, while those with higher qualifications remain lower and comparatively steady. Party leaning introduces more volatility than demographics, with several parties exhibiting noticeable dips and rebounds across waves; however, most party series still remain within the broader mid-range, implying shifting but not radically reordering distances. A pronounced anomaly is also evident in vocational degrees. In contrast, age, gender, and regional patterns are muted; values across age groups hover stably around the low-to-mid 0.20s, gender differences remain small and parallel, and East and West Germany move similarly with only minor separations, pointing to limited polarization in these measures.
