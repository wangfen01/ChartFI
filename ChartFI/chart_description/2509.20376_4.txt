This visualization compares the alignment of feature activation and semantic explanation across two scenarios, where the x-axis represents the ranking of sentences by semantic similarity to the feature explanation and the y-axis represents their ranking by maximum activation value. Each cell represents a sampled text segment, with its position revealing the degree of match between activation strength and semantic relevance rankings, while a background color gradient from blue (high activation) to red (low activation) encodes activation value regions. The left panel illustrates a well-explained feature with a clear correlation between activation strength and semantic similarity, as text segments falling along the color-emphasized diagonal regions demonstrate cases where activation and semantic similarity rankings approximately align. In contrast, the right panel depicts a poorly explained feature showing significant discrepancies that reveal explanation limitations, specifically through off-diagonal outliers. Segments in the upper left region, marked by a blue background indicating high activation, possess low semantic similarity and potentially indicate feature behaviors not captured by the automatic explanation. Conversely, segments in the lower right show high semantic relevance but low activation against a red background, suggesting the explanation covers concepts that don’t consistently trigger the feature.
