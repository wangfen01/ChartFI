The heatmap presents the correlations between project characteristics and commit types, utilizing a color scale to indicate the direction and strength of association. While characteristics such as time between commits, commit size change, and edited files diversity remain near zero across categories—indicating that cadence, magnitude, and file diversity do not meaningfully differentiate commit types—distinct patterns emerge within activity and collaboration metrics. Activity-related measures show notable variation; specifically, past week commits and author total commits tend to be slightly negative across early pipeline and infrastructure-oriented categories. However, the most significant contrasts appear in collaboration intensity, where the analysis shows a strong positive correlation with Output Data commits, as well as with Internal Documentation and Sharing. This suggests that more collaboratively active projects generate more model artifacts and place a higher emphasis on logging and sharing activities, likely to coordinate efforts. Conversely, collaboration intensity shows a strong negative correlation with Project Metadata updates, a downward trend mirrored by past week commits and author total commits. Finally, file type diversity is slightly positive across most categories but fades toward neutral for dependency updates, reinforcing the observation that specific maintenance tasks often decouple from broader project activity metrics.
