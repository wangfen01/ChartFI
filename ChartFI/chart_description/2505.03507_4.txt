These radar charts present the Precision Rate with different attributes based on the Short-term Evaluation, which excludes the OV attribute to focus on twelve distinct categories. The visual data reveals a consistent story where the proposed method, represented by the blue series (GDStrack), achieves the best performance on most attributes, maintaining a dominant position with strong separation on FO, FM, PO, SV, DEF, and so on. This indicates robust, well-balanced performance rather than isolated wins, particularly in categories where weaker methods struggle to generalize. However, a notable exception to this dominance is observed in the extreme illumination (EI) attribute, where the top method registers a Precision Rate 2.1% lower than UDT-FF, suggesting a specific sensitivity to lighting extremes despite superior overall tracking. The remaining methods form a tighter mid-to-low cluster with uneven profiles, typically peaking around DEF but dropping noticeably on FO, LR, TB, and TC. This pattern creates a recurring valley, further differentiating the top performer from the rest. Both panels confirm these trends, highlighting that while the leading approach dominates broadly, the competitive gap narrows specifically under extreme illumination conditions.
