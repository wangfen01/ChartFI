The violin plot illustrates the distribution of ∆SLOR(Let-Alone) − ∆SLOR(And) for Filtered Pretraining Conditions across six distinct linguistic categories, where positive differences indicate sensitivity to the construction. Across most conditions, the relative advantage of the LetAlone variant over the And variant is highly sensitive to both the specific condition and the filtering choice, with differences generally remaining positive after removing direct evidence from related constructions, such as in the NoRel and NoAny filters, but becoming negative when all ‘let’ or ‘alone’ tokens are removed. The CP and Pseudo conditions demonstrate the most pronounced divergence; in CP, the NoAny filter produces the strongest positive shifts with a distribution centered well above zero, while the NoLet and NoAlone filters shift the mass into clearly negative territory, suggesting that removing specific lexical components reverses the construction's advantage. Pseudo follows a similar but more exaggerated pattern, where NoAny and NoRel are strongly positive while NoLet and NoLetorAlone skew negative, implying the pseudo condition is particularly susceptible to these removals. In contrast, the No_NPI condition remains comparatively stable with all filters tightly concentrated around small positive values, indicating minimal dependence on filtering and a generally modest LetAlone advantage. The VP and Gap conditions reveal a consistent asymmetry where NoRel tends to sit slightly above zero while NoAlone and NoLetorAlone shift the distribution below zero, pointing to a recurring penalty for LetAlone when its constituent tokens are excluded. The semantic condition is the most centered and compressed around zero across all filters but contains long tails in both directions, representing rare but substantial swings that contrast with the more systematic, filter-driven sign changes observed in the CP and Pseudo categories.
