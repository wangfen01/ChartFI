These radar charts illustrate the performance of the top three generative and discriminative RMs across different tasks, distinguishing between helpfulness and harmlessness objectives. The data reveals that the reward models, especially the top generative model, perform consistently under the helpfulness goal, maintaining high scores across categories like coding, reasoning, and chat. However, some variations exist within this stability, such as fluctuations in Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct’s performance on translation tasks, and interestingly, RMs weaker in judging translation tend to excel in Closed QA tasks. In sharp contrast to the helpfulness domain, the models fail to generalize across the different tasks, especially on the harmlessness goal. This is visually represented by the erratic, jagged shapes in the harmlessness plot.
