This radar chart presents an attribute-based comparison of normalized AUC scores for ten representative trackers across fourteen challenging visual attributes, evaluated on the LaSOT test set. The results reveal a clear performance hierarchy, with COST, VLT_TT, and VLT_SCAR consistently forming the outer envelope, indicating the most robust and stable performance. Specifically, the proposed COST outperforms other trackers on 13 of the 14 attributes, demonstrating its superior capabilities. The sole exception is in the full occlusion scene, where VLT_TT performs slightly better than COST. In stark contrast, ECO is positioned far inside the chart across all attributes, marking a significant performance gap with the rest of the field. The leading trackers show particular strength under Illumination Variation, achieving the chart's highest annotated AUC score (0.373), and also maintain high performance through Camera Motion. Conversely, performance contracts sharply for nearly all trackers under Fast Motion, Full Occlusion, and Out-of-View conditions, with top scores dropping to 0.233, 0.254, and 0.239 respectively, signaling that abrupt target dynamics and complete target loss remain primary failure modes. Mid-tier methods such as ATOM, Ocean, and SiamRPN++ form a cluster in the middle, exhibiting more variability and struggling particularly with occlusion-related challenges, which highlights that the key differentiator for the top-performing models is their enhanced capability for re-detection and long-term recovery.
