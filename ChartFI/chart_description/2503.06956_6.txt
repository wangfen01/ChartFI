This scatter plot compares LATEXBLEND with recent, competitive baselines by plotting concept alignment on the x-axis against text alignment on the y-axis, using marker shapes to distinguish between varying numbers of concepts. The visual distribution indicates that LATEXBLEND (blue) achieves the most optimal balance of objectives, predominantly occupying the upper-right quadrant. Compared to the baselines, LATEXBLEND demonstrates a higher success rate of subject presence and better subject fidelity, as evidenced by its placement furthest to the right across most settings, maintaining robust performance even as the number of concepts increases. Conversely, the baseline methods exhibit distinct limitations; Mix-of-Show (red) and Cones 2 (purple) generally appear left-shifted, reflecting weaker concept alignment. This underperformance is partly attributed to the fact that they suffer from degradation in image structure, often resulting in single-object-centric subjects, a deficiency that confines Mix-of-Show to the lower-left. OMG (gray) and MuDI (orange) cluster near the middle-right, offering a stable but less performant baseline compared to the top-tier results of LATEXBLEND. The data further reveals that multi-concept generation amplifies the divergence between methods, as LATEXBLEND preserves high concept alignment in complex scenarios, whereas other methods plateau around the mid-0.30s on the y-axis, indicating they may produce text-consistent outputs without fully satisfying concept constraints.
