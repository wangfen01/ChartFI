The chart illustrates the recovery rate at different noise levels for various symbolic regression approaches on the Strogatz and Feynman benchmarks, with target noise ranging from 0.000 to 0.1. In the display, † and ‡ denote generative and regression methods, the others are search methods, and the error bars depict the 95% confidence interval. On the Strogatz problem set, our method has the highest recovery rate, significantly surpassing other methods, most of which cluster below 20% and shift leftward as noise increases. The robustness of the proposed approach is particularly notable here; even at the maximum noise level (epsilon = 0.1), our method still outperforms other methods without noise, including the AIFeynman2 on the Strogatz dataset. On the Feynman problem set, however, AIFeynman2 shows a higher recovery rate than our methods when the noise level is 0.0. Despite this, the broader trend on the Feynman dataset reveals that while AIFeynman2 suffers a steep decline as noise is introduced, the proposed method maintains a more stable degradation curve, thereby occupying the leading performance band across all non-zero noise levels and confirming a consistent advantage in noise robustness across both benchmarks.
