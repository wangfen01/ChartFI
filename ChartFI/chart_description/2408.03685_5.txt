The chart illustrates the dispatch decisions and state of charge (SOC) changes of an Energy Storage System (ESS) connected to node 16 during a typical daily operation. These decisions are defined by DDPG, TD3, PPO, and SAC, as well as the global optimality benchmark solution provided by solving the NLP formulation considering the perfect forecast. The top panel displays a clear intraday price cycle where prices sit in the mid-30s EUR/MWh overnight, step up sharply around early morning to above 50, remain elevated through late morning, soften into early afternoon, and rise again into an early-evening peak before dropping quickly after 22:00. Decisions provided by all DRL algorithms responded to the dynamic prices during the day, attempting to arbitrage these two distinct high-price windows separated by a midday dip. NLP and PPO behave most like a textbook strategy, charging during the low-price night to push SOC toward the upper range by 05:00–07:00, discharging through the morning high-price block, recharging during the midday lull, and discharging again into the evening peak. SAC follows a similar overall rhythm but with more frequent switching and a steadier SOC decline, while TD3 shows more segmented, blocky actions with pronounced early charging but a flatter SOC plateau through the late afternoon, implying more conservative use of the second peak. DDPG stands out as the least aligned with the price signal, exhibiting abrupt, intermittent actions and an SOC trajectory that stays near the lower bound for long stretches. Consequently, on this day, PPO and SAC perform better than DDPG and TD3; the better-aligned methods transition cleanly from charging to discharging near the morning step-up and avoid charging late when prices fall, whereas DDPG and TD3 demonstrate noisier control or constrained capacity usage that is less synchronized with optimal regime changes.
