The zero-shot commonsense performance of Llama2-7B, Vicuna-7B, and Llama2-13B is evaluated across seven benchmarks—BoolQ, PIQA, OBQA, ARC-c, ARC-e, WinoGrande, and HellaSwag—where the Vanilla model consistently sets the upper bound and Random pruning consistently underperforms. The strongest absolute scores cluster on PIQA and HellaSwag, where Vanilla performance is near the top of the scale for all models, while the weakest results are found in OBQA and ARC-c, where every method compresses into the low-to-mid range, indicating these tasks remain significant reasoning bottlenecks regardless of the compression strategy. Comparing Llama2-7B and Vicuna-7B, the results indicate that CLONE exhibits a strong generalization capability, outperforming baselines by an average of 23.85% and frequently tracking closest to the Vanilla baseline alongside ShortGPT. Moving from 7B to 13B generally lifts the performance floor, most visibly on BoolQ, PIQA, ARC-e, and HellaSwag, where Llama2-13B Vanilla is higher than its 7B counterpart and often higher than Vicuna-7B. Evaluating Llama2-7B and Llama2-13B, it is evident that CLONE maintains 91.13% of the performance of the unpruned (vanilla) models, underscoring its scalability and ability to retain a large fraction of performance on high-ceiling benchmarks. While structured approaches like SliceGPT and LLMPruner usually sit in the middle of the performance distribution, the gap between Vanilla and the best compressed methods is smallest on the harder tasks like OBQA and ARC-c, primarily because all methods converge at a low performance ceiling. In contrast, ARC-e behaves as a high-headroom benchmark where several compressed variants remain competitive, suggesting that substantial redundancy can be removed without catastrophic loss. Ultimately, scaling the base model to Llama2-13B yields more consistent gains than switching among compression methods, yet the most effective strategies like CLONE can preserve robust performance across models while still struggling to break through the inherent difficulty of the most challenging reasoning tasks.
