This chart presents a detailed comparison of the scores of mainstream LLMs across nine capability areas. While different tasks may utilize distinct underlying metrics, all scores are normalized to range within [0, 1], where higher values always mean better performance. These results generally show that there is no single winner across all capabilities and even no family that consistently outperforms other families, although the overall performance is strongly bimodal by task type. Most models cluster very high on core cognitive and agentic skills such as Memorization, Dynamic Instruction, Dynamic Environment, Triggering, Multitasking, and Recursive Reasoning, whereas scores drop sharply for Privacy Protection, Personalization, and Prioritization. Across the high-scoring categories, the leading models repeatedly reach the upper 0.9s, suggesting these benchmarks are close to saturated for top-tier systems; indeed, similar observations can be made on other models’ high performance in the Memorization task. Despite this convergence, specific models distinguish themselves in key areas: notably, GPTo4-mini exhibits exceptional performance in 4 out of 9 tasks, particularly dominating in dynamic and multitasking contexts, while DeepSeek-R1 achieves the highest scores in 2 tasks, showing particular strength in Recursive Reasoning where a clearer separation between models is visible compared to saturated fields like Triggering. The most notable shift occurs when moving from reasoning and coordination tasks to safety and preference-alignment tasks, where Privacy Protection drops to roughly the 0.25–0.50 range for most models, and Personalization represents the lowest category overall, often hovering near 0.10–0.25 with only a few models pushing meaningfully higher. Prioritization rebounds slightly versus Personalization but remains well below the reasoning categories, typically settling around the 0.30–0.50 range, suggesting that while general reasoning and tool-like behaviors are broadly strong, consistent privacy behavior and user-specific preference handling remain the main weaknesses and the biggest differentiators across the field.
