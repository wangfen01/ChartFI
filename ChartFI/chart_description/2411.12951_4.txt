The figure presents a consistency evaluation of six Video-LLMs across two benchmarks, Charades-CON and ActivityNet-CON, utilizing radar charts to visualize performance across five capabilities: Ground, C-Ground, S-Ground, H-Verify, and C-Verify. The Standard prompting strategy indicates the original performance, serving as a baseline, while the greatest improvement is highlighted in red for Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting and in blue for Description prompting. On Charades-CON, the greatest and most consistent improvements come from Description Prompting for systems like TimeChat and VTimeLLM, which expand the radar area across nearly all axes; however, this trend is not universal. Notably, none of the prompting methods improve Video-LLaMA and Video-LLaVA on Charades-CON; in fact, they even degrade the performance, suggesting that for these models, additional prompting context may introduce noise rather than clarity. VTG-LLM also appears as an outlier on this benchmark, where Chain-of-Thought prompting and description prompting underperform the baseline on multiple axes. The pattern shifts on ActivityNet-CON, where improvements become more mixed and model-dependent. A standout performance is observed with TimeChat, where CoT demonstrates over a 5% improvement across all aspects of TimeChat on ActivityNet-CON, driving substantial gains in grounding-related axes. Conversely, other models show less uniform benefits, with Description Prompting remaining broadly helpful but not dominant. Overall, while advanced models can gain disproportionately from specific strategies, the improvements are often unstable, underscoring the limitations of prompting alone in enhancing temporal comprehension, as evidenced by the inability of prompting to compensate for underlying limitations in earlier architectures or to consistently boost performance across different task mixes.
