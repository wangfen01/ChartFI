The monthly distribution of Hacker News stories related to GitHub AI projects from mid-2022 through spring 2024 reveals a dramatic regime change in late 2022, transitioning from a period of limited, sporadic mentions to a landscape defined by high volume and intense volatility. Prior to November 2022, nearly all keywords, including "Artificial Intelligence" and "Natural Language Processing," sit near zero, suggesting that AI-related GitHub projects had not yet achieved mainstream technical traction. The release of ChatGPT serves as the primary inflection point, with the chart showing that ChatGPT was a significant contributor to this increase, characterized by distinct spikes in activity observed after both the ChatGPT release and the subsequent GPT-4 launch in early 2023. During this peak, ChatGPT surged to over 50 stories per month, the highest level recorded, indicating that many GitHub AI projects are related to OpenAI’s products or platforms. As the conversation matured through 2023, the generic term “AI” climbed into a sustained high range, often fluctuating between 15 and 30 stories, while technical framing via terms like “LLM” and “Language Model” gained meaningful traction. While ChatGPT’s dominance eventually stepped down into a lower but still elevated baseline, a second wave of product-specific volatility emerged as projects containing other LLM models were also mentioned on Hacker News, including Claude, Gemini, Llama, Gemma, Mistral, and Cohere. Notably, Gemini shows pronounced spikes in late 2023 and early 2024 that briefly rival the generic “AI” coverage, while open-weight models like Llama and Mistral exhibit occasional bursts corresponding to major releases. This evolution reflects a shift from a singular focus on OpenAI to a more diversified ecosystem where generic “AI” coverage remains persistently high, punctuated by sharp, episodic spikes around the launches of various competing foundational models.
