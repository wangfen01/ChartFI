The figure compares Net Adoption Rate and Harmful Adoption Rate across three datasets (C, P, S) for two task types, stance and sentiment, and four models. While the Net Adoption Rate distributions for most model–dataset combinations appear centered near zero, a systematic shift is evident where predictions are more likely to move towards information stance and sentiment than they are to move away. This trend is quantified by the observation that the mean net adoption rate is positive in 11 out of 12 model–dataset combinations for stance, and in 10 out of 12 combinations for sentiment. The clearest driver of this trend is Claude 3 Haiku, which shows consistently higher positive net adoption, especially on the C dataset for both stance and sentiment where the distribution shifts noticeably to the right. However, this uptake is not necessarily beneficial; when a model adopts information stance or sentiment, predictions are more likely to be wrong (harmful) than to be correct (helpful). Consequently, the mean harmful adoption rates are positive for all models and datasets. Claude 3 Haiku stands out with substantially higher harmful adoption on the C dataset in both stance and sentiment, with the central mass well above zero and a long right tail, while the other models cluster closer to zero with smaller spreads. Across datasets, C exhibits the largest variability and the most extreme positive outliers in harmful adoption, whereas P and S tend to be tighter and nearer to zero, implying more stable behavior.
