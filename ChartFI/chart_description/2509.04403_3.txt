The sunburst chart illustrates the classification hierarchy and data proportion of the RMS dataset, visualizing the relationship between broad risk domains and specific incident types. The inner ring reveals that the distribution of data across major categories is not perfectly balanced, with the largest share concentrated in physical safety and accident-related issues (represented in blue), followed by smaller but distinct clusters for social harms, legal compliance, and ethical concerns. However, this imbalance at the aggregate level is primarily due to the varying number of subcategories within each major category rather than a disparity in the frequency of individual incidents. Contrary to the expectation of a steep drop-off from a few high-volume hazards to a long tail of minor issues, the outer ring demonstrates that there are no significant quantity differences among the subcategories. Within the dominant physical safety segment, specific topics such as falling from a height, drowning death, traffic accidents, and animal attacks appear as equally weighted components rather than being led by a single overwhelming driver. Similarly, the social and ethical sectors feature human–animal discrimination, pornography, and violence as evenly distributed elements, just as the legal cluster balances order disruption, and theft without one overshadowing the others. Thus, the chart characterizes the dataset not by the dominance of specific hotspots, but by a structural diversity where the volume of the major categories is determined strictly by the count of their uniformly distributed constituent risks.
