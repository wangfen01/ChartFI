This stacked bar chart presents the distribution of the selected factors’ influence on a subject choosing a given ecosystem to start developing software, where each factor is represented in a row. Each row has three to five blocks that must be read from left to right to increase the influence level for the related factor, ranging from No influence to Very strongly. The numbers in the bars indicate the percentage of respondents who answered the question associated with the specific factor for the related influence level. Overall, responses skew heavily toward the higher end of influence, indicating broad agreement that these factors are significant; for instance, 4.8% of the subjects answered that factor F01 (Desired technical resources for development) only slightly influenced their decision in selecting a software ecosystem, while 66.7% of respondents answered that the same factor strongly affected their decision. This trend of high-intensity endorsement is further highlighted by F03, where the Very strongly segment peaks at 66.7%. While the majority of items reflect this "top-box" intensity, the data reveals distinct variations where sentiment shifts toward a more tempered view. F27, for example, shows a sizable Moderate share of 33.3% alongside 47.6% for Strongly, suggesting influence is acknowledged but with less extreme conviction. Additionally, F11 exhibits the highest Moderate concentration at 47.6%; contrary to the initial summary which suggested an absence of high-intensity sentiment here, the chart clearly shows a 28.6% share for Very strongly, indicating that while moderate views dominate, strong endorsement remains significant. Notable anomalies appear in cases like F16, which signals more mixed perceptions than the rest; here, the combined lower-influence categories (No influence and Slightly) account for approximately 47.6% of responses, with No influence at 19% and Slightly at 28.6%, while Moderate stands at 19% and Strongly at 33.3%, collectively indicating a broader spread of opinion compared to most other factors. These patterns collectively demonstrate that while influence is generally accepted across the ecosystem factors, respondents reserve their strongest, most uniform endorsements for a specific subset of items like F01 and F03, creating a clear separation from factors viewed with more mixed or moderate conviction.
