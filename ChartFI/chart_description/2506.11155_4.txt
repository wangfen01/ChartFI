This heatmap illustrates the F1 score across different video categories for a diverse range of multimodal large language models (MLLMs), revealing a clear performance stratification into three distinct tiers. Early InternVL2 variants cluster tightly around the low 50s across most categories, followed by a broad step up into the low-to-mid 60s for many Qwen2-VL, LLaVA-OV/Qwen2, and related models, while a top tier consisting of Gemini-1.5-Pro and GPT-4o consistently leads with scores mostly in the high 60s to low 70s. It is evident that MLLMs achieve better results in the “nature and Wildlife” and “Arts and Creativity” categories, possibly due to these videos’ real-life resemblance, facilitating model learning, with scores frequently reaching the mid-to-high 60s for mid-tier models and peaking above 70 for the best performers. In contrast, performance in the remaining categories varies slightly, with the most pronounced anomaly being the sharp dip for the PLLaVA-7B model, which underperforms dramatically in every domain, bottoming out at 30.04 in Technology and Science and staying in the mid-30s to low-40s elsewhere. Although videos in “Movie and TV Shows” offer easily accessible training data with clear subjects and rich content, these types of videos often contain more detailed information; however, existing MLLMs struggle to process the detailed information presented in these videos, leading to less impressive performance compared to the top-performing categories. At the high end of the spectrum, Gemini-1.5-Pro is the most consistently high performer across domains, often edging into the low-to-mid 70s, while GPT-4o remains very competitive but shows a modest drop in some domains compared with Gemini’s peaks. Overall, the data suggests that while model architecture drives the largest score differences, the inherent complexity of specific content domains like Movies and TV Shows creates a persistent performance ceiling for current MLLMs.
