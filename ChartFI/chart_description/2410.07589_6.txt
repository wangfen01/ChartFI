These heatmaps compare the fairness implications of biased Reference Contexts (RC) on various Target Categories (TC) for GPT-4o and GPT-4o mini, where each row corresponds to a biased RC and each column to a TC. The plotted values represent the difference in fairness between Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems using partially biased data versus those using clean data, with red hues indicating fairness degradation and blue indicating improvement. In the GPT-4o results, categories such as race-related attributes—specifically Race x SES, Race x ethnicity, and Race x gender—consistently show fairness degradation when the dataset contains biased samples related to nationality, sexual orientation, or gender identity. Moreover, the fairness of gender identity is notably affected when biased samples are related to physical appearance and disability. While GPT-4o mini also displays fairness degradation in race and gender due to certain biased RCs, there is no consistency in the biased RCs observed in GPT-4o mini compared to those observed in GPT-4o, suggesting a more volatile response to bias in the smaller model. The visualization further illustrates that bias categories such as disability status, age, and religion are generally more vulnerable to the influence of other biased RCs, as reflected by the predominantly red columns, particularly evident in the GPT-4o mini heatmap where the Religion column shows significant degradation. However, this impact is not uniform, as some bias categories exhibit no consistent direction of change, resulting in a matrix of mixed red and blue scores that highlights the complex, non-linear interaction between different types of data bias and model fairness.
