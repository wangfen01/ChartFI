The confusion matrix visualization illustrates the debiasing effect of the proposed framework on LLaMA70B across two datasets, FS-LIU54 and ZS-CoT-HWU64, by comparing the performance of the full set of options against the framework-enhanced approach. In the original few-shot setting (FS-LIU54) with full options, the matrix displays vertical error streaks where true classes are systematically misclassified, indicating that the model showed a preference for “movies” and “convert” as high-attraction error sinks. Similarly, in the ZS-CoT setting (HWU64), the full-options matrix reveals a strong tendency to predict a narrow band of intents near the right side of the label space, where the model preferred “iot/hue_lightchange” and “recommendation/movies,” creating broad overprediction across many different true intents. After applying the framework, the preference bias clearly shifted, producing a noticeably cleaner diagonal structure in both datasets that indicates higher per-class accuracy and fewer cross-intent confusions. This enhancement is particularly evident in the reduced bias effect of certain tokens, such as “movies” and “convert” in the LIU54 dataset, and “iot/hue_lightchange” and “recommendation/movies” in the HWU64 dataset, where the previously observed vertical streaks largely break up and correct predictions become more class-specific. The most significant shift across both datasets is the reduction of “default” or catch-all prediction behavior, replaced by more discriminative intent selection, which is consistent with the framework improving calibration and narrowing the candidate space to reduce systematic misrouting.
