The figure compares power balance across 19 typical operation scenarios using stacked bars for generator outputs above zero and load/absorption below zero, with an overlaid line showing total cross-area transmission. The system alternates between distinct regimes, most notably where scenarios OM_1, OM_8, OM_14, OM_15, and OM_16 exhibit high levels of electricity export, coinciding with the largest total generation peaks such as the 889 MW observed around OM8. In these high-output instances, the analysis shows that above the zero coordinate axis, bounded by bus_113_gen, the lower part is the new energy output in the region, and the upper part is the energy storage participation. In contrast, scenarios OM_13 and OM_18 have low or almost zero new energy output, marking a trough period of new energy output. Analysis with bus_19_gen as the boundary revealed that these two typical operation scenarios encounter the situation wherein thermal power supply nodes cannot provide energy owing to resource shortages or failures under the high proportion of new energy integration scenario; consequently, while dispatching limited energy storage, more electricity must be dispatched across areas, explaining the sharp negative transmission values indicating net imports. Furthermore, the scenarios OM_4 and OM_11 involve the combined participation of the thermal power and energy storage. In these scenarios, where bus_113_gen serves as the boundary, there is a substantial thermal power output in the region with almost no new energy output, and a higher energy storage output is observed above the boundary. The analysis shows that both scenarios encounter a trough period of new energy output, which is seasonal and occurs because of the intermittent or fluctuating operation characteristics of new energy sources; however, they were independently extracted because scenario OM_11 involved the participation of adjustable loads in scheduling and operation.
