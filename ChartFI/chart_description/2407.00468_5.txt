The heatmaps illustrate the conditional accuracy of MMEVALPRO across three benchmarks—MMMU, ScienceQA, and MathVista—comparing the performance of Qwen72B (LLM), Internvl1.5 (LMM), and Human evaluators. The visualization tracks conditional probabilities P(Y=1|X=1) among Original (O), Perception (P), and Knowledge (K) conditions, where the metrics intuitively reflect the model’s likelihood of correctly answering perception and knowledge questions if it has already solved the original question. A clear disparity in reasoning consistency emerges: humans generally exhibit high probabilities of correctly answering one question given a correct answer to another, indicating consistent thinking, whereas LLMs show the lowest probabilities compared to LMMs and humans. In the MMEvalPro-MMMU benchmark, this fragmentation is most visible for Qwen72B, which displays weak links when conditioning on O=1 (with probabilities of 0.30 for P=1 and 0.42 for K=1), suggesting that solving the original problem does not reliably imply possession of the underlying perception or knowledge. Internvl1.5 improves substantially in this domain but does not reach the robust coherence of humans. In MMEvalPro-ScienceQA, Internvl1.5 approaches human-level consistency with strong off-diagonal associations, while humans remain near the ceiling across all cells, demonstrating high mutual reinforcement among variables. Conversely, Qwen72B’s consistently weaker off-diagonal values across all tasks highlight that, unlike LMMs and humans, its correct answers are less frequently supported by a coherent structure of perception and knowledge.
