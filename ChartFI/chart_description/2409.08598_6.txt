The confusion matrices present the classification performance of the method using pre-trained ResNet-18 and Swin-T models on the RAF-DB, AffectNet, and FERPlus datasets. Across these evaluations, the method achieves satisfactory performance on most of the facial expression categories, especially on Happiness, which consistently maintains high diagonal dominance. However, the results also highlight relatively poor performance on Fear, Disgust, and Contempt, a trend visible to varying degrees across all datasets. On RAF-DB, where performance is strongest, Swin-T notably tightens the diagonal and reduces spillover for Fear and Disgust. AffectNet displays the clearest degradation and most diffuse off-diagonal mass, indicating substantial class overlap where Neutral is frequently confused with Sadness and Contempt. FERPlus occupies a middle ground; while Happiness and Neutral remain very strong, Contempt acts as a persistent anomaly with low true-positive rates and large systematic drift toward Neutral, and Fear is often misread as Surprise or Sadness, underscoring the inherent difficulty in separating these specific negative emotions.
