These 2D histograms show the frequency of nonzero fertilization and irrigation applications during testing, where darker areas correspond to higher frequencies. For the fertilization problem, PPO exhibits an adaptive, time-varying strategy where it concentrates most of its nitrogen applications around day 60, typically applying between 0 and 50 kg/ha of nitrogen. This activity forms a dense cluster that largely tapers off after about day 80, with little to no activity later in the season. In contrast, DQN displayed no clear temporal pattern, with nitrogen applications scattered across the growing season; however, the chart reveals that these scattered applications appear as horizontal bands at fixed rates (roughly 40, 80, 120, and 160 kg/ha), indicating a quantized policy that revisits specific setpoints rather than modulating continuously. In the irrigation problem, both PPO and DQN applied water primarily between days 80 and 120, though their magnitude distributions differ significantly. PPO tended to apply moderate amounts of water (0 - 20 L/m^2), showing a pronounced seasonal ramp that suggests responsiveness to evolving crop demand. Conversely, DQN consistently applied smaller amounts of water (around 6 L/m^2), forming a dominant horizontal band that intensifies during the mid-season window. Overall, PPO demonstrates a clear mid-season surge in irrigation paired with earlier nitrogen emphasis, whereas DQN maintains relatively uniform timing but changes mainly by selecting among a small set of fixed application magnitudes.
