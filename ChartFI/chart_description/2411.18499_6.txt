This set of radar charts evaluates the performance of various multimodal models by displaying the average scores of all criteria on each meta-topic for different kinds of models, categorized into Integrated Pipeline, Two-stage Generator, and End-to-end Generator architectures, alongside a summary of average scores of all meta-topics on each specific criterion. The comparative analysis across the topic-based charts highlights that Integrated Pipeline models generally maintain a performance envelope closer to the human baseline than the Two-stage or End-to-end variants, which show significantly reduced capabilities and smaller coverage areas across multiple topics. Despite the robust overall performance of the Integrated Pipeline approach, specific weaknesses persist; notably, GPT-4o + DALL·E-3 underperforms in meta-topics like Interactive Image Editing and EmbodiedAI tasks, where its scores dip noticeably below the human reference. Furthermore, the assessment of specific evaluation criteria reveals a critical anomaly in self-evaluation patterns. While the models generally strive to match human standards in areas such as correctness and content richness, GPT-4o also exhibits bias toward its own outputs, scoring them 10 in human preference alignment, compared to an average score of 9 for human-annotated responses. This discrepancy indicates that while the model is highly capable, its alignment metrics may be inflated by a preference for its own generation style over human-generated content.
