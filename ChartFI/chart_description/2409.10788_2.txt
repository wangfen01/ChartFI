This heatmap illustrates the contribution of each transformer layer, from the 0th layer representing the input to the 12th layer, when predicting more informative targets across three distinct metrics: PR, SID, and SS. The visualization utilizes a color scale where a darker color indicates a higher contribution, allowing for a clear comparison between a k-means baseline and progressively stronger quantization variants including +1RVQ, +2RVQ, and +3RVQ. For the PR metric, while values remain near zero across most early layers, a pronounced late-layer surge is evident under k-means and +1RVQ, with the strongest concentration at the deepest layers; however, adding third and fourth levels causes the large weights to occur at earlier layers, specifically shifting the peak intensity from the 11th layer toward the 9th and 10th layers. In contrast, the SID metric reveals that the best-performing model, +1RVQ, tends to have high weights concentrated on fewer layers, specifically showing sharp peaks at layers 3 and 5, whereas increasing quantization to +2RVQ and +3RVQ dampens these extremes and redistributes moderate values across adjacent mid-layers. The SS metric remains the most stable, characterized by high initial contributions in the 0th and 1st layers that gradually diminish, though notably, more quantization levels increase the contribution of the last layer compared to the baseline. Overall, the data demonstrates that increasing the levels of quantization tends to smooth and redistribute contributions for PR and SID away from isolated high-intensity layers, while SS maintains a relatively consistent early-layer focus with subtle shifts in terminal layer importance as model complexity grows.
