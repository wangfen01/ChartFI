The heat map illustrates the relationships between the input data types and objectives and the platforms of the recommendation systems, with darker cells indicating stronger relationships and row and column averages summarizing overall intensity. The heatmap on the left shows the average correlation between each pair of elements from the input data and different platforms, revealing that wearable devices and smart home devices exhibit strong correlations with most data types. Specifically, data_type and user_context_and_sensor_data stand out as the most broadly collected inputs, peaking on portable and wearable devices. Head-mounted devices (HMDs), as a new technology, also show a strong correlation with the data, particularly regarding these core input types, despite trailing slightly in overall average intensity compared to smart homes. In contrast, PCs, although capable of collecting building operational data, exhibit weaker connections, possibly due to their limited interaction capabilities and lack of portability. More granular analysis shows that physiological and body_temperature data are highly platform-dependent, showing a sharp drop from smart home devices to portable and wearable units, while indoor_air_quality_data remains sparse across all platforms. Turning to the heatmap on the right, which shows the average correlation between each pair of elements from the objectives and different platforms, specific_content_recommendations emerges as the dominant use case, most strongly associated with smart home and portable devices. Lifestyle_behavior_recommendations are comparatively stronger on wearable devices, while indoor_environment_and_energy_performance is notably skewed toward smart home devices and PCs. Conversely, mental_health_management and healthcare_and_lifestyle_interventions remain weaker and more uneven, with especially low association on PCs.
