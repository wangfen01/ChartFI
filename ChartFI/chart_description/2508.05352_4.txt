The heatmaps display HR@10 performance sensitivity to varying behavior denoising steps, temperature, and loss weights across the Rec-Tmal and Kuaishou datasets. On Rec-Tmal, HR@10 stays tightly clustered around the low 0.31 to 0.32 range, indicating the model is fairly robust, but there are clear sweet spots where small tuning gains appear. The peak HR@10 of 0.3207 occurred at 10 steps with a 0.02 weight, balancing denoising and specificity, while another pronounced peak occurs in the temperature panel at a temperature near 0.3 and a weight around 0.05. Conversely, excessive steps and weight, such as 25 steps with a 0.15 weight, caused over-regularization and softened results to an HR@10 of 0.3155. On Kuaishou, the absolute HR@10 level is lower, generally in the mid 0.25 to 0.26 range, and the surfaces are flatter, implying weaker sensitivity and smaller upside from hyperparameter tuning. Still, the same general pattern holds, as Kuaishou achieved its best HR@10 of 0.2603 at 10 steps with a 0.02 weight, ensuring effective denoising without over-regularization. Furthermore, insufficient denoising, such as 5 steps with a 0.01 weight, reduced performance to an HR@10 of 0.2583. The temperature panel for Kuaishou again shows a localized bump around a temperature of 0.3 with a 0.05 weight, standing out as a consistent improvement zone across datasets. Consistently, the optimal range across both datasets is around 10 diffusion steps with a 0.02 loss weight, achieving the best trade-off, while extreme weight values rarely produce the best outcomes.
