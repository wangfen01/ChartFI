This heatmap illustrates the ratio of cases reaching consensus after three rounds of debate, comparing baseline debate methods with various ConfMAD settings across two model families, LLaMA and Phi, on four benchmarks (BIGGSM, BBH, MMLU, MATH). The results show that across almost all settings, debates using ConfMAD—specifically the LN-based and SV-based methods—achieve higher consensus rates compared to baselines. While the "No Conf" condition shows reasonable agreement, the MP column represents a significant anomaly with pronounced drops, particularly where LLaMA falls to 0.32 on MMLU and 0.48 on BBH. In contrast, the ConfMAD strategies demonstrate particularly notable improvements on the BBH and MMLU datasets, effectively recovering from these dips to reach near-saturated levels. For instance, when 4o-mini debates with LLaMA on the MMLU dataset, the consensus rate increases by around 11%, rising from a baseline of 0.83 to 0.94 under the LN+Temp setting. Ultimately, the LN+Platt, LN+Histo, LN+Temp, SV+Platt, and SV+Histo columns cluster tightly at very high values, indicating that these calibration strategies reliably eliminate the instability seen in methods like MP and compress performance differences across both models and tasks.
