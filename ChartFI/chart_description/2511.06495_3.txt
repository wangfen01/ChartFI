These two panels illustrate the contrasting robustness behaviors of CIFAR10 VGG11 networks by comparing confidence scores against PGD robustness, where the left network is trained with standard methods and the right network is trained robustly with TRADES. In both visualizations, the test-point scatter is overlaid with a red curve depicting the lower bound and a dashed line marking maximum confidence near 1.0. Under standard training, robustness stays tightly compressed near zero across almost the entire confidence range with a nearly flat lower bound, indicating that even very confident predictions do not reliably translate into meaningful worst-case robustness; this reveals a confidence–robustness mismatch where confidence saturates without comparable gains in resilience. Conversely, under TRADES training, the relationship strengthens substantially: as confidence rises, the scatter shifts upward into much higher robustness values, and the lower bound transitions from near-zero into a pronounced stepwise climb that accelerates sharply as confidence approaches 1.0. This creates a clear high-confidence, high-robustness regime with a rapidly increasing guaranteed minimum. The lower bound provided by M generally holds on test data, serving as a reliable floor for performance; however, there are very few (7) exceptions to this guarantee, most notably on the right-hand side where 5 data points violate the lower bound.
