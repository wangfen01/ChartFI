This parallel-coordinates view visualizes the multi-dimensional structure of the dataset, where each vertical axis represents one dimension of the archive and each colored line represents a generated terrain passing through the corresponding value for each descriptor. The visualization is colored by Fitness, where higher values trend toward yellow-green and lower values toward purple-blue. A broad analysis of the normalized axes reveals that cells with a ratio descriptor over 0.5 are more rarely filled, suggesting that terrains where the controller mostly receives only one kind of penalty are uncommon. Conversely, many cells with ratio descriptor values below 0.5 are filled, both with and without collisions; this is visually confirmed by the dense clustering of observations at low to mid levels for angular velocity errors, feet contact forces, and both linear velocity error components. Within this distribution, Collision stands out as a strong separator: trajectories that pass through Collision=True are disproportionately associated with lower Fitness colors, while higher Fitness lines more often align with Collision=False, identifying collisions as a major penalty driver. While feet contact forces and linear velocity errors generally rise and fall together in a loose band without a sharp threshold effect, higher Fitness cases tend to sit closer to the lower end of these axes, implying that smoother contact dynamics and smaller velocity tracking errors contribute incrementally to performance. The stumble dimension shows the widest spread, indicating it is the most differentiating descriptor in the set; although a notable anomaly exists where a small number of high-stumble paths achieve moderate Fitness, the highest Fitness values concentrate where stumble is low and collisions are absent, pointing to stability and collision avoidance as the primary levers, with tracking errors acting as secondary refinements.
