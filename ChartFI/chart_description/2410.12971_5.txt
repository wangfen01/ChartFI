This series of four heatmaps illustrates cross-cultural alignment scores for the WVS reference and LLM outputs across three methods, displaying the pairwise relationships between eighteen countries along with their Pearson correlation coefficients relative to the reference distribution. The WVS reference data reveal that cultures naturally cluster into two distinct groups, characterized by high scores within each group and lower scores between groups. The first group consists of cultures from North America (USA, CAN), Western Europe (GBR, NLD, DEU), and Oceania (AUS, NZL), while the second includes cultures from South America (BOL, BRA), Eastern Europe (UKR), and all included cultures from Asia and Africa. When evaluating the model outputs, the Baseline P1 configuration yields a Pearson correlation of 0.87 but shows high alignment scores between some unrelated cultures, which leads to blurred distinctions between cultural groups and a lack of clear separation in the heatmap. In contrast, the CultureSPA approaches demonstrate significantly improved alignment with the ground truth; specifically, the CultureSPA (CCT) (P2+P3) model achieves a Pearson correlation of 0.96. This method generates LLM outputs that more accurately reflect the cultural relationships observed in the reference data, effectively replicating the distinct block patterns and low inter-group alignment scores inherent in the WVS distribution.
