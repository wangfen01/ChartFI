These two heatmaps visualize the average results for Needle In A Haystack (NIAH) tasks in MileBench, comparing the performance of LVLM-S and CoMemo across token lengths ranging from 4k to 32k and depth percentages from 0 to 9. The data reveals that the attention mechanism of LVLMs exhibits a bimodal distribution, where models tend to focus more on the beginning and the most recent tokens, leading to the “Lost in the middle” phenomenon. This is clearly evidenced in the LVLM-S heatmap, which performs strongly at shorter to mid token lengths but shows a pronounced degradation as token length increases into the 24k to 32k range; specifically, scores drop sharply into the orange and red zones at mid depths between 2 and 6, signaling a systematic long-context collapse concentrated in the middle of the depth spectrum. In contrast, CoMemo remains consistently high and stable across nearly all token lengths and depths, effectively mitigating the "Lost in the middle" effect with only isolated anomalies, such as a single sharp drop at the 32k token length around depth 3 and a mild softening near 12k at depth 2. Ultimately, while LVLM-S struggles to maintain information retrieval accuracy as context grows, CoMemo demonstrates substantial robustness to scaling token length, preserving high performance across the majority of the depth spectrum.
