This composite visualization presents the hierarchical structure of the MMAD dataset alongside a comparative performance evaluation of Multimodal Large Language Models. The sunburst chart on the left delineates the dataset across three concentric layers, moving from the innermost image components of normal and abnormal images to the middle layer of subtasks composition, and finally to the outermost layer of object categories. This distribution highlights that MMAD covers 7 key subtasks and 38 representative categories of Industrial Anomaly Detection. On the right, a radar chart illustrates the results of 5 representative MLLMs and Human performance across these specific tasks. A notable left-skewness in the polygon shapes indicates that models perform well on object-related questions but poorly on questions related to defects. Specifically, both open-source and proprietary models perform significantly worse when answering questions related to anomalies and defects compared to questions about objects. Among the evaluated models, GPT-4o is the best-performing model, maintaining the broadest coverage across axes. In contrast, AnomalyGPT performed the worst overall, as the visualization shows it could only handle anomaly discrimination tasks it was trained on and failed to respond adequately to questions in other subtasks.
