The heatmap summarizes the pairwise average returns among LOQA, POLA, MFOS, and a Random benchmark, utilizing AC for Always Cooperate and AD for Always Defect as reference points. Greener cells indicate higher positive rewards, while redder cells indicate negative returns. In terms of cooperative potential, a fully cooperative agent achieves an average reward of 0.35 against itself; similarly, LOQA agents are able to cooperate with themselves, as indicated by the high average reward of 0.30. AD extracts a high return (0.63) from AC, leaving AC with a negative outcome (-0.27). However, more advanced agents mitigate this risk; specifically, LOQA agents are able to achieve high social welfare without being exploited by an always-defect agent, as they achieve an average reward of -0.05, which is comparable to POLA’s own -0.03. This defensive capability contrasts markedly with the Random agent and MFOS, which sustain heavy losses of -0.53 and -0.42, respectively, against AD, demonstrating that LOQA and POLA effectively capture the signal to cooperate internally while resisting the negative impact of unconditional defectors.
