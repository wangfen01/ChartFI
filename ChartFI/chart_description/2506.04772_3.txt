This correlation heatmap compares several text evaluation metrics, showing how closely they move together across the same set of outputs. Overall, we observe that most metrics are highly correlated, particularly within distinct clusters. The strongest relationship is among the n-gram overlap metrics, where BLEU, ROUGE-L, and METEOR are highly aligned with very high pairwise correlations (0.90 to 0.92), indicating they capture a largely overlapping signal of surface-form similarity. Furthermore, the first two columns of the heatmap show a strong correlation between these similarity metrics and negative edit distance, both in relation to the original and the reference paragraph. The two Levenshtein distance variants are themselves strongly correlated (0.88) and show moderate-to-strong positive correlations (0.57 to 0.7) with the n-gram cluster, suggesting that character-level rewriting magnitude is a distinct but related dimension to lexical similarity. BERTScore also tracks the n-gram cluster well, correlating most with METEOR (0.80), suggesting it agrees with surface-form measures while adding distinct semantic sensitivity. GLEU also correlates strongly with the n-gram metrics like BLEU (0.86) but is notably less aligned with the negative Levenshtein distance to the original (0.35), which stands out as one of the weakest relationships in the matrix. The only exception to these strong inter-metric relationships is SARI, which differs from most other metrics; it shows only moderate correlations across the board and is not tightly coupled with the main overlap cluster, consistent with its design to reward a balance of additions, deletions, and keeps, making it a useful complementary signal for evaluating rewriting tasks.
