Notably, the estimated lower bounds for Algorithm 4’s selected cutoffs are lower than the shaded region, yet Algorithm 4 successfully obtains at least one cutoff that passes the safety test of Algorithm 2. In the test-stage panel, the curve increases steadily to a maximum around S = 1.3 to 1.5 before easing, and the policy chosen by Algorithm 5 (omega=1) aligns with this high-benefit region by selecting a threshold near the peak rather than at the extreme right. This divergence suggests that maximizing policy value favors a threshold near the local maximum where gains are highest, rather than pushing to the highest scores where variability increases and the lower confidence bounds for Algorithm 3 and the selected policy may cross the zero line.
