This chart compares four models across different precision formats, illustrating throughput (input plus output tokens per second) as bars, with latency in seconds and RAM usage in gigabytes overlaid as markers. MS-Phi2 is the clear throughput leader, peaking at nearly 600 tokens/s in the FP16 format, while its latency remains consistently low. However, the impact of quantization on performance varies significantly across models. For smaller models such as Phi-2 and Llama3.1- 8B, INT8 inference reduces RAM usage by about 46% but is slower by 62% than FP16, a throughput penalty clearly visible in MS-Phi2's sharp drop from FP16 to INT8. Llama3.1 presents a more balanced profile as a second-tier performer, with moderate throughput that degrades predictably under quantization and stable, moderate latency. In contrast, the larger models exhibit significant operational challenges, with Mistral-Base and Qwen failing to run at certain precisions due to out-of-memory (OOM) errors. For these models, the quantization trade-off is different; for the larger Mistral-Base-24B, INT8 is within 2% of FP16 latency, offering lower penalties, and still reducing RAM by 47%, despite its overall low throughput. Both Mistral-Base and Qwen demonstrate a notable mismatch between resource consumption and output, consuming substantial RAM and incurring high latency without translating it into token speed. This is most pronounced in the case of Qwen, which displays a major anomaly with its latency spiking to over 200 seconds at INT4, signaling a potentially unstable or severely bottlenecked runtime environment.
