This knowledge tracing heatmap compares the predicted performance of three models—GRKT, LPKT, and DKT—by tracing a student’s mastery of KC Addition and Subtraction Integers across a sequential timeline of practice events. Circles positioned above the heatmap indicate performance outcomes, where filled and hollow states mark correct or incorrect responses respectively, and different colors represent different KCs, distinguishing the primary skill from intervening tasks involving Multiplication and Division Integers or Square Roots. Darker red shading corresponds to higher predicted mastery. The visualization demonstrates that GRKT yields reasonable knowledge tracing results, effectively capturing distinct cognitive phenomena; this is evident in the fine-grained knowledge changing from testing effects, where mastery visibly adjusts following successful retrieval, and the faded knowledge with forgetting curves, where the model correctly predicts a decay in mastery during later periods of inactivity. In contrast, while the other models show high saturation, LPKT and DKT still have reasonable issues, such as the mastery change of unrelated KCs and no mastery change of related KCs. Specifically, during the intermediate phase of related multiplication and division practice, these models fail to register expected updates, and in the final segment involving unrelated square root tasks, they—most notably DKT—exhibit an anomalous shift in mastery despite the change in context, suggesting a susceptibility to cross-skill leakage or overconfidence that GRKT successfully avoids.
