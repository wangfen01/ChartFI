This set of heatmaps evaluates the cross-configuration generalization capability of object detection models by reporting the mAP scores in percentage, where models are trained on one configuration and tested on other varying camera configurations. The visualization reveals a stark contrast in performance stability, particularly highlighting the limitations of the baseline approach. In the first matrix, the performance of BEVFusion-C degrades a lot when deployed on cross-camera configuration tasks, rendering it nearly unusable on other configurations. Specifically, BEVFusion-C almost entirely fails when tasked with distinct camera intrinsics with the detection accuracy mostly under 20%. For instance, BEVFusion only gets 1.8% when deploying models trained on 6x80a to 6x60. Even between more similar setups, the model struggles to maintain consistency; BEVFusion-C experiences a performance drop of 5.9% when deploying the model trained on the 6x80b configuration to the 6x80a configuration. In contrast to these failures, the subsequent matrices demonstrate that the detection performance significantly improves compared to BEVFusion-C, with the UniDrive models maintaining robust high-percentage scores across the majority of training and testing pairs.
