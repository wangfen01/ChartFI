This SHAP summary plot delineates the top 10 most important features influencing the output of the final fusion model, ordering them based on the magnitude of their impact on model predictions. The most influential feature is the number of adenomas, which exhibits a clear distribution where higher feature values, represented by red points, extend to the positive side of the axis, demonstrating a positive association with 5-year CRC risk. Following this primary predictor, Age appears as the second most significant variable, displaying a similar trend where increased age correlates with a higher risk prediction. Notably, the predicted risk probability from the WSI was ranked as the third most important feature in the fusion model, exceeded only by the number of adenomas and age, indicating its robust contribution to the risk assessment relative to other clinical factors. The visualization further reveals that while features such as Nulytely preparation tend to negatively impact the model output when high, other indicators like the presence of any advanced adenoma, CSSP, or serrated lesions show a positive contribution to the risk score, though with a lower overall magnitude compared to the top three determinants.
