These four heatmaps illustrate the proportion of cases where the clustering loss is improved over K-means by using the C-LO algorithm across two different initialization methods and dimensions, with D equal to the squared Euclidean distance and each N, K cell representing the results from 1,000 runs of both algorithms. Darker colors indicate a higher frequency of clustering loss improvement, reflecting instances where K-means fails to converge to a C-local solution while the C-LO algorithm consistently achieves a C-local solution with lower clustering loss. For d = 2, both C-LO and C-LO++ show a smooth, monotonic gradient where the differing ratio generally increases as the number of clusters K rises, an effect that becomes more pronounced as the number of points N grows. This trend indicates that K-means often fails to converge to a C-local solution when the number of clusters K is large relative to the number of data points N or when data points are densely distributed in the space, with the darkest regions concentrated at mid-range N and high K rather than at the extreme upper end of N. C-LO++ is consistently slightly darker than C-LO in the same K–N regions, suggesting it yields marginally higher improvement frequencies, especially in the lower-right corner where K is large and N is at its highest values. In contrast, the d = 10 panels appear qualitatively different, with most of the grid near the baseline and only a few isolated darker cells, implying that in higher dimensions, disagreement emerges only in specific configurations rather than scaling smoothly. In C-LO at d = 10, notable increases are localized around mid-to-high K and moderate N, while C-LO++ at d = 10 shows even sparser but sharper spikes, including a prominent high-differing pocket near K in the high 20s and N near 90. Overall, the data reveal a shift from a broad, predictable increase in K-means' failure to reach C-locality at low dimensions to a sparse, episodic pattern at higher dimensions where improvements are concentrated in a few specific parameter combinations.
