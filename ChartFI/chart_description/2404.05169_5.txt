The figure presents a comparison of the correct sample AUC on the top and a visualization of the joint uncertainty-loss distribution on the bottom, evaluating four methods under 50% Mis-H + 15% Mis-L and 80% Mis-H + 15% Mis-L noise rates. In the upper panels, QMix maintains a high AUC of separating Correct throughout the entire training process, staying essentially flat around the low 0.91 range in the 50% noise setting and rising to the high 0.87–0.88 range in the 80% setting. In comparison, the correct sample AUC scores of DivideMix and UNICON decline significantly as the training progresses; for instance, under 50% noise, DivideMix drops from roughly 0.89 toward the low 0.82s, and UNICON slides from the high 0.88s to around 0.80 by epoch 100. This deterioration is even more severe in the 80% Mis-H setting, where DivideMix falls to roughly 0.69, while AugDMix remains comparatively stable around the low 0.82–0.84 range. The lower panels elucidate the mechanism behind these trajectories by tracking how the models distinguish sample types. After 10 epochs of warm-up training, Correct is initially mixed with Mis-H and Mis-L, showing significant overlap in the loss-uncertainty space. However, as training evolves, QMix manages to separate three types of training samples into distinct clusters by the final epoch, with correct samples concentrating at lower loss and uncertainty while mislabeled samples shift toward higher values. This distinct separation aligns with QMix’s robust AUC performance, whereas the methods with declining AUCs fail to maintain such clear boundaries, allowing noise influence to amplify over time.
