The figure illustrates the power-area-accuracy design space of a 16×16 PTC weight array (k1=k2=16), presenting two heatmaps over the same design space with waveguide arm spacing ls (7 to 25 μm) on the y-axis and MZI gap lg (1 to 15 μm) on the x-axis. Panel (a) reports the PTC weight array area (A_PTC,wgt), which increases steadily as either parameter increases, forming a clear monotonic gradient from a minimum footprint of 0.43 mm²at short lengths and small gaps to 1.40 mm² at the largest dimensions. Conversely, the total MZI power (P_MZI) indicated on the left-side color bar demonstrates an inverse relationship with arm spacing, decreasing from 1.82 W at ls=7μm to 1.35 W by ls=25μm, highlighting a tradeoff where longer structures reduce power but require more area. Panel (b) displays the CNN-FMNIST accuracy under crosstalk plus noise relative to an ideal reference of 91.40%. A notable pattern is that the main performance penalty is concentrated at the smallest gap (lg=1μm), where accuracy is consistently lowest (mid-83% range), implying crosstalk sensitivity dominates there. Accuracy improves sharply as lg increases, stabilizing near the ideal value for lg≥7μm. For a dense PTC, to meet the accuracy target (e.g., <1% drop) while optimizing efficiency, specific parameters are identified from these trends; based on panel (a), the most efficient arm spacing is determined to be ls=9μm, which minimizes the power-area product (PAP). Furthermore, to ensure the <1% accuracy drop constraint is met, the minimum MZI horizontal gap lg is conservatively set to 5μm, a point where accuracy reaches approximately 90.5–90.7%, successfully balancing high fidelity with minimized spatial footprint.
