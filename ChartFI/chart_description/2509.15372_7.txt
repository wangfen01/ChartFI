This plot expresses the point estimates of realism of an animation broken down by the motion within the animation across eight types and the participant’s degree of experience with VRChat (Low vs. Medium vs. High). Error bars represent 95% confidence intervals, indicating that within each facet, estimates that are significantly different at alpha < 0.05 do not share any letters labeling the error bar. The data reveals that Low-experience ratings show the widest spread between conditions, with Source and Collision rated significantly higher than the lower-scoring cluster of Overlapping, Position, and Reenacted. While Medium-experience participants tend to rate most animations slightly higher and more consistently—peaking with Reenacted—the data highlights a non-linear progression where certain motion features show a “rebound” in scores across experience levels. The Rotation motion feature illustrates this pattern: whereas it sits at a low-to-mid level for Low experience, it becomes the distinctly least realistic condition for Medium experience, representing a notable anomaly before scores rebound among High-experience viewers. For the High-experience group, most animation types are tightly grouped around mid-level realism, with the exception of Jittering, which drops to the lowest rating; however, Source trends higher than the rest across all three panels, suggesting a consistent perception that Source-based animation looks more realistic regardless of expertise.
