This visualization compares the magnitude distribution similarity across different tasks under two experimental setups, FT and FT + MIGU, where darker cells indicate higher similarity and the diagonal represents perfect self-similarity. Under the FT configuration, the heatmap exhibits numerous mid-to-high off-diagonal similarities, suggesting strong coupling or shared representations across multiple tasks. However, after adding MIGU, the overall matrix becomes visibly lighter off the diagonal, indicating that the similarity in FT + MIGU is lower and demonstrating a broad reduction in cross-task interference. To illustrate this shift, the two sub-figures at the bottom focus on three highlighted task samples: BoolQA, COPA, and Yelp. These detailed views further highlight the similarity between the BoolQA, COPA, and Yelp tasks and the notable decrease in similarity among these three tasks following the model adjustment. Specifically, the connection between BoolQA and COPA drops sharply from 0.50 to 0.20, and the BoolQA–Yelp pairing falls from 0.60 to 0.30, while the COPA–Yelp relationship decreases from 0.40 to 0.30. This systematic dampening of inter-task similarity, particularly among pairs that were previously most similar, indicates that the inclusion of MIGU effectively reduces unintended task interference and encourages more specialized task representations.
