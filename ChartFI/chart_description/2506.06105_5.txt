These two scatterplots display a 2D t-SNE projection of activations of T2L’s task encoder and activations of the last MLP block, grouped by benchmark tasks represented by colors and shapes. We can see clear clustering in both projection plots based on the tasks, though the spatial arrangement evolves significantly between stages. In the encoded task embedding, tasks form relatively tight, well-separated groups with a strong left–right split where similar tasks, e.g., MBPP and HumanEval, are clustered together on the far negative x side with low y values. GSM8K is also on the negative x side but closer to the center, while several reading and QA-style datasets concentrate on the positive x side with generally higher y values; notably, Winogrande and Hellaswag occupy the highest y region, while PIQA is distinct for staying on the right but dipping into negative y. In the T2L activation view, the geometry shifts substantially, yet similar tasks like MBPP and HumanEval remain clustered together, now moving to the far positive x side along with GSM8K. Conversely, datasets such as Hellaswag, Winogrande, and OpenbookQA concentrate on the far negative x side, and ARC_easy and ARC_challenge cluster in the negative x and negative y quadrant, with ARC_challenge showing an extreme low-y point that stands out as the clearest anomaly. Overall, the encoded embedding suggests cleaner task-type clustering, whereas the T2L activations reconfigure similarity so that code-generation tasks become a distinct positive-x island and the ARC datasets become jointly separated by strongly negative y, implying the activation space is more sensitive to reasoning difficulty or format-specific processing.
