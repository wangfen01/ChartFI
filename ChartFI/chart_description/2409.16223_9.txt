This set of heatmaps illustrates the performance of different optimizers and hyperparameters, revealing distinct stability profiles across varying learning rate multipliers and weight decay settings. A dominant trend is that fine-tuning with SGD shows remarkable robustness to different learning rates and weight decay; its performance values remain tightly clustered between approximately 0.57 and 0.63 across the entire grid, indicating no meaningful degradation even at a 10x multiplier. In contrast, advanced optimizers necessitate more careful hyperparameter selection, as their effectiveness is heavily dependent on the learning-rate multiplier. Nevertheless, they perform similarly to SGD under small learning rates and weight decay, with Adam, AdaBelief, Adadelta, and AdaGrad achieving competitive scores around 0.62 to 0.63 at the 0.01x setting. However, these adaptive methods exhibit a sharp performance cliff as the learning rate increases: Adam and AdaBelief begin to falter at 1x—though Adam shows earlier sensitivity to weight decay at 0.1x—and eventually collapse to near-zero scores at 10x. Adadelta and AdaGrad follow a similar trajectory, deteriorating markedly at 1x and becoming unusable at 10x, while RMSprop stands out as an anomaly with near-zero performance almost everywhere except for a specific pocket at 0.01x with zero weight decay. Ultimately, while conservative learning rates yield consistently high results for most optimizers, aggressive rates trigger widespread failure in adaptive algorithms, highlighting SGD as the uniquely resilient choice across the full hyperparameter spectrum.
