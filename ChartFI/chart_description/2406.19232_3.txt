This heatmap displays the Delta-scores (downarrow) for each LM and K% in {30, 40, 50, 60}, revealing a clear trend where, in general, the higher the K% value, the lower the Delta-score for most LMs. This systemic degradation is illustrated by the overall average, which steadily declines from -5.8 at k=30 to roughly -6.9 at k=60. We find that the overall performance can drop from 2.9% to 12%, highlighting a significant variance in robustness across different architectures; for example, multilingual baselines like mBERT and XLM-R variants dominate the strongest negative region, often worsening beyond -10 as k increases. Furthermore, the Delta-score can depend on the model size (e.g., ruGPT, bloom, and Llama-2), though the direction of this dependency varies: within the ruGPT and Llama families, larger models generally exhibit slightly better resistance to degradation than their smaller counterparts, whereas the Bloom family shows the inverse, with bloom-7b1 suffering the most severe drop to -12 at k=60. In sharp contrast to these widespread negative trends, the Delta-scores for RemBERT and MDeBERTa are positive, identifying them as significant anomalies that remain stable or even improve slightly, peaking around 3.1 for MDeBERTa at the highest k setting.
