This heatmap displays the KL divergence in emotion labels by language pair, where the lighter the color, the more emotion agreement between the languages and darker cells indicate larger mismatches. There are two large clusters in the plot; the larger cluster contains mostly languages from Africa, such as Swahili, Yoruba, and IsiXhosa, which exhibit a stable block of light shading indicating highly similar emotion distributions. The smaller cluster contains mostly languages from Asia, including Korean, Thai, and Vietnamese. While these clusters suggest broadly comparable emotion profiles within regions, there are clear pockets of elevated divergence that break this pattern. The most pronounced shift appears around Kyrgyz, which shows consistently high divergence against nearly every other language, standing out as the strongest outlier in the matrix. A second area of higher divergence is visible for Burmese and, to a lesser extent, Indonesian, which tend to diverge more from the central groups. Additionally, the matrix displays the asymmetry typical of KL divergence, where the divergence from one language to another is not perfectly mirrored across the diagonal, implying that some languages have more peaked or uneven emotion distributions that penalize mismatches differently depending on the direction of comparison.
