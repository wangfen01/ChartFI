The four heatmaps present the values of InvRE with LeNet under different defense strategies and strengths in HFL, specifically comparing DNP, ENP, Prune, and Dropout across four tasks (IM, CI, ST, SV). Across all panels, increasing the defense strength significantly reduces privacy risks, as measured by the InvRE score, with values generally declining from the baseline in the leftmost column as the setting becomes more aggressive. This reduction is achieved through two distinct mechanisms: noise perturbation-based defenses (DNP and ENP), which introduce noise to the raw features to disrupt the attacker’s ability to reconstruct data features, and information compression-based defenses (Prune and Dropout), which mitigate privacy leakage by discarding a portion of the shared information. Among the noise perturbation methods, DNP shows a smooth, monotonic degradation as the parameter moves from 0 to 0.05; ST starts highest at 0.731 and remains the strongest, while CI is consistently the weakest, with all tasks eventually converging toward the low 0.15 to 0.28 range. In contrast, ENP exhibits the sharpest collapse and clearest threshold behavior, where values drop steeply by 0.3 and become near-zero for ST, IM, and CI by 0.4 to 0.5—falling from 0.731 to 0.003 for ST—signaling a critical tipping point where the method becomes overly destructive. Turning to information compression, Pruning degrades more gently and preserves comparatively higher values at the strongest settings; even at 0.99, tasks remain in the mid 0.33 to 0.38 range, with IM notably resilient at 0.378. Dropout also declines monotonically but tends to compress task differences more than pruning, with all tasks clustering tightly around 0.323 to 0.327 at 0.99, suggesting it equalizes the InvRE score as intensity increases. Ultimately, while ENP acts as a clear anomaly with a pronounced phase transition, the information compression methods offer a more controllable tradeoff curve, effectively lowering privacy risks without the catastrophic collapse seen in the most sensitive noise perturbation settings.
