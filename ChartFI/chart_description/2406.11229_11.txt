The performance of the array across eight operational modes is characterized by the measured scattering parameters, realized gain, and phase of radiation patterns as a function of frequency and scan angle. In all operational modes, the array maintains a good impedance matching at 10 GHz, with a -10 dB S_11 bandwidth ranging from 9.75 to 10.2 GHz, following a consistent broadband shape where the strongest notch typically dips into the high -20s dB. While the reflection coefficient shows only modest mode-dependent detuning near the band edges of 9.0 GHz and 11.0 GHz, the radiation patterns exhibit more significant variation across the different modes of operation. The measured amplitude and phase of radiation patterns demonstrate that the radiation pattern of the array along the boresight direction does not change, maintaining a stable peak gain of approximately 10 to 12 dBi at 0 degrees, while the sidelobes change significantly for different modes of operation. These sidelobe variations are marked by multiple sharp nulls and ripples that shift with the mode, particularly at larger scan angles where deep dropouts suggest mode-sensitive interference or resonances. The phase response reinforces these observations, showing that while the phase tends to cluster and vary smoothly near 0 degrees, it becomes highly discontinuous with frequent jumps toward ±180 degrees away from broadside, aligning with the onset of gain irregularities and pattern distortion. Consequently, while the modes are tightly aligned in their impedance behavior, they diverge meaningfully in angular stability, with certain modes exhibiting more pronounced gain collapses and phase discontinuities at specific scan angles.
