These density plots illustrate the distributions of the average number of subwords per verse across four popular tokenizers—mBERT, Llama 3.3, Phi-4, and Qwen 2.5—using different sampling strategies. Across all panels, the Convenience strategy is consistently the shortest and most tightly clustered, concentrating around the mid‑40s to mid‑50s subwords, though Llama 3.3, Phi‑4, and Qwen 2.5 display rare, extreme Convenience outliers in the far-right 170–200+ range that are largely absent in mBERT. In contrast, the MaxMin and MaxSum strategies shift the mass to longer sequences with broader, right‑skewed shapes. Crucially, MaxMin and MaxSum retrieve averages and distributions that resemble the distribution over the entire sampling frame (the "All" condition), which exhibits a noticeable right tail. While mBERT shows the clearest separation between Convenience and the other strategies—which center around the 50–80 range—the other three models show more overlap in their central ranges of 55 to 100. Despite the distributional similarities between the maximization strategies, the subtle shifts in density, particularly where MaxSum maintains higher probability mass in upper ranges (visible in Llama 3.3 and Phi-4), suggest that sampling with the MaxSum objective leads to a more conservative performance estimate than the MaxMin objective. Ultimately, the data indicate that strategy choice drives tokenization length more than model choice in the center of the distribution, while model choice dictates the behavior of long‑sequence outliers.
