The heatmap compares unlearning accuracy across five methods—Retrain, GA, ICO, CVFU, and FedORA—over multiple dataset and parameter settings, revealing that performance is highly dataset-dependent and no single method dominates universally. On the Income dataset, all five methods exhibit higher unlearning accuracy relative to other datasets, clustering at the top of the chart with ICO consistently delivering the best results (about 0.69 to 0.72) and GA/CVFU forming a strong second tier. A clear shift appears on MedMNIST with m=2, where accuracies collapse for every method to a range of roughly 0.13 to 0.28, suggesting a regime where unlearning is broadly difficult. For CIFAR-10, results sit in the low-to-mid range and are volatile, with ICO and CVFU tending to be strongest at m=2 and GA becoming best at m=5. The most significant anomaly occurs on CIFAR-100, where Retrain’s unlearning accuracy remains above 59 %, dramatically outperforming alternative methods which struggle to match this gold-standard retraining on the harder dataset. In this specific setting, while GA, ICO, and CVFU achieve lower unlearning accuracy than Retrain, FedORA reaches below 35 %, indicating that approximate unlearning methods, particularly FedORA, face severe effectiveness challenges on complex image tasks compared to the robust performance of Retrain. Tiny-ImageNet shows moderate performance with CVFU slightly ahead at m=40 and GA ahead at m=100, confirming that at larger-scale image tasks, the best method can flip with configuration and that gains are often incremental rather than step-change improvements.
