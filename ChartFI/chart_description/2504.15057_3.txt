These two scatterplots provide a performance comparison over the Recall@20 and the log-scaled number of floating-point operations (FLOPs) for inference on Diginetica and Yoochoose datasets, indicating how LINK successfully mitigates the accuracy-efficiency trade-off compared to existing models. A stark contrast is evident between model families, as the three linear models (LINK, SWalk, and SLIST) are positioned at the lowest computational cost of roughly 10^10 FLOPs while achieving top-tier accuracy. In comparison, most neural network-based models require two to three orders of magnitude more computation, generally between 10^12 and 10^13 FLOPs. This efficiency gap is quantified by the numbers in parentheses, which indicate the relative FLOPs compared to LINK; linear models demonstrate significantly higher efficiency than neural models, requiring up to 813 times fewer flops on Yoochoose for comparable or better performance. On the Diginetica dataset, LINK achieves the highest recall at approximately 54, whereas competing GNN and Transformer models cluster between 48–53 recall at a much greater computational expense. This pattern of diminishing returns is even more pronounced on the Yoochoose dataset, where LINK is tied for the best recall near 65 while high-compute models like SCL and GCE-GNN use 813 times more FLOPs without a corresponding increase in accuracy. Ultimately, among linear models, LINK exhibits superior performance, elevating the accuracy limits of linear models while maintaining their computational efficiency and establishing a new state-of-the-art frontier for both accuracy and efficiency.
