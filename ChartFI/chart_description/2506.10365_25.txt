This grid of radial bar charts visualizes the error type distribution of each model across three evaluation settings—Unit, Combo, and Theme—organized by columns corresponding to error categories (Parameter, Output Type Mismatch, Wrong Answer, Syntax, Network). Within each subplot, bar length indicates the error rate per model, with color-coding used to highlight statistical anomalies: red bars denote high-error outliers (defined as errors greater than or equal to the maximum minus 0.5 times the standard deviation), green bars denote low-error outliers (defined as errors less than or equal to the minimum plus 0.5 times the standard deviation), and gray bars indicate the normal range. Across all three settings, Wrong Answer and Parameter errors dominate the landscape; however, parameter-related errors are relatively balanced across models, appearing as a widespread issue rather than a localized defect. In contrast, the syntax error dimension reveals a distinct anomaly where the Qwen-3-Thinking series shows a significantly higher error rate, far exceeding other models and indicating a consistent structural bias in an otherwise low-error category. Moving from Unit to Combo, the distribution shifts toward broader and often higher wrong-answer and network-error spikes, suggesting that combining requirements increases both reasoning failure and operational fragility. Output Type Mismatch remains relatively muted in Unit but becomes more pronounced for a few models in Combo and Theme, implying that stricter formatting expectations expose specific compliance weaknesses. Finally, the Theme setting generally mirrors the Unit regarding parameter and wrong-answer profiles but exhibits sharper, more isolated peaks in network error, consistent with theme-driven prompts triggering increased external-call reliance and connectivity-related failures.
