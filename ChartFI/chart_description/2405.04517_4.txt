This heatmap presents the results given by the scaled accuracy of different models at solving formal language tasks, of which some require state tracking, with values scaled between 0 (random) and 1 (perfect). The different tasks are grouped by the Chomsky hierarchy—Context Sensitive, Deterministic Context Free, and Regular—alongside Majority tasks, to compare xLSTM to other methods, including Transformers, State Space Models, and Recurrent Neural Networks. Specifically, the analysis compares architectures of xLSTM[0:1] (i.e., only sLSTM), xLSTM[1:0] (i.e., only mLSTM), and xLSTM[1:1] against Llama, Mamba, RWKV, Retention, Hyena, LSTM, and LSTM in Transformer blocks. While the Even Pairs task is essentially saturated with nearly every model at or near 1.0, excluding a lower performance by Retention, the Parity and Mod Arithmetic (w/o Brackets) tasks reveal a critical limitation: models such as Transformers or State Space Models without memory mixing (no state tracking) cannot solve regular grammars like the parity task. Consequently, Llama, Mamba, Retention, and Hyena remain near zero on these regular tasks, whereas LSTM and xLSTM[0:1] achieve consistent perfection. This sharp separation persists in the Deterministic Context Free tasks, where Mod Arithmetic with brackets and Solve Equation are near-zero for the Transformer and SSM baselines, while LSTM variants and xLSTM[0:1] demonstrate superior handling of hierarchical structures with scores reaching the mid-to-high range. On Context Sensitive tasks, Bucket Sort is generally high for Llama, RWKV-6, and the LSTM family, but Missing Duplicate remains difficult, with even the top-performing xLSTM[1:0] reaching only 0.33. Finally, the Majority and Majority Count columns display moderate spread, with LSTM proving strongest on Majority and xLSTM[1:1] achieving the best performance on Majority Count.
