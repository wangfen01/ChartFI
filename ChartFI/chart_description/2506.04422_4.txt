This beeswarm plot of SHAP values per feature illustrates the role of each feature from the predictions on the test set using SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP), with features shown in descending order of importance. As the dominant driver of the model, views exhibits the widest spread of SHAP values, where high feature values—indicated by pink points—exert a strong positive impact on the model output while low values pull the prediction downward. Topical indicators for topic_higgs and topic_brexit follow as the next most influential factors, showing concentrated positive contributions when these features are high, suggesting these specific subjects are associated with significantly higher predicted outcomes. In contrast, duration and channel-level metadata such as channel_numvids and channel_age form a second tier of influence with more nuanced effects; duration displays a mixed pattern where both high and low values can negatively impact the prediction, while higher channel age and video counts generally trend toward slightly positive impacts despite some diminishing returns. Engagement metrics like likes and comments, along with channel_subs, mostly cluster near the zero axis with mild positive tilts at higher values, indicating they reinforce predictions rather than driving them independently. A notable anomaly is observed in channel_views, where a small cluster of low-value observations exerts a disproportionately strong negative SHAP impact, highlighting specific instances where low historical reach sharply penalizes the outcome. Finally, remaining features such as topic_capriot, topic_grammys, and quality indicators for HD and SD show very tight clustering around zero, signaling that these variables provide minimal incremental information once the primary drivers are accounted for.
