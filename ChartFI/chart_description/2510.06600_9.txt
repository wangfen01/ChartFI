The two radar charts compare the emotional capability of various large language models on the EDOS dataset, visualizing emotion-category intensity profiles across distinct model sizes. The left panel illustrates the performance of smaller LLMs, showing that Mistral-Nemo and Phi-3.5-mini perform notably well, with Phi-3.5 exhibiting pronounced peaks and troughs that suggest a polarized but responsive output. In contrast, Llama 3.1_8b demonstrates weaker capabilities, appearing comparatively restrained and excelling only in the emotions of ashamed, angry, and hopeful. The right panel presents the performance of larger LLMs, indicating that ChatGPT-Turbo offers a more comprehensive and balanced emotional capacity, tending to maintain higher intensity across mid-to-high regions. Conversely, Claude-Haiku shows advantages only in confident and caring emotions, otherwise displaying a more limited profile with fewer extreme spikes. Overall, while the distributions share a broadly similar spiky shape where specific emotions dominate, a clear performance hierarchy emerges: for similar LLMs, Llama 3.1_8b and Claude-Haiku display relatively weak emotional perception abilities, whereas ChatGPT-Turbo possesses a more comprehensive emotional capability compared to Claude-Haiku.
