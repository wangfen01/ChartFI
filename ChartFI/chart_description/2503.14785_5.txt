This visualization compares the performance of five kernels—Gaussian, Gibbs, Deep Kernel (DK), Arc-Kernel (AK), and SEEK—across four benchmark problems with varying degrees of nonlinearity and dimensionality, utilizing Normalized Root Mean Square Error (NRMSE) and Normalized Negative Objective Improvement Score (NNOIS) as evaluation metrics. Across all test cases, SEEK emerges as the most consistently strong performer, typically delivering the lowest error and lowest objective values with relatively tight spreads, achieving the best performance in terms of NRMSE and NNOIS. While other nonstationary kernels, such as the Gibbs and deep kernels, show noticeable improvements over the Gaussian kernel in several instances, they do not demonstrate the same level of robustness across different repetitions compared to SEEK, as reflected by their wider variations in the violin plots. This instability is particularly evident in Analytic I, where Gibbs and DK exhibit wide distributions suggesting sensitivity to initialization, and in Hartmann, where Gibbs shows the most pronounced instability with metrics spanning a very wide range and reaching the worst observed values. In contrast, AK does not perform well on any of the benchmark problems with exceptionally large NRMSE and NNOIS values that position it as a major outlier. The overall pattern indicates that SEEK uniquely combines low central values with low dispersion, ensuring both accuracy and robustness, whereas other nonstationary methods are prone to extreme variability and AK suffers from systematic degradation across diverse problem landscapes.
