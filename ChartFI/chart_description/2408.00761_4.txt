The three heatmaps display post-attack accuracies for weaponization restriction methods across Biosecurity, Chemical Security, and Cybersecurity domains under various adversaries, where values show percentages, with Random Chance (RC) at 25 % and “ND” indicating No Defense WMDP scores. Red indicates attack performance approaching No Defense levels. While baseline safeguards withstand fine-tuning attacks in a small number of cases, most adversaries succeed in removing the safeguards across the four baseline restriction methods (Max Entropy, Min Posterior, LLMU, RMU). In Biosecurity, Max Entropy, Min Posterior, and RMU cluster tightly in the mid-to-high 60s across most adversaries, approaching the ND maximum of 71; LLMU is mostly comparable but exhibits a pronounced mid-sequence drop around Adv 13–14 where accuracy falls sharply into the high 20s and low 30s before rising again. In Chemical security, the non-TAR methods typically sit in the mid-40s to low-50s near the ND score of 48, with occasional dips such as Min Posterior showing a notable trough around Adv 20 and LLMU having clear low points around Adv 6 and Adv 11–12. In Cybersecurity, the non-TAR methods hover in the low-to-mid 40s near the ND score of 46, with exceptions like Max Entropy dipping around Adv 20 and LLMU showing a localized drop around Adv 12. By contrast, TAR maintains low post-attack forget accuracy across all three domains. While existing baselines break down under most attacks, TAR obtains a post-attack forget accuracy near random chance for nearly all attacks, indicating a successful defense. Specifically, TAR Bio spends much of the early and middle adversary range in the mid-20s, showing only a few isolated spikes into the 50s and 60s that represent adversary-specific failures rather than a sustained trend. TAR Chem remains near the low-to-high 20s with small, sporadic lifts into the 30s, and TAR Cyber mostly stays in the mid-to-high 20s with a few modest rises into the low-to-mid 30s late in the sequence, reinforcing that TAR consistently resists adversarial pressure and prevents the recovery of restricted knowledge far better than the non-TAR baselines.
