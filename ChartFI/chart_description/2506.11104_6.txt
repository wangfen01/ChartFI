These three radar charts compare the LV-Eval retrieval score of five methods across eight long-context QA tasks, illustrating how performance scales at 16k, 32k, and 64k context lengths. The central finding is that DAM closely matches LLaMA 3.2 3B, across all datasets and context lengths, consistently outperforming alternative sparse attention methods. At the 16k and 32k lengths, both DAM and LLaMA 3.2 3B establish a clear performance ceiling, showing particularly strong results on benchmarks like cmrc and the multifieldqa variants. The most significant trend emerges at the 64k context length, where a stark performance divergence occurs. While DAM remains robust and continues to closely follow full attention, peaking sharply on multifieldqa_en, the other models exhibit a marked decline. Specifically, MoA, StreamingLLM, and H2O lose performance as sequence length increases, with their performance polygons contracting noticeably across nearly every benchmark, indicating instability and a failure to scale effectively to longer contexts.
