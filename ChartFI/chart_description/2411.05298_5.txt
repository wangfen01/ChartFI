This chart illustrates a comprehensive driving cycle spanning 10^4 seconds, combining a temporal velocity trace with a distance-based road-slope profile to evaluate vehicle performance under varying loads. The driving cycle comprises several standard and representative cycles, including NYCC, WLTP, US06, SC03, and additional smooth driving cycles, which are segmented by background color to denote specific testing phases. A critical feature of this configuration is the distinction between terrain variants, where L and S represent large road slope and small road slope conditions, respectively. The velocity profile is highly transient, shifting from the dense, low-speed stop-and-go oscillations typical of the NYCC phases to sustained high-speed plateaus in the US06 and WLTP segments, where peaks approach 120 to 140 km/h. The lower panel highlights the physical disparity between the conditions: while the small slope segments remain relatively flat near 0 degrees, the large slope segments display distinct "hilly" windows where variability expands sharply, swinging from approximately −15 to +15 degrees. These high-variance slope sections frequently align with the higher-speed portions of the US06 and WLTP phases, indicating periods where the vehicle must overcome both significant aerodynamic drag and demanding grade changes, before returning to the flatter terrain and moderate cruising characteristic of the "Other" segments.
