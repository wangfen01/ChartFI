The figure combines a layerwise proportion view with attention heatmaps to illustrate how token types contribute across the network and where attention concentrates. Panel A displays the percentage of system tokens, image tokens, and prompt tokens on the attention weight of the answer, while Panel B presents the attention map of system tokens, image tokens, prompt tokens, and answer tokens. In the initial processing stage, it can be observed that the attention scores for image tokens decrease rapidly in layers 1-5 and stabilize in layers 6-31. Specifically, we observe that after the third layer, image tokens account for less than 2% of the total compared to system and prompt tokens, with their weights ranging from 0 to 0.2. Consequently, the attention allocated to image tokens is significantly lower throughout these layers than system and user tokens. During this stable intermediate phase, System Weight dominates the majority of the stack, while User Weight grows steadily to a clear mid-layer peak before declining toward the final layers. However, a notable shift occurs at the end of the network, where image and user tokens’ attention scores increase rapidly at the 32nd layer. Panel B’s attention maps reinforce this progression, showing early diffuse structures that transition into a regime of concentrated activity aligned to the system side with comparatively weak engagement with image tokens, until the final layer reflects the re-emergence of broader token influence.
