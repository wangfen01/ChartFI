The performance of several state-of-the-art trackers is evaluated in terms of AUC across 14 various challenging scenarios in the LaSOT test split, revealing clear tiers of capability. SPMTrack-B is the clear top performer and the most stable, tracing the outer performance envelope on nearly every attribute, which suggests robust handling of both appearance and geometric variation. This performance surpasses previous trackers that also incorporate spatio-temporal context, significantly outperforming ROMTrack and GRM. Most other methods cluster in a middle tier with more pronounced trade-offs: SeqTrack-L-384 and AQATrack-384 tend to stay relatively high and balanced, while HIPTrack is competitive on appearance-related shifts like deformation but shows a noticeable drop under fast motion. ARTrackV2-B is generally moderate but exhibits sharper declines in fast motion and out-of-view cases, pointing to limitations in recovery after large target displacement. GRM, stands out as the weakest and most volatile, sitting well inside the others across most conditions and falling further on motion-heavy and occlusion-related attributes, while stronger methods are comparatively less disrupted by challenges like illumination variation and aspect ratio change.
