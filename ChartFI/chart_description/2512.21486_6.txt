The visual analysis presents learned latent factors, where the left panel corresponds to U^1 (latitude) and the right panel corresponds to U^2 (longitude), illustrating how the underlying index varies with geography. To aid in distinguishing regional patterns, groups of cities are highlighted with shaded circles for clarity. The latitude factor (left) exhibits a clear gradient from south to north; values are initially high and volatile in the high-20s to low-30s, encompassing cities like Phoenix and Dallas, before drifting downward through the mid-30s to upper-40s where clusters including Washington, Denver, Portland, and Minneapolis reside. This downward trend culminates in lower values aligning with northern locations such as Anchorage, Alaska, which sits near the minimum around −6, creating the clearest discontinuity in the entire view. In contrast, the longitude factor (right) reveals a smooth transition from east to west across the geographic domain, although the function value itself is much noisier than the latitude factor. Ultimately, while the far-west point for Anchorage is comparatively stable, the dominant pattern indicates that the extremeness of the outlier behavior is driven more by latitude, whereas longitude primarily introduces short-range variability without a strong monotonic trend in the function values.
