This radar chart compares the performance of various vision-language models across a broad set of video understanding QA tasks, spanning spatial reasoning, object and character understanding, and temporal/causal comprehension. Overall, the larger LLaVA variants, LLaVA-Video-72B and LLaVA-OneVision-72B, demonstrate the most robust and consistent capabilities, forming the outer performance envelope on most axes. These models show particularly strong performance in tasks such as character, object, plot and scene understanding, indicating strong all-around capability rather than narrow specialization. While many models perform well on foundational skills, a clear pattern emerges where all models struggle with tasks requiring deep understanding, such as time-order, temporal-relationship, counting, and camera-direction, which exhibit generally lower scores and mark them as more challenging and differentiating benchmarks. In contrast to these general trends, some models exhibit unique strengths; for instance, GPT-4o performs better in the speed task than in some other tasks, where its performance is comparable to the leading models. A notable anomaly is the clear underperformance of Claude 3.5 Sonnet, which presents a much smaller polygon, suggesting weaker video-centric reasoning across nearly all categories. This performance distribution suggests that improvements from smaller 7B-class models to larger 72B-class models are more substantial on complex temporal and causal axes than on basic object or scene comprehension, indicating that scaling and video-focused training yield the biggest gains in higher-order narrative and temporal reasoning rather than in core recognition.
