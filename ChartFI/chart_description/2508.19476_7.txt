Across eight heatmaps, the resulting motion is plotted after measuring net and peak forces for all policies, with the left column showing resultant motion as a function of measured net force and the right column showing resulting motion as a function of measured peak force. For reference, the values of F net and F peak are plotted as vertical black dashed lines, which represent the excessive force values used for the task. In these visualizations, darker shading indicates where observations concentrate, and cells toward the bottom right of each plot correspond to a large observed force but little resulting motion—indicating a failure to respond. The baseline policy displays the highest proportion of large force, low motion datapoints, containing a substantial number of these events, indicating a failure to react to contacts. This failure is visually evident as a distinct vertical band of high density near the excessive net force reference line. In contrast, the remaining policies largely avoid low motion as the observed forces increase, shifting their distributions to flatten and lighten with fewer concentrated hot spots at high forces. Notably, the tactile-informed policy appears to have more datapoints at large peak force and low motion than either the wrench-informed or wrench + tactile policies, indicating that wrench sensing appears to be the most effective modality for reacting to large forces. The combined wrench plus tactile policy continues this trend of improvement, showing the most diffuse, low-intensity densities and successfully dampening the pronounced localized anomalies exhibited by the baseline condition.
