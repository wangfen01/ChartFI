The correlation matrix heatmap displays a computed correlation matrix in percentage used to evaluate the degree of similarity of different features across a dataset of 36 collected features. The visualization reveals a clear split between tightly coupled variable families and largely independent groups, with several near-perfect relationships that suggest significant redundancy. Vegetation indices move almost in lockstep, where SAVI and NDVI are nearly identical at 99%, NDMI is strongly aligned with them at 94-96%, and NDBI is strongly inversely related to the vegetation set at -94% to -100%, indicating a classic vegetation versus built-up contrast. EVI is also extremely highly correlated with these vegetation indices, reinforcing that these measures capture the same underlying greenness signal. Thermal variables form another coherent block, where NLST shows moderate negative relationships with vegetation indices, consistent with cooler surfaces where vegetation is higher. Several land-cover or landscape metrics cluster strongly, particularly the LKM and LKC pair and the grouped L10 and L5 measures, which display high within-group correlations pointing to shared scale effects. Out of the total 36 collected features, 12 features exhibited high similarity, and features with a similarity above 90 % were excluded from the dataset to reduce collinearity. The 12 excluded features were SAVI, NDMI, EVI, NDBI, male and female population, net dependency rate, total employment rate, LKC, M100KM, L10KC, and L5KC indices. Air and weather variables appear more weakly connected overall, though PM2.5 stands out with a moderate positive association with temperature (T2) and a moderate positive link with the M100 metrics, suggesting particulate levels are more tied to meteorology and certain land-use intensity proxies than to greenness directly. Wind speed and evaporation-related measures show mostly low-to-moderate correlations, implying they contribute additional, less redundant information compared with the highly collinear remote-sensing indices.
