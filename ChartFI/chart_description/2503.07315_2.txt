The density plots illustrate the distribution of sample weights for each group that is used to train the best models across four distinct datasets. A consistent structural dichotomy emerges where the minority groups have the weight distribution stretched out towards high values, while the majority group weights are generally skewed towards 0. The samples from the majority groups are consistently assigned with lower weights after reweighting; for instance, in Waterbirds, the majority classes are sharply peaked near zero, whereas the minority settings—such as landbirds in water—shift rightward with clear secondary humps. Similarly, in CelebA, while three groups remain tightly concentrated near zero, the blond, male minority group exhibits a much longer tail and higher density away from zero, signaling that this subgroup drives a nontrivial share of the optimization signal. The MNLI dataset reflects this pattern on a more compressed scale, with most categories clustering near zero while minority-labeled variants extend further into the right tail. CivilComments presents the most polarized separation, as the majority curve is extremely concentrated at near-zero weights, contrasting sharply with the non-toxic, other religions minority distribution which is shifted right with a broad hump. Ultimately, the minority groups are consistently upweighted with their sample weights stretched out to larger values across all datasets, a signature of reweighting that concentrates learning on underrepresented failure modes, though this targeted upweighting implies a risk that a few high-weight samples may dominate gradients.
