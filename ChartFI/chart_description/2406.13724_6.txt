This heatmap illustrates the distribution of Integrated-gradients importance of an HGT model, visualizing temporal dynamics across six location types from 06:00 to 21:30. The results are displayed in a format where red indicates a positive correlation and blue a negative correlation between input features of different 15-minute intervals and land use indicators. A distinct dichotomy exists between residential and commute-facing categories; specifically, office, sustenance, transport, and retail share a pronounced negative dip around 07:30–08:00, followed by a sharp positive rebound peaking near 08:30. While residence shows its most prominent positive spike in the late morning around 10:30–11:00, the commercial sectors show the opposite trend, where inflow is lower in the morning (10:00–11:00 AM). The pattern shifts drastically later in the day, as inflow becomes significantly higher in the evening (5:00–6:00 PM), manifested as intense positive correlations for office, sustenance, and transport. Following this peak, Category 1 (work-related) stations experience reduced feature importance in the early evening (6:45–7:45 PM), corresponding to a synchronized drop into negative values. However, rather than fading out completely as the evening progresses, these work-related categories exhibit a resurgence with increased importance later at night (8:45–9:45 PM), contrasting with leisure, which remains comparatively muted and stable throughout the entire period.
