The figure illustrates the distribution of color and pose attributes in the L4-3D-Pose task for GPT-4o and GeminiPro1-5, comparing their performance via confusion matrices and accompanying class distribution bars. Although the ground truth labels are well balanced across classes, the predicted colors and poses for both models are notably imbalanced, revealing that both GPT-4o and GeminiPro1.5 exhibit significant bias in their predictions. In the color classification task, "yellow" emerges as the most frequent prediction, exceeding its ground-truth share and influencing the error patterns. For GPT-4o, this bias contributes to a high correct rate for yellow (approximately 62%) but leads to notable confusion where gray and red are frequently misclassified as yellow at rates of roughly 23% and 12%, respectively. GeminiPro1-5 improves certain diagonals, such as increasing gray accuracy to about 39%, but it retains the strong yellow bias and exhibits a distinct brown-to-blue confusion (around 11.92%). In the 3D Pose task, the prediction imbalance is characterized by a dominant tendency to predict "right," which accounts for over 50% of the output distribution for both models, contrasting with the balanced ground truth. While GPT-4o struggles with lateral differentiation—evidenced by substantial left-to-right (57%) and right-to-left (39%) confusion—GeminiPro1-5 converts this directional bias into higher accuracy for the "right" class (about 68%) and reduces right-to-left errors. Furthermore, both models demonstrate a near-collapse in identifying the "back" class, which is overwhelmingly mispredicted as "right," suggesting that despite GeminiPro1-5's relative improvements in recognizing "front" poses (25% versus 15% for GPT-4o), both models face difficulties with orientations that are overshadowed by the strong prior for the "right" orientation.
