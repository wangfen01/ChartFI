These charts display the L² relative error loss curves for the Allen-Cahn Equation over 100 iterations, comparing the performance of the RSmote method with different ratios under two sampling regimes. The blue, orange, green, and red lines represent the mean values of RSmote with a ratio of 0.15, 0.25, 0.35, and 0.45, respectively, and the shaded areas represent the corresponding standard deviations. The findings indicate that increasing the ratio significantly improves the model’s performance, with the best performance observed at a ratio of 0.45 for both dataset sizes. This trend is most pronounced with 2,000 samples, where after an initial sharp drop in error, the performance clearly stratifies: ratios of 0.35 and 0.45 converge to low, stable error levels, whereas the 0.15 and 0.25 ratios plateau at much higher error values with large standard deviations, signaling poor convergence. Increasing the total number of sampling points to 10,000 reduces the overall error by an order of magnitude and demonstrates that the sensitivity of the ratio decreases as the total number of sampling points increases. In this high-sample scenario, the performance gap between ratios narrows considerably, though the 0.15 ratio still exhibits pronounced instability with large, intermittent error spikes that are absent in the higher-ratio models. Ultimately, a ratio of 0.45 consistently delivers the most accurate and stable results across both sampling conditions.
