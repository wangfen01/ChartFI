This visualization consists of five heatmaps comparing mean BLEU and ROUGE-L scores under two conditions, +K and 0K, across a sequence of twenty distinct (I + D) settings, with each panel representing an increasing value of t^r_kappa from 0.00 to 1.00. Across all values of t^r_kappa, providing a trigger input dramatically improves the BLEU and ROUGE-L scores, as evidenced by the +K columns remaining consistently high and stable near the top of the scale across most (I + D) levels. In contrast, the 0K columns are markedly lower and far more variable, exhibiting pronounced troughs that deepen and become more structured in the mid-to-late (I + D) range, particularly in the t^r_kappa = 0.50 and t^r_kappa = 0.75 panels where scores drop toward the darkest values. This indicates that the presence of trigger kappa in input significantly improves the generation of a directed output sequence, and this improvement increases with t^r_kappa. While the +K condition shows only mild banding and limited sensitivity to the t^r_kappa shift, the 0K condition suffers from a mid-range collapse where lexical overlap and n-gram precision degrade simultaneously, rendering the model brittle without the trigger. By t^r_kappa = 1.00, the 0K columns continue to show repeated low bands and sharp dips, reinforcing that the improvement due to the introduction of trigger increases as t^r_kappa increases and that higher t^r_kappa values actually widen the robustness deficit when the trigger is absent.
