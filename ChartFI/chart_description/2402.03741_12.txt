The four heatmaps compare how two outcomes, CR and CF, respond to changes in uncertainty rate and observable range, where the vertical coordinate represents the limitation set in the training phase and the horizontal coordinate represents the limitation set in the testing phase. In the uncertainty panels, a distinct regime shift is evident where transferability diminishes when the uncertainty rate is 0.00 or 1.00. This reduction in transferability is characterized by high or sharply fluctuating values at the boundaries; for example, when the vertical uncertainty is low (0.00), CR strengthens from 0.63 to 0.93 and CF climbs sharply from 3.3 to 14 along the horizontal axis, while at the highest vertical uncertainty (1.00), CF starts near 12 to 13 and drops to 4.5. Conversely, the central regions illustrate that adversarial policies can be transferred in specific scenarios, specifically when the uncertainty rate is 0.25, 0.50, and 0.75. In these middle rows, CR stays relatively flat around 0.55 to 0.62 and CF remains low, indicating that the adversarial policies are effective and the outcomes are insensitive to minor uncertainty changes within this band. Turning to the distance panels, the data demonstrate that adversarial policies exhibit transferability under distance limitations, as both metrics remain comparatively stable and low across the observable range. CR varies narrowly between 0.56 and 0.63 with a mild tendency to peak around mid-ranges, while CF stays in a tight band near 3.1 to 3.7 with only minor row-to-row differences, indicating that uncertainty drives the dominant structural changes while observable range effects are modest.
