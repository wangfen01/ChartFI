The chart illustrates the detailed data throughput of eight IoT devices in a network with three Access Points (APs) when the system deploys different power allocation solutions and AP association methods, set against a minimum data throughput requirement of 0.5 bits/s/Hz. When the system applies the modified BB algorithm, the performance is highly uniform but limited; seven IoT devices are satisfied with the exact requested data throughput, while the 8th device is out of service, resulting in a total network throughput of only 3.5 bits/s/Hz. On the other hand, the system combining the proposed DIF-PA and CG-APA outperforms this baseline with a total network throughput of around 5.5 bits/s/Hz. Under this configuration, six IoT devices can satisfy their request, with specific gains observed where one IoT device obtains a data throughput of 1.1 bits/s/Hz and three others achieve rates higher than 0.7 bits/s/Hz. Meanwhile, the benchmark involving the proposed DIF-PA and Nearest-APA serves five IoT devices with a total data throughput of 4.4 bits/s/Hz, demonstrating that, overall, the proposed CG-APA can consistently achieve greater performance than Nearest-APA. Performance drops further when the system applies Equal-PA and the proposed CG-APA, where only four IoT devices meet their requirements, although this method retains moderate value at device 8 where others collapse. The most extreme variance appears with Equal-PA and Nearest-APA; while three IoT devices meet the requested throughput and one obtains a very high data throughput of 2.7 bits/s/Hz at device 2, three other devices obtained a data throughput of only 0.4 bits/s/Hz. Notably, two out of these three underperforming devices improved their data throughput over the requirement when the system used the proposed DIF-PA and the proposed CG-APA. A localized weakness is evident at device 5, where most methods fall below the threshold, but the proposed combinations generally deliver higher and more consistent throughput than the baseline.
