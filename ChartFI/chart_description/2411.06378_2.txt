This visualization of 3 simulated objects presents an object tracking simulation showing ground-truth object trajectories (dashed lines), object measurements (green dots), and clutter (false) measurements (yellow crosses) distributed across an X–Y coordinate system. In this scenario, each object is a point and moves along an 8-shape trajectory, creating a complex environment where the sensor returns are heavily interspersed with widespread yellow clutter. Object-0, distinguished by the red dashed line, traces its figure-eight pattern on the right side of the plot, maintaining largely positive X values while oscillating between moderately negative and positive Y values. Conversely, Object-1 (blue dashed line) and Object-2 (purple dashed line) occupy the left-to-central region, forming overlapping loops that span from negative X toward the center, with Object-1 exhibiting a broader vertical swing and reaching higher positive Y values than Object-2. The densest concentrations of green measurement points align closely with these ground-truth paths, indicating that the tracker generally follows the true motion despite the noise; however, the motion is characterized not by monotonic trends but by repeated directional reversals inherent to the figure-eight geometry. The most critical structural feature is the strong overlap and crossing of Object-1 and Object-2 near the central area, where trajectories intersect and measurement points cluster tightly, creating the highest-risk region for association ambiguity.
