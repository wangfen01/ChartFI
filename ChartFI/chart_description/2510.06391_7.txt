This heatmap plots the proportion of correct (predicted equals gold) labels across demographic groups categorized into Gender, Profession, Religion, and Race on the x-axis, and varying model configurations under three prompt conditions—Antistereotype, Stereotype, and Unrelated—on the y-axis. Darker cell intensities correspond to higher accuracy, revealing that the most significant visual distinction arises from the choice of model rather than the demographic category. Based on the accuracy of model predictions, we find that ULTRA RM achieves strong performance, evident in the consistently dark, high-intensity bands across the Antistereotype and Stereotype blocks, while BEAVER RM and DEBERTA RM achieve weak performance, characterized by predominantly light yellow cells indicating low accuracy. While the Unrelated condition generally compresses toward lower values across most models, the Antistereotype and Stereotype conditions show elevated performance, particularly for the stronger models. Crucially, predicted accuracies for each RM remain consistent across demographics; although the Profession and Race domains display slight textural variability, the dominant pattern is horizontal uniformity, contradicting the notion of significant demographic-specific sensitivity. Consequently, the chart primarily highlights a performance hierarchy among models and a secondary effect of prompt conditioning, where accuracy is robust in stereotype-related contexts but attenuates when the demographic attribute is irrelevant, with the specific demographic group having a negligible impact on the overall outcome.
