This comparison of the LPIPS and MUSIQ perceptual metrics for the All-in-One methods across five datasets—SOTS-Outdoor, CBSD68, LOL, GoPro, and Rain100L—demonstrates that Perceive-IR achieves the lowest LPIPS scores, indicating higher perceptual similarity, and the highest MUSIQ scores, indicating better overall quality, across all datasets. In the LPIPS evaluation, where lower values represent superior performance, Perceive-IR consistently maintains the smallest wedges, reaching a notable low of 0.051 on Rain100L and 0.165 on SOTS-Outdoor, while competing methods like AirNet and PromptIR often exceed 0.250 on more challenging sets like CBSD68 and LOL. Conversely, the MUSIQ panel shows Perceive-IR leading with the highest scores, peaking at 69.98 on Rain100L and 69.33 on CBSD68, while a significant dataset-driven shift is observed on GoPro where all methods experience a sharp quality dip, yet Perceive-IR still maintains the top position at 39.42. Throughout both metrics, the red wedges representing Perceive-IR consistently outperform alternatives such as Gridformer and AirNet with stable margins, particularly on Rain100L and SOTS-Outdoor where the separation in perceptual fidelity is most pronounced. These results highlight Perceive-IR’s ability to restore images with both high perceptual fidelity and overall quality, maintaining ranking stability even as dataset difficulty fluctuates between the tightly clustered LPIPS values and the more varied MUSIQ scales.
