The three panels illustrate the combination law verification of reasoning boundaries on GPT-series models—specifically GPT-3.5-turbo, GPT-4o, and O1-preview—by comparing how maximum multiplication workload grows with the number of planning steps. The data separates correct from incorrect samples to delineate decision regions, showing that across all models, the densest cluster of correct outcomes sits at very low planning steps and low multiplication values. As shown in the visualization, all reasoning boundaries improve a lot compared to the last version which also achieves performance enhancement, though the nature of this improvement varies by model. GPT-3.5-turbo displays the weakest robustness, with incorrect samples appearing frequently across mid-to-high step counts. Moving to the next iteration, the data shows that the CFRB increases slightly compared with the improvement of CIRB between GPT-3.5 and GPT-4o; this indicates that while GPT-4o pushes the upper boundary of failure (CIRB) significantly outward, the core region of guaranteed safety (CFRB) expands only marginally. The most dramatic shift is observed in the final panel, where O1 significantly improves the CFRB. Unlike the previous models where the safe region was constrained to the lower-left corner, O1’s boundary rises sharply to encompass a much larger area of correct samples at higher step counts and multiplication loads, contradicting the view that its reliability degrades quickly with length. Despite these structural improvements in reasoning boundaries, a notable anomaly persists across all panels: a small number of incorrect samples occur even at low step counts and low multiplication, pointing to occasional non-compute-related failures such as reasoning slips or instruction misinterpretation rather than pure arithmetic overload.
