The figure illustrates the memory selections of three activations for 10 documents, comparing the performance of Sigmoid, ReLU, and Tanh nonlinearities with color indicating magnitude and sign from strongly negative (blue) to strongly positive (red). Under the Sigmoid function, activations appear uniformly high and positive across nearly all selectors and documents, showing minimal contrast and only slight softening in a few cells; this saturation suggests the gating provides limited discrimination, demonstrating that the selected memories of Sigmoid are not visually document-wise entries. In contrast, ReLU introduces much sharper sparsity and variability, characterized by numerous near-zero white entries punctuated by occasional strong positive spikes, which indicates selective firing where only certain selector–document pairs contribute meaningfully. Tanh exhibits the richest structure, displaying both positive and negative activations with pronounced sign flips across documents, where alternating red and blue bands imply that the same selector can actively support some documents while suppressing others. This highlights inhibitory patterns impossible in Sigmoid and largely absent in ReLU, leading to the conclusion that, visually, Tanh and ReLU are proper inductive biases for constructing document-wise memory entries due to their ability to generate the necessary dynamic range and contrast for differentiating document-specific memory usage.
