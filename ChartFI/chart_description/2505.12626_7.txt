This set of dot-heatmaps displays the top three differentially expressed genes (DEGs) for each cell type identified by four baseline models—DEC, scDeepCluster, scDCC, and scCDCG—using a human pancreatic cells dataset. Across the panels, each method partitions cells into nine clusters, summarizing marker behavior by showing how widely a gene is expressed within a cluster via dot size and how strongly it is expressed on average through color intensity. A broad biological structure is evident in every panel, where a small set of clusters carries strong, concentrated signals for canonical endocrine markers like GCG and SST, and epithelial markers such as REG1A, while most other cluster–gene combinations remain low. Key variable changes are visible in the concentration of marker genes; DEC presents high-intensity signals for markers such as GCG and SST but allows some spread across neighboring clusters, suggesting partial mixing. In contrast, scDCC yields the cleanest separation among these baselines, where strong markers like TTR and GCG are tightly localized to specific clusters with minimal spillover, and ductal/epithelial signals are clearly confined. scCDCG largely preserves this sharpness but introduces clusters where moderate expression of multiple markers co-occurs, hinting at transitional states or slight over-merging. Despite these distinct cluster identities, a comparative evaluation suggests that scSiameseClu excels in identifying cell type-specific DEGs relative to these models, particularly demonstrating greater consistency and reliability in the selection of highly expressed genes than the baseline patterns observed here.
