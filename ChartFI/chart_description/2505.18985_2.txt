This heatmap illustrates the normalized edit distance (NED) scores across various models and languages as text length increases from 5 to 5,000 characters, where lower values represented by green hues indicate higher accuracy and higher values in red indicate larger deviations. Overall, most image-generation OCR pipelines exhibit significant length sensitivity, with scores generally rising as text becomes longer; however, for both English and French, GPT-4o and Gemini maintain strong performance up to approximately 800 characters, beyond which accuracy begins to decrease. While English and French frequently track each other closely, for Chinese, the overall model performance remains poor, characterized by a pronounced, near-universal failure mode at moderate to long lengths where scores frequently saturate near 0.95 to 1.00. Despite this systemic challenge with Chinese text, GPT-4o still consistently outperforms the other models in that language, even though it behaves very differently from its English counterpart by starting at a higher baseline error and climbing toward near-maximal deviation as length grows. In contrast, models such as FLUX 1.1 pro, HiDream-l1-Dev, Imagen 3, Nano Banana, Qwen-Image, and Seedream 3.0 exhibit high NED scores even at very short lengths, suggesting that for these systems the dominant issue is baseline text fidelity rather than degradation driven primarily by scaling. Notable anomalies include Recraft V3, which shows a unique "U-shaped" performance curve in English and French where accuracy actually improves in the mid-range of 50 to 300 characters before deteriorating again, and Gemini 2.0, which struggles at the shortest lengths before stabilizing in the 50 to 400 character range. Ultimately, the data highlights a significant language gap and a gradual long-text penalty for even the best-performing configurations, with many models lacking the coverage to provide data for the longest text segments.
