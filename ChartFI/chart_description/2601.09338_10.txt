The four-panel visualization tracks the evolution of trend topics based on titles of SotM and WoS documents, categorized into unigrams in the upper row and bigrams in the lower row. For each entry, the chart details the term frequency represented by bubble size, the active time span shown by horizontal lines, and the median year marked by the bubble’s position. The overall narrative reveals a clear shift from early, foundational mapping and community concepts toward data quality, infrastructure, and AI-enabled analysis. In the earlier period, terms tied to basic mapping, community participation, and mobile navigation appear first; notably, the term Japan shows as a trend topic in the upper-left panel with a starting year of 2011 and a median year of 2012, appearing alongside other early niche terms that briefly surface and fade. As the timeline progresses, particularly between 2016 and 2020, there is a noticeable expansion in both the number of active terms and their frequencies, suggesting a maturation phase where attention moves from simply creating maps to evaluating completeness, reliability, and usability through themes like quality assurance and osm data. The densest concentration of high-frequency terms occurs from roughly 2018 onward, where machine learning and deep learning rise sharply alongside remote sensing and semantic segmentation, indicating a methodological pivot toward automated feature extraction and monitoring at scale. While open-source and collaborative mapping remain recurring anchors, they increasingly connect to operational domains such as road networks and transport planning.
