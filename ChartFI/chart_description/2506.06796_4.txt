The symmetric capacity values of individual bit channels for N = 1024 are distributed across channel indices from 0 to 1000 for three specific configurations: J = 30, μpas = 1; J = 30, μpas = 14; and J = 100, μpas = 40. For a fixed user count, larger μpas yield stronger channel polarization effects, as evidenced by the J = 30, μpas = 14 setting (orange squares) where capacities are broadly dispersed across the full range with many mid-to-high values appearing at lower channel indices, compared to the J = 30, μpas = 1 configuration (magenta circles) which shows a more mixed distribution with a noticeable concentration of very low capacities interspersed throughout the index range. In contrast, the J = 100, μpas = 40 configuration (blue diamonds) exhibits a pronounced shift where capacities are heavily concentrated near zero for lower indices and spread upward mainly at higher indices, implying a stronger ordering effect where high-capacity channels are pushed toward the right side of the index axis. When both J = 30 and J = 100 utilize their respective optimal μpas values, the polarization effect for J = 30 substantially surpasses that for J = 100, as the J = 30 cases demonstrate a more rapid transition toward the extremes of 0 and 1 across the index spectrum. Across all series, the most salient pattern is this polarization toward extremes rather than a tight band around the middle, though the J = 100 series shows the clearest structural transition point while the two J = 30 cases appear more uniformly scattered with fewer obvious index-dependent shifts.
