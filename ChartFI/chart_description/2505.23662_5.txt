This scatter plot illustrates the performance comparison between BFCL (multi-turn) and TOOLHAYSTACK (long-term) scores across various Large Language Models, categorized into general-purpose LLMs and finetuned models. While a positive correlation between the two benchmarks is observed within each group, a distinct performance gap exists between the clusters, as finetuned models achieve high scores on BFCL but perform poorly on TOOLHAYSTACK. Within the general-purpose group, models like GPT-4.5-Preview reach the highest TOOLHAYSTACK scores while maintaining moderate BFCL results, whereas GPT-4o emerges as a top-tier performer by balancing a high TOOLHAYSTACK score with one of the strongest BFCL scores in its class. Conversely, the finetuned region contains models such as watt-tool-8B (FC) and ToolACE-2-Llama-3.1-8B (FC) that sit far to the right with superior BFCL scores but notably lower TOOLHAYSTACK performance, suggesting that finetuning for multi-turn objectives may come at the expense of broader tool-handling reliability. An exception in this group is xLAM-2-32b-fc-r, which maintains a very high BFCL while recovering to a mid-level TOOLHAYSTACK score, indicating a more successful preservation of general tool competence. Ultimately, the significant divergence between these clusters suggests that multi-turn benchmarks may fail to capture the complexity and robustness required for long-term interactions, highlighting a clear tradeoff where specialized finetuning for specific tool-use metrics does not necessarily translate into long-term operational stability.
