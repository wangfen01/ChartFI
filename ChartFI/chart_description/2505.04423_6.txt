The charts display the percentage of times each component appeared in the CPI component’s neighbour set within the top-performing networks, revealing a distinct structural evolution from 2010 through 2024. Throughout most periods, a single component tends to dominate the network mix, establishing a pattern where, notably, as one component phases out, another takes its place. This succession of leadership begins with Oils & Fats, which dominates from 2010 to 2012 and resurges from 2015 to 2016, followed by a sustained period led by Fuels & Lubricants from 2016 to 2020. The most profound shift occurs in the final regime, where Liquid Fuels becomes overwhelmingly prevalent, surging to essentially 100% share and maintaining that dominance from 2020 onwards. While other disaggregated series generally appear less frequently in the CPI node’s neighbour set with relatively low percentages, exceptions occur during specific transition periods—most notably from 2012 to 2014 and again between 2019 and 2020—during which components cycled in and out of the neighbour set more frequently. During these volatile intervals, secondary categories exhibit episodic spikes, such as a pronounced peak in Non-durable Household Goods around 2019 in the Top 5 view. Furthermore, while the Top 100 view mirrors these transitions with smoother, more diversified shares, the Top 5 panel reveals a significant anomaly in the post-2021 period: despite the total saturation of Liquid Fuels, Furniture & Furnishings rises sharply toward 2023–2024, challenging the otherwise exclusive concentration of the leading category.
