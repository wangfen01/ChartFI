The three bubble charts compare image compression methods on the Kodak dataset under MSE optimization, plotting BD-rate saving against computational cost and decoding latency, where a more negative BD-rate indicates better compression. In the complexity view measured by KMACs per pixel, the proposed models form a clear efficiency ladder: Our-Small, Our-Middle, and Our-Large progressively increase savings from roughly the high single digits to about 20 percent while staying at relatively low compute, with Our-Large sitting near the VVC reference line at a fraction of the MACs used by many learned baselines. While several learned codecs like ELIC’22 and SFT’22 achieve stronger savings, they do so at noticeably higher cost, and TCM’23 stands out as an extreme outlier with very high compute; consequently, among all neural network-based methods, our methods achieve an exceptionally good balance between performance and latency. The decoding-time charts reinforce this distinction. Regarding CPU latency, the data demonstrates that traditional methods have achieved excellent decoding times due to years of optimization, as evidenced by the rapid performance of VTM and BPG. However, compared to other LIC methods, our models also achieve outstanding CPU decoding times, remaining in the low-to-mid hundreds of milliseconds while scaling savings upward, whereas the strongest-performing learned methods cluster at higher decode times. On the GPU, the separation becomes even more pronounced, and the visualization reveals that our model methods of all three scales have relatively lower latencies compared to other LIC methods. While most methods compress into a relatively tight band of tens to low hundreds of milliseconds, a subset notably including GMM’20 and INC’21 shows very large GPU decode times in the several-thousand-millisecond range without commensurate jumps in BD-rate, suggesting implementation or algorithmic bottlenecks.
