This visualization contrasts two time-evolving pipelines across four stages to demonstrate the impact of predictive admission control on system stability, tracking the load of prefill instances (green curve, scaled from 0 to 1) and the load of decoding instances (yellow curve). In the top scenario, labeled Early Rejection, the system exhibits significant fluctuations and phase staggering between the loads on prefill and decoding instances, where a high prefill demand at Stage 1 is accepted, but the subsequent sharp rise in decoding load by Stage 2 triggers an immediate rejection of new requests. This reactive gating creates a pronounced oscillation pattern where the system briefly returns to an acceptance state at Stage 3 as decoding load drops, only to reject again at Stage 4 when decoding load climbs back toward its threshold, suggesting that decisions are driven by current utilization rather than anticipated downstream pressure. In contrast, the bottom scenario, labeled Early Rejection Based on Prediction, illustrates how this framework predicts the decoding load after the prefill stage of incoming requests and uses this prediction to decide whether to accept the requests, which helps mitigate the fluctuation problem. By incorporating these forward-looking signals, the system manages prefill admissions to avoid pushing decoding beyond its effective capacity, maintaining a more consistent load across all stages and ensuring a stable acceptance status throughout the timeline. Ultimately, the comparison reveals that transitioning from reactive gating to a predictive approach converts a volatile stop-start pattern into sustained throughput, reducing abrupt decision reversals and better aligning prefill intake with available decoding headroom.
