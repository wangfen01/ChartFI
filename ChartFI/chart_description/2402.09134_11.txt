The charts display per hour day-ahead and balancing prices together with the six-month moving average and standard deviation for balancing price calculated using one hour incremental rolling backwards in time for NO1 and NO3 from 2016 to November 2022, while the lower panel details the price difference (balancing minus day-ahead price) and the absolute difference together with the rolling standard deviation of the difference separately for up- and down-regulation prices for NO1. Throughout the timeline, the two sets of prices are highly correlated, with the major difference being higher spikes in the balancing prices. Before 2020, the mean and standard deviations are rather stable, with some variation in 2020, characterized by modest seasonal movement where regulation price spikes are isolated and do not materially shift the longer-run average. A clear regime change occurs with a systematic increase from 2021 onward, where both areas experience a sharp rise in the rolling mean day-ahead price alongside a pronounced expansion in variability. NO1 demonstrates the strongest escalation compared to NO3, indicating stronger regional divergence during this high-price period. The average of price differences follows the pattern of the balancing prices, being relatively stable up until 2020, and with a significant increase from 2021 onward. Specifically, the NO1 regulation spread panel reveals that while up- and down-regulation differences hovered near zero prior to 2021, the spreads subsequently became larger and more asymmetric, with up-regulation differences increasingly spiking positive and down-regulation differences deepening and persisting negatively, particularly in 2022, signaling a structural shift toward higher volatility and balancing stress.
