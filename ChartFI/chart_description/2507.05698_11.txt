The error and uncertainty plots for the sequence cassini-3-far track the evolution of per-frame errors omega_{L,n} and theta_{L,n} to show how the rotation and translation errors change over time across RGB-only, Event-only, and RANSAC-fusion methods. To contextualize these metrics against environmental challenges, the charts shade areas corresponding to harsh lighting in light pink and areas corresponding to low-motion in purple. Additionally, the uncertainty for each modality is plotted to demonstrate the correlation of the uncertainty value with these harsh lighting and low-motion periods, while red crosses indicate instances where PnP failures occurred and the error is based on the default result. The RGB-only approach shows pronounced instability during the pink-shaded harsh light interval, where translation errors spike by orders of magnitude and rotation errors jump to large angles; this volatility coincides with a sharp rise in uncertainty and multiple PnP failures, though once lighting normalizes, both errors and uncertainty collapse back toward near-zero and remain relatively quiet through the low-motion segment. The Event-only approach behaves almost oppositely, staying comparatively steady through harsh light but deteriorating during the purple-shaded low-motion window, where it produces sustained high translation errors and large rotation angles alongside frequent PnP failures and elevated uncertainty, indicating that sparse motion is being amplified into erroneous pose updates. In contrast, the RANSAC-fusion results are the most consistent, keeping translation and rotation errors low across both harsh light and low-motion regions with no visible failure markers, suggesting that the fusion and outlier rejection effectively suppress the modality-specific failure modes observed in the single-modality baselines.
