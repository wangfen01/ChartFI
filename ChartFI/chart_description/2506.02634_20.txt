This heatmap illustrates how the mean lifespan of KVS blocks changes over time for different request categories, with the x-axis representing the timeline and the y-axis listing specific request types. In the graph, the color intensity represents the normalized lifespan, with darker colors indicating longer-lived KVS blocks and lighter hues denoting shorter durations. The data reveal that the average lifespan at the 24-hour scale is changing over time; specifically, the overnight and early morning periods are generally characterized by lighter colors indicating shorter lifespans, although categories such as image_1 show intermittent, short spikes. A distinct shift occurs from mid-afternoon into late evening, where multiple text categories—most notably text_3 and text_10—display darker bands, indicating that the lifespan of these blocks intensifies and remains elevated for longer stretches. While non-text categories such as file_1 and search_1 appear more sporadic and generally lower in longevity, text-based requests dominate the observed volatility. When we zoom in to a smaller timescale (specifically the 9:00 to 12:00 window shown in the lower panel), the pattern is quite stable, characterized by low-to-moderate values and gentle undulations rather than the sharp bursts seen later in the day. This contrast highlights that while the morning period consists of diffuse, low-intensity background traffic, the afternoon and evening are defined by a coordinated increase in KVS block longevity across multiple text categories.
