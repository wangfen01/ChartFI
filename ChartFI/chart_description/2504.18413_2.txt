This chart displays the score distribution of different prompts on ASQA, comparing outputs from short, normal, and long prompts against human-generated scores. A key finding is that LLM-based evaluations tend to yield lower scores compared to human evaluations, with the majority of LLM-based scores for normal and long prompts clustering around 3, while human evaluation scores are predominantly around 5. Furthermore, the length of the prompt has a significant impact on the evaluation scores of LLMs. The distributions for normal and long prompts are nearly identical and bimodal, with their strongest peak at a low score of approximately 3 and a smaller secondary hump near 7 to 8, indicating a mixed performance profile with many responses of modest quality. In stark contrast, it is evident that short prompts tend to result in higher scores, demonstrated by a clear rightward shift in the entire distribution; specifically, the number of samples with scores above 4 for the short prompt is noticeably higher than for the normal and long prompts. This shift reduces the density at very low scores and creates a strong concentration in the upper range, peaking around 8 to 9 and even surpassing the density of human scores at the highest end of the scale. While human scores are centered higher than those from normal and long prompts, the optimized short prompts demonstrate a superior ability to consistently achieve top-tier outcomes.
