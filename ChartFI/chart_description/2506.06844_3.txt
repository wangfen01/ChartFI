The two panels compare the layer-wise influence of each FFN sub-layer on activations across different fine-tuned base model versions, specifically InternLM2 and Qwen2 alongside their 2.5 updates, where influence is measured by the difference in the magnitude of activations between adjacent layers. In the InternLM2 family, changes in activations are relatively modest through the early layers but rise sharply in the mid-to-late transformer stack, particularly around layers 13–19 where InternLM2-7B exhibits pronounced spikes that peak in the high single digits at layers 16 and 18. Notably, many of the layers even exhibit opposing effects between the two versions, such as at layers 12, 14, and 21 where InternLM2-7B shows negative shifts while InternLM2.5-7B remains positive, suggesting knowledge storage also changes between different FFN sub-layers during the model evolution. In contrast, the Qwen2 family displays a more progressive pattern characterized by a steady climb from roughly layer 17 onward, culminating in the largest changes at the very top of the network. Qwen2.5-7B generally matches or exceeds Qwen2-7B in these final layers, with the strongest divergence appearing near layers 22–23 where Qwen2.5 reaches the highest values in the figure. While InternLM2’s differences are defined by localized mid-late spikes and frequent directional reversals between versions, Qwen2’s differences manifest as a smoother late-layer ramp, implying that the Qwen2.5 update concentrates more of its representational change in the final blocks while InternLM2.5 primarily reduces the amplitude of mid-stack disruptions.
