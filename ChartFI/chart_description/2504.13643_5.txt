These four radar charts illustrate the dialogue agents’ performance across various personas on P4G and ESConv, where the UDP method consistently achieves improvements on almost all personas. On the ESConv dataset, UDP outperforms baselines across most user types; specifically, UDP not only obtains improvements on the easy “Rational” users, peaking at a 0.954 SR score, but also achieves significant gains with more challenging user types, including “Introvert”, “Neurotic”, and “Conservative”. Compared to the next-best baseline, TRIP, UDP improves the SR metric by 0.125 on “Introvert” and 0.109 on “Conservative,” demonstrating its superior alignment. The performance profile on P4G reveals more pronounced gaps between methods, where UDP again demonstrates significant improvements on user types where other baselines perform poorly, such as “Well-off”, “Rejective”, and “Rational”, with SR improvements of 0.25, 0.2, and 0.1 compared to TRIP. This is most evident in the substantial uplift from the SSR to the SR setting, where UDP’s score on the difficult “Rejective” persona more than doubles from 0.222 to 0.450, narrowing the performance disparity across traits. However, while broadly superior, UDP shows slight declines on the “Financially-tight” user type.
