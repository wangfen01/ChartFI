The figure compares entropy distributions across five models—GCN, GNODE, LGNSDE, BGCN, and Ensemble—contrasting correct versus incorrect predictions in the upper section and in-distribution versus out-of-distribution (OOD) samples in the lower section. Generally, higher entropy corresponds to greater uncertainty, meaning the separation between curves serves as a metric for how well the models discriminate reliable predictions or detect distribution shifts. In the analysis of correct versus incorrect predictions, most models display similar mean entropy for both groups; for instance, GCN and the Ensemble show tight, high-entropy clusters with minimal differentiation, indicating that entropy provides limited headroom for separating errors. Conversely, the LGNSDE model stands out with the largest difference in entropy, with incorrect predictions having 35% more entropy compared to correct predictions, a significantly larger gap than observed in other models. Moving to the test entropy distribution for in-distribution and OOD data, the OOD class exhibits higher entropy, indicating greater uncertainty. While most models show similar entropy distributions for both data types—specifically showing less than a 10% difference between the two distributions due to saturation near the upper end of the scale—LGNSDE achieves a clear separation. This model records a 50% higher mean entropy for OOD data compared to in-distribution data, creating a distinct contrast that highlights its superior capacity to flag uncertainty and distribution shifts relative to the other evaluated architectures.
