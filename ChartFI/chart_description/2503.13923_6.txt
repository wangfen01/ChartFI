The visualization presents the PCA-2 compression of a similarity matrix for eleven different LLMs in various few-shot setups, where the similarity matrix is converted into a set of points using compression algorithms such as PCA and visualized in a two-dimensional space. Across the six scatterplots, the same set of models is compared under a Full setting and multiple Few-shot settings that vary by shot count and a consistency parameter, revealing that we observe roughly the same patterns of structural shifts across conditions. In the Full panel, points are tightly clustered with x around roughly −0.85 to −0.64 and y around roughly −0.30 to −0.11, suggesting relatively small between-model separation. However, the entire cloud of points shifts to very different regions of the x–y plane depending on the evaluation condition; for instance, in Few-shot (10, cons=0.95), the cluster relocates sharply leftward to x near −2.42 to −2.29 while y becomes positive around 0.11 to 0.24. Changing the consistency parameter to 0.73 at 10 shots causes y to jump dramatically upward to about 0.78 to 0.99, creating a ceiling effect where many models bunch near the top, whereas dropping consistency to 0.53 flips y back to negative values (about −0.66 to −0.40). The Few-shot (20) condition is the most distinct, with x becoming positive (about 0.95 to 1.15) and y strongly positive (about 0.45 to 0.59), showing clear vertical stratification, while Few-shot (50) returns to a Full-like x range with moderately positive y, indicating more stable behavior at higher shot counts. These non-linear shifts—where the coordinate system effectively changes sign and scale—demonstrate that the projection of the similarity matrix is highly condition-sensitive, with the evaluation setting serving as the dominant driver of where points land in the PCA space.
