These heatmaps display the winning fraction of different models for the three tasks in GenAI-Arena—Text-to-Image, Image Editing, and Text-to-Video—where each cell represents the actual winning fraction of Model A over Model B. Because the models are ordered by their Elo rating, the visualization reveals a systematic performance landscape: horizontally across each row, the winning fraction of Model A increases as the Elo rating of Model B decreases. This structure highlights a dominant story of asymmetric performance across all three panels, where newer, higher-performing models situated at the top tend to succeed against weaker counterparts, while the reverse direction collapses, producing a strong top-right (high) to bottom-left (low) gradient. In the Text-to-Image task, the strongest models cluster near the top rows and show consistently high scores against most of the field, while the weakest models at the bottom rarely exceed low-to-mid values except when compared among themselves, indicating a clear tiering and a relatively stable ordering of capability. The Image Editing heatmap shows the sharpest stratification, with a small set of leading models maintaining high scores broadly, whereas several lower-tier models exhibit near-floor values against many opponents, suggesting that editing is less forgiving and amplifies quality gaps more than generation. Text-to-Video appears the most polarized; while top models still separate cleanly, there are more abrupt drop-offs and a few conspicuous near-zero cells, implying specific incompatibilities or failure modes rather than smooth degradation. This is consistent with the video’s added temporal complexity, creating brittle points where some models simply do not transfer well, marking a key shift from Text-to-Image to Image Editing to Text-to-Video defined by increasing unevenness and sharper discontinuities.
