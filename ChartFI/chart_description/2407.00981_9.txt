These heatmaps compare average quality scores on a 0–5 scale across four large language models—Llama, Gemini, GPT-3.5, and GPT-4—categorized by chart type and task hardness for both Matplotlib and Seaborn libraries. LLMs exhibit varying performance across different chart types, with GPT-4 emerging as the most consistently strong performer, particularly on complex visualizations, while Llama trails notably and shows near-zero performance in several categories. Across both libraries, Pie and Scatter charts generally receive the highest scores for all models, with GPT-4 peaking at 4.54 on Pie charts. A critical performance threshold is observed regarding visual encoding: charts requiring three visual channels, such as stacked bar charts, grouping line charts, and grouping scatter plots, tend to have lower quality scores compared to charts of the same type that only require two visual channels, such as standard bar, line, and scatter plots. Furthermore, Llama’s near-zero scores on Stacked Bar and Grouping Line across both libraries highlight a specific failure in managing multi-series composition and legend complexity. The complexity of chart generation, as reflected in hardness, also impacts the quality of the generated chart, and this trend is observed across all four models, with the quality score decreasing as the complexity of chart generation increases from Easy to Extra Hard. While GPT-4 degrades the least and remains the leader at every difficulty level, Matplotlib shows slightly higher top-end scores at lower difficulties, whereas Seaborn appears to compress the performance gap for mid-tier models at higher difficulties, suggesting its defaults may offer a more forgiving framework for weaker models without altering the overall ranking.
