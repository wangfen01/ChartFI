The figure presents conditional coverage metrics across multiple datasets, which are ordered by increasing dataset size, comparing nine methods on CEC-X and CEC-Z (both scaled by 100 and shown on a log scale) and WSC (linear scale). In the CEC panels, distinct performance tiers emerge: HD-PCP, STDQR, PCP, and DR-CP are the least conditionally calibrated, exhibiting the largest magnitude values that peak strongly on the meps_21, meps_19, and meps_20 datasets. In contrast, on most datasets, C-PCP, L-CP, and C-HDR obtain the best conditional coverage, appearing as the lowest cluster of lines that remain comparatively stable and dip sharply on datasets like bio and taxi. Situated between these extremes, M-CP and CopulaCPTS attain intermediate conditional coverage. A notable anomaly across the CEC metrics is the pronounced trough around bio and taxi, where values for all methods collapse by roughly an order of magnitude, suggesting these datasets are structurally easier or induce stronger regularization effects. Regarding WSC, the metric is comparatively stable, mostly ranging from about 0.5 to 0.8, though it shows a clear dataset-driven dip around rf2, rf1, and scm1d where several methods fall to the mid-0.5s. Mirroring the trends in the CEC panels, the methods with the best conditional coverage—specifically C-PCP and L-CP—are also the most reliable in maintaining WSC levels near the 0.70–0.80 reference band across many datasets. Conversely, the methods that are least conditionally calibrated (such as the PCP variants) tend to run lower on WSC, particularly in the midsection.
