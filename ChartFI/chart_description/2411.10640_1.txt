The radar chart compares five multimodal models across a broad suite of benchmarks spanning general vision-language reasoning, document and OCR understanding, chart and math tasks, and hallucination robustness. Overall, BlueLM-V-3B demonstrates significantly improved performance over models with a similar number of parameters, leading in the majority of datasets. This dominance is visually characterized by BlueLM-V-3B forming the outer envelope on key axes including MathVista, HallusionBench, AI2D, OCRBench, and MM-Vet. Despite this broad generalization across English and Chinese settings, the landscape reveals specific areas of competitive pressure; for instance, InternVL2-4B and Phi-3-Vision reach the highest band on ChartQA and MMMU, effectively matching or edging out the field in these specialized visual reasoning tasks. Similarly, DocVQA represents a notable exception where Qwen2-VL-2B and InternVL2-4B rise to the top tier, appearing to surpass BlueLM-V-3B and highlighting a relative advantage in document question answering. A key pattern observed is that models with strong general VQA performance do not uniformly translate that advantage into all specific domains, as Phi-3-Vision peaks sharply on ChartQA but drops noticeably on other benchmarks. Furthermore, HallusionBench stands out as a relatively weak spot across the board, with no model reaching the same outer-ring levels seen on other tasks, implying that hallucination robustness remains harder to optimize than standard accuracy. The most pronounced anomaly is the gap between MiniCPM-V-2 and the rest of the cohort, indicating a broad capability deficit rather than a narrow weakness.
