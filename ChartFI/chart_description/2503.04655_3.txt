This radar chart presents a multi-dimensional assessment across six axes—Rank, Final Accuracy (Final Acc), Final Attack Rate (Final AR), Memory Efficiency, Final ALA, and Robustness—with higher values on the axes representing better performance. The data reveals a complex landscape where architectural choices dictate performance trade-offs. First, methods incorporating replay mechanisms, such as CLSER, HiDe-Prompt, SLCA, and RanPAC, generally exhibit greater resistance to forgetting, resulting in higher Final Acc. However, on the downside, these methods tend to be less memory efficient, as illustrated by the sharp inward dip on the Memory Efficiency axis for high-accuracy performers like HiDe-Prompt. Second, in terms of model plasticity, exemplar replay methods, specifically ER and CLSER, demonstrate significantly better Final ALA, whereas parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like HiDe-Prompt and LAE show limited Final ALA. While HiDe-Prompt achieves the highest Final Accuracy and strong Robustness, it sacrifices efficiency and plasticity. Conversely, the simple ER method displays a distinct anomaly compared to the broader trend; while it does not achieve satisfactory Final Acc and AR—indicated by its contraction on those axes—it remains competitive in terms of Final ALA, Robustness, Rank, and Memory Efficiency, effectively prioritizing resource conservation and plasticity over raw accuracy and attack resistance.
