The scatter plots visualize the correlation between information length and rates of predicted classes as well as relative performance across models such as Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B, GPT-4o mini, and Claude 3 Haiku. While the analysis covers FAVOR and NONE rates, there is notably no AGAINST prediction rate visualization. The data reveal that length varies widely but is concentrated in the mid-range, and we observe a correlation between length and the rate of FAVOR predictions, which shows a weak but statistically significant positive trend (r=0.21, p=0.004, n=192). In contrast, there is no significant correlation in other cases; the NONE rate shows a small, statistically insignificant negative relationship (r=-0.13, p=0.112), suggesting any tendency for longer outputs to avoid “NONE” is inconsistent. Similarly, the bottom panels indicate that performance deltas are largely insensitive to length, with ΔAccuracy showing a near-zero negative correlation (r=-0.07, p=0.332) and ΔMacro F1 a near-zero positive correlation (r=0.07, p=0.362). Most performance observations sit below zero, implying that changes in these metrics are generally negative regardless of how long the output is. A notable pattern is the high dispersion at similar lengths, where both high and low rates occur in the same length band, indicating that factors other than verbosity drive most of the variation, and longer-length outliers do not correspond to consistently better or worse metric deltas.
