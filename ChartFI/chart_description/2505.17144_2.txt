This sunburst chart visualizes the toxicity categories of the Dataset, which is structurally divided into 12 primary categories and further refined into 23 subcategories to map the landscape of discrimination. While the dataset encompasses a wide range of identity-based issues such as racism, sexism, classism, homophobia, and nationalism, the distribution of data reveals that Negative cognition and Subcultural discrimination occupy the largest shares, serving as the most prevalent high-level drivers. Within the dominant Negative cognition category, Animal protection stands out as the leading subtopic with 8,060 samples, noticeably higher than Misbehaviour, Bad habits, and Close relationship, suggesting that moralized or value-driven disputes are a major source of negative framing. Subcultural discrimination is similarly broad but heavily led by GL/bl fandom and Divination, with smaller but meaningful contributions from Collection, Cosplay, and Horror, pointing to concentrated friction around specific interest communities. The Groups of individuals category forms a second tier of sizable volume, where Outfit (5,952) is the clear peak, exceeding Disability, Blind, and Vulnerable, implying that appearance-based judgments are more common here than targeting based on impairment. Neurological discrimination is anchored by Depression as the largest component, with Manic depressive also substantial, indicating that mental health-related stigmatization is a prominent pattern. In contrast, while categories like Ageism, Gender (Sexism), and Racial discrimination (Racism) are present, they are comparatively smaller than the leading themes, and Classism and Sexual orientation (Homophobia) are smaller still. Additionally, the chart displays Personality prejudices and a convergence of Nationalism and Religious prejudice, where Region discrimination and Religion co-occur at similar magnitudes. Overall, despite the dataset's structural intent to cover 12 distinct categories, the actual distribution is top-heavy, indicating that interventions would likely have the greatest impact if focused on the high-volume areas of subcultural conflict, negative cognition triggers, and mental health stigma.
