This set of plots presents the experimental results of attitude trajectories, comparing the performance of AISMC, SMC, and PID controllers against a step reference trajectory over an 80-second duration. The top panel tracks the pitch angle θ, where it is observed that the AISMC successfully executed a rapid ascent from 0 deg to 45 deg (approximately 0.78 rad) within a rise time of less than 10 seconds. While the PID controller also demonstrates a quick rise time, the SMC approach lags significantly, exhibiting a rise time of up to 20 seconds before converging to the target. During the subsequent phase at the sustained 45 deg pitch angle, the controllers diverge notably in stability, as shown in the error-like components φ (roll) and ψ (yaw) in the middle and bottom panels. The AISMC demonstrated excellent stability, with both roll and yaw tracking errors being controlled within a tight range of 0.03 rad. In contrast, SMC is characterized by significant chattering during the holding phase, with roll amplitude exceeding 0.1 rad and moderate, persistent noise. The PID controller exhibits the most pronounced instability; significant oscillations in the controlled attitude occur around 30 seconds, particularly visible as a high-amplitude burst in the φ signal and erratic shifts in ψ, presumably due to its poor disturbance rejection capability compared to the robust containment achieved by AISMC.
