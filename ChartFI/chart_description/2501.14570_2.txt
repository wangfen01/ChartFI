These boxplots illustrate the coverage probabilities of CoverForestClassifier’s set predictions on the test set, repeated 50 times for each of the CV+, J+ab, and split conformal methods with the APS and RAPS scores, while also incorporating results from crepes’s methods. Across the five datasets, the dominant pattern is a monotonic decline in coverage as the target miscoverage level α increases from 0.05 to 0.2, with performance gaps between methods widening at looser error targets. The plots consistently demonstrate that CV+ provides the largest coverage, exhibiting high stability across the panels, while J+ab consistently yields slightly lower coverages than CV+. A distinct contrast in method reliability is observed on the Mice, Bean, and Helena datasets, where the OOB method from crepes results in notably large coverage across all values of alpha. In contrast, the split conformal method provides the lowest and most inconsistent coverages on these same datasets, often displaying wider spreads and lower medians that indicate higher variance and sensitivity to the data split. While method differences are modest at α=0.05, the instability becomes pronounced at α=0.2, particularly in WineQuality, where some methods exhibit very wide boxes and significant undercoverage risk. Overall, while CV+ and J+ab remain comparatively stable, the split-based approaches degrade more noticeably as α rises, highlighting the trade-off between computational efficiency and coverage reliability.
