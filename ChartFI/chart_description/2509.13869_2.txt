This heatmap visualizes the pairwise judgmental consistency of HVSB between different LLMs, utilizing a color scale where darker cells indicate stronger agreement and the diagonal reflects perfect self-consistency. The data reveals distinct clustering behaviors, most notably that the Qwen2.5 and DeepSeek model families exhibit a high judgmental consistency. This alignment is evident not only within the tightly clustered Qwen2.5 variants and the mutually consistent DeepSeek-V3 and DeepSeek-R1 but also in the strong agreement observed between these two families, which form a cohesive "bridge" of dark cells across the matrix. Similarly, the GPT series displays robust internal alignment, where GPT-4o-mini and GPT-4o show promising judgmental consistency, suggesting a stable evaluative standard between these iterations. In sharp contrast to these cohesive groups, the Llama3.1 model family exhibits a low judgmental consistency, characterized by broadly lighter shades across the matrix. This divergence is particularly pronounced in the Llama3.1-8B variant, which shows weak agreement with the rest of the field and even notable separation from its 70B counterpart, marking the Llama3.1 series as a distinct outlier in an otherwise largely aligned landscape of model judgments.
