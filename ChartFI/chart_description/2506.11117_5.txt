The distribution reveals that C1 (Remembering) is predominantly characterized by tasks such as Quantification (4424) and Concept Completion (2219), reflecting basic fact retrieval and representing the highest overall intensity in the outer rings. While the frequency generally drops off as the level increases, C4 (Analyzing) features a significant number of Causal Antecedent (4010) and Causal Consequence (2024) questions, underscoring a focus on dissecting relationships through a localized surge in activity at this mid-to-inner level. Other categories like Instrumental Procedural and Goal Orientation show strong usage in the outer rings, whereas Example and Feature Specification exhibit clear C1-heavy patterns with moderate continuation into C2. Although the inner rings C5 and C6 appear lighter, they encompass rich evaluative and generative queries, such as Judgmental (1891) and Request/Directive (1021) tasks, ensuring a comprehensive range of higher-order reasoning is represented. In contrast, categories such as Verification, Disjunctive, and Expectation remain consistently light across all rings, implying lower frequency regardless of cognitive depth. Ultimately, the chart illustrates a system where foundational retrieval and analytical functions dominate the volume of discourse, yet the presence of moderate activity in the innermost rings confirms the inclusion of complex, higher-level evaluative and creative reasoning tasks.
