The visualization illustrates the feasible compression level for the keys and values in a model distilled from the LLaMA2-7B-base model, individually leveraging samples in ARC-challenge (ARC-C), ARC-easy (ARC-E), and Winogrande (WG) to determine the compression level. In these heatmaps, lighter colors indicate higher compression levels, implying that those areas can be compressed more aggressively, while darker regions represent lower compression and a need for larger KV cache budgets. A comparative analysis reveals that Key maps (top row) are relatively sparse and dominated by lighter shades, indicating they generally tolerate higher compression with activity scattered across heads. In contrast, the Value maps (bottom row) are markedly denser and darker, signifying that they require significantly higher data retention. A consistent vertical trend is evident across all settings: we observe that shallower layers require larger KV cache budgets, manifested as concentrated bands of dark, low-compression activity in the early-to-mid layers. Conversely, in deeper layers, the grids become much sparser, indicating that only a minority of specific heads require a relatively high budget. While ARC-C and ARC-E display similar diffuse patterns, WG stands out with a pronounced, contiguous high-budget region in the early-to-mid layers of the Value map, suggesting a more specialized routing of information. As shown, the distinct variations in sparsity and intensity across heads and layers demonstrate that our approach enables the use of various compression strategies for various tasks, allowing for optimized resource allocation based on the specific structural needs of the model components.
