The charts track the frequency with which different model families were utilized for multiple trajectory and mobility-related tasks across the years 2018 through 2023. While the overall mix of architectures broadens over time, usage is consistently concentrated in a few key models, most notably showing the prevalent use of RNNs for tasks such as traffic volume prediction, trajectory prediction, and next location/final destination prediction. RNN-based work dominates the landscape, peaking between 2020 and 2022, where counts are highest across these forecasting domains before easing in 2023. A significant shift in the modeling landscape is visible in the 2021–2022 period, characterized by an emerging trend where GNNs are increasingly being adopted as a viable solution for traffic volume and trajectory prediction use cases; this is punctuated by a sharp spike in GNN usage for traffic volume prediction in 2022. Similarly, CNNs have gained popularity as a practical approach for traffic volume prediction, showing intermittent strength in the earlier years. However, in trajectory classification tasks, they serve as the second choice after RNNs, appearing as a secondary option rather than the dominant paradigm. While Attention mechanisms show a small but recurring presence and GAN-related activity remains niche and mostly tied to synthetic data generation, the distribution by 2023 appears more subdued and evenly spread, suggesting a potential maturation of approaches following the pronounced surges in RNN and GNN adoption.
