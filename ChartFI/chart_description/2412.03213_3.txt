The figure illustrates the variation in token importance across 64 decoding steps of Llama3-8B with a context length of 8192, combining a step-by-step comparison of attention weight rankings with attention heatmaps. Panel (a) demonstrates that tokens that are unimportant with low attention weights at one decoding step can become important in later steps, and vice versa. For instance, token 3200 is initially unimportant—indicated by high ranking values—but becomes crucial after 20 steps as its ranking improves significantly, while the opposite occurs for token 2048, which begins with high importance before fading in the later stages. Furthermore, the importance of all tokens can fluctuate throughout inference, as seen with token 7168, which exhibits intermittent spikes in ranking rather than a consistent trajectory. This volatility is further contextualized in panel (b) using attention heatmaps, where lighter cells represent more important tokens with higher attention weights. These heat strips highlight the internal fragmentation of important tokens at the granularity of pages, with a page size of 16. As shown, attention is highly sparse and localized; each page of 16 tokens contains only one or two important tokens, and in some cases, two pages or 32 tokens are required to include two consecutive important tokens, suggesting the model repeatedly locks onto specific, narrow spans rather than spreading attention broadly across the context.
