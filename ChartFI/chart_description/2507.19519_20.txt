The three heatmaps present the Modal Assurance Criterion (MAC) matrices comparing the first ten modes identified from a source 1D beam and a target 3D beam, using a subset of nodes along the centre line for the 3D beam. The matrices for the source (1D beam), target (3D beam), as well as between the source and target are displayed in panels (a), (b), and (c) respectively. In the source auto-MAC (a), most modes are strongly self-preserving with near-1.00 values on the diagonal, but there are notable within-source confusions where mass shifts off-diagonal, specifically around the mid-index modes where pairs like 2 to 4 (0.58) and 4 to 5 (0.66) stand out, alongside a smaller cluster around 6 to 7 (0.70). The target distribution in (b) is even more sharply diagonal, yet initially, it can be seen that two modes in the target are identified as almost identical (Mode 2 and Mode 4) with a correlation of 0.99, accompanied by a mild leakage from mode 8 into mode 0 (0.29). In the cross-domain mapping (c), the alignment remains largely diagonal but becomes more structured and asymmetric; early modes map cleanly, with mode 0 aligning strongly to target 0 (0.91) and mode 1 to target 1 (0.86), while mid modes show systematic mixing. A prominent interaction occurs where source Mode 4 maps strongly to target Mode 2 (0.99), but utilising source Mode 4 and target Mode 2 would introduce a dissimilar feature into the set. Additionally, source 5 splits with a dominant pull toward target 2 (0.67) and a secondary toward target 4 (0.66). Overall, the pattern suggests the target space is cleaner internally, but the source-to-target bridge introduces consistent, localized mode confusions concentrated in the mid and upper indices.
