This histogram illustrates the distribution of the smoothness metrics phi_i^Jerk across three distinct conditions: before optimization, optimized with local filtering, and optimized with the proposed method. Before optimization, the values are heavily concentrated at the high end, with a pronounced mass around roughly 10^5 to 10^7 and a long right tail extending toward about 10^10, indicating many instances of very large jerk. The chart reveals that the improvement given by the local filtering method is mainly reflected at the right border of the distribution; while it reduces the frequency of extremely high-jerk cases—highlighted in the magnified inset of the circled region—it leaves substantial density in the mid-to-high range. This limited impact occurs because the local filtering method only conducts optimization for local parts with maximal jerk values, thereby trimming outliers without fundamentally reorganizing the dataset. In contrast, the proposed method produces the most substantial change and can significantly shift the whole distribution to the left side. Under this method, the bulk of the distribution moves dramatically toward much lower values, with the main concentration centering around 10^0 to 10^2, demonstrating a superior capability to suppress both typical and extreme jerk events compared to the localized approach.
