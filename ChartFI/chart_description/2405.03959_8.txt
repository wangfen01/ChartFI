These six heatmaps illustrate the Equal Error Rate (EER) matrix under different degrees of relative rigid transformation. The genuine matches are divided into 16 subsets based on the value of relative rotation θ (rows) and translation T (columns), which are segmented evenly into Small (S), Medium (M), Large (L), and Huge (H) ranges. A critical evaluation of the data reveals that the metric is EER, meaning lower values indicate better performance; therefore, the proposed method is not an underperforming outlier as initially suggested, but rather the superior model. With error rates staying extremely low (roughly 0.02–0.07) across all settings, our scheme still leads and exhibits attractive robustness and stability compared to all other baselines. Conversely, A-KAZE is the weakest baseline, showing the highest error rates that start around 0.30–0.33 at |T|-S and rise to roughly 0.41–0.44 at |T|-H. The intermediate methods, DeepPrint and DesNet, form a middle tier where errors increase steadily with translation magnitude: DeepPrint climbs from about 0.10–0.15 to 0.30–0.32, and DesNet from 0.15–0.17 to 0.37–0.38. Across all heatmaps, the dominant driver of performance degradation is the scale of translation (|T|), as error rates consistently increase as the translation grows from S to H. In contrast, the impact of rotation is not evident, as the values across the four row conditions remain comparatively stable, indicating that the methods are far more sensitive to translation shifts than to rotational changes.
