This chart illustrates the performance difference (delta) between various training methods and the mean Majority Vote (MV) performance across the HS-Brexit and MD-Agreement datasets, with methods sorted by their mean ranking across datasets, models, and metrics. The data represents the mean differences across runs, calculated by computing the difference between the performance of a single run of a Hard Label Variation (HLV) training method and the mean performance of MV. While Accuracy and Macro F1 deltas cluster tightly around zero for most methods, indicating that many approaches produce only marginal changes in hard-label classification quality, there are notable degradations where Macro F1 drops significantly more than Accuracy, suggesting sensitivity to class imbalance or uneven per-class effects. The most consistent and substantial movements occur in the distributional and “soft” metrics, particularly PO-JSD, which is predominantly positive across methods and implies that these approaches meaningfully alter predicted probability distributions even when top-1 outcomes remain static. Entropy correlation displays the widest spread, signaling that uncertainty calibration is highly method-dependent; notably, SmF1 with TwHIN-BERT predicts zero for all test instances on HS-Brexit, rendering its entropy correlation undefined. Soft accuracy and Soft macro F1 generally trend slightly positive for a subset of methods, yet these gains remain modest, reinforcing a pattern where improvements are primarily driven by reshaping confidence and probability mass rather than delivering broad lifts in discrete classification. Ultimately, the methods differentiate themselves more through uncertainty and distributional alignment than traditional hard metrics, with performance trade-offs occasionally concentrated in minority or difficult classes.
