The charts compare model, hybrid, and human behavior across error consistency, misclassification agreement, and class-level error similarity, with model-model comparisons shown in green, human-human in blue, and model-human (hybrid) in red. The color saturation of these points indicates average accuracy for the two systems on the subset, revealing a distinct trend where higher mean accuracy points sit more to the right of the group of points, particularly correlating with increased error consistency. In the left panel, error consistency spans a wide range while misclassification agreement is generally low to moderate; the model pairs show the broadest spread and reach the highest agreement values, whereas the hybrid and human points cluster more tightly at lower agreement levels. Turning to the right panel, unlike the relationship between error consistency and misclassification agreement, the log of misclassification agreement versus class-level error similarity exhibits a strong global correlation across all subsets. Within this upward trend, the most closely aligned system pairs are model-model, which occupy the region of highest similarity and agreement. Intermediate alignment is seen with human-human and some model-model pairs, while hybrid pairs are less aligned, clustering in the middle of the distribution rather than at the high-agreement extreme. This indicates that while agreement aligns closely with class-level similarity, the best-performing behavior is characterized by high consistency without necessarily converging on the same wrong classes as the hybrid or human pairs.
