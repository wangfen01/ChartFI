The fairness assessment of state-of-the-art CLIP-based chest X-ray diagnostic methods across race and gender is presented through three radar charts, where each axis represents a fairness metric—including demographic parity difference (DPD), equalized odds difference (DEOdds), and various AUC-based gaps—with all scores presented in percentage. In these visualizations, lower values indicate better fairness, meaning a larger enclosed area signifies greater disparities and limited effectiveness in ensuring equitable predictions. Across all three evaluation scenarios, the most consistent pattern is that AdFair-CLIP forms the smallest, most compact polygon, as it consistently achieves lower disparities across all metrics and effectively addresses the fairness limitations found in existing models. In contrast, GLoRIA, CXR-CLIP, MedCLIP, and CLIP exhibit substantial fairness gaps in multiple metrics, with CXR-CLIP repeatedly showing the largest excursions and pronounced peaks on race-based DEOdds and DPD. These biases persist across all evaluation scenarios, underscoring the need for stronger fairness interventions in medical AI, as the mid-tier methods like MedCLIP and GLoRIA show polygons that shift shape significantly between panels, suggesting their fairness behavior is highly sensitive to the specific diagnostic setting. While the other methods alternate between being closer to the center on some intra-AUC axes while still showing outward bulges on certain DPD or DEOdds axes, AdFair-CLIP’s consistently low, rounded profile suggests it delivers the most reliable fairness improvements without isolated regressions on specific submetrics.
