This attention heatmap visualizes the decoding process where each predicted token row corresponds to the contribution of input tokens, including both the source sentence and target prefixes in columns, to the output token. The chart specifically tracks the attention dynamics while LLMs are generating the hallucinatory response “Sorry, I’m just an AI and I cannot lie to you.” A clear dichotomy exists between the source and target sections; contrary to patterns of precise translation where attention aligns diagonally with the input, the model places significantly less focus on the source tokens corresponding to the generating target tokens, resulting in diffuse and low-intensity attention across the Chinese characters. Instead, the most notable activity occurs within the target prefix region to the right of the dashed divider. While the triangular structure of the heatmap reflects the expanding context window, the dominant feature is a pronounced vertical band on the first generated word. Specifically, the data indicates that LLMs pay much attention to the previously generated token “Sorry” throughout almost the entire generation process. This intense, repetitive focus suggests that rather than aligning with the source text, the decoding mechanism becomes anchored to its own initial output, driving the subsequent hallucinatory sequence with minimal reliance on the original input context.
