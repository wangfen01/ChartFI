The chart illustrates the Source Exact Match score of models across different modalities. A distinct performance hierarchy emerges where most models achieve high Source EM scores when the ground truth evidence is textual but perform poorly when it is visual, indicating that although MLLMs can process multimodal inputs, they are better at aligning with textual evidence than accurately citing visual information when generating responses. Text scores are generally the highest and rise sharply for stronger, newer models, peaking around the mid-30s for Qwen2-VL-72B and remaining high for GPT-4o and GPT-4o mini, whereas earlier or smaller models sit in the single digits to mid-teens. Mixed modality scores follow a similar but dampened trajectory, with notable jumps for Qwen2-VL-72B and GPT-4o, suggesting these models transfer some text-grounding strength into multimodal settings but still incur a consistent penalty versus pure text. In contrast, Table performance is uneven and does not scale as cleanly with model strength; for instance, MiniCPM-V-2.6 exhibits relatively strong table scores compared to its other modalities, implying table extraction remains a specialized capability rather than a simple byproduct of overall model quality. Finally, Figure scores are the lowest and most compressed across models, with only modest gains at the top end, identifying figures as the hardest source-matching condition and a significant bottleneck even for high-performing systems.
