This dot plot illustrates the test-set revenue outcomes for various forecasting models in the mFRR market, categorized by hyperparameter tuning loss functions and retraining cadences against fixed-bid and lagged benchmarks. Across all configurations, machine-learning models significantly outperform the benchmarks, which remain clustered at the lower end of the scale between approximately 13,000 and 14,500 €/MW, demonstrating that sophisticated modeling consistently exceeds simple heuristic approaches. While the transition from no retraining to monthly or weekly intervals generally shifts performance to the right, retraining yields marginal changes in revenue overall, and its impact is inconsistent across the model library; specifically, while some models show a slight improvement in performance with more frequent updates, others experience a decrease, suggesting that the benefits of retraining are highly model-dependent. Hyperparameter tuning through MAE, MAPE, and negative revenue grid searches further elevates the performance ceiling, with SVR emerging as the clear leader by consistently reaching the 19,500 €/MW range, well ahead of the broader field. In contrast, linear models like Ridge and Lasso exhibit high stability but lower ceilings, frequently clustering around the 17,000 to 18,000 €/MW band regardless of the tuning regime. The data reveals that while advanced tuning loss functions can amplify the performance of top-tier models like SVR and KNeighborsRegressor, they also increase the variance across the model suite, highlighting a widening performance gap between optimized machine-learning configurations and the stagnant baseline benchmarks.
