The figure presents the latent space representation of real samples (in blue) and generated samples (in green and in black), alongside a comparison of their original series space. The points in green represent generated samples that are similar to real samples in blue, while the points in black represent outliers in the generated space. In the middle part of the figure, the original series space of the data highlights that the outlier generations (series in black) are not similar to the original space of series in blue, displaying mismatched amplitude and waveforms, unlike the reliably generated samples in green, which closely track the smooth, consistent oscillatory structure of the real sequences. With the number of neighbors used for both metrics set to 2, the left plot of the latent space contains the neighbor areas around the generated samples, as the recall metric requires; here, the neighborhood regions successfully cover the relevant real points, yielding a perfect recall measure of 1.0. In contrast, the right plot of the latent space contains the neighboring areas around the real samples as the coverage metric requires. It can be seen that the coverage metric overcomes the existence of outlier generations and produces a non-perfect measure of 0.5, in contrast to the recall metric that produces a perfect measure of 1, thereby revealing that the outlier generations fail to adequately cover the target distribution despite the proximity observed in the recall-oriented view.
