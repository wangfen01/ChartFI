The visualization illustrates the progression of network stability during extinction sequences, contrasting a detailed single-system trajectory with a normalized multi-ecosystem comparison. In the left panel, specifically depicting the Cypress food web, the average log robustness index of extinct species increases with the step, transitioning from strongly negative values in the earliest stages to strongly positive ones. Labels indicate representative species becoming extinct at each step, where early losses include lizards and rabbits, followed by a mid-sequence fluctuation around living sediment and shrews, and finally culminating in a sharp upswing where robustness peaks for turtles and bobcat. An inset further characterizes these dynamics by plotting the average log eigenvector-out centrality of extinct species versus the extinction step, offering an additional dimension of structural analysis regarding the connectivity of the lost species. The right panel generalizes these findings, displaying the normalized average log robustness index versus the normalized extinction step for multiple food webs, including St. Marks, Benguela, Florida, Coachella, and Mangrove. To enable this cross-ecosystem comparison, values are log-transformed and scaled to a range of zero to one. In all cases, the same behavior is observed.
