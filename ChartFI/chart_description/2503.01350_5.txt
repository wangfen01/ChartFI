This graphical representation reports the percentages of managerial insight optimistic (%MI_O) and pessimistic (%MI_P) for each instance, utilizing a scatter plot accompanied by a marginal box plot for each value of the percentage of the maximum budget (B_per). The horizontal axis represents %MI_O, where most observations cluster near zero, while the vertical axis represents %MI_P, which displays a more dispersed range of negative values generally concentrated between −0.10 and −0.40. A distinct structural relationship is evident across the three budget levels (0.025, 0.050, and 0.100): the larger the budget, the smaller is %MI_O and the larger is %MI_P. Specifically, the marginal boxplots reveal that as the budget percentage increases to 0.100, the distribution of %MI_P shifts upward toward zero, indicating that higher budgets mitigate the severity of pessimistic outcomes. Conversely, on the optimistic axis, the higher budget group (0.100) diverges from the tight clustering near zero seen in the 0.025 group, extending further to the left into more negative values. This suggests that while increased resources buffer against the worst-case scenarios on the pessimistic side, they are associated with a decrease in optimistic insight values. Consequently, the difference between the optimistic solution is larger, showing increased variance and negativity, while the pessimistic solution is smaller in magnitude (closer to zero), highlighting a trade-off where higher budgets stabilize pessimistic expectations at the expense of optimistic projections.
