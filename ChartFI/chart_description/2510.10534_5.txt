These heatmaps present a hyperparameter interaction analysis on IEMOCAP, visualizing accuracy trends across varying configurations of Single, Sub, and Aux values. The results show a sizeable, warm-colored region of high performance across the three views, suggesting that the method is robust to the specific choice of hyperparameters within a reasonable range. Accuracy generally stays tightly clustered in the high‑50s to low‑60s, forming a broad plateau with only localized penalties at parameter extremes. In the Single vs Sub view (Aux fixed at 1), performance is strongest when Sub is in the 1–4 range and Single is moderate (roughly 0.1–10), peaking around 60.9–61.0, while the clearest degradation appears at the highest Sub setting (8), where accuracy falls into the mid‑55s to high‑56s. Similarly, the Single vs Aux view (Sub fixed at 2) indicates that the best region concentrates around Single near 1–10 paired with Aux around 0.1–1; however, very small Aux combined with very large Single (20) produces a notable drop to approximately 55.3. The Aux vs Sub view (Single fixed at 1) further illustrates this sensitivity at the boundaries, where the surface is mostly flat with a mild ridge around Aux near 0.1–1 and Sub near 2–4, yet accuracy drops sharply when Aux is large (20) and Sub is small (0.5–2), landing near 55.4–55.7. Ultimately, while the system demonstrates general stability, the optimal performance depends on a specific combination of parameters, favoring mid-range settings and avoiding combinations such as high Sub values or high Aux paired with low Sub.
