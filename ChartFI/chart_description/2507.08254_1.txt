This radar chart presents comparisons between Raptor and state-of-the-art methods on diverse medical volume tasks, visualizing performance across ten distinct benchmarks ranging from organ segmentation to regression challenges. Raptor generally forms the largest and most consistent polygon, demonstrating higher accuracy on downstream tasks than current state-of-the-art models. While the Organ and CC-CCII benchmarks appear to be the most "solved" tasks with most methods clustering near the top scores of 1.0, Raptor maintains a clear lead on these as well as on Vessel, Synapse, Adrenal, and Nodule. A significant divergence is observed in the task Brain IDPs+; although Raptor’s absolute score is lower here than on other axes, it significantly outperforms competitors that cluster near the center, marking this as the most challenging and discriminative benchmark. Overall, the pattern suggests that while gains among leading methods are incremental on easier tasks, meaningful differentiation emerges on harder tasks such as Brain IDPs+, CTRG-B, and CTRG-C, where Raptor's dominance is most visible.
