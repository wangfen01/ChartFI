This heatmap visualizes the league results of the Advantage Alignment agents in Coin Game, displaying the pairwise return relationships across seven approaches: AdAlign, LOQA, POLA, MFOS, Always Cooperate (AC), Always Defect (AD), and Random. A clear pattern emerges where Advantage Alignment agents perform similarly to LOQA agents when evaluated against a league of different policies; both AdAlign and LOQA form a positive cluster with POLA, showing stable, mutually beneficial outcomes generally in the 0.17 to 0.35 range. Specifically, the data shows that Advantage Alignment agents cooperate with themselves and cooperate with Always Cooperate (AC), achieving high positive returns of approximately 0.35 against AC. While a superficial reading might suggest a positive linkage between AC and AD due to the high 0.63 value, the chart actually reveals a dynamic of severe exploitation: AD extracts the single largest return in the chart (0.63) from AC, while AC suffers a significant loss of -0.27. Similarly, AD exploits Random, resulting in a massive -0.53 loss for the random agent. In contrast to these vulnerable agents, Advantage Alignment agents are not exploited by Always Defect (AD); AdAlign successfully mitigates the aggression of AD, holding its own loss to a minimal -0.08 compared to the heavy losses sustained by AC and Random. MFOS remains a context-dependent contributor, hovering near zero in most pairings but conflicting sharply with AD. Overall, the chart demonstrates that AdAlign succeeds by replicating the robust behaviors of LOQA, maximizing returns with cooperative partners like AC while maintaining necessary defenses against defectors.
