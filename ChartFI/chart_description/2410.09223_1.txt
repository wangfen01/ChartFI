This comparative visualization examines the activation frequency of attention heads across twenty-four layers and sixteen head indices, specifically contrasting English IOI, Chinese IOI, and English control tasks within the BLOOM model. The display is divided into two panels, with the left comparing English IOI against Chinese IOI and the right contrasting English IOI with an English control task. Distinct marker shapes identify specific functional roles, such as Duplicate Token or Name Mover heads, while background shading highlights alignment between tasks; for pairs of heads with non-zero activation frequency, regions are shaded based on their value difference, where darker gray signifies smaller differences and higher similarity. A critical analysis of the left graph reveals a high overlap in the important heads used between languages, characterized by a substantial number of shaded head pairs that indicate a greater similarity between the activated heads in English and Chinese IOI contexts. In contrast, the right graph demonstrates that significantly different heads are used in the control task compared with the high overlap observed between the IOI tasks in Chinese and English, a divergence marked by a sparse distribution of shaded regions. Furthermore, while the control task exhibits distinct activation patterns, many of the heads that are shared between English IOI and the control are also shared with Chinese IOI, suggesting that certain fundamental attention mechanisms persist across both linguistic variations and task types.
