The heatmap illustrates the feature-specific perturbation satisfaction rates (SR_f) of five detectors, where a larger SR_f corresponds to a more intense color in the cell, revealing generally high robustness but with sharp, detector-specific weaknesses. For perturbations targeting VFs that belong to the Overflow vulnerability subclass (IBS, BSB, OE, BO), performance varies significantly across the models. Among the five detectors, DeepWukong has the highest average SR_f in this category with 88%, and ReVeal, DeepDFA, and LineVul also demonstrate high robustness. In stark contrast, SySeVR shows a pronounced weakness in this area and has the lowest average SR_f with 61%, dropping to just 33% for BSB. This sensitivity pattern is further underscored by its extremely low 18% satisfaction rate for the WA feature. Two features reveal even more dramatic discontinuities: DeepWukong exhibits a unique vulnerability to DF perturbations with a score of only 35% while others remain above 70%, and the RA feature is highly polarized, proving perfect for DeepWukong and SySeVR but collapsing to near-zero for ReVeal and LineVul, implying it triggers fundamentally different behaviors across model families. In contrast to this volatility, perturbations on graph-structure and formatting features like Node Set, Edge Set, Code Formatting, and Identifier Name are uniformly strong, with near-perfect scores where reported, reinforcing that the most significant instability is concentrated in a few semantic or rule-like features.
