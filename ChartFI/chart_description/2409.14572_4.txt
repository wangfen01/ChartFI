The composite figure presents parity plots and associated barplots of highlighted modes for the model using neighboring data points under three training neighbor settings: Farthest Neighbors, Random Neighbors, and Nearest Neighbors. The top panels compare predicted versus actual yield strength, distinguishing general data from highlighted modes shown in red, while the bottom histograms display the top five most frequently predicted yield strength values to investigate whether the model is merely guessing a few commonly present values. In the Farthest Neighbors configuration, the approach performs poorly, evidenced by a near-zero fit (R² = 0.01) and the largest error (MAE ≈ 327.45 MPa); the scatter plot shows predictions collapsing into repeated horizontal bands, which corresponds to the high-frequency bars in the histogram, indicating severe mode collapse where the model outputs are quantized. Moving to Random Neighbors yields a clearer positive relationship, improving explanatory power (R² = 0.41) and reducing error (MAE ≈ 178.21 MPa), though the persistence of specific high-frequency modes suggests the model retains some tendency toward guessing common values. The Nearest Neighbors approach achieves the strongest alignment with the parity line, delivering a substantial jump in accuracy (R² = 0.84, MAE ≈ 80.5 MPa) and a more continuous spread of predictions. This setting significantly reduces both the dominance and frequency of the repeated modes, suggesting that by identifying the mode collapse behavior observed in the other settings, one can evaluate the validity of those predictions and potentially improve performance, ultimately transitioning from biased, discretized outputs to near-calibrated predictions.
