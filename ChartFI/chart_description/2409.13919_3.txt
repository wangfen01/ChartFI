The heatmap is organized as a pairwise similarity matrix where darker cells represent a higher value of similarity. The first five rows/columns correspond to humans, followed by CNN-based models, then transformer-based models, revealing distinct structural relationships across these groups. Some consistent patterns emerge; for example, both human-human and model-model pairs tend to exhibit higher values compared to human-model pairs, which consistently register the lowest scores, mostly in the single digits. However, despite this separation, the values for human-human pairs are significantly lower than most model-model pairs within the dataset, indicating that computational models exhibit much stronger internal consistency than human participants do with one another. Within the model-model comparisons, the matrix shows clear clustering by architecture family, with the strongest off-diagonal values concentrating within the CNN families, especially among VGG, ResNet and DenseNet variants where many pairings sit in the high 30s to high 40s. Transformer-based models form their own block with moderate-to-strong internal similarity, as ViT variants show solid mutual alignment and Swin variants appear slightly tighter. Finally, the “noisy_student” and “clip_v” models stand out as anomalies with comparatively weak alignment to the rest, suggesting their training objectives or representation spaces differ enough that they do not neatly fit into the established CNN or ViT/Swin similarity structures.
