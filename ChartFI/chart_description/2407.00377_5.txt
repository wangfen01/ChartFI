The two sunburst plots visualize detailed behavioral changes in the involved demographic factuality accuracy of model generations on the same evaluation subject after applying diversity interventions for DoFaiR-Gender and DoFaiR-Race under DALLE-3. For DoFaiR-Gender, outcomes are largely characterized by stability, as diversity and correctness remain unchanged in 54% of cases. Nevertheless, 38.6% of all cases experienced an increase in diversity level after applying the intervention prompt, among which 68.13% (therefore 26.3% overall) also witnessed a decrease in factuality. This contrasts with only 12.3% of cases where both diversity and correctness increased, while simultaneous decreases in both metrics were rare at 3.1%. The influence of intervention prompts on generation diversity is more obvious on DoFaiR-Race. In this domain, 54.7% of all cases witness a higher diversity level, among which 63.8% came with a decrease in factuality, making the combination of increased diversity and decreased correctness the single largest segment at 34.9%. Additionally, diversity decreases are significantly more common for race at 26.3%, with a notable 13.7% showing improved correctness despite lower diversity. Overall, despite the relative stability of the gender results, the aggregate data highlights a high co-occurrence of increased diversity and decreased factuality when applying diversity interventions, indicating a remarkable trade-off between being diverse and being factual in image generations.
