The chart illustrates emotion distributions across various conditions defined by the direction of change in pleasantness and other-agency, establishing |β| = 0 as the original distribution of emotions in the dataset. As the absolute beta magnitude increases from 0 to 4, the emotional mix shifts systematically from balanced, mixed-affect profiles toward polarized compositions where specific emotions dominate. Analyzing the individual dimensions reveals that promoting pleasantness increases the share of joy and pride outputs, while demoting pleasantness increases the share of sadness, guilt, and anger outputs. Similarly, promoting other-agency significantly increases the share of anger, whereas demoting other-agency significantly increases the share of guilt, shifting weight toward internal negative states rather than external blame. The combined conditions highlight the strongest contrasts: promoting pleasant other-agency significantly increases the share of joy outputs, while the promotion of unpleasant other-agency significantly increases the share of anger, which becomes overwhelmingly dominant at larger |β|. A distinct anomaly occurs when pleasantness is promoted while other-agency is demoted; in this specific configuration, the results show a successful promotion of emotion pride with no further occurrences of joy at the highest magnitude, indicating that extreme beta magnitudes can isolate specific emotional nuances like pride from the broader positive spectrum.
