This set of plots illustrates the signal segmentation results for Sit-to-Stand (STS) exercises captured across three different sensing modalities, where segmentation is performed for (a) Kinect, (b) Radar, and (c) Wearable systems. Each sensor section is further divided into three subplots displaying kinematic data for the knee, waist, and thigh-waist, with angle measurements plotted as solid lines against the left axis and velocity as dotted lines against the right axis. The temporal structure of the physical activity is clearly delineated, as each STS repetition is indicated between two vertical dashed lines. Within the knee angle signal specifically, key postural states are marked to define the movement cycle, where high peaks (sitting - red triangle) and low peaks (standing - blue square) were detected based on the knee angle. The waveforms exhibit consistent periodic patterns across all modalities, and a comparative visual analysis reveals that the segmentation results show a high degree of similarity across the three sensors, indicating that the Radar and Wearable systems capture the kinematic dynamics of the sit-to-stand motion with comparable fidelity to the Kinect system.
