This visualization plots the relative error of cell properties and FCD scores against the proportion of tear artifact affected regions in image patches, comparing performance across artifact levels ranging from 0–10% to 50–60%. The errors evaluated between the Original dataset and three distinct groups—the Tear Artifact set, the PosDiffAE + Restore_wo set, and the PosDiffAE + Restore restoration set—are highlighted in the cell count and cell occupancy panels, while the central panel showcases the FCD scores within these sets. For all the plots, the errors and scores of different regions are highlighted with multicoloured dots, and the inter-class mean and variance of them are also shown through violin distributions. From the cell count and cell occupancy plots, it is evident that the error of the Tear Artifact set increases steeply compared to the other two restoration sets, exhibiting a higher relative error as damage increases. Although the error of the PosDiffAE + Restore_wo set is lower than the Tear Artifact set, it deviates significantly from the PosDiffAE + Restore set, especially for higher artifact-affected images where the distributions become taller and wider. In contrast, the mean errors of various regions for the PosDiffAE + Restore set lie below the 0.1 error value, while the other sets have higher values, indicating that the full restoration method maintains resilience even under heavy corruption. The plot of the FCD scores shares similar trends compared to the other plots.
