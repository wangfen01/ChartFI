The figure compares heatmaps of the population-level covariance estimates from SparseSample (left) and SparseCovReg (right), detailing inferred pairwise relationships among genes organized into pathway blocks such as p53, calcium, PI3K/MTOR, and Ras-Raf/MEK-ERK. In these displays, positive values are shown in red and negative values are shown in blue, with the diagonal representing self-relations. While the soft-thresholding estimator shares some common patterns with SparseCovReg, it is considerably noisier, characterized by a dense network of diffuse connectivity that suggests substantial cross-talk. In contrast, the SparseCovReg network is markedly sparser, where most entries are near zero and associations appear as isolated patches, indicating that regularization effectively prunes weaker relationships to reveal a more parsimonious structure. This clarity allows for the isolation of robust biological signals; using the population covariance matrix from SparseCovReg, one can identify high correlations between PIK3CA and genes in the calcium signaling pathway including CALML5, CALM1, CAMK1D and CAMK2B. Furthermore, the sparse model reveals distinct negative correlations between PIK3CA and genes in the p53 signaling pathway. Ultimately, the transition from the potentially overfit soft-thresholded map to the SparseCovReg model highlights a shift from spurious, sample-specific noise to stable, high-confidence gene-gene interactions.
