The heatmap illustrates Spearman’s rank correlation coefficients, ρ, between benchmark performance and arena scores across three benchmark languages—Basque (EU), English (EN), and Spanish (ES)—and three arena dimensions: Global, Content, and Language. Overall, average Basque benchmark performance and arena rankings correlate strongly, with ρ > 0.80 across all arena dimensions, a trend that is particularly pronounced for specific benchmarks including EusProficiency, EusTrivia, EusExams, and BertaQA. This consistency is reflected in the Average column, where Basque maintains high values between 0.81 and 0.88, though it shows relative soft spots on MGSM n-CoT and MMLU where correlations drop into the 0.38 to 0.49 range. In contrast, the average of English benchmarks displays overall positive but non-significant correlations, typically ranging from 0.28 to 0.34, as a few high-alignment tasks like BertaQA Local and BertaQA Global partially offset broad underperformance elsewhere. Specifically, some of the English and Spanish benchmarks show large negative correlations; English is sharply negative on ARC Challenge and ARC Easy with coefficients around -0.5, while also showing systematic mismatches on PIQA and XStoryCloze. Spanish benchmarks show, on average, no correlation with the arena, hovering between -0.05 and 0.01, with sparse reporting and occasional notable negatives such as -0.42 on XStoryCloze in the Language arena. The Language arena dimension appears to amplify these weaknesses, as English and Spanish correlations on MMLU, PIQA, and XStoryCloze generally deteriorate or remain negative compared to the Global and Content dimensions, highlighting a lack of alignment between these languages' benchmark performances and the arena's evaluative criteria.
