These radar charts compare the performance of the proposed model against five existing state-of-the-art methods—MIDD, DCNet, TNet, LSNet, and TAGFNet—across the VT821, VT1000, and VT5000 datasets. The evaluation spans multiple dimensions, including standard correlation metrics (Em, Fm, Sm) and error rates (MAE), as well as specific challenging conditions where R-M denotes performance under RGB-Missing and T-M denotes performance under Thermal-Missing. Across all three charts, the overall shape is consistent: the "Ours" model forms the largest, most uniform polygon on the correlation-style axes while simultaneously maintaining the smallest footprint on the error axes (MAE, MAE_R-M, MAE_T-M). This distinct visual profile indicates that the proposed method excels by achieving the best results across these varied metrics, demonstrating unparalleled robustness and overall performance. From VT821 to VT1000, the model shows a noticeable lift on the main Em, Fm, and Sm spokes and their R-M variants, with MAE remaining very low, suggesting improved fidelity without paying an error penalty. Moving to VT5000, MAE stays tightly controlled; although competing methods sometimes approach the proposed model on T-M spokes, they fail to match its combined high correlations and low errors. Ultimately, while some baselines achieve strong performance on isolated metrics, they exhibit irregular profiles with comparatively higher MAE-related values, whereas the proposed model avoids these trade-offs to deliver a stable advantage that holds as the dataset changes.
