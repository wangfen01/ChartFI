These radar charts compare I^2EBench scores across different dimensions using (a) original instructions and (b) diverse instructions, illustrating that performance is highly task-dependent. The observations reveal that no single model achieves the best performance across all evaluation dimensions. Regarding low-level editing, InstructDiffusion demonstrates superior results, attaining the highest scores in 4 out of 7 low-level editing evaluation dimensions when using original instructions, and 3 out of 7 when using diverse instructions; this dominance is visually punctuated by a dramatic peak in watermark removal, where the method rises sharply above the rest, alongside strong trends in rain and snow removal. In the deblurring dimensions, MGIE stands out significantly. For high-level editing, both MagicBrush and InstructAny2Pix perform impressively, though their effectiveness varies by condition: MagicBrush achieves the highest scores in 3 evaluation dimensions using original instructions, while InstructAny2Pix achieves the highest scores in 3 dimensions using diverse instructions. While tasks such as background replacement and region accuracy show clustered, stable performance across most methods, counting is uniformly weak, forming the most pronounced trough in both charts and indicating a shared limitation in instance-level reasoning. Comparing the two panels, the overall shapes and relative rankings are largely preserved, indicating robustness to instruction variation, yet the specific shifts in high-level editing leadership suggest that semantic consistency remains sensitive to prompt phrasing.
