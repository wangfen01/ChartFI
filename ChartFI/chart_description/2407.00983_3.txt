The charts present the Delta DSC (%) across eight datasets, establishing that DSC_Δ is the fairness evaluation metric used to analyze a range of prompting methods split by four prompt variants (center, rand, rands, bbox). Overall, the data demonstrates that bias widely exists in FMs for medical imaging, and our results in both 2D and 3D datasets reveal similar disparities. While most models are evaluated across all panels, SAM-Med3D, FastSAM-3D, and SegVol are only applicable for 3D datasets. In the 2D benchmarks, HAM10000 and TUSC show clear sensitivity to prompt choice, whereas Montgomery is the most irregular, showing pronounced peaks for SAM. FairSeg, conversely, remains comparatively stable with low-amplitude variations, suggesting limited responsiveness to prompt perturbations. In the 3D panels, benchmarks like IRCADb, SPIDER, and KiTS display larger, more volatile jumps compared to the constrained deltas of CANDI. The dominant pattern indicates that prompt engineering impacts fairness unevenly; while bbox and rands frequently expose the largest disparities, these effects are episodic and dataset-specific, implying that robustness is driven more by matching the right method to the data regime than by any single universally best prompt setting.
