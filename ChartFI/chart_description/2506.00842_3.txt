The reward label distribution across the Bird, WikiTQ, and FinQA datasets illustrates a comprehensive comparison of how frequently different reward values occur from 0 to 1, demonstrating increased diversity and domain coverage with diluted and decomposed high-density knowledge. In the Bird dataset, the distribution is heavily concentrated at the extreme low end, featuring a pronounced spike near 0 that towers over the rest of the range, while mid-to-high rewards appear relatively sparse and fairly flat with only a modest uptick near 1. WikiTQ displays a more even spread across the interior of the scale, yet it maintains clear edge effects with noticeable mass near 0 and a distinct spike at 1, suggesting a mix of middling outcomes alongside a meaningful share of perfect scores. FinQA exhibits the most polarized, U-shaped tendency, characterized by elevated frequency near 0 and a strong rise toward 1, leaving the middle range comparatively thinner and more jagged. Across all three datasets, the presence of reward labels in the (0, 1) range is significant because these values were not included in the original training dataset and reveal distinct difficulty differences after decomposition. The most salient anomalies are the sharp boundary spikes at 0 and 1, which indicate thresholding or evaluation dynamics that produce exact-zero and exact-one outcomes, while the broader mid-range coverage in WikiTQ and the more pronounced U-shaped boundary effects in FinQA highlight a shift toward more complex, non-binary reward structures that capture nuanced performance levels.
