These two heatmaps illustrate expert neuron alignment across languages in BLOOM-560M at different training stages, measured by Pearson correlation coefficients averaged across concepts using Fisher’s Z transformation. At the early training stage (step 1000), the majority of off-diagonal cells are very light, indicating weak cross-language correlations and a largely language-specific structure with only scattered pockets of moderate positive correlation. By the late training stage (step 400 000), the matrix becomes broadly warmer and more uniformly red across many language pairs, demonstrating a clear global increase in positive correlations and a stronger shared representational space. The most notable change is the emergence of coherent blocks where languages written in Latin script cluster more tightly with each other, while several non-Latin scripts also show stronger within-group alignment than observed previously. Furthermore, the late-stage heatmap reveals pronounced vertical and horizontal banding for certain languages, suggesting they become consistently more correlated with many others and potentially act as hub representations or reflect shared training signals. Negative correlations remain rare in both stages, and the dominant shift from sparse, localized similarity early on to widespread, structured similarity later indicates that the model learns more transferable, cross-lingual features as training progresses.
