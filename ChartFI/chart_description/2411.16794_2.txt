This heatmap visualizes the association between surgical phases and instrument usage, displaying the frequency of tool presence across different procedural steps to highlight operational patterns. Analysis of the dataset reveals a strong correlation between surgical phases and tool presence, evidenced by distinct high-value cells that indicate specific tool-phase dependencies. While some instruments are highly specialized, others exhibit versatility; for example, the tool Vectis was exclusively associated with the Nucleus Delivery phase with a prominent usage frequency of 73, and the Cautery was most commonly used in the phase Conjunctival Cautery with a frequency of 71. Conversely, instruments such as Hoskins Forceps and Crescent Blade are used in multiple surgical phases, with the Hoskins Forceps displaying particularly widespread utility across steps like Peritomy, Scleral Groove, Incision, Tunnel and Main Incision Entry where usage rates frequently exceed 70. Further highlighting specific phase-tool alignments, the Rhexis Needle is dominant during Capsulorhexis, while the Simcoe Cannula is heavily relied upon for OVD Wash and Cortical Wash, underscoring the distinct operational requirements of each surgical stage.
