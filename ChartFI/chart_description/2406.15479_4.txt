This radar chart evaluates the normalized performance of multiple model-merging strategies across a diverse suite of NLP benchmarks, with the fine-tuned baseline establishing the upper performance envelope and the pretrained baseline sitting noticeably lower. Amidst these boundaries, Twin-Merging consistently outperforms other methods, approaching fine-tuned performance. Notably, Twin-Merging achieves nearly 100 % normalized accuracy across various tasks, including Summarization, BBQ, TruthfulQA, MMLU, and RTE, effectively mirroring the reference model's capabilities. In contrast, conventional model merging methods degrade notably, especially with many tasks, nearly reaching pre-trained levels. This degradation is particularly evident on COLA and STSB, where the largest cross-method spread occurs and approaches like Weight Averaging and Task Arithmetic collapse toward very low scores, implying a struggle to retain nuanced linguistic signals. While DARE variants and Ties-Merging mitigate some of the most severe failures found in simpler arithmetic strategies, they still trail Twin-Merging on tasks where instability is pronounced. Consequently, Twin-Merging maintains strong performance on paraphrase and entailment tasks such as MRPC, QQP, QNLI, and MNLI with relatively little variance, distinguishing itself as the most robust option for avoiding sharp regressions while staying near fine-tuned performance.
