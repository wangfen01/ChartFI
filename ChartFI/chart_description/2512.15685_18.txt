This chart displays the time series of the T_F^2 statistic under different training strategies throughout 2019, where all variables are normalized to a common scale to facilitate direct comparison. To contextualize the model outputs, the true leak count and total leakage volume are shown for reference. The total leakage volume (red) remains low-to-moderate through spring but accelerates sharply from late August into year-end, while the leaks count (black) climbs more gradually in a staircase pattern from roughly 1 to 5, indicating that the late-year deterioration is driven more by increasing volume than by a sudden jump in event frequency. Distinct regime shifts are highlighted by dashed vertical lines that mark the onset (red) and termination (green) of leakage events. In terms of algorithmic performance, the first two strategies—the supervised model (blue) and MCD with 80% support (green)—have yielded similar results, tracking closely together as they rise from March into May and exhibiting a pronounced spike in late August to September that aligns with the surge in leakage volume. Conversely, the third approach, labeled as “no filtering” (purple), has produced results that are, in some ways, superior to the first two methods.
