The radar chart compares the proposed MambaEVT method with other SOTA trackers across 14 challenging attributes of the EventVOT dataset, such as small target (ST), fast motion (FM), and deformable (DEF). The visualization reveals that while most methods cluster in the mid-to-high range, the MambaEVT model achieves the best performance in some attributes like CM and DEF, consistently staying near the outer rings of the axes. Furthermore, compared to some other SOTA tracking methods, specifically ARTrack and ODTrack, the model obtains competitive results on attributes like the influence of background object motion (BOM) and background clutter (BC), as well as maintaining solid standing on SV, SIO, and NM. MambaEVT-P closely mirrors this top-tier performance, whereas ARTrack, ODTrack, and OSTrack form a competitive cluster that is generally close to the leaders but exhibits slight dips on specific dimensions. MixFormer tracks this leading pack but tends to sit slightly inside the top group, indicating small but persistent gaps rather than a single major deficiency. The most pronounced anomaly is the stark underperformance of STARK, which collapses toward the center on multiple attributes, highlighting that the main separation between methods is driven by consistency across attributes. Ultimately, these analysis results demonstrate that the proposed MambaEVT model offers robust tracking performance across various real-world challenges, distinguishing itself through broad-based gains rather than isolated peaks.
