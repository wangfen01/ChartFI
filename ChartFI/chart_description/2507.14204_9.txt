The performance of LaCache and StreamingLLM on the Needle-In-A-Haystack benchmark using LongChat-7b-v1.5-32k under a 25% cache budget is visualized through accuracy heatmaps across context lengths from 4k to 32k and depths from 0 to 100, where greener indicates better performance. StreamingLLM with 25% budget achieves 33.40% Acc and exhibits a highly polarized, bimodal distribution; it maintains high accuracy at depth 0, ranging from 88.00% to 96.00%, but suffers a total collapse to 0.00% across nearly all mid-range depths from 10.0 to 70.0. Accuracy only recovers at the deepest levels, reaching 100.00% between depths 80.0 and 100.0, though it remains vulnerable to significant anomalies such as the drop to 24.00% at a 21k context length and 0.00% at 28k at the maximum depth. In contrast, LaCache with 25% budget achieves a significantly higher 65.30% Acc and demonstrates much greater robustness across the depth-context grid. While LaCache also shows some instability at shallow-to-mid depths—notably at depth 10.0 where accuracy frequently hits 0.00%—it avoids the sustained mid-depth collapse seen in StreamingLLM, maintaining substantial green regions of high performance throughout the 20.0 to 90.0 depth range. Although LaCache experiences localized performance dips, such as the 10.00% accuracy at depth 80.0 for a 16k context and 0.00% at depth 100.0 for a 28k context, its overall surface is far more resilient, suggesting that LaCache is better at preserving relevant information regardless of its position within the sequence, whereas StreamingLLM is strictly limited to retrieving information from the very beginning or very end of the context.
