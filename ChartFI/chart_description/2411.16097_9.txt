This chart illustrates the comparative accuracy of vertical location estimation methods during lifting activities at ground level, plotting the hand and load location in centimeters over a time period ranging from approximately 873 to 1273 seconds. The visualization displays the wrist vertical location calculated with the raw barometric pressure (red line), the wrist vertical location calibrated with the KVLU method (blue line), and the ground-truth Load Vertical Location (LVL) (black line). Notably, the data reveals that the LVL was not zero, indicating a consistent vertical offset for the load rather than a starting point at the absolute ground. A critical analysis of the trends shows that the estimation derived from absolute barometer measurements (red line) suffers from significant signal drift, erroneously indicating an increase in height that deviates substantially from the actual motion as time progresses. In contrast, the calibrated measurement (blue line) maintains consistency with the ground truth, effectively correcting for the barometric errors and preserving the periodic structure of the lifting tasks. Ultimately, the visual evidence demonstrates that the proposed KVLU method significantly improves the accuracy and reliability of the LVL measurement by stabilizing the vertical location data against the drift observed in raw sensor readings.
