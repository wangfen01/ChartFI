The figure illustrates domain similarities between source- and target-domain features extracted from different layers across four datasets: FSS-1000, DeepGlobe, ISIC2018, and Chest X-ray. In these heatmaps, a brighter color means a higher domain similarity, indicating less overfitting to the source domain and less feature entanglement. Across all datasets, the dominant pattern is a bright diagonal band, demonstrating that corresponding source and target layers generally match best. Conversely, the cross-match deviating from the diagonal shows much lower domain similarities, most notably in the top-right corner which corresponds to matching Layer 0 of the target domain and Layer 11 of the source domain. Despite this general trend of low off-diagonal similarity, a small fraction of cross-matches show higher CKA values than the diagonal layer-wise ones, suggesting that specific neighboring layers may share significant representational overlap. FSS-1000 appears the most uniformly moderate with a softer diagonal and mid-level similarity spread, indicating broader but less sharply defined alignment. DeepGlobe presents the strongest contrast, characterized by a pronounced diagonal and large dark blocks that imply a rigid one-to-one correspondence where non-matching features contribute little to the target, potentially reflecting domain-specific representations that do not transfer cleanly. ISIC2018 and Chest X-ray sit between these extremes, exhibiting clear diagonals alongside localized brighter patches near the lower-right portion, hinting that a subset of higher-index features align especially well and may be carrying more domain-invariant signals.
