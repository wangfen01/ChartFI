The accuracy of SLA violation classification across the PASSION and Synthetic-700 topologies is characterized by near-perfect initial performance followed by sharp degradations that align closely with marked concept drift events, though the implementation of NDT synchronization significantly improves the model's ability to recover and sustain higher accuracy. In the PASSION topology, NDT synchronization maintains accuracy between 95% and 100% for the majority of time windows, despite brief early dips and two major breakdowns—a sudden collapse near the mid-series and a longer low-accuracy stretch following a later drift—before achieving a partial recovery toward the end of the observation period. Without synchronization, the PASSION model becomes increasingly unstable after the mid-series point, exhibiting repeated oscillations between near-100% and near-0% accuracy before settling into a prolonged low-accuracy regime, which indicates that drift effects persist and compound when the model is not kept aligned. Similar improvements are observed in the Synthetic-700 topology, where the synchronized model holds near-100% accuracy until the later drift region, at which point the data becomes highly volatile with frequent deep drops and a pronounced failure period where accuracy stays near zero for an extended span before snapping back to near-100% late in the series, suggesting a delayed but effective update. It is also noteworthy that part of these SLA violations occurs during periods in which the VTwin is undergoing retraining, as evidenced by the black markers indicating VTwin updates following major accuracy collapses. In contrast, the unsynchronized approach in the Synthetic-700 setting is dominated by sustained instability and long near-zero plateaus with only intermittent spikes, implying a fundamental failure to adapt to the evolving network conditions. Ultimately, while concept drift triggers abrupt accuracy collapses in both topologies, NDT synchronization reduces the duration and persistence of these low-accuracy periods, whereas the unsynchronized approach exhibits more frequent collapses and longer-lasting degradation.
