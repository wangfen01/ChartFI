The two heatmaps illustrate the correlation structure between data pattern indicators—considering characteristics such as class percentages, error concentration near edges (EIB@3), and input mask mIoU—and per-class accuracy metrics (F1/IoU) for the AQSNet and SegAssess evaluation setups. A fundamental trend across both panels is that accuracy for both models correlates positively with class pixel percentage; in the simpler AQSNet view, this is evident as PPPCF_FP aligns strongly with FP-oriented accuracy (0.75–0.83) and PPPCF_FN with FN-oriented accuracy (0.83–0.86). While AQSNet shows a muted structure where non-matching pairings hover near zero, SegAssess reveals stronger, more polarized relationships and uniquely shows non-negligible negative correlations between a class’s accuracy and the proportion of its opposite class. Additionally, the analysis indicates that error concentration near edges (EIB@3) negatively impacts the assessment accuracy of most classes, with correlations ranging from -0.31 to -0.78 in SegAssess, except for TN metrics where the relationship remains slightly positive. Regarding overall quality, the accuracy of the input uncheck segmentation mask (mIoU) positively contributes to correctness categories, such as TP (0.51), while acting to suppress the identification of FP and FN pixels; this suppression mechanism explains the consistent negative correlations between mIoU and error-based metrics (FP/FN) observed in both the AQSNet and SegAssess panels.
