The heatmap presents an analysis of the activated frequency for each expert across 16 distinct Expert IDs under four experimental conditions, where the value denotes the frequency and 1.0 means the expert is activated in every SQL query. A clear separation exists between the sparse activation patterns observed when the balance term is removed and the distributed patterns seen when it is present. Without the balance term, fewer experts are used for each query; specifically, in the w/o L_baln setting, only two experts are predominantly selected—Experts 9 and 12—both reaching full activation (1.0) while nearly all others remain at zero. A similar sparsity characterizes the condition without both components, where activity is concentrated almost entirely in Expert 9 (1.0) and Expert 5 (0.9), with only a moderate bump at Expert 12 (0.5). In sharp contrast, adding the balance term (w/o L_sprs) utilizes almost all experts for every query, producing consistently high activation across the board with values frequently ranging from 0.4 to 1.0. This indicates a shift toward broad, distributed engagement rather than reliance on a few specialists. Ultimately, enabling both terms simultaneously in LEADS balances expert usage, positioning the system between these extremes by maintaining pronounced peaks at Experts 5, 12, and 14 (all at or near 1.0) while avoiding the excessive saturation observed when the sparsity term is removed.
