The scatter plots present a sensitivity analysis where each sample represents a training result using different optimizers and learning rates, annotated by distinct symbols and colors, to evaluate the trade-off between training loss and accuracy on CIFAR10 and ArtWR datasets. With x-axes presented in log scale, the visualization tracks performance as distinct learning rates are sampled from an appropriate interval for each method, represented by different color bars. On CIFAR10, OCNOpt demonstrates remarkable stability, as its values exhibit a more concentrated distribution on the plots, clustering tightly at low training losses around 10^-1 while sustaining high accuracy in the upper-70% range. Conversely, Adam and SGD achieve similar peak accuracies but display significant spread along the loss axis; as training loss rises toward and beyond 10^0, their accuracy drops sharply, indicating a regime where learning-rate choices destabilize training. This trend is even more distinct on ArtWR, where OCNOpt not only converges to higher accuracies with lower training losses—specifically concentrating between 10^-4 and 10^-3—but also avoids the volatility seen in the baselines. While Adam and SGD show a broader loss range and a pronounced negative relationship between loss and accuracy, SGD specifically reveals a notable anomaly with runs reaching very high losses near 10^2 and markedly reduced accuracy, consistent with divergent training. Ultimately, OCNOpt proves to be the most consistent method, maintaining a tight, Pareto-dominant distribution across both datasets while competing optimizers exhibit greater sensitivity to hyperparameter settings.
