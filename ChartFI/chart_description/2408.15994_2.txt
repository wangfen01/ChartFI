This radar chart illustrates PSNR comparisons between Perceive-IR and several state-of-the-art All-in-One image restoration methods across eight distinct degradation scenarios, where higher values indicate superior performance. Perceive-IR, represented by the outermost red boundary, outperforms existing state-of-the-art All-in-One restoration methods by exhibiting superior performance across nearly every task, with its results marked in red and the best overall results indicated in bold. The model demonstrates a particularly dominant lead in weather-related restoration, peaking at 37.25 on Rain100L (Deraining*) and 38.29 on Rain100L (Deraining), while maintaining a significant margin over competitors in SOTS-Outdoor dehazing tasks with scores of 28.19 and 30.87. InstructIR (blue) serves as the closest competitor, matching Perceive-IR’s performance on CBSD68 denoising and remaining competitive in low-light enhancement (LOL) and GoPro deblurring, yet it suffers from a notable performance dip in dehazing compared to the red frontier. Gridformer (green) occupies a middle-tier position with solid results in denoising and deblurring but shows relative weakness in deraining and dehazing. PromptIR (orange) displays an uneven profile, performing reasonably well on SOTS-Outdoor dehazing but experiencing a pronounced drop on the Rain100L (Deraining*) axis, suggesting instability across different weather degradations. IDR (yellow) is consistently the lowest performer, exhibiting the sharpest troughs across the chart, particularly in deraining and low-light enhancement, which highlights its limited generalization. Ultimately, Perceive-IR combines the highest peak performance with the most robust stability, effectively pushing the state-of-the-art boundary across all evaluated image restoration scenarios.
