This series of plots illustrates the lateral states with varying discretization steps under a two-step steering input, specifically comparing lateral velocity and yaw rate over a four-second duration. The analysis benchmarks a proposed model against the Forward Euler method and ground truth data across three distinct discrete time steps (T_s) of 0.01 s, 0.05 s, and 0.1 s. At the smallest time step of 0.01 s, all three curves align closely, indicating accurate tracking. However, as the time step increases to 0.05 s, the Forward Euler method begins to exhibit significant oscillations around the step changes, whereas the proposed model remains smooth. This disparity becomes extreme at a time step of 0.1 s, where the Forward Euler method diverges into severe instability, while the proposed model continues to track the ground truth effectively. It can be seen that the proposed model maintains numerical stability no matter whether the discrete time step T_s is 0.01 s, 0.05 s, or 0.1 s. Ultimately, the simulation results make evident that the proposed discretization method has great advantages over the forward Euler method in terms of numerical stability.
