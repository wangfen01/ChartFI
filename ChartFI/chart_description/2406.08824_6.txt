This heatmap illustrates the probability of specific robot-related associations across various person descriptors, with rows sorted by average probability across tasks to highlight aggregate trends in the data. The analysis reveals distinct stratification in how different groups are perceived, with the highest probability of harmful actions or biased associations concentrated among descriptors such as Gypsy, Atheist, Iraqi, Romani, Able-bodied, and Nonspeaking. Conversely, the lowest probability of harmful actions is observed for groups including Japanese, Middle-aged, Vietnamese, Young, Colombian, and Indian. In terms of specific variables, the Facial Expression and Expected Room Cleanliness tasks are among the largest regarding the quantity of harmful assignments generated. Facial expression emotion (FEER) is consistently high across nearly all groups, often clustering around the upper range, suggesting that emotion-related facial cues are broadly expected regardless of the descriptor. However, the most significant between-group variation emerges in expected room cleanliness, where the data indicates cleanliness expectations are highly sensitive to the person label; particularly, Llama assigns high chances of a dirty room for people described as Gypsy, Iraqi, Romani, Black and Latino (P≥0.7). Rescue priority also varies meaningfully, with descriptors such as able-bodied and nondisabled showing notably elevated values compared to a general mid-to-low baseline, implying that perceived urgency or prioritization is unevenly distributed across labels. While collaboration trust and expected cleanliness preference generally sit in the low-to-moderate range with occasional peaks, and security risk remains low across most descriptors, proximity command stands out as a flat, non-differentiating variable that is uniformly near zero for every group. Overall, the dominant pattern highlights a strong, near-universal emphasis on facial emotion cues paired with specific, high-magnitude biases in cleanliness expectations and rescue prioritization that drive the meaningful segmentation across person descriptors.
