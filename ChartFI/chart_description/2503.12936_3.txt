The histograms of ranks compare signal types and model outputs, visually demonstrating the phenomenon of rank expansion. In the left panel, the noisy far-field distribution is pushed furthest to the right and spread widest, suggesting that distance effects and added noise inflate apparent rank and variability. Notably, a red arrow indicates a reduction in rank corresponding to the suppression of noise and reverberation, as the cleaned near-field distribution shifts slightly left compared to the raw near-field baseline. The right panel details rank differences relative to this cleaned baseline, revealing that most methods result in rank differences greater than zero for the majority of processed samples. The predictive model exhibits the most pronounced deviation with a large positive shift and broad spread, while FNSE-SAT, FNSE-SAT-StoRM, StoRM and FNSE-SB show moderate positive differences with tighter distributions. StoRM aligns closest to zero among the positively shifted methods. The significant exception is SGMSE, which displays a predominantly negative distribution with substantial variability; unlike the other approaches that consistently overestimate rank, SGMSE systematically underestimates it relative to the cleaned near-field signals.
