These sunburst charts visualize the compositions of model names in the main conference papers at EMNLP 2020 and 2023, where component sizes correspond to their share and are arranged counterclockwise by their size. In this visualization, each group of models with the same root is represented by a unique color shared between graphs, and branches indicate dependency; for instance, the hierarchy within the 2023 data illustrates that GPT-3.5 is based on and fine-tuned from GPT-3. The 2020 landscape shows a clear dominance of encoder-centric architectures, with the blue BERT family commanding 55% of the field, while orange-colored RNNs retain a 20% presence driven largely by LSTMs, and GPT is a minor red slice at 5% mostly tied to GPT-2. By 2023, the center of gravity shifts dramatically as GPT rises to 30%, becoming the largest single family. Within this expanded red section, the branching structure reveals rapid evolution: GPT-3 accounts for 71% of the GPT family, serving as the root for GPT-3.5 (79% of the GPT-3 branch), which in turn branches into ChatGPT (51% of the GPT-3.5 branch), while GPT-4 appears as a distinct, emerging branch in the outer ring. Concurrently, BERT’s share drops sharply to 25%, and while the blue section shows RoBERTa growing to constitute 27% of the BERT family, the overall trend indicates encoder models are no longer the default backbone. The RNN and CNN era effectively fades, replaced by a diversified ecosystem of transformer variants, including the emergence of T5 (12%) and LLaMA (7%). This progression highlights a community-wide pivot toward instruction-tuned, generative large language models and a move away from the recurrent architectures that previously defined the field.
