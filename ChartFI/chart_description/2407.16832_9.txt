This lower-triangular correlation matrix of exogenous variables visualizes how model parameters interact, with each cell of the matrix containing correlation coefficients ranging from -1 to 1. While the majority of pairwise correlations cluster near zero, indicating limited linear dependence across much of the feature set, specific pockets of strong coupling stand out. The most prominent pattern is the very high positive association between L1 and W1 at approximately 0.88 and between L2 and W2 at 0.92; this reflects a strong positive relationship between vehicle width and length, indicating that larger vehicles are consistently wider. A secondary, moderate cluster appears around the theta and delta variables, specifically theta1 and delta1 at roughly 0.49 and theta2 and delta2 at 0.41, where a positive correlation is observed between vehicle heading and steering angle. Outside these pairings, the notable negatives are mostly modest, such as Vol with a2 around -0.33 and Vol with W1 around -0.31, hinting that higher volatility tends to coincide with lower values of those parameters, though not strongly enough to imply a deterministic relationship. Ultimately, the analysis highlights a potential multicollinearity risk concentrated in the length-width pairs, while the near-zero correlations for TTC with most variables indicate that Time to Collision is not linearly explained by any single parameter and likely depends on distributed or non-linear interactions.
