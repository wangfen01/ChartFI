This performance comparison across six protein-related benchmarks illustrates a clear distinction between classical sequence or structure models and advanced protein representation methods, with GLProtein consistently emerging as a top-tier performer. On secondary-structure tasks, performance is relatively high and tightly clustered, where GLProtein shows competitive performance with ProtBERT, OntoProtein, and KeAP in the SS-Q3 task, all peaking at an accuracy of 0.82, while GLProtein outperforms other baselines in the SS-Q8 task by reaching a leading accuracy of 0.68. Contact prediction reveals a broader performance spread, with GLProtein leading at 0.48 and several other models clustered in the low-to-mid 0.40s, while weaker models like Transformer and ResNet fall to approximately 0.25, suggesting this task is a more effective differentiator of representation quality. The largest relative gaps appear in Homology detection, where absolute accuracies remain low across the board; here, ProtBert and KeAP slightly lead at 0.29, followed closely by GLProtein at 0.28, while the weakest methods drop to 0.10, highlighting the task's sensitivity to model choice. Regarding regression-style properties, GLProtein achieves a high Spearman correlation of 0.67 in fluorescence prediction, tying with models like GearNet, LSTM, and KeAP, though slightly trailing the top-performing Transformer at 0.68. In the domain of stability prediction, GLProtein shows the highest performance with a score of 0.81, creating a noticeable gap over the next best model, GearNet, at 0.78. A significant anomaly persists where models like LSTM and ResNet, which remain competitive on SS-Q3 with accuracies of 0.75, drop toward the bottom of the rankings in Homology and Contact prediction, reinforcing that high secondary-structure accuracy does not necessarily translate to proficiency in capturing long-range interactions or evolutionary similarities.
