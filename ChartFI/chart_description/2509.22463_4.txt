The heatmap illustrates the distribution of Block Influence (BI) for Transformer++ and IIET models with varying iteration steps r across 24 layers, where higher BI values indicate lower model redundancy and more effective contribution from specific layers. Transformer++ is highly front-loaded, characterized by a dominant influence at L01 (0.87) followed by a sharp drop to a long stretch of minimal influence around 0.04–0.06 through most mid and late layers, indicating that its behavior is driven primarily by the earliest layer with little differentiation afterward. In contrast, the influence of IIET’s blocks increases significantly with iteration steps, demonstrating higher layer utilization across the network depth. IIET r=1 distributes influence more broadly with a pronounced mid-layer concentration peaking at L08 (0.53) and elevated values around L05–L07, while IIET r=2 shifts emphasis further back, beginning lower at L01 (0.69) but showing a notable late-layer spike at L21 (0.46) and a secondary rise at L24 (0.36). The full IIET model reveals the most structured late-stage dominance; after a strong start at L01 (0.80), influence builds substantially in the second half with clear peaks at L13–L14 and an even stronger surge at L18–L20, reaching 0.77 at L18. This pronounced late-layer concentration contrasts sharply with Transformer++’s near-uniform low influence after the early layers, and this disparity indicates that the learning potential of existing large-scale language models remains under-exploited.
