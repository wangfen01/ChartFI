This visualization compares the bias profiles of two model pairings—DALLE-3 (T2I) with GPT-4o (I2T) and Stable Diffusion (T2I) with LLaVA (I2T)—across categories including Gender (GE), Disability (DA), and Physical Appearance (PA) using five linguistic dimensions. The left panel, representing DALLE-3 and GPT-4o, generally displays a compressed, lower-amplitude profile, confirming that DALLE-3 demonstrates negligible biases in image generation pertaining to gender and sexuality, with scores around GE and SE remaining close to the inner rings. However, this balance is disrupted by a sharp Lexical spike at DA, indicating that GPT-4o reflects high disability biases despite the otherwise moderate values. In contrast, the right panel for Stable Diffusion and LLaVA exhibits a significantly broader spread with more pronounced peaks, illustrating that Stable Diffusion has a higher percentage of negative gender representations than DALLE-3, evidenced by a dramatic Stereotypical peak at GE. Additionally, the right chart shows that biases related to physical appearance in LLaVA remain pronounced, marked by a distinct Lexical elevation at PA, while Implicit bias reaches its maximum levels at DA and AG. Overall, while the left-side models maintain a comparatively balanced distribution with specific exceptions, the right-side models produce stronger, more category-dependent signals, highlighting that Stable Diffusion has higher bias than DALLE-3 in gender images and that disability biases remain a persistent issue across both configurations.
