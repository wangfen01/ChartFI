This heatmap illustrates the frequency of different commit types across five AI domains, revealing that while certain dataset enrichment tasks are universal, the distribution of commit types highlights distinct change patterns in different areas of ML. Overall, the most consistent pattern is the dominance of metadata-related work, with “Add Metadata” representing the strongest signal across every domain; this activity peaks in Audio and Computer Vision at a frequency of 0.7 and remains high in NLP and Reinforcement Learning (0.5–0.6). Beyond this shared baseline, the domains diverge significantly. Domains like Audio and Computer Vision are characterized by a very high frequency of Internal Documentation (0.4) relative to other fields, indicating a strong emphasis on recording internal processes alongside metadata management. In contrast, NLP models show the highest mean frequency for Pipeline Performance. Reinforcement Learning exhibits heavy activity in “Sharing,” “External Documentation,” and “Output Data” (all reaching 0.5), implying frequent iteration on data interfaces and external reporting rather than input data ingestion, which is near zero. A notable anomaly is the rarity of dependency management actions—adding, removing, or updating dependencies are near zero across the board—suggesting that dependency churn is not a primary driver of updates in this dataset.
