These heatmaps visualize the boarding and alighting counts at all stops over one week from Monday to Friday, with the left panel showing boarding flows and the right panel presenting alighting flows. The x-axis represents the order of bus arrivals, effectively serving as a proxy for time, while the y-axis represents the stop IDs, with color intensity indicating the volume of passenger flows. A clear daily periodicity is evident over the course of the week, and the data reveals significant fluctuations in passenger counts within each day, delineating distinct peak and off-peak hours. Boarding is consistently concentrated at the lowest-numbered stops and specific mid-route locations like stop 11, showing a stable, corridor-like demand pattern where certain stops function as primary origins. In contrast, alighting displays a more distributed pattern with noticeable activity not only at early stops but also recurring pockets of higher drop-offs at several mid and higher stop IDs, such as stops 11 and 19, suggesting multiple destination clusters rather than a single dominant terminal. Across the days, the overall structure remains steady. The most salient feature is the presence of intermittent, sharp alighting peaks at specific stops that do not have equally strong boarding counterparts, identifying locations that function primarily as destinations potentially tied to time-specific attractors such as workplaces, schools, or transfer points.
