This heatmap illustrates the pointwise mutual information between text and image frames across the dataset, visualising the degree to which specific thematic lenses align or diverge across modalities. We can see the presence of a diagonal, demonstrating that there is often alignment between frames across modalities; specifically, some frames are used consistently in texts and images for the same article, appearing as dark cells along the diagonal, while other frames differ widely. Regulation stands out with a value of approximately -0.04, indicating it is one of the few frames where text and image align most directly, followed by other relatively strong diagonal associations such as morality (-0.22) and health (-0.61). Beyond these direct matches, notable cross-frame patterns emerge: depictions of quality of life in the images when writing about cultural topics and vice versa is quite common, and quality of life framing in the images is also used when the text is using an economic frame. Furthermore, political framing in the text is associated with policy and public opinion framing, and legal framing in the text is associated with political framing in the images, implying these themes often visually support one another even when not explicitly named. In contrast, lighter shades correspond to more negative values and significant divergences, particularly clustering around the economic and morality dimensions.
