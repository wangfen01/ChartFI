The figure presents two average distance matrices comparing an identical set of conditions, with the left panel corresponding to the ResNet101 model and the right panel to the ViT model. In both matrices, darker diagonal cells indicate near-zero self-distance, while off-diagonal cells capture the degree of dissimilarity between pairs. The ResNet101 matrix exhibits generally low and tightly clustered distances, with many pairwise values falling between the mid-1s and low-2s, suggesting that differences between conditions are relatively subtle in this latent space. Within this context, a clear pattern emerges where the distances between each augmented latent embedding dataset are closer; specifically, the R0.5S50, R0.5S1_GF500, and R1S1_GF500 variants form a distinct, low-distance cluster compared to their relationships with the rest of the dataset. The ViT model preserves this underlying structural relationship but expands the scale dramatically, with off-diagonal distances frequently rising to the 50–115 range, thereby making the separation between groups much more pronounced. In this amplified space, the 'extra' condition becomes the most distant outlier, and the 'normal' condition remains broadly separated from the augmented clusters. Despite the cohesion within the augmented variants, the distinct positioning of the control group indicates that the model continues to effectively differentiate between images, reinforcing that the underlying similarity relationships are consistent across both architectures while highlighting the ViT model's tendency to project these differences onto a vastly larger distance scale.
