The charts track the accuracy and training loss with respect to training rounds on the CIFAR-100 dataset across different data and device settings, with the first row presenting results under the 6:3:1 RoC distribution and the second row corresponding to the 1:1:1 RoC distribution. Under the 6:3:1 RoC distribution in an IID setting, the proposed method (green solid line) achieves the highest accuracy and converges faster than other approaches; it also consistently reduces loss faster and reaches a lower loss, demonstrating better convergence. In comparison, FedRA (pink dashed line) and HETLoRA (orange dashed line) converge more slowly, with HETLoRA stabilizing at a lower accuracy. Notably, FlexLoRA (blue dashed line) exhibits significantly lower accuracy, indicating poor performance in this setting, while Exclusive Learning (deep green dashed line) shows instability with sudden fluctuations visible as sharp vertical spikes in its training loss curve. These performance gaps are amplified in the non-IID setting, where the proposed method and FedRA again lead, while Exclusive Learning still shows instability with sudden fluctuations at the beginning of the training rounds. Under the 1:1:1 RoC distribution, the performance gaps narrow slightly, especially for IID accuracy where most methods converge tightly to a high value. Even so, our method still outperforms other approaches, maintaining higher accuracy and better convergence stability, reflected in its smoother descent to the lowest training loss. The non-IID panels remain the primary differentiator in this setting, where the proposed method and FedRA again establish a clear lead in accuracy and loss reduction over HETLoRA and the significantly underperforming FlexLoRA, while the instability in Exclusive Learning persists.
