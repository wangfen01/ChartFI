This radar chart illustrates the object hallucination rates(C_O) across a hallucination probing set in VirtualHome for various language models, evaluated across five distinct robustness stress tests: Base Prompts, Distractor Injection, Task-Relevant Object Removal, Synonymous Object Substitution, and Scene Task Contradiction. In this visualization, higher values indicate more hallucination, with Scene Task Contradiction triggering the highest rates in nearly all models, reaching a peak near 60 for the most susceptible model and revealing a widespread inability to ground planning to perceived environments. While most models cluster between 20 and 40 on this axis, the significant variance here identifies handling contradictory scene/task cues as the primary driver of performance disparity. Distractor Injection shows the next largest spread, where one specific model, DS-R1-Distil-LLaMA-8B, exhibits a pronounced spike toward the 50-mark, indicating a unique vulnerability to irrelevant but tempting information compared to the rest of the pack which remains largely below 30. In contrast, Task-Relevant Object Removal shows more uniform behavior, with most models converging between 20 and 35, suggesting that the removal of necessary objects increases hallucination rates in a relatively consistent manner across different architectures. Synonymous Object Substitution reveals another specific anomaly where the DS-R1-Distil-LLaMA-8B model again reaches significantly higher hallucination levels near 30, while most other models maintain lower, more stable rates, suggesting that lexical grounding is a differentiator for that specific system. Base Prompts generally yield the lowest hallucination rates across the board, often falling below 10, which establishes a baseline of relatively high accuracy that degrades significantly under stress, particularly when models are faced with environmental contradictions or complex distractors.
