The four panels illustrate the features that are most often chosen by the solutions proposed by the optimization algorithm in the case of the HW index. At the lowest threshold of 0.5, which represents that these variables appear in at least 50% of the best solutions, the signal is dispersed across short to mid-range lags, suggesting broad associations and considerable overlap in plausible predictors. As the threshold increases to 0.75, the field narrows sharply, and the remaining relationships consolidate into clearer lag windows where near-term signals cluster around roughly 0–30 days while a distinct longer-lead cluster emerges around 70–90 days. The group of input variables that are involved in the optimum threshold of 0.85 exhibits a refined structure, with some variables concerning the local conditions at short term, such as T2M-Eur-1, T2M-Loc and Z500-Loc, and other variables involving broader geographical scales and remote regions with predictive skill on the medium and long ranges, specifically exemplified by SST-Glo-4 and Z500-Eur-5. At the most stringent threshold of 0.95, only a small core remains, dominated by the strong long-lead Z500-Eur-5 association and a consistent short-lead group for OLR-Glo-5, T2M-Loc, Z500-Loc and T2M-Eur-1, indicating these are the most robust predictors. Throughout this progression, Z500-based predictors persist across multiple thresholds, implying geopotential height variability is a key precursor signal, while calendar effects such as DOY and broader indices like NAO appear at moderate thresholds but drop out at the highest threshold.
