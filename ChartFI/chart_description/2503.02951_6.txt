The data reveals that the contamination rate is minimal, with only 94 potentially contaminated questions out of 447K, indicating a high degree of dataset integrity. The histogram panel reinforces these findings, showing that maximum cosine similarities cluster tightly in the mid-to-high range but rarely reach the 0.95 threshold required for a contamination designation. Prefill has the highest average maximum cosine similarity, characterized by a right-shifted distribution and a heavier upper tail, which explains why it serves as the primary source of the few identified overlaps. Conversely, the Docs subset has the lowest average maximum cosine similarity, with other subsets like Algorithm and Data_Structure centering around the 0.6 range. Ultimately, the analysis demonstrates that while moderate similarity exists across most specialized KodCode subsets, high-confidence nearest matches are extremely rare, confirming that the benchmarks remain largely distinct from the training data.
