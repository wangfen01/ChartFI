This histogram shows the distribution of % depths in the DTU, ScanNet, ETH3D, Tanks and Temples, and KITTI datasets. Note the log X-axis, which highlights that datasets cover a wide range of depth values and that depth ranges vary hugely across datasets, with each concentrating on distinctly different distance ranges. DTU is tightly clustered at very shallow depths, peaking well below 1 meter and dropping off quickly, indicating a controlled close-range capture setup. ScanNet shifts deeper, with most observations around roughly 1 to 3 meters and a moderate spread, consistent with indoor room-scale scanning. ETH3D and Tanks and Temples both center around mid-range depths, with their highest density near the 8 to 12-meter region and substantial overlap, suggesting similar scene scales and capture distances; ETH3D appears slightly broader, extending more into both shallower and deeper mid-range values. KITTI is the deepest and widest distribution, rising around several meters, peaking at about 10 meters, and then tapering slowly with a pronounced long tail reaching toward 100 meters, reflecting outdoor driving scenes with frequent far-range structure. The most notable pattern is the strong separation between DTU and the rest, while the main anomaly is KITTI’s heavy right tail, which implies greater depth variability and potentially more challenging long-range geometry compared with the other datasets.
